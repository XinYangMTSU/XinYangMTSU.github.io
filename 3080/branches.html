üìò Calculus: The Study of Continuous Change

Calculus is a branch of mathematics focused on instantaneous rates of change and the summation of infinitely small quantities. It consists of two core areas:

  • Differential Calculus: studies change and slopes (e.g., velocity, growth rate)
  • Integral Calculus: deals with accumulation and area under curves

Founders: Gottfried Leibniz (German polymath) and Isaac Newton (English polymath).

Leibniz published his work first. The modern consensus is that both developed their ideas independently.
