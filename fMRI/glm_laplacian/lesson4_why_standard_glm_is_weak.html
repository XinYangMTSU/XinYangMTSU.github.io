Lesson 4 — Why Standard GLM Is Weak

Why independent vertexwise estimation can be noisy and why cortical geometry matters.
IndexLesson 3Lesson 4Lesson 5Lesson 6Lesson 7Lesson 8Lesson 9Lesson 10

1. Standard GLM treats vertices independently

The same design matrix \(X\) is used at every vertex, but the ordinary solution estimates each vertex separately.

\[\hat{\beta}_n=(X^\top X)^{-1}X^\top y_n\]
This means vertex 20 does not “talk to” neighboring vertex 21 during estimation.

2. Why that can be weak

Real cortical activation often forms regions, not isolated one-vertex spikes.

More plausible smooth map

3. The cortex is a mesh, not a list

Your surface data lives on vertices connected by triangles. Neighboring vertices are anatomically related.

Toy cortical mesh
The innovation is to use this mesh structure during estimation, instead of only during visualization.

4. Bias–variance intuition

Ordinary GLM
Low bias, but noisy estimates. High variance can create isolated false-looking peaks.
Regularized GLM
Slightly biased toward smooth maps, but often much lower variance.
\[\text{Expected error} = \text{Bias}^2 + \text{Variance}\]
