Lesson 1 — Standard First-Level GLM

Understanding the single-vertex model before adding surface Laplacian regularization

Goal of this lesson

What are we learning?

Before we can understand the new Surface Laplacian Regularized GLM, we need to understand the ordinary first-level GLM used in the current surface-based fMRI pipeline.

In the attached project, the surface pipeline fits a first-level GLM after projecting the 4D volume onto the cortical surface. The slides describe this as solving \(y = X\beta + \varepsilon\) independently at every surface vertex.

1. The data at one surface vertex

After projecting the fMRI volume to the cortical surface, each vertex has a BOLD time series. For this localizer dataset, there are:

\[ T = 128 \quad \text{time points} \]

So at one vertex, the measured signal is a column vector:

\[ y = \begin{bmatrix} y_1 \\ y_2 \\ y_3 \\ \vdots \\ y_{128} \end{bmatrix} \in \mathbb{R}^{128 \times 1} \]

Each entry \(y_t\) is the measured BOLD intensity at time point \(t\).

Visual intuition

At each surface vertex, we observe a 128-point BOLD signal.

2. The design matrix \(X\)

The design matrix tells the model what task-related signal should look like over time. It is built from events, the HRF model, derivative terms, and drift regressors.

\[ X \in \mathbb{R}^{128 \times 18} \]
DimensionMeaning
128 rowsone row per fMRI volume / time point
18 columnstask regressors + HRF derivatives + drift regressors
Each column of \(X\) is a predicted time course for one regressor.

3. The unknown coefficients \(\beta\)

At one vertex, the GLM estimates one coefficient for each column of the design matrix. Since \(X\) has 18 columns, \(\beta\) has 18 entries.

\[ \beta = \begin{bmatrix} \beta_1 \\ \beta_2 \\ \vdots \\ \beta_{18} \end{bmatrix} \in \mathbb{R}^{18 \times 1} \]

A large positive coefficient means that the corresponding regressor explains the measured BOLD signal well at this vertex. A value near zero means that regressor does not explain much signal at this vertex.

4. The first-level GLM equation

\[ \underbrace{y}_{128 \times 1} = \underbrace{X}_{128 \times 18} \underbrace{\beta}_{18 \times 1} + \underbrace{\varepsilon}_{128 \times 1} \]
SymbolMeaningShape
\(y\)measured BOLD time series at one vertex\(128 \times 1\)
\(X\)design matrix\(128 \times 18\)
\(\beta\)unknown effect sizes\(18 \times 1\)
\(\varepsilon\)unexplained noise\(128 \times 1\)
Dimension check: \(X\beta\) has shape \((128 \times 18)(18 \times 1)=128 \times 1\), so it can be compared directly with \(y\).

5. What does GLM fitting do?

The model chooses \(\beta\) so that the predicted signal \(X\beta\) is close to the observed signal \(y\).

\[ \hat{\beta} = \arg\min_{\beta} \frac{1}{2} \|y-X\beta\|_2^2 \]
1
Compute the predicted BOLD time series: \[ \hat y = X\beta \]
2
Compute the residual: \[ r = y-\hat y = y-X\beta \]
3
Choose \(\beta\) that makes the squared residual as small as possible: \[ \|r\|_2^2 = r^\top r \]

6. Deriving the ordinary least-squares solution

Start with the objective:

\[ J(\beta) = \frac{1}{2} \|y-X\beta\|_2^2 \]

Rewrite the norm:

\[ J(\beta) = \frac{1}{2} (y-X\beta)^\top(y-X\beta) \]

Take derivative with respect to \(\beta\):

\[ \nabla_\beta J = X^\top(X\beta-y) \]

Set derivative equal to zero:

\[ X^\top(X\hat\beta-y)=0 \]

Expand:

\[ X^\top X\hat\beta - X^\top y=0 \]

Solve for \(\hat\beta\):

\[ \boxed{ \hat\beta = (X^\top X)^{-1}X^\top y } \]
This formula is the mathematical baseline. In real fMRI software, additional details such as temporal autocorrelation, drift modeling, and masking are handled carefully, but this is the core idea.

7. What happens across the whole surface?

The same GLM is repeated at every vertex. The design matrix \(X\) stays the same. Only the measured BOLD signal \(y\) changes.

Current approach: fit one independent GLM at each surface vertex.
\[ \hat\beta_n = (X^\top X)^{-1}X^\top y_n, \qquad n=1,\ldots,N \]

8. Why Lesson 1 matters for the new project

The proposed project does not change the events, HRF, design matrix, or contrasts. It changes how the coefficient maps are estimated across space.
