Logistic Regression Example

Predicting Student Exam Pass/Fail Based on Study Hours

The Problem

We want to predict whether a student will pass (1) or fail (0) an exam based on the number of hours they studied.

Our Training Data

Student Study Hours (x) Result (y)

Making Predictions

Let's predict for different study hours:

Example 1: Student studied 1 hour

Step 1: Calculate z

z = -4.0 + 2.0 √ó 1 = -2.0

Step 2: Apply sigmoid

P(Pass) = 1 / (1 + e2.0) = 1 / (1 + 7.389) = 0.119

Result: 11.9% chance of passing ‚Üí Predict FAIL

Example 2: Student studied 2 hours

Step 1: Calculate z

z = -4.0 + 2.0 √ó 2 = 0.0

Step 2: Apply sigmoid

P(Pass) = 1 / (1 + e0) = 1 / (1 + 1) = 0.50

Result: 50% chance of passing ‚Üí Right at the decision boundary!

Example 3: Student studied 3 hours

Step 1: Calculate z

z = -4.0 + 2.0 √ó 3 = 2.0

Step 2: Apply sigmoid

P(Pass) = 1 / (1 + e-2.0) = 1 / (1 + 0.135) = 0.881

Result: 88.1% chance of passing ‚Üí Predict PASS

Example 4: Student studied 4 hours

Step 1: Calculate z

z = -4.0 + 2.0 √ó 4 = 4.0

Step 2: Apply sigmoid

P(Pass) = 1 / (1 + e-4.0) = 1 / (1 + 0.018) = 0.982
