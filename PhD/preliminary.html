4

Ordinary GLM solution

The standard GLM chooses \(B\) so that \(XB\) is close to \(Y\).

\[ \hat B = \arg\min_B \frac12\|Y-XB\|_F^2 \]
\[ \hat B_{\text{OLS}}=(X^\top X)^{-1}X^\top Y \]
Measured signal, fitted signal, and residuals
Key limitation: this estimates each vertex independently. Neighboring vertices do not communicate.
5

Why ordinary GLM is weak

The cortex is not a bag of unrelated points. It is a connected surface. Neighboring vertices are usually related.

Suspicious isolated spike
More plausible smooth region
Ordinary GLM can produce high-variance, fragmented maps because it does not use surface geometry.

7

Understand the Laplacian penalty

For one beta map \(b\), the Laplacian penalty is:

\[ b^\top Lb=\frac12\sum_{i,j}W_{ij}(b_i-b_j)^2 \]
Large penalty: neighboring vertices have very different beta values.
Small penalty: neighboring vertices have similar beta values.
Plain English: the method pays a cost when activation jumps sharply between neighboring vertices.
Why is it called “Laplacian”?

The Laplacian is a mathematical operator that measures how different a value is from its neighbors.
In continuous space, it uses derivatives.
On a surface mesh, it becomes the matrix \(L = D - W\).
That’s why it’s called the “Laplacian” — it’s the discrete version of the same concept.
8

New regularized GLM

We add the Laplacian penalty to the ordinary GLM objective.

\[ \hat B = \arg\min_B \frac12\|Y-XB\|_F^2 + \frac{\lambda}{2}\operatorname{tr}(BLB^\top) \]
TermMeaning
\(\frac12\|Y-XB\|_F^2\)fit the measured BOLD data
\(\frac{\lambda}{2}\operatorname{tr}(BLB^\top)\)make beta maps smooth along cortex
\(\lambda\)controls smoothness strength
When \(\lambda=0\), this becomes ordinary GLM. Larger \(\lambda\) means stronger smoothness.
9

Derive the final equation

Start with the objective:

\[ J(B)= \frac12\|Y-XB\|_F^2+ \frac{\lambda}{2}\operatorname{tr}(BLB^\top) \]

Take derivatives:

\[ \nabla_B\frac12\|Y-XB\|_F^2=X^\top(XB-Y) \]
\[ \nabla_B\frac{\lambda}{2}\operatorname{tr}(BLB^\top)=\lambda BL \]

Set gradient to zero and rearrange:

\[ X^\top(XB-Y)+\lambda BL=0 \]
\[ X^\top XB+\lambda BL=X^\top Y \]
This equation combines the temporal GLM model and the spatial surface geometry.

12

Explain the innovation

Standard GLM asks:
At each vertex, what task regressors explain the BOLD signal?
Proposed method asks:
What activation maps explain BOLD while being smooth along cortex?

The innovation is not a new design matrix. The innovation is a new estimator for the beta maps.

\[ \text{standard GLM}+\text{surface geometry}=\text{regularized GLM} \]

14

Defense explanation script

Standard first-level GLM estimates each cortical vertex independently. However, cortical fMRI signals are spatially structured on a connected surface. We introduce a Laplacian regularized GLM that keeps the same design matrix and contrasts but adds a surface-geometry penalty. This encourages neighboring vertices to have similar beta values, reducing variance and improving stability. We evaluate the method through semi-synthetic recovery, held-out prediction, reproducibility, and smoothness analysis.
