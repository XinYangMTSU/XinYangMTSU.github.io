🇬🇧 Modern English Alphabet

  • Did you know? Unlike Latin or Etruscan, each English letter has a spoken name!
  • That’s why kids learn "A-B-C-D" as "ay, bee, cee, dee" and not just sounds.
  • 🎵 This makes English fun and musical when singing the alphabet song! 🎶

Letter Name Modern Use
AA🔤 Start of the alphabet, common vowel
BBee🅱️ Used in words like "ball"
CCee🌊 "C" can sound like "k" or "s"
DDee🔤 "D" in words like "dog"
EE📚 Common vowel in English words
FEf🎏 Found in "fish" and "fun"
GGee🎸 Can be hard ("go") or soft ("giant")
HAitch🏠 "H" as in "house"
IEye👁️ Vowel in words like "ice"
JJay🌟 "J" as in "jump"
KKay🔑 "K" in "kite" or "key"
LEl🍋 "L" in "lemon"
MEm🍈 "M" in "moon"
NEn📰 "N" in "newspaper"
OOh🔵 Common vowel, "O" in "open"
PPee🍍 "P" as in "pineapple"
QQueue👑 "Q" in "queen" (always with "u")
RAr🚗 "R" in "road"
SEss🐍 "S" in "snake"
TTee🌳 "T" in "tree"
UYou☂️ "U" in "umbrella"
VVee🎻 "V" as in "violin"
WDouble-U🌊 "W" as in "water"
XEx❌ "X" in "xylophone" or "box"
YWhy🍋 "Y" in "yellow"
ZZee (US) / Zed (UK)🦓 "Z" in "zebra"

🕰️ English Alphabet Timeline

📅 Time Period 📝 Event
🏺 Old English Based on Latin with some extra letters (like "þ" and "ð")
🏛️ Middle English Latin alphabet becomes more standard, "J," "U," "W" added
📜 Early Modern English Spelling and pronunciation begin to settle (Shakespeare's era)
🇬🇧 Modern English Standard 26-letter alphabet used worldwide
