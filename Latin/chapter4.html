🏡 Chapter 4 – A Day in the Life of Ancient Romans

👋 Come explore how ancient Roman kids lived their daily lives! 📜

🏠 Inside a Roman Home

Romans lived in homes called domus if they were rich, or insulae (apartments) if they were poor.

  • atrium – the central room where guests entered
  • triclinium – dining room with couches
  • culina – kitchen
  • cubiculum – bedroom
🏡 Fun Fact: Roman houses had no glass windows! They used curtains or wooden shutters instead!

👩‍🏫 Roman Kids Like You!

Roman children learned reading, writing, and math. Boys went to school with a magister (teacher) while girls often learned at home.

They played games like ball toss, spinning tops, and even a version of tic-tac-toe!

  • puer = boy
  • puella = girl
  • ludus = school or game
🏀 Fun Fact: Roman kids played with dolls, carts, dice, and marbles just like kids today!

🌾 What Did They Eat?

Romans enjoyed simple meals. They ate bread, cheese, fruits, olives, and sometimes fish or meat.

  • panis = bread
  • caseus = cheese
  • uva = grape
  • aqua = water
🧀 Did you know? Romans didn’t use forks! They used spoons and their fingers.

👕 Roman Clothing

Romans wore tunics every day. Rich men wore togas; women wore long stolas.

  • toga = a formal robe for men
  • tunica = a simple tunic
  • stola = dress for women
💼 Fun Fact: Only Roman citizens could wear a toga!

🏢 The Marketplace

The forum was a busy place where people bought food, clothes, and tools.

  • mercator = merchant
  • pecunia = money
  • cibus = food
💸 Fun Fact: Roman coins often had emperors' faces on them!

🎠 Bath Time!

Rich Romans relaxed in public bathhouses. They cleaned, exercised, and socialized there.

  • balneum = bath
  • caldarium = hot room
  • frigidarium = cold room
🚿 Fun Fact: Romans used olive oil instead of soap to clean their skin!

🎯 Games & Fun!

Romans played games with dice (“alea”), hoops, balls, and knucklebones.

  • ludus = game
  • pila = ball
  • talus = knucklebones (like jacks)
🚀 Did you know? Roman kids even played pretend chariot races!

🕰️ A Roman Kid's Typical Day

Let's see what a day was like for a Roman child!

  • ⏰ Wake Up: Around sunrise (~6:00 AM). No alarm clocks – the morning light woke them up!
  • 🧼 Morning Routine: Wash face and hands with water (no toothpaste yet!)
  • 🍞 Breakfast ("ientaculum"): Bread, cheese, fruit, and water.
  • 🏫 School Time: School started early, around 7:00 AM.
  • 📖 What They Learned: Reading, writing, math, poetry, and sometimes Greek.
  • 🎨 Subjects: Grammar, rhetoric, arithmetic, music, philosophy (for older students).
  • 🥗 Lunch ("prandium"): Around noon – cold leftovers, bread, fruits, olives.
  • 🏠 End of School: About 2:00 PM.
  • 🍽️ Dinner ("cena"): Around 4:00–5:00 PM – stew, bread, veggies, fish, meat.
  • 🛏️ Bedtime: Around 7:00–8:00 PM after sunset!
🌟 Fun Fact: Rich Roman kids had slaves who carried their heavy schoolbooks!
