Word Bank: floral, florist, flourish, floriculture

1. A person who sells flowers is a __________.
2. She wore a __________ dress with flowers on it.
3. The business began to __________ after spring.
4. __________ is the science of growing flowers.

Word Explanations with Audio

"Flos" is a Latin root that means "flower". Many English words come from this root! For example:

Floral - having to do with flowers.
Florist - a person who sells flowers.
Flourish - to grow or develop successfully.
Floriculture - the science of growing flowers.

Word Building Diagram

Flos + -al ➡ Floral
Flos + -ist ➡ Florist
Flos + -ish ➡ Flourish
Flos + culture ➡ Floriculture

Breakdown Explanations

  • 🌸 Flos + -al = Floral: "Flos" means flower and "-al" means "relating to." Together, "Floral" means "relating to flowers."
  • 🌷 Flos + -ist = Florist: "Flos" means flower and "-ist" means "a person who does something." A "Florist" is someone who sells flowers.
  • 🌻 Flos + -ish = Flourish: Originally from "flos," but now means "to grow" or "to thrive" 🌱. Related to the idea of blooming like a flower.
  • 🌼 Flos + culture = Floriculture: "Flos" + "culture" (growing/cultivation) means "the growing of flowers."
  • 📚 The Latin word "flos" means "flower", but in Latin, when combining roots into new words, "flos" often becomes "flor-" as a stem (base form) when adding prefixes or suffixes. This makes the word sound smoother and stick to Latin grammar rules.
  • 🪶📜 flos + -ist ➡ flor + ist = florist, flos + -al ➡ flor + al = floral,
  • 🔍 "Flourish" comes from the Latin root flos (flower) and Old French flourir, meaning "to thrive or bloom.
  • 🎯 Flos + culture ➡ flor + culture ➡ floriculture (In Latin, when combining two roots and the second root starts with a consonant, Latin often inserts a connecting vowel—most commonly "i"—to make the word easier to pronounce.)
