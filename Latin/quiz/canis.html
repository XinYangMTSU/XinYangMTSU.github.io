🐕 CANIS Fun Quiz! 🐾

Word Bank: canine, kennel, cynophobia, canid

1. The word __________ refers to anything related to dogs.
2. A place where dogs are kept is called a __________.
3. The fear of dogs is called __________.
4. Wolves, foxes, and dogs are all part of the __________ family.

Word Explanations with Audio

"Canis" is a Latin root that means "dog". Many English words come from this root! For example:

Canine - relating to dogs.
Kennel - a place where dogs are kept.
Cynophobia - fear of dogs.
Canid - a member of the dog family, like wolves or foxes.

Word Building Diagram

Canis + -ine ➡ Canine
Canis ➡ Kennel (via Old French "kenil")
Cyno- + -phobia ➡ Cynophobia
Canid ➡ Dog Family

Breakdown Explanations

  • 🐶 Canis + -ine = Canine: "Canine" means related to dogs.
  • 🏠 Kennel: Comes from Old French but still linked to dogs.
  • 😨 Cyno + phobia = Cynophobia: "Cyno" comes from Greek "kyon" (dog), "phobia" means fear.
  • 🦊 Canid: Refers to any member of the dog family like wolves, foxes, and domestic dogs.
