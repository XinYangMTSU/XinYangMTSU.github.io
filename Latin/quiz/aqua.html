Word Explanations with Audio

"Aqua" is a Latin root that means "water". Many English words come from this root! For example:

Aquarium - a place where water animals live.
Aquatic - living or growing in water.
Aquifer - an underground layer that holds water.
Aquamarine - a blue-green color, like the sea.

Word Building Diagram

Aqua + -arium ➡ Aquarium
Aqua + -tic ➡ Aquatic
Aqua + -fer ➡ Aquifer
Aqua + marine ➡ Aquamarine

Breakdown Explanations

  • 🏛️ Aqua + -arium = Aquarium: "Aqua" means water and "-arium" means "a place for." Together, "Aquarium" means "a place for water," or "a place for water animals." When two identical vowels meet, we often drop one.
  • 🌊 Aqua + -tic = Aquatic: "Aqua" means water and "-tic" means "relating to." So "Aquatic" means "relating to water," or "living or growing in water."
  • 💧 Aqua + -fer = Aquifer: "Aqua" means water and "-fer" comes from "ferre," meaning "to carry." An "Aquifer" is "something that carries or holds water," like an underground water source. In classical Latin, when a root ending in a vowel (like "aqua") is joined with "fer", an "i" is inserted to "glue" them together. This "i" is actually part of a combining vowel rule in Latin morphology.
  • 🌅 Aqua + marine = Aquamarine: "Aqua" means water and "marine" means "of the sea." Together, "Aquamarine" refers to "water of the sea," often describing its blue-green color.
  • 📚 "-arium", "-tic", and "-fer" are bound morphemes, they cannot stand alone as words. The hyphen shows it's an affix, not a full word. "marine" is a full, independent word in English, it doesn’t need a hyphen.
