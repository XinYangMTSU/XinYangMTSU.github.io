🔢 Chapter 3 – The Latin Roman Numerals

Romans didn’t use numbers like 1, 2, 3... They used letters to count! These are called Roman numerals, and you can still find them today — on clocks, buildings, movie titles, and even Super Bowls!

Let's see how numbers evolved over time — from tally marks to the numbers we use today!

Time Period / Origin System Number Style Notes
🏞️ Ancient Times Tally Marks | || ||| |||| Simple marks to count objects or days
🏺 Ancient Egypt Hieroglyphic Numerals 𓏺𓎆𓍢 (symbols for 1, 10, 100) Used symbols for 1, 10, 100, etc. Based on repeated icons.
🏛️ Romans Roman Numerals I, II, III, IV, V, X, L, C Letter-based system. Still used today on clocks, books, etc.
📜 India (500s CE) Brahmi / Early Hindu Numerals १ २ ३ ४ ५ ६ ७ ८ ९ ० Invented the concept of zero and place value.
✍️ Islamic Golden Age (700–1200 CE) Arabic Numerals ١ ٢ ٣ ٤ ٥ ٦ ٧ ٨ ٩ ٠ Spread the Indian system across the Middle East, refined the shape of the digits.
📚 Medieval Europe (1100s–1400s) Western Arabic (Hindu–Arabic) 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8 9 0 Europeans adopted the system via trade, science, and Fibonacci’s Liber Abaci (1202).
💻 Modern Worldwide Use Decimal System 1–100… We now use 0–9 in a base-10 system, everywhere from calculators to computers!

🏛️ Fun Examples

  • Super Bowl L = Super Bowl 50
  • Year MMXXV = 2025
  • Rocky II = Rocky 2
  • Clock faces often show IV as IIII for 4

🧠 Did You Know?

Romans didn’t use a number for zero! They only used positive whole numbers for counting.
