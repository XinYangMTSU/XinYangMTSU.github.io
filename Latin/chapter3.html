🔢 What are Roman Numerals?

Roman numerals are a number system invented by the Romans. Instead of 1, 2, 3… they used special letters like I, V, and X! Let’s see how they count from 1 to 100:

Here are the main Roman numeral letters and their values:
  • I = 1
  • V = 5
  • X = 10
  • L = 50
  • C = 100
  • D = 500
  • M = 1000
Fun Fact: The Romans didn’t have a symbol for zero! 🧐 They only counted what they could see!

🏛️ The Roman numeral system is not positional, which makes math hard—there's no place value like in 123 (hundreds/tens/units).

🧮 How do Roman numerals work?

Roman numerals are formed by combining these letters in certain ways:

  • To add, you put the smaller number after the larger: VI = 5 + 1 = 6
  • To subtract, you put the smaller number before the larger: IV = 5 - 1 = 4

For example:

  • VIII = 5 + 3 = 8
  • IX = 10 - 1 = 9
  • XL = 50 - 10 = 40
  • LX = 50 + 10 = 60
  • XC = 100 - 10 = 90
  • CLVI = 100 + 50 + 5 + 1 = 156
  • CCXLVIII = 100 + 100 + (50 - 10) + 5 + 3 = 248
  • MMXXV = 1000 + 1000 + 10 + 10 + 5 = 2025

Once you know these rules, you can build any number with just a few symbols!

🏛️ Where Do We Still See Roman Numerals Today?

Even though we don’t use Roman numerals in everyday math, we still see them in many cool places!

  • 🕰️ Clock faces – Numbers like IV, IX, and XII appear on Roman-style clocks.
  • 📚 Book chapter numbers – Chapters in books often use Roman numerals (e.g., Chapter IX).
  • 🎬 Movie titles – Like Rocky II or Super Bowl XLVIII!
  • 📜 Ancient inscriptions – Buildings, statues, and coins from ancient Rome often used Roman numerals for dates.
  • 🏫 School emblems and diplomas – Many schools use Roman numerals to show founding years (e.g., MDCCLXXVI for 1776).

Roman numerals are like a secret code from history—still hiding in plain sight today!

📊 How Did Numbers Evolve?

Let’s explore how different cultures across history wrote numbers—and how we got the system we use today!

  • 🌿 Tally Marks – The earliest counting system used by prehistoric people (~30,000 BCE).
  • 🏺 Egyptian Hieroglyphs – Used around 3000 BCE for trade, taxes, and construction.
  • 🔺 Babylonian Cuneiform – From Mesopotamia (~2000 BCE), used base-60 system for astronomy and recordkeeping.
  • 🐉 Chinese Rod Numerals – Used in ancient China (~1000 BCE) for calculations on counting boards.
  • 🏛️ Roman Numerals – Popular in ancient Rome (~500 BCE), used letters like I, V, X to count.
  • 🕉️ Indian (Brahmi) – Appeared in India (~300 BCE), first to include zero and place value.
  • ✍️ Arabic Numerals – From the Islamic Golden Age (~700 CE), spread the Indian number system with stylized digits.
  • 🌐 Modern Decimal – The numbers 0–9 we use today, standardized worldwide by ~1500 CE.
