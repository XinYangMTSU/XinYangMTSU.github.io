🔢 What are Roman Numerals?

Roman numerals are a number system invented by the Romans. Instead of 1, 2, 3… they used special letters like I, V, and X! Let’s see how they count from 1 to 100:

Here are the main Roman numeral letters and their values:
  • I = 1
  • V = 5
  • X = 10
  • L = 50
  • C = 100
  • D = 500
  • M = 1000
Fun Fact: The Romans didn’t have a symbol for zero! 🧐 They only counted what they could see!

🧮 How do Roman numerals work?

Roman numerals are formed by combining these letters in certain ways:

  • To add, you put the smaller number after the larger: VI = 5 + 1 = 6
  • To subtract, you put the smaller number before the larger: IV = 5 - 1 = 4

For example:

  • VIII = 5 + 3 = 8
  • IX = 10 - 1 = 9
  • XL = 50 - 10 = 40
  • LX = 50 + 10 = 60
  • XC = 100 - 10 = 90

Once you know these rules, you can build any number with just a few symbols!
