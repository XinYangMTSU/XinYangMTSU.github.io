🌟 vaunt / vaunted /vɔːnt/

Part of speech: Verb (*vaunt*), Adjective (*vaunted*)

📖 Vaunt (verb):

To boast or brag about something—especially in a showy, prideful way.

Example: He constantly vaunts his business success.

📖 Vaunted (adjective):

Describes something that has been praised or advertised—often too much or with irony.

Example: The team’s vaunted offense failed in the final game.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Boast
  • Brag
  • Show off
  • Flaunt
  • Hype up
  • Praise (excessively)

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let's explore the origin of "vaunt" and "vaunted".

📝From Old French vanter, from Latin vanitare = “to talk emptily or boastfully,” based on vanus, meaning “empty” or “vain.”

💡 So when someone vaunts, they're speaking with “empty pride”—and something vaunted may be praised without real substance.
