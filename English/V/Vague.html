🌫️ vague /veɪɡ/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Not clearly expressed, defined, or understood; lacking detail or certainty.
含糊的；模糊不清的；不明确的；不清晰的。

Example: He gave only a vague answer when asked about the plan.
例句：当被问到这个计划时，他只给出了一个模糊的回答。
Example: I have a vague memory of visiting this place as a child.
例句：我依稀记得小时候来过这个地方。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Unclear 不清楚的
  • Ambiguous 模棱两可的
  • Hazy 朦胧的；模糊的
  • Indefinite 不明确的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Vague" comes from Latin vagus, meaning “wandering” or “uncertain.” It entered English through French.
“Vague” 源自拉丁语 vagus，意为“游荡的”或“不确定的”，通过法语进入英语。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: vagus – wandering, uncertain
拉丁语 vagus：漂泊的，不确定的

Old French: vague – indistinct
古法语 vague：模糊的

Modern English: vague – not clearly defined
现代英语 vague：不清晰的，不明确的

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Unclear Neutral The instructions were unclear. Direct
Ambiguous Formal The law's language is ambiguous. Critical
Vague Standard She gave a vague response. Neutral / Slightly negative
