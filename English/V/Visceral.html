🫀 visceral /ˈvɪsərəl/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Visceral refers to a deep, instinctive feeling that comes from the body or emotions rather than the intellect. It's something you feel strongly and physically, like a "gut reaction."
visceral 意为“发自内心的、直觉的”，强调一种来自本能或身体的强烈情感反应，而非理性思考。

Example: She had a visceral reaction of fear when she heard the sudden loud noise.
例句：听到突如其来的巨响时，她本能地感到恐惧。
Example: The movie evoked a visceral sense of sorrow and empathy.
例句：这部电影激发了一种发自内心的悲伤和同理之情。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Instinctive 本能的
  • Gut-level 直觉层面的
  • Raw 原始的
  • Emotional 情绪化的
  • Deep-seated 根深蒂固的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Visceral" comes from Latin viscera, meaning "internal organs." It originally referred to the body’s physical organs but evolved to describe deep emotional responses that feel like they're coming from the body itself.
“visceral” 来自拉丁语 viscera，意思是“内脏”。最初用于描述身体器官，后来引申为发自内心、强烈而直接的情感反应。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: viscera – internal organs
拉丁语 viscera：内脏
⬇️
Modern English: visceral – instinctive or deeply emotional response
现代英语 visceral：本能的、深层次的情感反应
