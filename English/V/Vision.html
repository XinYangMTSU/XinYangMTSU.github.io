👁️ vision /ˈvɪʒən/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

1. The ability to see. 2. The ability to imagine a future goal or idea with wisdom and creativity.
1. 视力；视觉。
2. 对未来的构想；远见卓识。

Example: She has perfect vision in both eyes.
例句：她双眼视力完好。
Example: The company’s success is driven by the CEO’s vision for the future.
例句：这家公司的成功来自于CEO对未来的远见。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Sight 视力
  • Foresight 远见
  • Insight 洞察力
  • Dream 梦想

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Vision" comes from Latin visio, meaning “the act of seeing.”
vision 来自拉丁语 visio，意为“看见的动作”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: videre – to see → visio – sight
拉丁语 videre：看 → visio：视觉
⬇️
Middle English: visioun – supernatural sight or dream
中古英语 visioun：神视或异象
⬇️
Modern English: vision – sight or forward-thinking idea
现代英语 vision：视觉或前瞻性构想

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Sight Neutral He lost sight in one eye. Physical / Neutral
Insight Formal Her insight helped solve the issue. Intellectual / Respectful
Vision Neutral / Inspirational The founder had a clear vision for change. Positive / Ambitious
