🏚️ vacant /ˈveɪkənt/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Not occupied; empty. Can refer to a space, position, or even a facial expression that lacks thought or emotion.
空着的；无人居住的；空缺的；（表情）茫然的。

Example: The room was completely vacant.
例句：这个房间完全空着。
Example: There is a vacant position in the marketing department.
例句：市场部门有一个职位空缺。
Example: He stared ahead with a vacant expression.
例句：他面无表情地盯着前方。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Empty 空的
  • Unoccupied 无人占用的
  • Available 可用的
  • Blank 空白的；茫然的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Vacant" comes from Latin vacare, meaning “to be empty” or “to be free.” It entered English in the late 14th century to describe unoccupied space or positions.
vacant 源自拉丁语 vacare，意为“空的”或“空闲的”。14 世纪末进入英语，用于描述无人占用的空间或职位。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: vacare – to be empty
拉丁语 vacare：空的
⬇️
Old French: vacant – unoccupied
古法语 vacant：空缺的
⬇️
Modern English: vacant – empty or unoccupied
现代英语 vacant：空的；无人使用的
