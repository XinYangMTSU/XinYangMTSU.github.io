🌪️ vortices /ˈvɔːrtɪˌsiːz/

Part of speech: Noun (plural of “vortex”)
词性：名词（“vortex”的复数形式）

📖 Meaning:

Whirling masses of fluid or air, such as whirlpools or tornadoes; places of intense movement or activity.
旋涡；气旋；剧烈运动或混乱的中心。

Example: The aircraft was caught in several air vortices during the storm.
例句：飞机在暴风中被卷入多个空气旋涡中。
Example: The city’s protests became emotional vortices of anger and hope.
例句：这座城市的抗议活动成了愤怒与希望交织的情绪旋涡。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Whirlpools 漩涡
  • Spirals 螺旋形
  • Swirls 旋动
  • Tornadoes 龙卷风

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Vortex" comes from Latin vortex or vortēx, meaning “eddy” or “whirlpool,” derived from vertere, “to turn.”
vortex 源自拉丁语 vortex 或 vortēx，意为“涡流”或“漩涡”，其词根 vertere 意为“旋转”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: vertere – to turn
拉丁语 vertere：旋转
⬇️
Latin: vortex – whirl, eddy
拉丁语 vortex：涡流
⬇️
Modern English: vortices – plural of vortex, spiral motion or chaos
现代英语 vortices：vortex 的复数形式，指旋转或混乱的中心
