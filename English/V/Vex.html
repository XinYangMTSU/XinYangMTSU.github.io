🌟 vex /vɛks/

Part of speech: Verb

📖 As a verb (action):

To vex someone means to annoy, worry, or frustrate them—especially with something trivial or repetitive.

Example: The constant noise from the neighbors began to vex her. (= It annoyed her a lot.)

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Annoy
  • Irritate
  • Frustrate
  • Bother
  • Disturb
  • Pester

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "vex".

📝It comes from the Latin verb: vexare, which means "to shake, jolt, harass."

  • vexare = to shake or trouble

✍️ The word entered English in the 15th century, carrying the meaning of troubling or bothering someone.
