😠 vex /vɛks/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To annoy, worry, or upset someone, especially with trivial matters.
使恼火，使烦恼，尤指因琐事而使人心烦意乱。

Example: The constant noise began to vex the neighbors.
例句：持续不断的噪音开始惹恼邻居们。
Example: Her careless attitude vexes him to no end.
例句：她漫不经心的态度让他非常恼火。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Annoy 惹恼
  • Irritate 激怒
  • Provoke 挑衅
  • Bother 烦扰

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Vex" comes from Latin vexare, meaning “to shake or disturb.”
vex 源自拉丁语 vexare，意为“摇动”或“打扰”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: vexare – to shake, disturb
拉丁语 vexare：摇动，扰乱
⬇️
Old French: vexer – to harass
古法语 vexer：骚扰，烦扰
⬇️
Modern English: vex – to annoy or irritate
现代英语 vex：使恼火，使烦恼

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Annoy Neutral The buzzing sound annoyed me. Common / Everyday
Irritate Neutral / Slightly Formal The smoke irritated his eyes. Physical / Emotional
Vex Formal / Literary The delay vexed everyone in the room. Frustrated / Elevated tone
