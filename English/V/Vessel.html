🚢 vessel /ˈvɛsəl/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A container for holding liquids; also used to refer to a ship or a large boat; or a tube in the body that carries blood or other fluids.
容器；船只；血管（或其他体液管道）。

Example: She poured the soup into a large ceramic vessel.
例句：她把汤倒进一个大的陶瓷容器里。
Example: The cargo vessel docked at the harbor this morning.
例句：这艘货船今天早上停靠在港口。
Example: The surgeon carefully examined the blood vessels during the operation.
例句：外科医生在手术中仔细检查了血管。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Container 容器
  • Ship 船只
  • Craft 船（较小）
  • Blood vessel 血管

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Vessel" comes from Old French vaissel, from Latin vascellum, a diminutive of vas meaning “container or dish.” It later came to refer to ships and bodily fluid tubes.
“Vessel” 源自古法语 vaissel，拉丁语 vascellum（vas 的小词），vas 意为“容器、器皿”，后引申为船只和体内流体通道。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: vas – container
拉丁语 vas：容器

Old French: vaissel – dish, ship
古法语 vaissel：器皿、船

Modern English: vessel – container, ship, or bodily tube
现代英语 vessel：容器、船只或体内通道

📊 Comparison
