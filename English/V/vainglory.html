🌟 vainglory /ˈveɪnˌɡlɔːri/

Part of speech: Noun

📖 Meaning:

Vainglory means excessive pride in oneself, especially without real cause. It refers to boastfulness, shallow pride, and seeking admiration for unworthy or exaggerated achievements.

Example: His endless bragging about minor accomplishments revealed his vainglory. (= His pride was hollow and exaggerated.)

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Boastfulness
  • Arrogance
  • Conceit
  • Self-importance
  • Vanity
  • Hubris

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "vainglory".

📝It comes from two Middle English words combined: vain (meaning empty) + glory (meaning fame or honor).

  • Vain = empty, worthless
  • Glory = honor, praise

✍️ The idea is that the "glory" a person shows is **empty**, **without real value**, or **undeserved**.

💡 The word was used heavily in religious writings (especially Christianity) to warn against pride that disconnects someone from true humility and virtue.
