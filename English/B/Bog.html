🪵 bog /bɑːɡ/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

As a noun: A wet, spongy area of land, often like a swamp or marsh.
（名词）沼泽地；湿软泥泞的地区。

As a verb (usually passive, “bogged down”): To be stuck or slowed down, especially in work or difficulty.
（动词，常用于“bogged down”）陷入困境；被拖慢。

Example (noun): The hikers got lost in the bog.
例句（名词）：徒步者在沼泽中迷路了。
Example (verb): We got bogged down in paperwork.
例句（动词）：我们被一堆文书工作拖住了。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Swamp 沼泽
  • Marsh 湿地
  • Mire 泥潭；困境
  • Quagmire 泥泞地；困境

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Bog" likely comes from the Irish or Scottish Gaelic word bogach, meaning "soft" or "boggy ground."
bog 可能源自爱尔兰语或苏格兰盖尔语 bogach，意为“湿软的地面”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Gaelic: bogach – soft, marshy ground
盖尔语 bogach：松软的泥地
⬇️
Modern English: bog – wetland or figurative trouble
现代英语 bog：沼泽地或比喻上的困境
