🧱 beam /biːm/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

As a noun: A long, sturdy piece of wood, metal, or other material used in construction to support weight, such as a floor or roof.
名词：梁，支撑地板或屋顶等结构的长条状材料。

As a verb: To shine brightly, or to smile broadly with happiness or pride.
动词：发光；洋溢笑容。

Example (noun): The ceiling was supported by thick wooden beams.
例句（名词）：天花板由粗木梁支撑。
Example (verb): She beamed with pride after receiving the award.
例句（动词）：她因获得奖项而喜形于色。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Girders 大梁
  • Support 支撑物
  • Ray (light) 光线
  • Grin (smile) 露齿而笑

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Beam" comes from Old English bēam, meaning “tree” or “wooden post.” It evolved to mean a long support in buildings, and also came to describe rays of light and radiant expressions.
“Beam” 源自古英语 bēam，意为“树木”或“木柱”，后发展为“建筑支梁”以及“光线”、“笑容”等含义。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: bēam – tree, wooden post
古英语 bēam：树木，木柱

Middle English: beme – a support or shaft of light
中古英语 beme：支柱或光线

Modern English: beam – structural support or ray of light / smile
现代英语 beam：建筑梁，光束，笑容
