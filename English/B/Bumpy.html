🪨 bumpy /ˈbʌmpi/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Having a surface that is not smooth; full of jolts or uneven areas.
崎岖不平的；颠簸的。

Example: We drove along a bumpy road through the forest.
例句：我们沿着一条颠簸的森林小路行驶。
Example: It was a bumpy flight because of the turbulence.
例句：由于气流颠簸，这趟航班一路不平稳。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Uneven 不平坦的
  • Rough 粗糙的
  • Jolty 颠簸的
  • Rugged 崎岖的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Bumpy" is derived from the word "bump," which has been used since the 16th century, possibly of Scandinavian origin, meaning a light blow or a raised area. The suffix "-y" turns it into an adjective.
“Bumpy” 源于单词 “bump”（隆起、撞击），该词自16世纪以来就已使用，可能源自斯堪的纳维亚语，加上形容词后缀“-y”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Middle English: bump – a light knock or swelling
中古英语 bump：轻轻的撞击或隆起
⬇️
Modern English: bumpy – having many bumps or jolts
现代英语 bumpy：颠簸的，有许多隆起或震动的
