🌟 baffling /ˈbæflɪŋ/

Part of speech: Adjective

📖 Definition:

If something is baffling, it is very confusing, puzzling, or difficult to understand.

Example: The instructions were so baffling that no one could figure out how to build the toy.
Example: His behavior was completely baffling to his classmates.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Confusing
  • Puzzling
  • Perplexing
  • Mysterious
  • Incomprehensible

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "baffling" comes from the verb baffle, which originated in the late 16th century, possibly from French beffler, meaning to trick or deceive.

📝 Over time, the word evolved to mean "to bewilder" or "to confuse," and "baffling" became the adjective form.

🧭 Word Evolution

Possibly French: beffler → to deceive
⬇️
English: baffle → to confuse
⬇️
Modern English: baffling → confusing or puzzling
