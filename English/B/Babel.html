🏗️ Babel /ˈbeɪbəl/ or /ˈbæbəl/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

1. A scene of noisy confusion, often due to people speaking different languages at once.
2. (Biblical) The Tower of Babel: a story in Genesis where humanity tried to build a tower to heaven, and God confused their language.
1. 嘈杂混乱的场面，常因语言不通而引起；
2. （圣经）巴别塔，人类试图建塔通天，结果被上帝变乱语言。

Example: The market was a babel of voices, each shouting in a different tongue.
例句：市场上一片嘈杂，每个人都在用不同的语言喊叫。
Example: The classroom turned into a babel when students started talking over each other.
例句：学生们开始你一言我一语，教室顿时变得乱成一团。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Uproar 喧嚣
  • Commotion 骚动
  • Din 嘈杂声
  • Chaos 混乱

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Babel" comes from the Hebrew word Bāḇel, meaning Babylon. In the Bible, it refers to a tower built by people who all spoke one language—until God disrupted their speech to prevent their prideful unity.
“Babel” 来自希伯来语 Bāḇel，意为“巴比伦”。在《圣经》中指人类因傲慢而试图建塔直达天堂，上帝遂变乱他们的语言，使他们分散各地。

🧭 Word Evolution

Hebrew: Bāḇel – Babylon
希伯来语 Bāḇel：巴比伦

Latin: Babylon – biblical city
拉丁语 Babylon：圣经中的城

Modern English: Babel – noisy confusion, especially of languages
现代英语 Babel：嘈杂混乱（尤指语言上的）
