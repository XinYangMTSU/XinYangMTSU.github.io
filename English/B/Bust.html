💥 bust /bʌst/

Part of speech: Verb / Noun
词性：动词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

(verb) To break, explode, or prove something false; (noun) a failure or an arrest.
（动词）打破、爆炸或揭穿；（名词）失败或逮捕。

Example: The police busted the illegal operation.
例句：警方捣毁了这个非法活动。
Example: Their business went bust after a few months.
例句：他们的生意几个月后就破产了。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Break 打破
  • Smash 粉碎
  • Collapse 崩溃
  • Expose 揭露

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Bust" comes from the American slang of the 1800s, originally meaning "burst."
bust 来自 19 世纪的美式俚语，最初意为“爆裂”。

🧭 Word Evolution

English slang: bust – break or fail
英语俚语 bust：打破或失败
⬇️
Modern Use: bust – break, expose, collapse
现代用法 bust：破裂、揭露、崩溃

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Break Neutral He broke the vase accidentally. General
Collapse Neutral / Formal The company collapsed during the recession. Serious / technical
Bust Informal / Slang They busted the myth using science. Playful / emphatic
