🌟 bleak /bliːk/

Part of speech: Adjective

📖 Meaning:

Bleak describes something that is cold, empty, or depressing. It can refer to physical places, emotional moods, or situations without hope or comfort.

Example: The landscape was bleak and barren after the storm. (= It looked empty and lifeless.)
Example: The future looked bleak for the struggling company. (= Things seemed hopeless.)

🧬 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Desolate
  • Dreary
  • Gloomy
  • Hopeless
  • Cold
  • Empty

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˈɛtəˌmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word bleak comes from Old Norse bleikr, meaning pale or white. It later came to describe places that were cold and exposed, and eventually emotional states that felt depressing or hopeless.

🪠 Word Evolution

Old Norse: bleikr (pale, white)
⬇️
Middle English: bleke
⬇️
Modern English: bleak
