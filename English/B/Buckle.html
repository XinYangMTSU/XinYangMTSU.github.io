🔩 buckle /ˈbʌk.əl/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

As a noun: A clasp or fastening device used for joining two ends of a belt or strap.
名词：带扣；用于连接皮带或背带两端的扣件。

As a verb: To bend, give way, or collapse under pressure or force; also, to fasten something using a buckle.
动词：弯曲、变形、屈服（通常因压力）；也可表示“扣住、扣上”。

Example (noun): He tightened the strap and secured it with a metal buckle.
例句（名词）：他收紧了背带，并用金属扣扣住。
Example (verb): The floor buckled under the weight of the heavy machinery.
例句（动词）：地板在沉重机器的压力下发生了变形。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Bend 弯曲
  • Collapse 倒塌
  • Warp 变形
  • Fasten (as verb) 扣住

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Buckle" comes from Old French bocle, meaning "boss of a shield" or "buckle", and from Latin buccula, a diminutive of bucca meaning “cheek” or “mouth.” It evolved to refer to fastening devices and later to the idea of bending or giving way under pressure.
“Buckle” 源自古法语 bocle，意为“盾牌中心凸起”或“带扣”，更早来自拉丁语 buccula（小颊、扣件），原义与面部有关，后发展为“扣住”与“弯曲屈服”两个意思。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: buccula – cheekpiece, small boss
拉丁语 buccula：面颊饰、小扣件

Old French: bocle – fastening or shield boss
古法语 bocle：扣具，凸起

Modern English: buckle – a device to fasten or a verb meaning to bend or yield
现代英语 buckle：带扣；弯曲、屈服
