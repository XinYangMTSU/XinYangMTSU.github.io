🌟 Bizarre /bɪˈzɑːr/

Part of speech: Adjective

📖 Meaning:

Bizarre means very strange or unusual, especially in a surprising or unsettling way.

Example: The story took a bizarre turn when the cat started talking.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Weird
  • Strange
  • Odd
  • Unusual
  • Quirky
  • Peculiar

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word bizarre comes from French, which borrowed it from Italian bizzarro, meaning "angry or eccentric." Over time, it evolved to mean strange or fantastical.

🧭 Word Evolution

Italian: bizzarro = angry, eccentric
⬇️
French: bizarre = odd, fantastic
⬇️
English: bizarre = very strange or unusual /bɪˈzɑːr/

📊 Comparison

Word Intensity Example Tone
Odd Mild That’s an odd outfit. Curious
Weird Moderate He acted really weird today. Unsettling
Bizarre Strong It was a bizarre dream about flying fish. Surreal / shocking
