🏛️ bureaucracy /bjʊˈrɑːkrəsi/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A system of government or business that is run by many departments and officials following fixed rules; often used to describe overly complex administrative procedures.
官僚制度；官僚作风；繁琐的行政程序。

Example: The reforms are intended to reduce unnecessary bureaucracy in education.
例句：这些改革旨在减少教育系统中不必要的官僚作风。
Example: Applying for a visa can involve a lot of bureaucracy.
例句：申请签证可能涉及大量的繁文缛节。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Red tape 繁琐手续
  • Administration 行政管理
  • Government agency 政府机构
  • Officialdom 官僚机构

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Bureaucracy" comes from the French word bureaucratie, which combines bureau ("desk" or "office") and the Greek suffix -kratia ("power" or "rule"). It originally referred to government by office desks, i.e., administration by officials.
bureaucracy 来自法语 bureaucratie，词根为 bureau（办公桌、办公室）与希腊语后缀 -kratia（统治）。最初指“由办公室管理的政府”，即“官僚制度”。

🧭 Word Evolution

French: bureaucratie – rule by offices
法语 bureaucratie：由办公室管理
⬇️
Modern English: bureaucracy – rule by officials, often rigid
现代英语 bureaucracy：官僚统治，制度僵化
