🛏️ bolster /ˈboʊlstər/

Part of speech: Verb / Noun
词性：动词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

(v.) To support or strengthen; to reinforce something that is weak or uncertain.
(n.) A long, thick pillow that is placed under other pillows for support.
（动词）支持；增强；加强
（名词）长枕；垫枕

Example: The new evidence bolstered his argument in court.
例句：新证据增强了他在法庭上的辩护。
Example: She leaned back against a soft bolster on the couch.
例句：她靠在沙发上的长枕上休息。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Strengthen 加强
  • Support 支持
  • Reinforce 巩固
  • Boost 提升

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Bolster" comes from Old English bolster, meaning a long pillow or cushion, which later took on the figurative sense of support or reinforcement.
“Bolster” 源自古英语 bolster，原意为长枕或靠垫，后来引申为“支持”或“加强”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: bolster – cushion or pillow
古英语 bolster：靠垫或枕头

Middle English: expanded meaning to support or prop up
中古英语：引申为“支撑、增强”

Modern English: bolster – strengthen or support
现代英语 bolster：加强、支持

📊 Comparison

Word Context Example Use Tone
Support General She supported his decision. Neutral
Reinforce Military / Structural The soldiers reinforced the barricade. Technical / Strong
Bolster Formal / Emotional / Legal The study bolsters the theory. Professional / Persuasive
