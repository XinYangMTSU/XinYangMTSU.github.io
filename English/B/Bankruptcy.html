💸 bankruptcy /ˈbæŋkrəptsi/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

The legal status of being unable to repay debts owed to creditors; financial failure.
破产；无力偿还债务的法律状态。

Example: The company filed for bankruptcy after several unprofitable quarters.
例句：公司在连续几个季度亏损后申请了破产。
Example: Personal bankruptcy can have long-lasting effects on credit scores.
例句：个人破产会对信用评分造成长期影响。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Insolvency 资不抵债
  • Financial ruin 经济崩溃
  • Default 违约
  • Collapse 倒闭

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Bankruptcy" comes from Italian banca rotta, meaning "broken bench," referring to the practice of breaking a banker’s bench when they failed. It was adopted into French and then English.
“Bankruptcy” 源自意大利语 banca rotta，意为“破碎的长凳”，指的是古代银行家破产时，其长凳被打碎作为标志。

🧭 Word Evolution

Italian: banca rotta – broken bank/bench
意大利语 banca rotta：破碎的银行/长凳

French: banqueroute – financial failure
法语 banqueroute：经济破产

Modern English: bankruptcy – legal financial collapse
现代英语 bankruptcy：破产的法律状态

📊 Comparison

Word Context Example Use Tone
Insolvency Legal / Financial The firm showed signs of insolvency before declaring bankruptcy. Formal
Collapse General / Business The sudden collapse of the startup shocked investors. Dramatic
Bankruptcy Legal / Business He filed for bankruptcy protection under Chapter 11. Neutral / Technical
