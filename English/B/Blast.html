💥 blast /blæst/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

(n.) A strong explosion or sudden loud noise; a gust of wind; a fun or exciting event.
(v.) To explode or destroy with a powerful force; to make a loud noise; to strongly criticize.
（名词）爆炸，猛烈的一阵（风、声音）；（动词）爆破，轰击，大声播放，猛烈批评。

Example: There was a loud blast in the distance.
例句：远处传来一声巨响。
Example: The music was blasting from the speakers.
例句：音乐从音响中震耳欲聋地播放出来。
Example: We had a blast at the party last night!
例句：我们昨晚在派对上玩得很开心！

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Explosion 爆炸
  • Burst 爆发
  • Gust 一阵（风）
  • Criticize 批评
  • Party (informal) 派对（俚语）

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Blast" comes from Old English blǣst, meaning "a blowing or puff of wind." It is related to the word "blow" and evolved to describe explosions and loud events.
“Blast” 源自古英语 blǣst，意为“吹气、风的一阵”，后演变为指爆炸或巨响。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: blǣst – a blowing, gust
古英语 blǣst：一阵风，吹气

Modern English: blast – explosion, burst of sound, fun
现代英语 blast：爆炸，响声，狂欢

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Explosion Neutral The explosion shook the entire building. Serious
Burst Neutral A burst of laughter filled the room. Lively
Blast Flexible The blast shattered the windows. Vivid / Dramatic / Informal
