🧁 batter /ˈbætər/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

As a noun: A semi-liquid mixture of flour, eggs, and milk or water used in cooking.
（名词）面糊；一种用于烹饪的半液体混合物，通常由面粉、鸡蛋和牛奶或水制成。

As a verb: To strike repeatedly with force.
（动词）连续猛击；猛打。

Example (noun): Pour the batter into the pan and bake for 30 minutes.
例句（名词）：把面糊倒入烤盘，烘烤30分钟。
Example (verb): The waves battered the coastline all night.
例句（动词）：海浪整夜猛烈拍打海岸线。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Mixture 混合物（名词义）
  • Dough 面团（相似但更稠）
  • Pound 猛击（动词义）
  • Strike 打击（动词义）

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Batter" (the noun) comes from Old French batre meaning “to beat,” related to Latin battuere (to beat), referring originally to the act of beating ingredients together.
“Batter”（名词）源自古法语 batre，意为“打、搅打”，来自拉丁语 battuere（敲打），最初指搅拌食材的动作。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: battuere – to beat
拉丁语 battuere：敲打

Old French: batre – to strike or beat
古法语 batre：敲击，打

Modern English: batter – food mixture or to hit repeatedly
现代英语 batter：面糊或连续击打
