❗ blunder /ˈblʌndər/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

(n.) A careless or serious mistake.
(v.) To make a careless or stupid mistake; to move clumsily.
(名词）疏忽错误；（动词）犯错；笨拙地行动。

Example: It was a major blunder to delete the final report without a backup.
例句：没有备份就删除最终报告是个严重失误。
Example: He blundered into the meeting late and unprepared.
例句：他迟到并毫无准备地闯入了会议。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Mistake 错误
  • Slip-up 小失误
  • Error 过失
  • Fumble 失手；笨拙

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Blunder" likely comes from the Old Norse word blundra, meaning “to shut one's eyes” or “act blindly,” suggesting carelessness or lack of awareness.
“Blunder” 可能源自古诺斯语 blundra，意思是“闭上眼睛”或“盲目行动”，暗指粗心或缺乏判断。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old Norse: blundra – to shut one’s eyes
古诺斯语 blundra：闭上眼睛

Middle English: blunderen – to stumble or act blindly
中古英语 blunderen：跌跌撞撞或盲目行事

Modern English: blunder – a careless mistake
现代英语 blunder：粗心大意的错误
