🔪 blunt /blʌnt/

Part of speech: Adjective / Verb
词性：形容词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

As an adjective, blunt means not sharp or pointed, or speaking in a direct, sometimes harsh way.
As a verb, it means to make something less sharp or to reduce the intensity or force of something.
作为形容词，“blunt” 意为不锋利的，或说话直率而可能显得生硬的；作为动词，意为使变钝，或减弱某事的强度。

Example (adj): The knife was too blunt to cut the bread.
例句（形容词）：这把刀太钝了，切不了面包。
Example (adj): She gave a blunt reply: “No.”
例句（形容词）：她直截了当地回答：“不。”
Example (verb): The edge of the blade was blunted by repeated use.
例句（动词）：刀刃因反复使用而变钝。
Example (verb): His shock was blunted by years of experience.
例句（动词）：多年的经验让他的震惊感减弱了。

🧩 Synonyms

  • Dull 钝的
  • Direct 直率的
  • Frank 坦率的
  • Soften 减弱
  • Deaden 使迟钝

🔍 Etymology of the word

📚 "Blunt" comes from Middle English blont meaning “dull,” possibly of Scandinavian origin.
“blunt” 源自中古英语 blont，意为“迟钝的”，可能源自斯堪的纳维亚语系。
