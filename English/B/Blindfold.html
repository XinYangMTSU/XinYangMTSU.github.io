🙈 blindfold /ˈblaɪndˌfoʊld/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

Noun: A piece of cloth tied over someone's eyes to prevent them from seeing.
名词：蒙住眼睛的布；眼罩。

Verb: To cover someone's eyes with a blindfold.
动词：用布蒙住某人的眼睛。

Example: The prisoner was blindfolded before being led away.
例句：犯人在被带走前被蒙住了眼睛。
Example: We played a game where one person was blindfolded and had to guess who touched them.
例句：我们玩了一个游戏，一个人被蒙住眼睛，要猜是谁碰了他。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Eye covering 眼罩
  • Mask 遮眼物
  • Veil 面纱
  • Blind 遮挡物

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Blindfold" is a compound word from “blind” (unable to see) and “fold” (as in a piece of cloth or covering). It dates back to Middle English.
blindfold 是由 blind（看不见的）和 fold（折叠、布料）构成的复合词，源于中世纪英语。

🧭 Word Evolution

Middle English: blyndfolde – blind covering
中世纪英语 blyndfolde：遮眼物
⬇️
Modern English: blindfold – cloth covering the eyes
现代英语 blindfold：眼罩，蒙眼布
