🧵 befit /bɪˈfɪt/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To be appropriate or suitable for someone or something; to be fitting or proper in a certain situation.
适合；与…相称；与身份、场合等相配。

Example: He wore a suit that befit the seriousness of the occasion.
例句：他穿了一套与场合的庄重氛围相称的西装。
Example: Her humble attitude befits someone of true greatness.
例句：她谦逊的态度正与真正伟大之人相称。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Suit 适合
  • Match 与…相配
  • Accord with 与…一致
  • Be appropriate for 适用于

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Befit" comes from the Middle English word befitten, combining the prefix “be-” (intensifier) with “fit” (suitable). It literally means “to make fit” or “to be fitting for.”
“Befit” 来自中古英语 befiten，由前缀 be-（表示加强）和 fit（适合）组成，字面意思是“使合适”或“适合于”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Middle English: befitten – to make suitable
中古英语 befiten：使适合

Modern English: befit – to be proper for
现代英语 befit：适合；相配

📊 Comparison
