🧊 brittle /ˈbrɪtl/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Hard but easily broken or cracked; not flexible or durable.
坚硬但容易碎裂的；不灵活或不耐用的。

Example: The cookies were too brittle and crumbled in my hands.
例句：这些饼干太脆了，在我手里就碎了。
Example: Her voice sounded brittle with tension.
例句：她的声音因紧张而显得脆弱。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Fragile 易碎的
  • Breakable 易破的
  • Delicate 脆弱的
  • Crisp 脆的（用于食物）

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Brittle" comes from Old English brytel, meaning “easily broken,” related to the word “break.”
brittle 源自古英语 brytel，意为“易碎的”，与 break（打碎）有关。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: brytel – easily broken
古英语 brytel：容易碎裂的
⬇️
Middle English: brittel – fragile
中古英语 brittel：易碎的
⬇️
Modern English: brittle – hard but easily broken
现代英语 brittle：坚硬但容易碎裂

📊 Comparison
