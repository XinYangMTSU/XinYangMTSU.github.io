🤯 absurd /əbˈsɜːrd/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Absurd means wildly unreasonable, illogical, or inappropriate. It can also describe something so strange or ridiculous that it seems laughable or impossible.
absurd 意为“荒谬的、不合逻辑的”，也可指荒唐得令人发笑或不可信的事。

Example: The idea that the Earth is flat is completely absurd.
例句：认为地球是平的，这种想法完全是荒谬的。
Example: He wore an absurd costume to the serious meeting.
例句：他穿着一套滑稽荒唐的服装去参加严肃的会议。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Ridiculous 荒唐的
  • Ludicrous 可笑的
  • Preposterous 荒谬可笑的
  • Nonsensical 毫无意义的
  • Illogical 不合逻辑的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Absurd" comes from Latin absurdus, meaning “out of tune,” and later came to mean “irrational or unreasonable.” It originally referred to sounds that didn’t fit or made no sense.
“absurd” 源自拉丁语 absurdus，原意是“走调的”，后来引申为“不合理的、荒谬的”，最初用于描述不和谐或无意义的声音。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: absurdus – out of tune, discordant
拉丁语 absurdus：不和谐的，荒谬的
⬇️
Middle English: absurd – unreasonable
中古英语 absurd：不合理的
⬇️
Modern English: absurd – ridiculous or nonsensical
现代英语 absurd：荒唐的、毫无道理的

📊 Comparison

Word Usage Context Example Tone
Ridiculous Common speech That idea is ridiculous. Humorous / critical
Ludicrous Describing something laughably wrong The accusation was ludicrous. Dismissive / mocking
Absurd Logic, reasoning, emotion It’s absurd to deny climate change. Serious / philosophical / funny
