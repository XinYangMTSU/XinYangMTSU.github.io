🔥 aggressively /əˈɡrɛsɪvli/

Part of speech: Adverb
词性：副词

📖 Meaning:

Aggressively means in a forceful, determined, or energetic way. It can describe both physical behavior and attitudes or actions in work, learning, or competition.
aggressively 意为“咄咄逼人地；积极主动地”，可以描述强势的行为，也可指在学习、工作或竞争中的决心和活力。

Example: He aggressively argued his point during the debate.
例句：他在辩论中强硬地表达了自己的观点。
Example: She studied aggressively in preparation for the exam.
例句：她为考试而拼命学习，极其投入。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Forcefully 有力地
  • Boldly 大胆地
  • Energetically 充满活力地
  • Assertively 坚定自信地
  • Vigorously 积极地，猛烈地

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Aggressively" is derived from the word aggressive, which comes from Latin aggredi meaning "to attack or move toward". The adverb form describes the **manner** of such behavior.
“aggressively” 源自形容词 aggressive，该词来自拉丁语 aggredi，意为“攻击”或“接近”。副词形式用于描述这种行为方式。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: aggredi – to attack or approach
拉丁语 aggredi：攻击或靠近
⬇️
English: aggressive – hostile or assertive
英语 aggressive：有攻击性或咄咄逼人
⬇️
English: aggressively – in a forceful or determined way
英语 aggressively：以坚定或强硬的方式
