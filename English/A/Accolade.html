🏅 accolade /ˈækəleɪd/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

An award or expression of praise or approval.
奖赏；赞扬；荣誉。

Example: She received the highest accolade for her contribution to science.
例句：她因对科学的贡献而获得最高荣誉。
Example: The novel won critical accolades across the globe.
例句：这部小说在全球范围内赢得了评论界的高度评价。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Award 奖项
  • Honor 荣誉
  • Praise 称赞
  • Tribute 致敬

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Accolade" comes from the Latin word collum meaning "neck", referring to the ceremonial act of touching a knight’s shoulder with a sword during knighting.
accolade 源自拉丁语 collum（脖子），指的是封爵仪式中用剑轻触肩膀的动作，象征表彰。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: collum – neck
拉丁语 collum：脖子
⬇️
Old French: accoler – to embrace
古法语 accoler：拥抱
⬇️
Modern English: accolade – honor, award
现代英语 accolade：赞誉、荣誉

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Honor Neutral / Formal She was honored with a lifetime achievement award. Respectful / Celebratory
Praise Neutral He received praise for his work ethic. Positive / Encouraging
Accolade Formal The film received the highest accolade at the festival. Prestigious / Ceremonial
