🏔️ avalanche /ˈævəlæntʃ/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A large mass of snow, ice, and rocks falling rapidly down a mountainside.
从山坡上快速滑下的大量雪、冰和岩石；雪崩。

Also used metaphorically to describe a sudden overwhelming quantity of something.
也可以比喻地指突如其来的大量事物。

Example: Several climbers were caught in an avalanche on the north slope.
例句：几位登山者在北坡遭遇了雪崩。
Example: The company received an avalanche of complaints after the price hike.
例句：涨价后，该公司收到了大量投诉。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Snow slide 雪滑
  • Deluge (figurative) （比喻）洪水般的事物
  • Flood (of something) 大量（某物）
  • Landslide (related but includes soil/rock) 山体滑坡（含土石）

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Borrowed from French avalanche, from the dialectal word lavanche (to descend), derived from Latin labere, meaning “to slip” or “to fall.”
来自法语 avalanche，源自方言词 lavanche（下滑），再追溯到拉丁语 labere，意为“滑动”或“坠落”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: labere – to slip, fall
拉丁语 labere：滑动、坠落
⬇️
French: avalanche – mass of falling snow
法语 avalanche：雪崩
⬇️
Modern English: avalanche – snow slide or overwhelming rush
现代英语 avalanche：雪崩，或突如其来的大量事物
