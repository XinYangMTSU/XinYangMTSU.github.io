🌟 aspiring /əˈspaɪərɪŋ/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Having a strong desire to achieve something, typically a particular career, goal, or status.
有志向的；有抱负的；渴望成功（尤指在事业上）的。

Example: She’s an aspiring writer working on her first novel.
例句：她是一位有志成为作家的新人，正在写她的第一本小说。
Example: The workshop was designed for aspiring musicians and performers.
例句：这个工作坊是为有志成为音乐家和表演者的人开设的。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Ambitious 有雄心的
  • Hopeful 抱有希望的
  • Motivated 积极进取的
  • Driven 有动力的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Aspiring" comes from the Latin aspirare, meaning “to breathe toward” or “to seek to reach,” combining ad- (toward) + spirare (to breathe).
“Aspiring” 来自拉丁语 aspirare，意思是“朝……呼吸”或“渴望达到”，由前缀 ad-（朝向）和动词 spirare（呼吸）组成。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: aspirare – to breathe toward, strive
拉丁语 aspirare：朝向呼吸，努力追求

Middle English: aspire – to strive to attain
中古英语 aspire：渴望获得

Modern English: aspiring – having ambition to succeed
现代英语 aspiring：有志向的，有抱负的

📊 Comparison

Word Tone Meaning Example
Ambitious Neutral / Positive Having strong determination to succeed He is very ambitious in his career goals.
Aspiring Positive Having the desire to achieve something She's an aspiring film director.
Dreaming Gentle / Idealistic Having hopes and imagination about success He’s dreaming of becoming a pilot.
