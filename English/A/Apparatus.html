🧪 apparatus /ˌæpəˈrætəs/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

1. A set of tools, machines, or equipment used for a specific purpose.
一套设备、装置或仪器，用于特定目的。

2. The structure or organization of a system, especially in politics or the body.
系统的结构或机制，尤其指政治或生理系统。

Example: The laboratory is equipped with modern scientific apparatus.
例句：这个实验室配备了现代科学仪器。
Example: The government apparatus failed to respond quickly to the crisis.
例句：政府体系未能对这场危机迅速作出反应。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Equipment 设备
  • Device 装置
  • System 系统
  • Mechanism 机制

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Apparatus" comes from Latin apparatus, meaning “preparation” or “equipment,” from the verb apparare ("to prepare"). It entered English in the 17th century.
“Apparatus” 源自拉丁语 apparatus，意为“准备”或“设备”，来自动词 apparare（准备）。17 世纪进入英语。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: apparare – to prepare
拉丁语 apparare：准备

Latin noun: apparatus – equipment, tools
拉丁名词 apparatus：设备，工具

Modern English: apparatus – equipment or system structure
现代英语 apparatus：仪器，组织机构

📊 Comparison

Word Use Formality Example
Device Tool or machine Neutral This device helps monitor heart rate.
System Organized structure Neutral The education system needs reform.
Apparatus Tool set or structure Formal The rescue apparatus was deployed quickly.
