🌟 artistry /ˈɑːr.tɪ.stri/

Part of speech: Noun

📖 As a noun (a thing):

Artistry means creative skill or ability in art, music, writing, or any other field of expression.

Example: Her dancing displayed great artistry and emotion.

📖 Broader meaning:

It also refers to the imaginative or skillful way something is done, not just in the arts but in life, cooking, or leadership.

Example: The chef’s artistry was evident in every dish.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Creativity
  • Skill
  • Craftsmanship
  • Expression
  • Flair
  • Imagination

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "artistry".

📝It comes from the word artist (Latin ars = art) + suffix -ry, which forms nouns of quality or state.

  • ars = art or skill
  • -ry = state or practice of

✍️ The word came into English in the 18th century to describe the creative quality of an artist’s work.
