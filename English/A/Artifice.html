🪄 artifice /ˈɑːrtɪfɪs/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

Clever or cunning devices or expedients, especially used to trick or deceive others.
巧妙手段；诡计；狡猾的计谋，尤指用于欺骗的。

Example: The magician's performance was full of artifice and illusion.
例句：魔术师的表演充满了诡计和幻觉。
Example: Her praise was mere artifice, aimed at winning his trust.
例句：她的赞美只是手段，目的是赢得他的信任。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Deception 欺骗
  • Trick 诡计
  • Ruse 花招
  • Stratagem 计谋

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Artifice" comes from Latin artificium, meaning “craftsmanship” or “trick.” It combines ars (skill) and facere (to make).
“Artifice” 来自拉丁语 artificium，意为“技巧”或“诡计”，由 ars（技巧）和 facere（做）组成。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: artificium – craft, trick
拉丁语 artificium：技艺、诡计
⬇️
Old French: artifice – clever trick
古法语 artifice：巧妙的诡计
⬇️
Modern English: artifice – cunning skill or deception
现代英语 artifice：狡猾的技巧或欺骗手段
