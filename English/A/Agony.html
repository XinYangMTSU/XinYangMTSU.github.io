😫 agony /ˈæɡəni/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

Extreme physical or mental pain or suffering.
极度的身体或精神上的痛苦。

Example: He was in agony after breaking his leg during the game.
例句：比赛时腿骨折后他痛苦万分。
Example: The parents went through agony waiting for news about their child.
例句：父母在等待孩子消息时承受着极大的精神折磨。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Pain 疼痛
  • Torment 折磨
  • Anguish 剧痛；极度痛苦
  • Suffering 痛苦

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Agony" comes from the Greek word agōn, meaning “struggle” or “contest.” It originally referred to intense physical effort in a competition and later came to mean intense pain.
agony 源自希腊语 agōn，意为“斗争”或“比赛”。最初指激烈的体力活动，后来演变为“极度痛苦”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Greek: agōn – struggle, contest
希腊语 agōn：斗争，比赛
⬇️
Latin: agonia – severe mental struggle
拉丁语 agonia：剧烈的心理挣扎
⬇️
Modern English: agony – extreme pain or suffering
现代英语 agony：极度痛苦或折磨

📊 Comparison
