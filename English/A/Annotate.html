🖊️ annotate /ˈænəˌteɪt/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To add notes to a text, diagram, or piece of information to explain or comment on it.
为文本、图表或信息添加注释或解释。

Example: The students were asked to annotate the poem with their own interpretations.
例句：学生们被要求为这首诗写上自己的理解注释。
Example: Scientists annotated the genetic sequence to identify specific functions.
例句：科学家为基因序列添加注释，以识别特定功能。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Comment 评论
  • Explain 解释
  • Note 注释
  • Highlight 标注

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Annotate" comes from Latin annotatus, past participle of annotare, meaning “to note down.”
annotate 源自拉丁语 annotatus，是 annotare（记下、做笔记）的过去分词。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: annotare – to note, mark
拉丁语 annotare：记下，做标记
⬇️
Modern English: annotate – to add notes of explanation or comment
现代英语 annotate：添加解释性注释或评论

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Comment Neutral She commented on each paragraph in the margin. General
Highlight Neutral / Informal I highlighted the important definitions. Visual / supportive
Annotate Neutral / Formal Please annotate the article for class discussion. Academic / explanatory
