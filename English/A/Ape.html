🦍 ape /eɪp/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

(Noun) A large primate without a tail, such as a gorilla or chimpanzee. (Verb) To imitate someone or something, often clumsily or mockingly.
（名词）类人猿，如大猩猩或黑猩猩。
（动词）模仿（常指笨拙或讽刺地模仿）。

Example: The zoo has several species of apes, including orangutans and gorillas.
例句：这家动物园有几种类人猿，包括猩猩和大猩猩。
Example: He aped his teacher’s voice to make the class laugh.
例句：他模仿老师的声音逗全班同学笑。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Monkey (noun) 猴子（名词）
  • Imitate (verb) 模仿（动词）
  • Mimic 模仿
  • Copycat 效仿者

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Ape" comes from Old English apa, which is related to similar words in Germanic languages. The verb sense (to mimic) developed later in Middle English.
“Ape” 源自古英语 apa，与其他日耳曼语言中的类似词有关。“模仿”这个动词义是在中古英语时期发展出来的。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: apa – monkey or ape
古英语 apa：猴或猿

Middle English: ape – also used figuratively for imitators
中古英语 ape：也用于指模仿者

Modern English: ape – both noun and verb
现代英语 ape：既是名词也是动词

📊 Comparison

Word Context Example Use Tone
Mimic Speech / Behavior She mimicked her brother’s voice perfectly. Playful / Neutral
Copy General They copied the homework answers. Neutral / Slightly negative
Ape Imitation / Animal He aped the actor’s mannerisms in a funny way. Comic / Sometimes rude
