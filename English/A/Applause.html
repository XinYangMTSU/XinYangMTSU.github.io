👏 applause /əˈplɔːz/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

Clapping of hands by an audience to show approval, enjoyment, or praise.
观众鼓掌以表示赞许、欣赏或称赞。

Example: The speaker received loud applause at the end of the lecture.
例句：演讲结束时，讲者赢得了热烈的掌声。
Example: Her performance was met with thunderous applause.
例句：她的表演赢得了雷鸣般的掌声。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Clapping 鼓掌
  • Cheering 欢呼
  • Ovation 喝彩；欢迎
  • Acclaim 喝彩；称赞

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 From Latin applaudere, meaning “to clap hands,” from ad- (to) + plaudere (to clap).
来自拉丁语 applaudere，意为“鼓掌”，由 ad-（向）和 plaudere（拍手）组成。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: applaudere – to clap
拉丁语 applaudere：鼓掌
⬇️
Old French: applaus
古法语 applaus：掌声
⬇️
Modern English: applause – approval shown by clapping
现代英语 applause：以鼓掌表示的认可

📊 Comparison

Word Form Example Use Context
Applause Noun The crowd gave a round of applause. Public Events / Performances
Ovation Noun She received a standing ovation. Formal / Honoring
Cheers Noun (plural) The team heard cheers from the crowd. Sporting / Festive
