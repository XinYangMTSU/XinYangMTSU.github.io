✅ affirm /əˈfɜːm/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To state something as true or confirm it with confidence; to express agreement or support, often publicly or formally.
断言；确认；公开或正式地表示支持或同意。

Example: She affirmed her commitment to equal rights during the interview.
例句：她在采访中确认了自己对平等权利的承诺。
Example: The judge affirmed the lower court’s decision.
例句：法官确认了下级法院的裁决。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Confirm 确认
  • Declare 宣称
  • Assert 断言
  • Support 支持

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Affirm" comes from Latin affirmare, meaning “to make steady or strengthen,” from ad (to) + firmare (to make firm).
“Affirm” 源自拉丁语 affirmare，意为“坚定地说”或“加强”，由 ad（向）+ firmare（使坚固）组成。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: affirmare – to assert, to make firm
拉丁语 affirmare：断言，使坚定

Old French: affermer – to confirm
古法语 affermer：确认

Modern English: affirm – to state or confirm as true
现代英语 affirm：确认；断言

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Strength Example Use
Assert Neutral Strong He asserted his innocence.
Affirm Formal Strong The leader affirmed her support for the policy.
Confirm Neutral Medium The test confirmed their theory.
