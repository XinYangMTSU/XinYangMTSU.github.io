📜 Aesop /ˈiːsɒp/

Part of speech: Proper Noun
词性：专有名词

📖 Meaning:

Aesop is the name of a legendary ancient Greek storyteller, famous for writing short tales known as fables, which often feature animals and teach moral lessons.
伊索：古希腊著名寓言家，以撰写以动物为主角、寓意深刻的短篇故事而闻名。

Example: Aesop’s fables like "The Tortoise and the Hare" are still told to children around the world.
例句：伊索寓言如《龟兔赛跑》至今仍在全球各地讲给孩子听。
Example: Many of Aesop’s stories use animals to represent human traits.
例句：伊索的许多故事用动物来象征人的性格特征。

📖 Historical Background

🏛️ Aesop is believed to have lived in ancient Greece around the 6th century BCE. Though there is little historical evidence of his life, his fables have been passed down for centuries and translated into many languages.
据说伊索生活在公元前6世纪的古希腊，虽然关于他的生平资料甚少，但他的寓言世代流传，并被翻译成多种语言。

🧭 Influence and Legacy

Aesop’s name has become synonymous with short moral tales. His stories continue to be part of children's literature, school curricula, and global storytelling traditions.
“伊索”这个名字几乎成为寓言故事的代名词。他的故事至今仍是儿童文学、学校课程和全球讲故事传统的重要组成部分。

Aesop’s stories are still used today to teach values such as honesty, patience, and humility.
