⚖️ acquit /əˈkwɪt/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To formally declare someone not guilty of a criminal charge in a court of law.
宣布（某人）无罪；在法庭上正式判定无罪。

Example: The jury acquitted her of all charges.
例句：陪审团宣布她在所有指控中无罪。
Example: He was acquitted due to lack of evidence.
例句：由于证据不足，他被判无罪。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Exonerate 使免罪
  • Absolve 免除责任
  • Clear 澄清罪名
  • Vindicate 辩护；证明无罪

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Acquit" comes from Old French acquiter, based on Latin ad- ("to") + quitare ("to set free"). Originally meant to release someone from a debt or obligation.
acquit 源自古法语 acquiter，来自拉丁语 ad-（向）+ quitare（释放），最初意为解除债务或义务。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: quitare – to release
拉丁语 quitare：释放
⬇️
Old French: acquiter – to free from blame
古法语 acquiter：使无罪
⬇️
Modern English: acquit – to declare someone not guilty
现代英语 acquit：宣布无罪

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone

Vindicate Formal The evidence vindicated his actions. Defensive / Justifying
