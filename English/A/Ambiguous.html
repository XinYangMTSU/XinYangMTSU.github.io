⚖️ ambiguous /æmˈbɪɡ.ju.əs/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

If something is ambiguous, it has more than one meaning or is unclear, so people can interpret it in different ways.
ambiguous 意为“模棱两可的”或“含糊不清的”，可以有不止一种解释或理解方式。

Example: His message was so ambiguous that no one knew what he really meant.
例句：他的信息太模糊了，没人知道他真正的意思。
Example: The ending of the movie is deliberately ambiguous.
例句：电影的结局故意设计得模棱两可。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Unclear 不清楚的
  • Vague 含糊的
  • Obscure 晦涩的
  • Equivocal 模棱两可的
  • Open to interpretation 可多种理解的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Ambiguous" comes from Latin ambiguus, meaning "uncertain or changeable," from ambigere — “to wander or go around”.
ambiguous 源自拉丁语 ambiguus，意为“不确定的、变化不定的”，来自 ambigere（徘徊、来回移动）。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: ambigere – to go around, to hesitate
拉丁语 ambigere：徘徊，犹豫
⬇️
Latin: ambiguus – uncertain
拉丁语 ambiguus：不确定的
⬇️
English: ambiguous – unclear or having more than one meaning
现代英语 ambiguous：模棱两可的，有多重含义的
