😮 awe /ɔː/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

Awe is a feeling of wonder, admiration, and sometimes fear, usually inspired by something extremely powerful, beautiful, or vast.
awe 意为“敬畏、惊叹”，是一种由强大、美丽或广阔事物引发的敬佩与惊奇感，有时还伴有一丝恐惧。

Example: We stood in awe of the Grand Canyon’s beauty.
例句：我们站在大峡谷前，满怀敬畏地欣赏它的美。
Example: The audience watched in awe as the fireworks lit up the sky.
例句：烟花点亮天空时，观众们惊叹不已。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Wonder 惊奇
  • Admiration 敬佩
  • Reverence 崇敬
  • Astonishment 惊讶
  • Marvel 奇迹、赞叹

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Awe" comes from Old English ege, meaning "terror, dread," and Old Norse agi. Over time, the meaning shifted toward reverent wonder.
awe 源自古英语 ege（恐惧）和古诺斯语 agi，后来逐渐演变为“敬畏、惊叹”的含义。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old Norse: agi – fear, dread
古诺斯语 agi：恐惧
⬇️
Old English: ege – fear, terror
古英语 ege：恐惧，惊惧
⬇️
Modern English: awe – wonder mixed with respect or fear
现代英语 awe：敬畏，惊叹

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Wonder Neutral The children looked at the stars in wonder. Curious / amazed
Reverence Formal He spoke of the tradition with reverence. Respectful / solemn
Awe Elevated / Emotional They stood in awe before the ancient temple. Powerful / majestic
