🌟 avant-garde /ˌævɒ̃ˈɡɑːrd/

Part of speech: Noun or adjective

📖 As a noun (a thing):

The avant-garde refers to new and experimental ideas and methods in art, music, or literature, especially those that challenge traditional norms.

Example: The gallery features works by artists of the avant-garde.

📖 As an adjective (a description):

When something is described as avant-garde, it means it is innovative, ahead of its time, or radically experimental.

Example: Her avant-garde fashion designs turned heads at the runway show.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Innovative
  • Experimental
  • Radical
  • Cutting-edge
  • Progressive
  • Groundbreaking

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "avant-garde".

📝It comes directly from French: avant = "before" and garde = "guard". Originally a military term meaning "vanguard" or "advance guard."

  • avant = before
  • garde = guard

✍️ The term was later adopted to describe people or works at the forefront of artistic and cultural change.

💡 In English, it entered use in the early 20th century to describe radical art movements like Dadaism, Surrealism, and Modernism.
