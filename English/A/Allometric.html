🌀 allometric /ˌæləˈmɛtrɪk/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Allometric refers to the study or measurement of the growth of a part of an organism in relation to the entire organism. It often describes how shape or proportion changes as size increases.
allometric 指的是研究生物体某一部分相对于整体的生长比例，常用于描述随体积增加而发生的形状或比例变化。

Example: The allometric growth of a tree’s trunk and branches allows it to support more leaves as it matures.
例句：树干与树枝的异速生长使它随着成熟能够支撑更多叶子。
Example: Researchers analyzed the allometric scaling of brain size relative to body mass across species.
例句：研究人员分析了不同物种中大脑大小相对于体重的异速比例变化。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Proportional scaling 比例缩放
  • Relative growth 相对生长
  • Biological scaling 生物尺度
  • Geometric scaling 几何缩放
  • Growth ratio analysis 生长比率分析

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Allometric" comes from the Greek roots allo meaning "other" and metron meaning "measure", referring to "different measure" or "unequal growth".
allometric 源自希腊语前缀 allo（其他）和 metron（测量），表示“不同的度量”或“异速生长”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Greek: allo + metron – other + measure
希腊语：allo（其他）+ metron（测量）
⬇️
Modern Biology: allometric – describing unequal growth or size relationships
现代生物学：allometric 描述不同部位或种类间不同比例的生长关系
