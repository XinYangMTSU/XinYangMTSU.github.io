🎯 aficionado /əˌfɪʃəˈnɑːdoʊ/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A person who is very knowledgeable and enthusiastic about a particular subject, activity, or interest.
某方面的行家；热衷者；爱好者。

Example: She’s a true jazz aficionado who owns hundreds of vintage records.
例句：她是真正的爵士乐迷，收藏了数百张老唱片。
Example: He’s a coffee aficionado and can tell the origin of a bean just by its aroma.
例句：他是咖啡爱好者，仅凭香味就能分辨咖啡豆的产地。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Enthusiast 热衷者
  • Fan 粉丝
  • Devotee 狂热爱好者
  • Connoisseur 鉴赏家；行家

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Aficionado" comes from Spanish, originally meaning “one who has affection.” It derives from the verb aficionar (to inspire affection) and ultimately from Latin affectio (affection, disposition).
“Aficionado” 来自西班牙语，原意是“充满热情的人”，词源为动词 aficionar（激发兴趣），再追溯至拉丁语 affectio（感情，性情）。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: affectio – affection, disposition
拉丁语 affectio：感情，性情

Spanish: aficionado – person with strong liking
西班牙语 aficionado：热衷者

Modern English: aficionado – a passionate fan or expert
现代英语 aficionado：热情的粉丝或专家

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Fan Informal He's a big soccer fan. Casual
Connoisseur Formal She’s a wine connoisseur. Sophisticated
Aficionado Neutral / Formal He’s a film aficionado who attends festivals. Passionate / Knowledgeable
