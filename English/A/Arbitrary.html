🎲 arbitrary /ˈɑːrbətreri/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Based on random choice or personal whim, rather than any reason or system; not fixed by rules.
任意的；武断的；没有根据或系统的。

Example: The decision to change the deadline felt arbitrary and unfair.
例句：更改截止日期的决定显得任意而不公平。
Example: He made an arbitrary choice without considering the consequences.
例句：他在未考虑后果的情况下作出了一个随意的选择。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Random 随机的
  • Capricious 变化无常的
  • Unreasoned 无理由的
  • Whimsical 异想天开的；反复无常的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Arbitrary" comes from Latin arbitrarius, meaning “depending on the will or judgment of someone,” from arbiter meaning “judge or ruler.”
“Arbitrary” 源自拉丁语 arbitrarius，意为“依靠某人意志或判断”，来自 arbiter，意为“仲裁者、裁判”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: arbiter – judge or decision-maker
拉丁语 arbiter：裁判，决策者

Late Latin: arbitrarius – based on personal discretion
晚期拉丁语 arbitrarius：依据个人判断的

Modern English: arbitrary – based on random choice or whim
现代英语 arbitrary：依据随机选择或个人喜好
