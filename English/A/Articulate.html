🗣️ articulate /ɑːrˈtɪkjʊlət/ (adj), /ɑːrˈtɪkjʊleɪt/ (verb)

Part of speech: Adjective / Verb
词性：形容词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

As an adjective: Able to express ideas clearly and effectively in speech or writing.
形容词：能清晰而有效地表达思想的。

As a verb: To express (an idea or feeling) fluently and coherently; to pronounce clearly.
动词：清楚地表达（想法或感情）；清晰地发音。

Example (adj): She is an articulate speaker who inspires her audience.
例句（形容词）：她是一位善于表达、能激励听众的演讲者。
Example (verb): He struggled to articulate his emotions after the accident.
例句（动词）：事故后他难以清楚地表达自己的情感。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Eloquent 雄辩的
  • Expressive 善于表达的
  • Well-spoken 口齿伶俐的
  • Communicate 沟通；表达

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Articulate" comes from Latin articulatus, past participle of articulare meaning “to divide into joints,” from articulus (a small connecting part or joint).
“Articulate” 来自拉丁语 articulatus，是 articulare（分成关节、清晰表达）的过去分词，源于 articulus（小关节）。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: articulare – to utter distinctly, to divide into joints
拉丁语 articulare：清晰地说出；分成关节

Modern English: articulate – to express fluently and clearly
现代英语 articulate：清楚、流利地表达

📊 Comparison
