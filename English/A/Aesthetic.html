🎨 aesthetic /esˈθetɪk/

Part of speech: Adjective / Noun
词性：形容词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

(Adj.) Concerned with beauty or the appreciation of beauty. (Noun) A set of principles guiding the work of an artist or artistic movement.
（形容词）审美的；美学的；有关美的。
（名词）美感；美学标准。

Example: The minimalist aesthetic emphasizes simplicity and clean lines.
例句：极简主义美学强调简洁与线条感。
Example: Her room has a very cozy and vintage aesthetic.
例句：她的房间有一种非常舒适而复古的美感。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Artistic 艺术的
  • Visual 视觉的
  • Beautiful 美丽的
  • Stylish 有风格的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Aesthetic" comes from the Greek word aisthētikos, meaning “perceptible by the senses” or “sensitive.” It entered English in the 18th century through German philosophical texts.
“Aesthetic” 源自希腊语 aisthētikos，意为“感官可感知的”或“有感知力的”，18世纪通过德语哲学文献进入英语。

🧭 Word Evolution

Greek: aisthētikos – sensitive, of perception
希腊语 aisthētikos：敏感的，感知的

German: ästhetisch – pertaining to beauty
德语 ästhetisch：与美相关的

Modern English: aesthetic – concerned with beauty and art
现代英语 aesthetic：关于美与艺术的

📊 Comparison
