🛠️ accustom /əˈkʌstəm/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To make someone or yourself familiar with something, or used to a situation or condition.
使习惯于；使适应。

Example: It took a while for him to accustom himself to the cold weather.
例句：他花了一段时间才适应寒冷的天气。
Example: You’ll soon accustom yourself to the new schedule.
例句：你很快就会习惯新的时间安排。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Adapt 适应
  • Acclimate 使适应（新环境）
  • Familiarize 使熟悉
  • Adjust 调整，适应

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Accustom" comes from Old French acostumer, based on Latin consuetudo meaning “custom” or “habit.”
“Accustom” 源自古法语 acostumer，源于拉丁语 consuetudo，意为“习惯，习俗”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: consuetudo – habit, custom
拉丁语 consuetudo：习惯，风俗

Old French: acostumer – to make familiar
古法语 acostumer：使熟悉

Modern English: accustom – to make used to
现代英语 accustom：使习惯

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Adapt Neutral They had to adapt quickly to the new rules. Practical
Acclimate Formal / Technical She needed time to acclimate to the altitude. Neutral
