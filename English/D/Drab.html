🌟 drab /dræb/

Part of speech: Adjective or noun

📖 As an adjective (describing word):

Drab means dull, lacking color, excitement, or interest. It can describe things that are boring in appearance or spirit.

Example: The walls were painted a drab gray. (= The gray color was dull and uninteresting.)

📖 As a noun (a thing):

Drab can also be a dull, yellowish-brown color.

Example: He wore a uniform of military drab. (= A dull, brownish color.)

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Dull
  • Boring
  • Lifeless
  • Monotonous
  • Uninspiring
  • Colorless

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "drab".

📝It likely comes from the Old French word "drap", meaning cloth, and Middle English "drabbe", which referred to coarse, dull fabric.

  • drap (French) = cloth
  • drabbe (Middle English) = dull grayish cloth

✍️ The association with dull fabric gave rise to the figurative meaning of "dull, lacking interest."
