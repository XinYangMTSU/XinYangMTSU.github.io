🌟 diving header /ˈdaɪvɪŋ ˈhɛdər/


Part of speech: Noun phrase

📖 As a noun (phrase):

A diving header is a dramatic soccer move where a player dives forward through the air to strike the ball with their head—often while attempting to score a goal.

Example: The striker scored with an incredible diving header in the final minutes of the game.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Header
  • Flying header
  • Head strike
  • Headed goal

🔍 Etymology of the phrase /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the phrase "diving header".

📝It combines the verb dive (to plunge headfirst) with the noun header (a hit using the head in soccer).

  • Dive: To leap or plunge forward or downward
  • Header: A soccer move using the head to strike the ball

✍️ The phrase came into use through soccer commentary and became popularized through highlight reels and sports journalism.

💡 A diving header emphasizes agility, bravery, and goal-scoring flair.
