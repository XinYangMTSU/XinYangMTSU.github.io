🤐 dumb /dʌm/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Dumb has a few meanings, but most commonly it refers to someone or something that is not smart or acts in a silly or unwise way.
dumb 常用来形容某人或某事不聪明、愚蠢或不理智。

Example: That was a dumb mistake — I should have double-checked the numbers.
例句：那是个愚蠢的错误——我本该再检查一下数字。
Example: He made a dumb joke that nobody laughed at.
例句：他讲了个没人笑的冷笑话。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Foolish 愚蠢的
  • Stupid 傻的
  • Unwise 不明智的
  • Clueless 一无所知的
  • Silly 傻傻的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Originally, "dumb" meant "unable to speak," from Old English dumb meaning "silent or mute." Over time, the meaning expanded to imply a lack of intelligence.
“dumb” 原意是“不能说话”，来自古英语 dumb，意为“沉默的、哑的”。后来它的含义逐渐扩展为“缺乏智慧”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: dumb – mute, unable to speak
古英语 dumb：哑的，不能说话
⬇️
Modern English: dumb – unintelligent or foolish
现代英语 dumb：不聪明的，愚蠢的

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Stupid Neutral / Informal That was a stupid idea. Blunt / critical
Foolish Neutral It was foolish to ignore the warning. Softer / less harsh
Dumb Informal / Casual That was a dumb move. Casual / slightly harsh
