🔄 detour /ˈdiːtʊr/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

Noun: A longer or less direct route taken to avoid something or to reach a destination differently.
名词：绕行路线；迂回路线。

Verb: To take a longer route to avoid a problem or reach a different place.
动词：绕道；绕行。

Example (noun): We had to take a detour because the main road was closed.
例句（名词）：因为主路关闭，我们只好绕道而行。
Example (verb): The driver detoured around the traffic jam.
例句（动词）：司机绕过了交通堵塞路段。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Bypass 绕行
  • Alternate route 替代路线
  • Deviation 偏离；偏航
  • Roundabout way 绕行路径

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Detour" comes from the French word détour, meaning "a turning aside" or "a deviation." It originated from the Latin dis- (apart) + tornare (to turn). It entered English in the mid-18th century.
“Detour” 源自法语 détour，意为“绕行”或“偏离”。词根来自拉丁语 dis-（分开）和 tornare（转向）。18 世纪中叶进入英语。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: dis- + tornare – to turn away
拉丁语 dis- + tornare：转开；偏离

French: détour – a deviation
法语 détour：绕行

Modern English: detour – a different or longer route
现代英语 detour：绕行路线；绕道
