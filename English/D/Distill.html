🧪 distill /dɪˈstɪl/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To purify a liquid by heating and cooling; to extract the essential meaning or most important aspects of something.
通过加热和冷却来净化液体；提炼出某事物的本质或最重要方面。

Example: The scientist distilled the liquid to remove impurities.
例句：科学家通过蒸馏去除液体中的杂质。
Example: She distilled the main points from the lengthy report.
例句：她从冗长的报告中提炼出要点。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Purify 净化
  • Extract 提取
  • Refine 提炼
  • Condense 浓缩

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Distill" comes from Latin destillare, meaning "to trickle down" or "drop by drop."
distill 来自拉丁语 destillare，意为“滴下”或“逐滴流下”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: destillare – to drip or trickle
拉丁语 destillare：滴下
⬇️
Middle English: distillen – to refine through vapor
中古英语 distillen：通过蒸汽精炼
⬇️
Modern English: distill – to purify or extract essence
现代英语 distill：净化或提炼精华

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Purify Neutral The water is purified before use. Scientific
Extract Neutral They extracted key insights from the data. Analytical
Distill Neutral / Formal The book distills complex theories into simple concepts. Concise / Refined
