🌧️ damp /dæmp/

Part of speech: Adjective, noun, or verb

📖 As an adjective:

"Damp" describes something that is slightly wet or moist, often in an unpleasant way.

Example: The clothes were still damp after hanging outside all night.

📖 As a noun:

Refers to moisture, often unwanted, especially in walls or air.

Example: The basement smells of damp.

📖 As a verb:

To make something slightly wet or reduce its force or intensity.

Example: The excitement was damped by the rain.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Moist
  • Wet
  • Humid
  • Clammy
  • Muggy

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "damp" dates back to the 14th century.

📝Originating from Middle Dutch "dampe" meaning "vapor, steam."

💡 It evolved to describe something moist or slightly wet in English.

🧭 Word Evolution

Middle Dutch: dampe (vapor, steam)
⬇️
English: damp (moist, slightly wet)
