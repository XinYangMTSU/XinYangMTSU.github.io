🏷️ dub /dʌb/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meanings:

1. To give someone or something a name or title (often informal or humorous).
为某人或某物起绰号（常带幽默或非正式语气）。

Example: The press dubbed her "The Queen of Pop."
例句：媒体称她为“流行音乐女王”。

2. To replace the original sound in a film or video with another language or voice.
为电影或视频配音（常指翻译成另一种语言）。

Example: The movie was dubbed into Chinese.
例句：这部电影被译制成中文。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Name 命名
  • Label 贴标签
  • Entitle 授予称号
  • Voice-over (for films) 配音（影视）

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Dub" (meaning to name) comes from Middle English duben, meaning "to make a knight" or "to confer a title." Its modern film/audio sense came into use in the 20th century.
“Dub”（命名）源自中古英语 duben，意为“授予骑士称号”。现代“配音”之意起源于 20 世纪电影工业的发展。

🧭 Word Evolution

Middle English: duben – to knight, name
中古英语 duben：授予称号

Modern English: dub – to name or to apply new sound
现代英语 dub：命名；配音

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Name Neutral They named the baby Olivia. Neutral
Label Neutral He was unfairly labeled as lazy. Critical
Dub Informal / Technical The movie was dubbed in five languages. Descriptive
