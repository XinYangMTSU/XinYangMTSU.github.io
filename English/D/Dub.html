🌟 dub /dʌb/

Part of speech: Verb or noun

📖 As a verb (action):

To dub someone means to give them a name or title. It also refers to replacing the original sound in a movie with a translated version.

Example: The knight was dubbed Sir Lancelot by the king. (= He was given a title.)
Example: The movie was dubbed into Spanish. (= The dialogue was replaced with Spanish audio.)

📖 As a noun (a thing):

A dub can refer to a version of a film with the original dialogue replaced by one in another language.

Example: I prefer watching the original Japanese version, but the English dub is pretty good too.

🧬 Synonyms /\u02c8s\u026an\u0259\u02ccn\u026amz/

  • Name
  • Label
  • Title
  • Call
  • Translate (for audio)

🔍 Etymology of the word /\u02c8\u025bt\u0259\u02ccm\u0251\u02d0l\u0259d\u0292i/

📚 The word "dub" comes from Middle English dubben, meaning to make someone a knight by tapping them on the shoulder with a sword.

📍 Later, it also took on new meanings in music and film—such as giving a nickname or replacing audio.
