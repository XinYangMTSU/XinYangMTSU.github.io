💀 doomed /duːmd/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Certain to suffer a terrible fate; destined to fail, be destroyed, or experience misfortune.
注定遭受不幸的；必然失败或毁灭的。

Example: The mission was doomed from the start due to poor planning.
例句：由于计划不周，这项任务从一开始就注定要失败。
Example: They stared at the dark clouds, sensing the picnic was doomed.
例句：他们望着乌云，感觉野餐注定泡汤。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Fated 注定的
  • Condemned 被宣判的；注定的
  • Cursed 被诅咒的
  • Destined (to fail) 注定（失败）的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Doomed" comes from the word "doom," which originated from Old English dōm, meaning “judgment” or “fate.” Over time, it came to imply a terrible or inevitable fate.
“Doomed” 来自“doom”，该词源自古英语 dōm，意为“审判”或“命运”，后来演变为“悲惨或不可避免的命运”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: dōm – judgment, decision
古英语 dōm：审判，裁决

Middle English: doome – fate, condemnation
中古英语 doome：命运，审判

Modern English: doomed – destined for disaster
现代英语 doomed：注定遭遇不幸
