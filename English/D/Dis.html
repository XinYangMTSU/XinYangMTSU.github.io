🌟 dis /dɪs/

Part of speech: Verb or noun (informal / slang)

📖 As a verb (action):

To dis someone means to disrespect them, especially by insulting or dismissing them in a rude way.

Example: He felt disrespected when his friend dissed his outfit in front of everyone.

📖 As a noun (a thing):

A dis is a disrespectful or insulting remark.

Example: That comment was a major dis to her work.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Insult
  • Mock
  • Roast
  • Shade
  • Put down
  • Slam

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Dis" originated as an abbreviation of the word disrespect, and it became popular in African American Vernacular English (AAVE) and hip-hop culture in the late 20th century.

💡 It eventually spread into mainstream slang and is now widely understood as an informal way to describe insults.

🧭 Word Evolution

English: disrespect → dis (slang) /dɪs/
