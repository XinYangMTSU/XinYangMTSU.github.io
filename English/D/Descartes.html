Portrait of René Descartes by Frans Hals

👨‍🏫 Who was Descartes?

René Descartes (1596–1650) was a famous French philosopher, mathematician, and scientist. He is often called the "Father of Modern Philosophy."

Famous Quote: "Cogito, ergo sum." (= "I think, therefore I am.")

🔬 Contributions:

  • 📐 Invented the coordinate system in mathematics (Cartesian coordinates)
  • 🧠 Known for developing a method of doubt to reach foundational truths
  • 📘 Author of *Discourse on the Method*

🧩 Name Origin (Etymology)

📚 Descartes is a French surname. It is the Latinized form of his original name, "Renatus Cartesius."

  • René = reborn (from Latin Renatus)
  • Cartesius = Latinized surname Descartes

💡 That’s why we call his graph system "Cartesian coordinates" — it comes from the Latin version of his name!
