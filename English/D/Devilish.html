😈 devilish /ˈdev.əl.ɪʃ/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Devilish means wicked, mischievous, or extremely difficult in a playful or clever way.
“devilish” 意指顽皮的、邪恶的，或形容某事非常棘手但有趣。

Example: She flashed a devilish grin before revealing the surprise.
例句：她露出一个狡黠的笑容，然后揭示了惊喜。
Example: The professor gave us a devilish math problem to solve.
例句：教授给我们出了一道极其棘手的数学题。

🧩 Synonyms

  • Mischievous 调皮的
  • Wicked 邪恶的
  • Tricky 棘手的
  • Cunning 狡猾的
  • Playful 爱玩的

🔍 Etymology of the word

📚 “Devilish” comes from “devil” + “-ish,” forming an adjective that originally meant “of or like a devil,” and later came to describe mischievous or tricky behavior.
“devilish” 由 “devil”（魔鬼）加上形容词后缀 “-ish” 构成，原意为“像魔鬼一样”，后来引申为“调皮或棘手的行为”。
