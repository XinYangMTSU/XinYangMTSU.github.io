✊ defiance /dɪˈfaɪəns/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

Open resistance or bold disobedience to authority, rules, or expectations.
公然反抗；挑衅；违抗。

Example: She stared in defiance at the officer, refusing to back down.
例句：她带着挑衅的目光盯着警官，拒绝退让。
Example: His defiance of the dress code got him suspended.
例句：他公然违反着装规定，被停学处理。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Rebellion 叛逆
  • Resistance 抵抗
  • Disobedience 不服从
  • Boldness 大胆

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Defiance" comes from the Middle French word défiance, meaning “challenge,” and from Latin dis- (apart) + fidere (to trust). Originally, it meant distrust or renunciation of trust.
“Defiance” 源自中古法语 défiance，意为“挑战”，更早来自拉丁语 dis-（分开）+ fidere（信任），最初含义是“不信任”或“拒绝信任”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: disfidere – to distrust
拉丁语 disfidere：不信任

Old French: desfiance – challenge or resistance
古法语 desfiance：挑战或抵抗

Modern English: defiance – bold refusal or resistance
现代英语 defiance：大胆拒绝或反抗
