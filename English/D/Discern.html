👁️ discern /dɪˈsɜrn/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To discern means to see, recognize, or understand something, especially something that is not obvious or is difficult to detect.
discern 的意思是“辨别、识别、看出”，尤其是指那些不明显或难以察觉的事物。

Example: It was hard to discern what she was feeling just by looking at her face.
例句：光看她的脸，很难看出她的感受。
Example: Scientists try to discern patterns in the data.
例句：科学家试图从数据中识别出规律。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Detect 察觉
  • Perceive 感知
  • Recognize 识别
  • Distinguish 区分
  • Observe 观察

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Discern" comes from Latin discernere, meaning "to separate, distinguish," from dis- (apart) + cernere (to sift or perceive).
discern 来自拉丁语 discernere，意为“分开、区分”，由 dis-（分离）和 cernere（筛选、察觉）组成。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: discernere – to separate, to perceive
拉丁语 discernere：分开，察觉
⬇️
Old French: discerner – to distinguish, recognize
古法语 discerner：区分，识别
⬇️
Modern English: discern – to detect or recognize clearly
现代英语 discern：清晰地识别或察觉

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Recognize Neutral She recognized his voice immediately. Factual
Distinguish Formal It’s important to distinguish fact from opinion. Analytical
Discern Formal / Reflective He could discern a trace of sadness in her tone. Nuanced / perceptive
