⚔️ decisive /dɪˈsaɪsɪv/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Having the power or quality of deciding; showing determination or producing a definite result.
果断的；决定性的；明确的。

Example: The general gave a decisive order that changed the course of the battle.
例句：将军下达了一道果断的命令，改变了战局。
Example: Her decisive manner inspired confidence in the team.
例句：她果断的态度让团队充满信心。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Determined 坚定的
  • Resolute 坚决的
  • Critical 关键的
  • Final 最终的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Decisive" comes from the Latin word decidere, meaning "to cut off" or "to decide." It entered English via French as a term describing firmness and finality.
“Decisive” 源自拉丁语 decidere，意为“切断”或“决定”，通过法语进入英语，用于描述果断和决定性。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: decidere – to cut off, to decide
拉丁语 decidere：切断，决定

French: décisif – decisive
法语 décisif：果断的，决定性的

Modern English: decisive – firm, conclusive
现代英语 decisive：果断的，明确的

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Determined Neutral She was determined to succeed. Persistent
Critical Formal It was a critical moment in the match. Urgent / Key
Decisive Standard His decisive action ended the argument. Commanding / Strong
