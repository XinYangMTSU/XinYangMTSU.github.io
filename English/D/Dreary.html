🌟 dreary /ˈdrɪəri/

Part of speech: Adjective

📖 Meaning:

Dreary describes something that is dull, bleak, depressing, or lifeless, often making you feel sad or bored.

Example: It was a cold, dreary day with gray skies and steady rain.

🥉 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Dull
  • Boring
  • Bleak
  • Gloomy
  • Melancholy
  • Desolate

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Dreary originates from Old English drēorig, which meant sorrowful or bloody. Over time, the word evolved to describe anything that makes you feel down or depressed.

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: drēorig (sad, grievous)
⬇️
Middle English: dreiry
⬇️
Modern English: dreary /ˈdrɪəri/
