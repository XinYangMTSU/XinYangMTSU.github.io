😟 dismay /dɪsˈmeɪ/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

A feeling of shock, sadness, or disappointment; to cause someone to feel shocked or discouraged.
惊愕；沮丧；失望。动词形式表示使某人感到惊愕或沮丧。

Example: To her dismay, the concert was canceled at the last minute.
例句：令她沮丧的是，音乐会在最后一刻被取消了。
Example: He was dismayed by the lack of progress.
例句：他对缺乏进展感到失望。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Disappointment 失望
  • Discouragement 沮丧
  • Shock 震惊
  • Displeasure 不满

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Dismay" comes from Old French esmaier, meaning “to frighten,” and from the Latin root ex- (out of) + magare (to trouble). Over time, it came to mean emotional distress or sadness.
“Dismay” 源自古法语 esmaier，意为“使害怕”，其词根包括拉丁语的 ex-（向外）和 magare（使不安），后来演变为表示情感上的沮丧或不安。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old French: esmaier – to frighten
古法语 esmaier：使害怕

Middle English: dismaien – to alarm or sadden
中古英语 dismaien：使惊愕或沮丧

Modern English: dismay – sadness, fear, or disappointment
现代英语 dismay：悲伤、惊讶或失望

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Disappointment Neutral She felt disappointment when the plan failed. Sad
Shock Neutral He was in shock after hearing the news. Sudden / Intense
