🌟 defy /dɪˈfaɪ/

Part of speech: Verb

📖 As a verb (action):

To defy someone or something means to openly resist or refuse to obey. It can also mean to challenge something or to go against expectations or norms.

Example: She defied her parents and moved to another country to pursue her dreams.

📖 Other meanings:

It can also mean to withstand something difficult or to be impossible to explain or believe.

Example: His strength in the face of adversity defies belief.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Resist
  • Disobey
  • Challenge
  • Oppose
  • Withstand
  • Confront

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "defy".

📝It comes from the Old French word desfier, meaning “to challenge, to renounce one's allegiance.” This comes from Latin dis- ("away") + fidus ("faithful").

  • dis- = away, apart
  • fidus = faithful, loyal

✍️ Over time, “defy” came to mean resisting authority or expectations, especially boldly or publicly.

💡 This root also appears in other English words like "fidelity" and "confidence."
