🙇 deference /ˈdefərəns/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

Polite submission and respect shown to another person, especially due to their experience, status, or age.
尊重；敬意，尤指对年长者或有经验者的尊敬。

Example: He bowed his head in deference to the queen.
例句：他向女王低头致敬。
Example: Out of deference to his teacher, he remained silent.
例句：出于对老师的尊重，他保持沉默。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Respect 尊重
  • Regard 敬意
  • Honor 敬重
  • Reverence 敬畏

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Deference" comes from Latin deferre, meaning “to bring down” or “submit,” which became the Old French deferer, meaning “show respect.”
“Deference” 源自拉丁语 deferre，意为“带来、服从”，后演变为古法语 deferer，意为“表示尊敬”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: deferre – to submit or yield
拉丁语 deferre：服从、顺从

Old French: deferer – to show respect
古法语 deferer：表示尊敬

Modern English: deference – respectful submission
现代英语 deference：表示尊重的顺从

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Respect Neutral I respect her opinion. General
Reverence Formal They held the monk in reverence. Spiritual / Elevated
Deference Formal She spoke with deference to her elders. Polite / Submissive
