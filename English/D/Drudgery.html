🌟 drudgery /ˈdrʌdʒəri/

Part of speech: Noun

📖 Meaning:

Drudgery means hard, boring, or repetitive work, especially work that feels like a chore and lacks creativity or excitement.

Example: Copying the same code over and over again felt like pure drudgery.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Toil
  • Grind
  • Labor
  • Hard work
  • Monotony
  • Routine work

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "drudgery" comes from the old English word drudge, which referred to someone who does dull, menial work.

  • drudge = a person who works hard at boring tasks
  • -ery = a suffix meaning “a kind or class of work”

💡 So “drudgery” literally means the kind of work that a drudge would do — repetitive, tedious labor.

🧭 Word Evolution

Middle English: druggen (to toil)
⬇️
Modern English: drudgery = dull and fatiguing work

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Toil Formal / Poetic He toiled in the fields all day. Heavy / effortful
Work Neutral I have a lot of work to do. General
Drudgery Descriptive / Emotional Filing papers is pure drudgery. Negative / dull
