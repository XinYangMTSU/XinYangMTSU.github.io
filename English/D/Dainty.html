🧁 dainty /ˈdeɪn.ti/

Part of speech: Adjective & Noun
词性：形容词 & 名词

📖 Meaning:

As an adjective, dainty means delicately small and pretty, or refined in taste or manners.
As a noun, it refers to something that is delicious or a small treat.
作为形容词，“dainty” 意指娇小精致的，或品味高雅的；作为名词，指的是美味的小点心或精致的东西。

Example (adj): She wore a dainty silver necklace.
例句（形容词）：她戴着一条精致的银项链。
Example (noun): The bakery offered a variety of dainties.
例句（名词）：那家面包店提供各种精致美味的点心。

🧩 Synonyms

  • Delicate 精致的
  • Elegant 优雅的
  • Petite 娇小的
  • Refined 文雅的
  • Treat 美味

🔍 Etymology of the word

📚 "Dainty" comes from Middle English deinte, from Old French deintie, meaning "delightful thing," from Latin dignitas meaning "worthiness, excellence."
“dainty” 源自中古英语 deinte，来自古法语 deintie，意为“令人愉快的事物”，其根源是拉丁语 dignitas，意为“值得、优秀”。
