🌾 ditch /dɪtʃ/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

As a noun: A narrow channel dug in the ground, typically used for drainage, irrigation, or as a boundary.
名词：沟渠；通常用于排水、灌溉或作为边界。

As a verb (informal): To abandon or get rid of something or someone; to skip or leave something suddenly.
动词（非正式）：抛弃，摆脱；突然离开或逃避某事。

Example (noun): Water flowed along the ditch beside the road.
例句（名词）：水沿着路边的沟渠流动。
Example (verb): They ditched school and went to the beach instead.
例句（动词）：他们逃了课，去了海滩。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Trench 沟渠
  • Channel 水道
  • Abandon (verb) 抛弃
  • Escape (verb) 逃脱；溜走

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Ditch" comes from Old English dīc, meaning “dike” or “trench.” It is related to the word "dyke" and originally referred to a trench with the earth piled on one side to form a wall.
“Ditch” 源自古英语 dīc，意为“堤坝”或“沟渠”，与 dyke（堤）相关，最初指挖出的沟并将土堆成堤。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: dīc – trench, embankment
古英语 dīc：沟渠，堤坝

Middle English: dyche – water channel
中古英语 dyche：水沟

Modern English: ditch – narrow trench or to abandon
现代英语 ditch：沟渠；抛弃、逃避
