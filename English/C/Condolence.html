🕊️ condolence /kənˈdoʊləns/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A condolence is an expression of sympathy, especially when someone has experienced a loss, such as the death of a loved one.
condolence 指的是对他人表示同情与哀悼，特别是在对方经历亲人去世等悲痛时刻。

Example: We sent flowers and a card to offer our condolences.
例句：我们送了花和一张卡片以表示哀悼。
Example: He received many messages of condolence after his father's passing.
例句：他父亲去世后收到了许多慰问信息。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Sympathy 同情
  • Commiseration 怜悯
  • Comfort 安慰
  • Support 支持
  • Compassion 关怀

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Condolence" comes from the Latin condolere, meaning “to suffer with.” It combines con- (with) and dolere (to grieve or feel pain).
“condolence” 来自拉丁语 condolere，意为“共同悲伤”，由 con-（与……一起）和 dolere（感到悲痛）组成。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: condolere – to feel grief with someone
拉丁语 condolere：与他人一起哀悼
⬇️
French: condoléance – expression of sympathy
法语 condoléance：表示哀悼
⬇️
Modern English: condolence – words or actions showing sympathy
现代英语 condolence：表达同情或哀悼的言语或行动

📊 Comparison with Related Words
