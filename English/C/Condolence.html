🌟 Condolence /kənˈdoʊləns/

Part of speech: Noun

📖 Meaning:

A condolence is an expression of sympathy, especially for someone who has experienced the death of a loved one.

Example: She sent a card of condolence to her friend after the funeral.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Sympathy
  • Compassion
  • Comfort
  • Support
  • Empathy

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "condolence" comes from the Latin word condolere, which means "to suffer together."

📝It is made up of the prefix con- meaning "together" and dolere meaning "to grieve" or "to feel pain."

  • con- = together
  • dolere = to feel pain

💡 So, to offer condolences literally means to share in someone's sorrow or grief.
