🔀 crook /krʊk/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

(noun) A dishonest person, especially a criminal; (verb) to bend something, especially a part of the body.
（名词）骗子，罪犯；（动词）使弯曲，尤其指身体的一部分。

Example: The police finally caught the crook who had been stealing credit card information.
例句：警方终于抓住了那个窃取信用卡信息的骗子。
Example: He crooked his finger to signal her to come closer.
例句：他弯了弯手指，示意她靠近。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Thief 小偷
  • Swindler 骗子
  • Fraudster 诈骗者
  • Bend 弯曲

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Crook" comes from Old Norse krókr, meaning “hook” or “bend.”
crook 来自古斯堪的那维亚语 krókr，意为“钩子”或“弯曲”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old Norse: krókr – hook, bend
古斯堪的那维亚语 krókr：钩子、弯曲
⬇️
Middle English: crook – staff with a bend, shepherd’s crook
中古英语 crook：弯曲的手杖、牧羊杖
⬇️
Modern English: crook – dishonest person or to bend
现代英语 crook：不诚实的人或使弯曲
