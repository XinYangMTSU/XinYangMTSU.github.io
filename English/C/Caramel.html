🍬 caramel /ˈkærəˌmɛl/ or /ˈkɑːrməl/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

Caramel is a sweet, chewy, light brown food made by heating sugar. It is often used in candies, desserts, and sauces.
caramel 指一种甜的、有嚼劲的浅棕色食品，由加热糖制成，常用于糖果、甜点和酱料中。

Example: She loves caramel on her ice cream.
例句：她喜欢在冰淇淋上加焦糖。
Example: The smell of caramel filled the kitchen.
例句：厨房里充满了焦糖的香味。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Toffee 太妃糖
  • Sugar syrup 糖浆
  • Butterscotch 奶油糖
  • Candy 糖果
  • Fudge 软糖

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Caramel" comes from the French word caramel, which came from Spanish caramelo, and originally from Late Latin cannamellis ("sugar cane").
caramel 源自法语 caramel，来自西班牙语 caramelo，最初源于晚期拉丁语 cannamellis，意为“甘蔗”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Late Latin: cannamellis – sugar cane
晚期拉丁语 cannamellis：甘蔗
⬇️
Spanish: caramelo – sweet candy
西班牙语 caramelo：甜糖果
⬇️
French: caramel – sugar syrup
法语 caramel：糖浆、焦糖
