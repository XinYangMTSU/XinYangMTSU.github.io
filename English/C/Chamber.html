🏛️ chamber /ˈtʃeɪmbər/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A large room, especially used for a formal or specific purpose; an enclosed space or compartment inside a structure or organism.
房间；（尤指）大房间、议事厅；密室；体内的腔室。

Example: The king met with his advisors in the royal chamber.
例句：国王在王室大厅中会见顾问。
Example: The heart has four chambers that pump blood through the body.
例句：心脏有四个腔室，用于向全身输送血液。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Hall 大厅
  • Room 房间
  • Vault 拱顶室；密室
  • Compartment 隔间；舱室

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Chamber" comes from Old French chambre, from Latin camera meaning “vaulted room.” Originally referred to any private or enclosed space.
“Chamber” 源自古法语 chambre，来自拉丁语 camera，意思是“拱形屋顶的房间”，最初指任何私密或封闭的空间。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: camera – vaulted room
拉丁语 camera：拱形房间

Old French: chambre – room
古法语 chambre：房间

Modern English: chamber – a formal room or enclosed space
现代英语 chamber：正式场所或封闭空间

📊 Comparison

Word Meaning Example Use Tone
Room Any enclosed space in a building The room was decorated with paintings. Neutral
Chamber A formal or enclosed space The judge entered the court chamber. Formal / Historical
