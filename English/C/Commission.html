🏛️ commission /kəˈmɪʃən/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

(Noun) A group officially authorized to perform a duty; an order granting authority; a payment to an agent for services.
(Verb) To give an order for or authorize the creation of something.
（名词）委员会；授权；佣金。
（动词）委托；任命。

Example: She received a commission to paint the mural.
例句：她接到了绘制壁画的委托。
Example: The artist was commissioned by the city council.
例句：这位艺术家受市议会的委托。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Assignment 任务
  • Authorization 授权
  • Mandate 指令
  • Delegation 委派

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Commission" comes from Latin committere, meaning “to unite, bring together, entrust,” from com- (together) + mittere (to send).
“Commission” 源自拉丁语 committere，意为“委托、联合”，由 com-（一起）和 mittere（发送）组成。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: committere – to entrust, commit
拉丁语 committere：委托，承担

Old French: commission – a charge or authorization
古法语 commission：指令，委任

Modern English: commission – the act of granting power, or a group tasked with duties
现代英语 commission：授权行为，或执行任务的组织
