🚸 crosswalks /ˈkrɔːsˌwɔːks/

Part of speech: Noun (plural)
词性：名词（复数）

📖 Meaning:

Marked parts of a road where pedestrians have the right to cross safely.
人行横道；行人可安全穿越的道路标记区域。

Example: Always stop for pedestrians at crosswalks.
例句：在有人行横道时应当为行人停车让行。
Example: The city added more crosswalks near the school for safety.
例句：市政府在学校附近增设了更多人行横道以提高安全性。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Pedestrian crossing 人行横道
  • Crossing 穿越处
  • Zebra crossing (UK) 斑马线（英式英语）
  • Walkway 步道

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Crosswalk" is a compound word formed from “cross” (to go across) and “walk.” It came into use in American English in the early 20th century to refer to designated pedestrian paths across roads.
“crosswalk” 是由 “cross（穿越）” 和 “walk（步行）” 组合而成的复合词，20世纪初在美式英语中开始使用，表示为行人设定的穿越道路区域。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: cros – to go across
古英语 cros：穿过
+
Middle English: walken – to walk
中古英语 walken：行走
⬇️
Modern English: crosswalk – pedestrian crossing zone
现代英语 crosswalk：人行横道
