🌟 confine /kənˈfaɪn/

Part of speech: Verb

📖 As a verb (action):

To confine someone or something means to keep them within limits or to restrict their movement or actions.

Example: The patient was confined to bed for a week. (= They had to stay in bed and couldn’t move around freely.)

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Restrict
  • Limit
  • Restrain
  • Imprison
  • Trap
  • Keep in

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "confine" comes from the Latin word confinare, meaning "to border, to limit."

  • con- = together
  • finis = boundary, end

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Restrict Formal Restrict access to data Technical / precise
Trap Informal Trap a mouse in a box Urgent / physical
Confine Neutral / Formal Confined to a wheelchair Restrictive / clinical
