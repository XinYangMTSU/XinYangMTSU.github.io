🍪 crumble /ˈkrʌm.bəl/

Part of speech: Verb / Noun
词性：动词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

As a verb: To break or fall apart into small fragments, especially over time or under pressure.
动词：破碎；崩塌；（常因压力或时间）碎成小块。

As a noun: A dessert made with fruit topped with a crumbly mixture of flour, butter, and sugar.
名词：水果酥（甜点），由水果和碎面包屑混合层组成。

Example (verb): The old wall began to crumble after years of neglect.
例句（动词）：那堵老墙年久失修，开始崩塌。
Example (noun): Apple crumble with ice cream is one of my favorite desserts.
例句（名词）：苹果酥配冰淇淋是我最喜欢的甜点之一。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Collapse 倒塌
  • Disintegrate 瓦解
  • Break apart 破碎
  • Fall to pieces 碎裂

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Crumble" comes from Middle English crumblen, a frequentative of crimen meaning “to break into crumbs,” from Old English cruma (crumb).
“Crumble” 来自中古英语 crumblen，是 crumb（碎屑）的派生形式，源自古英语 cruma，意为“小碎片”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: cruma – crumb
古英语 cruma：碎屑

Middle English: crumblen – to break into crumbs
中古英语 crumblen：弄碎

Modern English: crumble – to fall apart or a dessert
现代英语 crumble：崩塌；水果酥

📊 Comparison

Word Type Meaning Example Use
Collapse Verb Fall down suddenly The tower collapsed during the storm.
Crumble Verb / Noun Fall apart slowly or in pieces The building began to crumble after decades.
