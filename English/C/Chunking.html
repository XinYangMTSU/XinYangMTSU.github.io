🧩 chunking /ˈtʃʌŋkɪŋ/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

Chunking is a psychological method of organizing information into smaller, manageable units to enhance memory and comprehension.
chunking 是一种心理学方法，通过将信息组织成更小、更易管理的单元，以提高记忆和理解能力。

Example: Chunking can help students memorize complex data more easily.
例句：组块法可以帮助学生更轻松地记忆复杂的数据。
Example: Phone numbers are easier to recall when using chunking.
例句：使用组块法记电话号码更容易。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Grouping 分组
  • Segmenting 分段
  • Partitioning 划分
  • Clustering 聚类

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Chunking" originates from the English word "chunk," meaning a thick piece or lump, indicating breaking information into manageable "chunks."
chunking 来自英文单词 chunk，意为厚块或团块，表示将信息分成易于管理的小块。

🧭 Word Evolution

English: chunk – a thick piece or lump
英文 chunk：厚块或团块
⬇️
English (modern): chunking – breaking down into smaller parts
现代英语 chunking：将事物分解成更小的部分
