🩸 cruelty /ˈkruːəlti/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

Cruelty means behavior that deliberately causes pain or suffering to others, whether physical or emotional. It can apply to humans, animals, or situations.
cruelty 指故意造成他人身体或心理痛苦的行为，可以用于描述对人或动物的残忍行为。

Example: The cruelty of the war left many people traumatized.
例句：战争的残酷让许多人身心受创。
Example: Animal cruelty is punishable by law in many countries.
例句：在许多国家，虐待动物是违法行为。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Brutality 野蛮；残忍
  • Violence 暴力
  • Inhumanity 不人道
  • Malice 恶意
  • Harshness 严酷

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Cruelty" comes from the Old French word crualte and Latin crudelitas, from crudelis meaning "cruel, hard-hearted." The word has long been associated with deliberate harm.
“cruelty” 来自古法语 crualte 和拉丁语 crudelitas，源自 crudelis，意思是“残忍的、冷酷的”。该词长期与故意造成伤害联系在一起。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: crudelis – cruel, merciless
拉丁语 crudelis：残忍的，无情的
⬇️
Old French: crualte – cruelty
古法语 crualte：残酷
⬇️
Modern English: cruelty – intentional harm or harshness
现代英语 cruelty：有意的伤害或残忍

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone

Inhumanity Formal The inhumanity shown in the prison was appalling. Ethical / Moral outrage
Cruelty Neutral Cruelty to animals is illegal. Unjust / harsh
