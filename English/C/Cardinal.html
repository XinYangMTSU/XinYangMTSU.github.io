🧭 cardinal /ˈkɑːrdɪnəl/

Part of speech: Adjective / Noun
词性：形容词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

As an adjective: Of the greatest importance; fundamental.
形容词：极其重要的；基本的。

As a noun: A senior official of the Roman Catholic Church; or a North American songbird with bright red feathers (male).
名词：红衣主教（天主教高级神职人员）；北美红雀（一种雄鸟呈鲜红色）。

Example (adj): Honesty is a cardinal virtue in all relationships.
例句（形容词）：诚实是所有关系中的基本美德。
Example (noun - church): The cardinal led the Easter Mass at the cathedral.
例句（名词-神职）：红衣主教主持了复活节的弥撒。
Example (noun - bird): A cardinal perched on the tree outside my window.
例句（名词-鸟类）：一只红雀栖在我窗外的树上。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Essential 必要的
  • Fundamental 根本的
  • Primary 首要的
  • Chief 主要的；最高级的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Cardinal" comes from Latin cardinalis meaning “serving as a hinge,” from cardo (hinge). It later came to mean something that everything else depends on.
“Cardinal” 源自拉丁语 cardinalis，意思是“像铰链一样起关键作用的”，源自 cardo（铰链），后来引申为“基本的、重要的”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: cardo, cardinalis – hinge, essential pivot
拉丁语 cardo（铰链）、cardinalis（核心的）

Old French: cardinal – important church figure
古法语 cardinal：天主教高级神职人员

Modern English: cardinal – fundamental (adj), or a bird / church figure (noun)
现代英语 cardinal：基本的（形容词），红衣主教或红雀（名词）

📊 Comparison

Word Part of Speech Meaning Example
Essential Adjective Absolutely necessary Water is essential for life.
Cardinal Adj / Noun Fundamental or a church official/bird Kindness is a cardinal rule.
