📘 compulsory /kəmˈpʌlsəri/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

That must be done because of a law or a rule; required.
必须做的；强制性的；义务的。

Example: Education is compulsory for children in most countries.
例句：在大多数国家，儿童教育是强制性的。
Example: Wearing seat belts in cars is now compulsory by law.
例句：现在法律规定汽车必须系安全带。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Mandatory 强制的
  • Obligatory 义务的
  • Required 必须的
  • Binding 有约束力的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Compulsory" comes from the Latin word compellere, meaning "to drive together, force."
compulsory 来自拉丁语 compellere，意为“强迫”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: compellere – to force
拉丁语 compellere：强迫
⬇️
Middle English: compulsorie – enforced, required
中古英语 compulsorie：强制的
⬇️
Modern English: compulsory – required by law or rule
现代英语 compulsory：法律或规定要求的

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Mandatory Formal Attendance at the training is mandatory. Official
Obligatory Formal There are obligatory fees for all applicants. Legalistic
Compulsory Formal Wearing helmets is compulsory in this area. Directive
