🤕 clumsy /ˈklʌm.zi/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Moving or doing things in a way that is not smooth or graceful; awkward or lacking skill.
行动或做事笨拙，不灵巧；笨手笨脚。

Example: He’s so clumsy—he drops things all the time.
例句：他真是太笨了，总是把东西掉在地上。
Example: The clumsy design made the device hard to use.
例句：这个笨拙的设计让设备难以使用。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Awkward 笨拙的
  • Uncoordinated 动作不协调的
  • Gauche 笨拙的（尤指社交）
  • Inept 无能的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Clumsy" comes from the word clumsen in Scandinavian languages, meaning "to be numb or paralyzed".
clumsy 来自斯堪的纳维亚语 clumsen，意为“麻木的”或“瘫痪的”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old Norse: klumsa – to make speechless, paralyze
古诺斯语 klumsa：使说不出话，瘫痪
⬇️
Middle English: clomsen – to be numb or awkward
中古英语 clomsen：迟钝、笨拙
⬇️
Modern English: clumsy – awkward, ungraceful
现代英语 clumsy：笨拙、不灵巧

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Awkward Neutral He gave an awkward wave before leaving. Social / Mildly Negative
Inept Formal The response to the crisis was inept and slow. Critical / Harsh
Clumsy Neutral She made a clumsy attempt to fix the chair. Descriptive / Sometimes Humorous
