🌟 choreography /ˌkɔːriˈɑːɡrəfi/

Part of speech: Noun

📖 Meaning:

Choreography is the art or practice of designing sequences of movements in which motion, form, or both are specified. It is especially used for ballet, dance, or performance art.

Example: The choreography in the musical was breathtaking, with dancers moving in perfect harmony.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Dance design
  • Movement composition
  • Stage movement
  • Dance arrangement
  • Artistic staging

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s explore the origin (etymology) of the word "choreography".

📝 It comes from the Greek words: khoreia (dance) and graphia (writing).

  • khoros = dance
  • graphia = writing

✍️ It originally meant "dance writing" or the art of writing out dance movements.

💡 In modern English, choreography refers to the creative process of designing and directing dance performances.
