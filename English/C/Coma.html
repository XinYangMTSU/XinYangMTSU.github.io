🛌 coma /ˈkoʊ.mə/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A coma is a deep state of unconsciousness from which a person cannot be awakened and does not respond to normal stimuli like pain or sound.
coma 是一种深度昏迷状态，病人无法被唤醒，对疼痛或声音等刺激没有反应。

Example: After the accident, he was in a coma for three weeks.
例句：事故后，他昏迷了三周。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Unconsciousness 无意识状态
  • Deep sleep 深度睡眠
  • Stupor 昏迷
  • Unresponsiveness 无反应状态
  • Insensibility 无知觉

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "coma" comes from the Greek word kōma, meaning "deep sleep".
coma 一词来自希腊语 kōma，意为“深度睡眠”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Greek: kōma – deep sleep /ˈkoʊ.mə/
希腊语 kōma：深度睡眠
⬇️
Latin: coma – medical term adopted in Latin
拉丁语 coma：被用作医学术语
⬇️
English: coma – deep unconscious state 英语 coma：深度昏迷状态
