🌟 cognizant /ˈkɒɡnɪzənt/

Part of speech: Adjective

📖 Definition:

If you are cognizant of something, you are aware of it or understand it clearly.

Example: She was fully cognizant of the risks involved in the experiment.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Aware
  • Conscious
  • Mindful
  • Alert
  • Informed

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "cognizant" comes from Latin roots.

📝It derives from the Latin verb cognoscere meaning "to know".

  • co- = together
  • gnoscere = to know, recognize

💡 It entered English in the 1800s as a formal word meaning "aware" or "having knowledge."

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Aware Neutral I’m aware of the deadline. Everyday / clear
Conscious Formal / Neutral He’s conscious of his tone when speaking. Precise / thoughtful
Cognizant Formal She’s cognizant of the legal issues. Academic / professional
