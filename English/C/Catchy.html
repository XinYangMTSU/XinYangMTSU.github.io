🎵 catchy /ˈkætʃi/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Catchy describes something (especially music, a phrase, or an idea) that is easy to remember and often sticks in your mind.
catchy 形容某物（尤其是歌曲、短语或想法）朗朗上口、易于记忆、令人印象深刻。

Example: The commercial had a catchy jingle that everyone kept singing.
例句：那则广告有一段朗朗上口的广告歌，大家都在哼唱。
Example: Her new song is so catchy, it’s stuck in my head!
例句：她的新歌太洗脑了，我一直在脑海里哼唱！

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Memorable 难忘的
  • Snappy 简洁有力的
  • Hooky 朗朗上口的（口语）
  • Infectious 传染性的；易传唱的
  • Appealing 吸引人的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Catchy" is formed from the verb catch + the suffix -y, meaning “likely to catch attention or stick in memory.”
catchy 源自动词 catch（抓住）加上形容词后缀 -y，意思是“容易吸引注意或令人记住的”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: ceacian – to seize or grasp
古英语 ceacian：抓住、把握
⬇️
Middle English: catchen – to capture
中古英语 catchen：捕捉
⬇️
Modern English: catchy – memorable and appealing
现代英语 catchy：朗朗上口的，有吸引力的
