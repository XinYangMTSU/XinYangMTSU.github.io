✍️ couplet /ˈkʌp.lət/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A couplet is a pair of lines in a poem, usually joined by rhyme and forming a unit.
couplet 指的是诗歌中两行成对的诗句 (对联)，通常押韵并表达一个完整的意思。

Example: Shakespeare often ended his sonnets with a rhyming couplet.
例句：莎士比亚常用押韵对句结束他的十四行诗。
Example: "Good night! Good night! Parting is such sweet sorrow / That I shall say good night till it be morrow."
例句：“晚安！晚安！别离是如此甜蜜的忧伤 / 以至我会一直说晚安直到天明。”（莎士比亚《罗密欧与朱丽叶》中的对句）

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Rhyming pair 押韵句对
  • Verse pair 诗句对
  • Stanza (loosely) 节（广义）

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "couplet" comes from the French word couplet, meaning “two pieces” or “a little pair.” It is a diminutive form of couple.
“couplet” 一词源自法语 couplet，意为“两件东西”或“一对”，是 couple（配对）的小词形。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old French: couplet – a little pair
古法语 couplet：一小对
⬇️
Modern English: couplet – two rhymed lines of verse
现代英语 couplet：押韵的两行诗句
