🕯️ commemorate /kəˈmeməreɪt/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To commemorate means to remember and show respect for someone or something, especially with a ceremony, monument, or tradition.
commemorate 意为“纪念”，指通过仪式、纪念碑或传统方式来缅怀某人或某事。

Example: A statue was built to commemorate the hero's sacrifice.
例句：一座雕像被建来纪念那位英雄的牺牲。
Example: We commemorate Earth Day each year with environmental events and awareness campaigns.
例句：我们每年通过环保活动和宣传来纪念地球日。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Honor 致敬
  • Celebrate 庆祝
  • Observe 纪念
  • Memorialize 缅怀
  • Pay tribute to 向……致敬

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Commemorate" comes from Latin commemorare, meaning "to remember together," formed from com- (together) + memor (mindful).
commemorate 源自拉丁语 commemorare，意为“共同记住”，由 com-（一起）和 memor（记得）组成。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: commemorare – to remember together
拉丁语 commemorare：共同记忆
⬇️
Old French: commemorer – to remember or honor
古法语 commemorer：缅怀，纪念
⬇️
Modern English: commemorate – to mark or honor with ceremony or memory
现代英语 commemorate：通过仪式或记忆来纪念

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Celebrate Neutral We celebrated the anniversary with a party. Joyful
Honor Formal They held a service to honor the fallen. Respectful
Commemorate Formal / Ceremonial A monument was built to commemorate the battle. Solemn / historical
