🔁 continuum /kənˈtɪn.ju.əm/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A continuum is something that changes gradually or exists as a whole, without clear or abrupt breaks between parts. It’s often used to describe a range or progression.
“continuum” 指的是一个连续体，表示变化是逐渐的，没有明确的分界，常用于描述一个范围或演变过程。

Example: Intelligence exists on a continuum and cannot be reduced to simple categories.
例句：智力是一个连续体，不能简单地归类。
Example: The spectrum of political beliefs forms a continuum rather than separate camps.
例句：政治观点的范围构成了一个连续体，而非截然不同的阵营。

🧩 Synonyms

  • Range 范围
  • Spectrum 光谱；连续体
  • Progression 进程；连续性
  • Sequence 序列
  • Flow 流动

🔍 Etymology of the word

📚 "Continuum" comes from Latin continuum, meaning “a continuous thing,” from continere, meaning “to hold together.”
“continuum” 源自拉丁语 continuum（连续体），来自 continere，意为“保持在一起”。

📊 Comparison with Related Words
