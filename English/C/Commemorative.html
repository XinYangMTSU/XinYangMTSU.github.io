🕊️ commemorative /kəˈmɛmərətɪv/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Serving to honor or preserve the memory of a person, event, or thing.
纪念的；用以纪念的。

Example: They held a commemorative ceremony for the fallen soldiers.
例句：他们为阵亡士兵举行了纪念仪式。
Example: She bought a commemorative coin from the museum.
例句：她从博物馆买了一枚纪念币。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Memorial 纪念的
  • Honorary 荣誉的
  • Tributary 致敬的
  • Remembrance 回忆的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Commemorative" comes from Latin commemoratus, past participle of commemorare ("to call to mind"), from com- (together) + memor (mindful).
“Commemorative” 源自拉丁语 commemoratus，是 commemorare 的过去分词，由 com-（一起）和 memor（记得）组成，意为“纪念”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: commemorare – to remember together
拉丁语 commemorare：共同记住

Old French: commémoratif – commemorative
古法语 commémoratif：纪念的

Modern English: commemorative – serving as a memorial
现代英语 commemorative：用于纪念的

📊 Comparison

Word Context Example Use Tone
Memorial Formal / Ceremonial A memorial plaque was placed at the site. Solemn
Honorary Award / Title He received an honorary degree. Respectful
Commemorative Cultural / Historic They issued a commemorative stamp. Respectful / Neutral
