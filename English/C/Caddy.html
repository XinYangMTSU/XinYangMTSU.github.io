🏌️ caddy /ˈkædi/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A caddy is a person who carries a golfer’s clubs and gives advice and assistance during a game.
caddy（球童）指的是在高尔夫比赛中，负责为球手背球杆并提供建议和协助的人。

Example: The caddy handed the golfer the right club for the shot.
例句：球童为球手递上了合适的球杆来击球。
Example: He worked as a golf caddy during summer break.
例句：他在暑假期间做过高尔夫球童的工作。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Assistant 助手
  • Helper 帮手
  • Attendant 服务员
  • Supporter 支持者
  • Guide 引导者

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 “Caddy” comes from the Scottish word caddie, meaning “helper or errand boy,” originally derived from the French word cadet, meaning “younger son” or “junior.”
“caddy” 来自苏格兰语 caddie，意为“帮手”或“跑腿”，源自法语 cadet，意为“年幼的儿子”或“小辈”。
