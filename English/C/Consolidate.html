🧱 consolidate /kənˈsɑː.lə.deɪt/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To make something stronger or more solid; to combine things into a more effective or coherent whole.
使加强、巩固；把多项合并成一项更有效或更有条理的整体。

Example: The company plans to consolidate its operations in one location.
例句：公司计划将其业务整合到一个地点。
Example: She consolidated her power after winning the election.
例句：她在赢得选举后巩固了自己的权力。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Strengthen 加强
  • Unify 统一
  • Combine 合并
  • Secure 稳固

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Consolidate" comes from Latin consolidare, meaning "to make firm or solid."
consolidate 来自拉丁语 consolidare，意为“使坚固”或“加固”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: consolidare – to make firm
拉丁语 consolidare：使坚固
⬇️
Middle French: consolider – to strengthen
中古法语 consolider：加强
⬇️
Modern English: consolidate – to strengthen or combine
现代英语 consolidate：加强或合并

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Strengthen Neutral Regular exercise strengthens your muscles. Practical
Unify Formal / Political The leader worked to unify the divided nation. Inspirational
Consolidate Formal The company consolidated its assets to increase efficiency. Strategic
