🛠️ clunky /ˈklʌŋki/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Clunky describes something that is heavy, awkward, or not graceful, especially in design, appearance, or function. It can also refer to language or software that feels unnatural or poorly constructed.
clunky 形容某物笨重、不灵活、不美观，常用于描述设计、语言或软件笨拙、不顺畅。

Example: The old computer had a clunky interface that was hard to use.
例句：那台旧电脑的界面很笨重，使用起来不方便。
Example: He wore clunky boots that made loud noises with every step.
例句：他穿着一双笨重的靴子，每走一步都会发出响声。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Awkward 笨拙的
  • Bulky 体积大的
  • Unrefined 粗糙的
  • Unwieldy 难操作的
  • Cumbersome 笨重的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Clunky" derives from the word clunk (a heavy dull sound) + -y (adjective-forming suffix). It originally described heavy or noisy physical objects but has since expanded to describe awkward digital or linguistic structures.
clunky 来自动词 clunk（发出沉闷声）加上形容词后缀 -y，最初形容笨重或发声大的物体，现在也用于形容数字产品或语言结构笨拙。

🧭 Word Evolution

Onomatopoeia: clunk – dull heavy sound
拟声词 clunk：沉闷的重击声
⬇️
Modern English: clunky – awkward, heavy, or unrefined
现代英语 clunky：笨拙、沉重或粗糙
