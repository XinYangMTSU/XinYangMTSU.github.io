😨 consternation /ˌkɑːnstərˈneɪʃn/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

Consternation is a feeling of sudden anxiety, shock, or confusion, often caused by something unexpected or disturbing.
consternation 意为“惊愕；震惊；突然的困惑和不安”，通常由出乎意料或令人不安的事情引起。

Example: The announcement of the sudden exam caused consternation among the students.
例句：突然宣布考试让学生们惊愕不已。
Example: To their consternation, the flight had been cancelled without notice.
例句：让他们惊讶的是，航班没有任何通知就被取消了。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Dismay 沮丧；惊愕
  • Alarm 惊慌
  • Shock 震惊
  • Panic 恐慌
  • Confusion 困惑

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Consternation" comes from Latin consternatio, from consternare meaning “to throw into confusion or dismay.” It refers to being mentally unsettled by sudden events.
“consternation” 源自拉丁语 consternatio，来自 consternare，意思是“使陷入混乱或惊恐”，指被突发事件扰乱心理状态。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: consternare – to confound or throw into confusion
拉丁语 consternare：使惊慌、混乱
⬇️
Latin noun: consternatio – dismay
拉丁语 consternatio：震惊、沮丧
⬇️
Modern English: consternation – shock, anxiety, confusion
现代英语 consternation：震惊、不安、困惑

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Dismay Neutral To his dismay, the results were poor. Disappointed / shocked
Panic Informal She panicked when she lost her phone. Fearful / urgent
Consternation Formal / Literary The news was met with widespread consternation. Shocked / unsettled
