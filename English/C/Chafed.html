🔥 chafed /tʃeɪft/

Part of speech: Verb (past tense)
词性：动词（过去式）

📖 Meaning:

To have become sore or irritated due to rubbing or friction; also used figuratively to express irritation or annoyance.
因摩擦而变得疼痛或发炎；也可引申为“感到烦恼或恼火”。

Example: His skin chafed under the rough collar.
例句：他脖子被粗糙的衣领摩擦得发炎了。
Example: She chafed at the restrictions placed on her freedom.
例句：她对自由受到限制感到恼火。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Rub 摩擦
  • Irritate 刺激；惹恼
  • Gall 擦伤；激怒
  • Vex 烦恼
  • Annoy 惹恼

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Chafe" comes from Middle English chaufen, meaning “to warm” or “make hot,” from Old French chaufer and Latin calefacere ("to make warm"). The meaning evolved to include irritation from heat or friction.
“Chafe” 源自中古英语 chaufen，意为“加热”，来自古法语 chaufer 和拉丁语 calefacere（使变暖），后来词义发展为“因摩擦或热而引起的不适”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: calefacere – to make warm
拉丁语 calefacere：加热

Old French: chaufer – to warm or heat
古法语 chaufer：加热

Middle English: chaufen – to warm; rub
中古英语 chaufen：加热；摩擦
