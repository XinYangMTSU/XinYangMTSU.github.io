🔨 carve out /kɑːrv aʊt/

Part of speech: Phrasal Verb
词性：短语动词

📖 Meaning:

To create or establish something through effort, often by cutting or removing a part; can refer to space, time, or opportunity.
通过努力或削减来开辟、赢得、挤出时间（空间、机会）等。

Example: She carved out a successful career in science.
例句：她在科学领域开创了成功的事业。
Example: I need to carve out some time for exercise this week.
例句：我这周得挤出一些时间来锻炼。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Establish 建立
  • Create 创造
  • Secure 争取
  • Dedicate (time) 专门腾出（时间）

🔍 Etymology of the phrase /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Carve out" originates from the literal act of carving material to shape something. Over time, it gained metaphorical meaning: to forcefully create or claim a portion of something.
“Carve out” 源自字面上雕刻物品的动作，后来引申为“努力争取、挤出或开辟”的含义。

🧭 Phrase Evolution

Literal: Carve out a statue from stone
字面义：从石头中雕刻出一座雕像

Figurative: Carve out time or a niche
引申义：挤出时间或开创一片天地
