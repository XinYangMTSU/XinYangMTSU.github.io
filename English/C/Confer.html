🎓 confer /kənˈfɜːr/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To grant or bestow something (such as a right, degree, honor, or benefit); to discuss something with others to make a decision.
授予；给予（如权利、学位、荣誉等）；商议；协商。

Example: The university will confer degrees at the commencement ceremony.
例句：大学将在毕业典礼上授予学位。
Example: She conferred with her advisor before making the decision.
例句：她在做出决定前与导师商议过。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Grant 授予
  • Bestow 给予
  • Award 颁发
  • Consult 商议

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Confer" comes from Latin conferre, meaning "to bring together" or "to consult," from com- (together) + ferre (to carry).
“Confer” 来自拉丁语 conferre，意为“带到一起”或“商议”；由 com-（一起）和 ferre（携带）组成。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: conferre – to bring together
拉丁语 conferre：共同带来，商议

Middle English: conferen – to compare or consult
中古英语 conferen：比较，协商

Modern English: confer – to grant or consult
现代英语 confer：授予，商议

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Grant Neutral The city granted him permission to build. Official
Bestow Formal They bestowed honors upon the soldiers. Respectful
Confer Formal The board conferred the title of Professor Emeritus. Academic / Diplomatic
