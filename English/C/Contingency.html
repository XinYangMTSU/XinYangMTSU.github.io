🔄 contingency /kənˈtɪn.dʒən.si/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

An event or condition that might happen but is not certain; a possible future event or situation, especially one that may cause problems.
可能发生但不确定的事件或情况；突发情况；应急事件。

Example: We must prepare for every contingency that might disrupt the project.
例句：我们必须为可能中断项目的任何突发情况做好准备。
Example: The budget includes a contingency fund for unexpected expenses.
例句：预算中包括一笔应急资金，用于意外支出。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Possibility 可能性
  • Emergency 紧急情况
  • Crisis 危机
  • Eventuality 可能发生的情况

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Contingency" comes from the Latin word contingentia, meaning “chance” or “possibility,” from contingere meaning “to happen, befall.”
contingency 源自拉丁语 contingentia，意思是“偶然”或“可能性”，来自 contingere，意为“发生”或“降临”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: contingere – to happen, to touch
拉丁语 contingere：发生，接触
⬇️
Latin: contingentia – chance, accident
拉丁语 contingentia：偶然事件
⬇️
Modern English: contingency – possible future event
现代英语 contingency：可能发生的事件

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
