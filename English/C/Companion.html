👫 companion /kəmˈpænjən/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A person or animal that spends time with someone or goes somewhere with them. A companion can also refer to a close friend or someone who provides company.
陪伴者；同伴；朋友；伴侣。

Example: The dog has been his faithful companion for ten years.
例句：这只狗是他十年来忠实的伙伴。
Example: She traveled the world with her lifelong companion.
例句：她和终生伴侣一起周游世界。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Friend 朋友
  • Partner 伙伴
  • Mate 伴侣
  • Associate 同伴；同事

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Companion" comes from the Latin com- (together) + panis (bread), originally meaning “someone you share bread with.” It entered English in the 14th century to mean a person who accompanies another.
“Companion” 源自拉丁语 com-（一起）和 panis（面包），原意为“一起吃面包的人”。14 世纪进入英语，指陪伴者或同伴。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: com- (together) + panis (bread)
拉丁语 com-（共同）+ panis（面包）

Old French: compaignon – someone who shares
古法语 compaignon：共享的人

Modern English: companion – a friend, partner, or someone who provides company
现代英语 companion：朋友、伴侣或陪伴者
