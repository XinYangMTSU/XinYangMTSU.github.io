💵 cash out /kæʃ aʊt/

Part of speech: Phrasal verb
词性：动词短语

📖 Meaning:

To sell an investment or convert an asset into cash, often by withdrawing from a business or account.
将投资变现或把资产转换成现金，通常指退出某个业务或账户。

Example: She decided to cash out her shares and retire early.
例句：她决定套现股票，提前退休。
Example: He cashed out of the startup after it was acquired.
例句：公司被收购后，他把股份套现退出了创业项目。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Liquidate 变现
  • Withdraw 提款
  • Sell off 出售
  • Exit 退出

🔍 Etymology of the phrase /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Cash out" comes from the literal act of exchanging chips or assets for cash, especially in gambling or banking contexts.
cash out 来自字面意思，最初指在赌博或银行中将筹码或资产兑换成现金。

🧭 Phrase Evolution

Origin: Casino and finance usage – to exchange value for cash
词源：最初用于赌场和金融领域——将价值兑换成现金
⬇️
Modern Usage: Common in startups, retirement, and investing
现代用法：常用于创业、退休和投资场景

📊 Comparison

Phrase Formality Level Example Use Tone
Liquidate Formal The company liquidated its assets to pay debts. Financial / Legal
Withdraw Neutral I withdrew $500 from my account. Everyday / Banking
Cash out Informal / Business He cashed out after the stock price doubled. Casual / Financial
