✊ clench /klɛntʃ/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To close your hands, teeth, or body tightly, especially because of anger or pain.
（尤指因愤怒或疼痛而）紧握、咬紧、绷紧。

Example: He clenched his fists in frustration.
例句：他懊恼地握紧了拳头。
Example: She clenched her jaw to keep from crying.
例句：她咬紧牙关以控制住哭泣。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Grasp 抓紧
  • Squeeze 紧握
  • Tighten 收紧
  • Clutch 抓住

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Clench" likely comes from Middle English clenchen, related to Old English clencan, meaning “to hold fast or make tight.”
clench 可能源自中古英语 clenchen，与古英语 clencan 有关，意为“紧紧抓住”或“使变紧”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: clencan – to hold tight
古英语 clencan：紧抓
⬇️
Middle English: clenchen – to fasten tightly
中古英语 clenchen：紧系、紧握
⬇️
Modern English: clench – to tighten or close firmly
现代英语 clench：紧闭、紧握

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Grasp Neutral She grasped the rope firmly. Firm / Focused
Clutch Neutral / Emotional The child clutched his mother tightly. Protective / Anxious
Clench Neutral / Descriptive He clenched his fists in anger. Tense / Intense
