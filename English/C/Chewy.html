🍬 chewy /ˈtʃuːi/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Having a texture that requires a lot of chewing; often used to describe certain candies, meats, or baked goods.
需要多次咀嚼的；有嚼劲的（常用于描述糖果、肉类或烘焙食品）。

Example: The cookies were soft and chewy in the center.
例句：这些饼干中心柔软有嚼劲。
Example: I love chewy caramel candy—it lasts longer!
例句：我喜欢有嚼劲的焦糖糖果——可以吃更久！

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Gummy 胶状的，有弹性的
  • Tough 韧的，难嚼的
  • Springy 有弹性的
  • Rubbery 像橡皮的，有韧性的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Chewy" comes from the verb “chew” + suffix “-y,” which forms adjectives. “Chew” comes from Old English ceowan, meaning “to bite or gnaw.”
“Chewy” 由动词 chew（咀嚼）和形容词后缀 -y 组成。chew 源自古英语 ceowan，意为“咬、啃”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: ceowan – to bite or gnaw
古英语 ceowan：咬或啃

Modern English: chewy – requiring chewing; having a tough or elastic texture
现代英语 chewy：需要咀嚼的；有韧性的质地
