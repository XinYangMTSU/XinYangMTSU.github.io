🧹 chore /tʃɔːr/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A routine or minor task, especially one that is done regularly around the house.
日常琐事；家庭杂务。

Example: Doing the dishes is my least favorite chore.
例句：洗碗是我最不喜欢的家务。
Example: The children each have a weekly chore list to complete.
例句：孩子们每人都有一份每周的家务清单需要完成。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Task 任务
  • Duty 职责
  • Housework 家务
  • Errand 差事

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Chore" originates from Middle English chare or chore, meaning “a turn of work,” related to Old English cearu (care or concern).
“Chore” 源自中古英语 chare 或 chore，意为“一项工作”，与古英语 cearu（关心、照料）有关。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: cearu – care, concern
古英语 cearu：关心，照料

Middle English: chare – a turn of work
中古英语 chare：一项工作

Modern English: chore – a routine household task
现代英语 chore：家庭日常杂务

📊 Comparison

Word Context Example Use Tone
Task General She has many tasks to finish today. Neutral
Errand Outside the home I ran a few errands after work. Informal
Chore Home / Routine Cleaning the bathroom is a weekend chore. Routine / Mundane
