⚔️ confront /kənˈfrʌnt/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

Confront means to face someone or something, especially in a challenging or direct way.
confront 的意思是“面对，尤其是以直接或具有挑战性的方式面对某人或某事”。

Example: She decided to confront her boss about the unfair treatment.
例句：她决定当面对老板提出不公平待遇的问题。
Example: The police confronted the suspects in the alley.
例句：警察在小巷中与嫌疑人正面交锋。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Challenge 挑战
  • Face 面对
  • Oppose 反对
  • Encounter 遭遇
  • Tackle 应对

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Confront" comes from Latin confrontare, meaning “to border on” or “to face.” It is formed from com- (together) + frons (forehead, front).
confront 源自拉丁语 confrontare，意为“面对”或“正对”，由 com-（共同）和 frons（额头，正面）组成。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: confrontare – to face or oppose
拉丁语 confrontare：面对，对抗
⬇️
Old French: confronter – to confront
中古法语 confronter：对质，面对
⬇️
Modern English: confront – to face boldly or directly
现代英语 confront：勇敢或直接面对

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Challenge Neutral She challenged the decision in court. Assertive
Oppose Formal They opposed the new policy. Critical
Confront Neutral / Direct He confronted the intruder. Bold / serious
