🤝 compromise /ˈkɑːmprəˌmaɪz/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

As a noun: An agreement in which both sides make concessions.
名词：妥协；折中；各方让步后达成的协议。

As a verb: To settle a dispute by mutual concession; to weaken or put at risk.
动词：妥协；让步；危及；损害。

Example: After hours of discussion, they reached a compromise.
例句：经过几个小时的讨论，他们达成了妥协。
Example: She refused to compromise her principles.
例句：她拒绝在原则问题上妥协。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Agreement 协议
  • Settlement 解决方案
  • Middle ground 中间立场
  • Trade-off 权衡

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Compromise" comes from the Latin compromissum, meaning “a mutual promise.” It entered English in the 15th century to describe an agreement by mutual consent.
“Compromise” 来自拉丁语 compromissum，意为“共同承诺”。15世纪进入英语，用来描述双方同意下达成的协议。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: compromissum – mutual agreement
拉丁语 compromissum：共同承诺
⬇️
Old French: compromis – settlement
古法语 compromis：解决协议
⬇️
Modern English: compromise – agreement by concession
现代英语 compromise：通过让步达成的协议
