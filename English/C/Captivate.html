✨ captivate /ˈkæptɪveɪt/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To captivate means to attract and hold someone's attention or interest completely, often by being charming, beautiful, or impressive.
captivate 的意思是“迷住、吸引”，通常指因魅力、美丽或令人印象深刻而牢牢吸引他人的注意。

Example: The performer captivated the audience with her voice.
例句：那位表演者用她的嗓音迷住了观众。
Example: His storytelling captivated everyone in the room.
例句：他的讲故事技巧吸引了在场所有人。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Charm 魅力
  • Fascinate 使着迷
  • Enchant 使入迷
  • Mesmerize 使迷住
  • Attract 吸引

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Captivate" comes from the Latin word captivare, meaning “to capture,” from captivus (captive), derived from capere — “to take or seize.”
captivate 源自拉丁语 captivare，意为“捕获”，由 captivus（俘虏）和 capere（拿、抓）演变而来。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: capere → captivus → captivare – to take, captive, capture attention
拉丁语 capere → captivus → captivare：抓住，俘虏，吸引注意
⬇️
Middle English: captivate – to charm or seize emotionally
中古英语 captivate：以情感打动、吸引
⬇️
Modern English: captivate – to attract and hold attention completely
现代英语 captivate：迷住、牢牢吸引注意
