🌀 convoluted /ˈkɑːnvəˌluːtɪd/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Extremely complex and difficult to follow; twisted or tangled in structure or reasoning.
复杂的，费解的；（结构或逻辑）盘绕的、错综复杂的。

Example: The explanation was so convoluted that no one understood it.
例句：解释过于复杂，以至于没人能听懂。
Example: The movie has a convoluted plot full of twists and surprises.
例句：这部电影的情节错综复杂，充满了转折和惊喜。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Complicated 复杂的
  • Twisted 扭曲的
  • Intricate 错综的
  • Confusing 令人困惑的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Convoluted" comes from Latin convolutus, the past participle of convolvere, meaning “to roll together.”
convoluted 源自拉丁语 convolutus，是 convolvere（卷在一起）的过去分词，意指“盘绕在一起”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: convolutus – rolled together
拉丁语 convolutus：缠绕在一起
⬇️
English: convoluted – twisted; complex or difficult
英语 convoluted：盘绕的；难理解的
