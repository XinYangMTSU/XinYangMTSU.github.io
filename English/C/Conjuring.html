✨ conjuring /ˈkʌndʒərɪŋ/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

The act of performing magic tricks or calling upon supernatural forces; the process of bringing something to mind vividly, as if by magic.
变魔术；施展魔法；像变魔术般地唤起或使人想起某事。

Example: The magician’s conjuring left the audience amazed.
例句：魔术师的变魔术表演令观众惊叹不已。
Example: The film is a masterful conjuring of childhood memories.
例句：这部电影巧妙地唤起了童年的回忆。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Magic 魔法
  • Summoning 召唤
  • Evocation 唤起
  • Illusion 幻象

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Conjuring" comes from the verb "conjure," from Latin conjurare meaning “to swear together, to invoke,” from com- (together) + jurare (to swear).
“Conjuring” 源自动词 conjure，来自拉丁语 conjurare，意为“共同宣誓”或“召唤”，由 com-（一起）和 jurare（宣誓）组成。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: conjurare – to swear together, to invoke
拉丁语 conjurare：联合发誓，召唤

Old French: conjurer – to plot, invoke, charm
古法语 conjurer：阴谋、施法、召唤

Modern English: conjuring – performing magic or summoning memories
现代英语 conjuring：施展魔法或唤起回忆
