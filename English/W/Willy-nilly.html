🎲 willy-nilly /ˌwɪliˈnɪli/

Part of speech: Adverb / Adjective
词性：副词 / 形容词

📖 Meaning:

As an adverb: Whether one likes it or not; without order or planning.
副词：无论愿不愿意；杂乱无章地；毫无计划地。

As an adjective: Lacking organization or control; random or chaotic.
形容词：混乱的；随意的；无条理的。

Example (adverb): We were dragged willy-nilly into the debate.
例句（副词）：我们被迫卷入了这场辩论，无论愿不愿意。
Example (adjective): The willy-nilly arrangement of furniture made the room look messy.
例句（形容词）：家具随意摆放使房间看起来很凌乱。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Haphazardly 杂乱地
  • Unwillingly 不情愿地
  • Chaotically 混乱地
  • Randomly 随意地

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Willy-nilly" comes from the Old English phrase will he, nill he, meaning “whether he wants to or not.” Over time, it evolved into a humorous-sounding reduplication used to describe something done without planning or unwillingly.
“Willy-nilly” 源自古英语表达“will he, nill he”，意思是“无论他愿不愿意”。后发展为押韵的幽默短语，用于形容无计划的或被迫的行为。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: will-he, nill-he – whether he wants or not
古英语：愿意与否

Middle English: wille nille – willing or unwilling
中古英语：愿或不愿

Modern English: willy-nilly – without choice or order
现代英语：无序地；不情愿地
