🔄 wind up /waɪnd ʌp/

Part of speech: Phrasal Verb
词性：动词短语

📖 Meanings:

1. To end up in a situation, often unexpectedly
最终处于（常出人意料的）某种状态或境地

Example: He wound up in New York without a job or a place to stay.
例句：他最后流落到纽约，没有工作也没有住处。

2. To finish something
结束某事

Example: Let’s wind up the meeting and go to lunch.
例句：我们把会议结束，然后去吃午饭吧。

3. (Informal) To annoy or tease someone (British English)
（英式英语，非正式）戏弄或惹恼某人

Example: Are you trying to wind me up?
例句：你是想气我吗？

🧩 Related Expressions

  • End up 最终成为
  • Finish off 完成，结束
  • Mess with (for teasing) 戏弄，耍人

🔍 Etymology

📚 Originally referred to turning a key to tighten a spring (like winding up a clock or toy). Over time, it gained metaphorical meanings related to **concluding** or **leading to an outcome**.
最初指的是用钥匙拧紧发条（比如钟表或玩具），后来引申为“结束”或“导致某种结果”。
