🧠 wit /wɪt/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

Wit refers to the ability to say or write things that are clever and usually funny.
wit 指“机智”，尤其是以巧妙而风趣的方式表达思想的能力。

Example: She is known for her quick wit and clever remarks.
例句：她以反应敏捷和巧妙的话语而闻名。
Example: The author's wit shines through in every chapter.
例句：这位作者在每一章中都展现出她的机智。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Humor 幽默
  • Sharpness 机敏
  • Cleverness 聪明
  • Wordplay 文字游戏
  • Banter 打趣

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Wit" comes from Old English wit, meaning “understanding, knowledge, sense”.
wit 来自古英语 wit，意为“理解、知识、理智”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: wit – knowledge, understanding
古英语 wit：知识，理解
⬇️
Middle English: wit – mental sharpness, cleverness
中古英语 wit：头脑敏捷，机智
⬇️
Modern English: wit – clever and often humorous expression
现代英语 wit：聪明且通常带幽默感的表达

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Humor Neutral He has a good sense of humor. Casual / friendly
Sharpness Formal Her mental sharpness is impressive. Serious / intellectual
Wit Neutral / Witty His wit kept everyone laughing. Clever / humorous
