🌟 wacky /ˈwæki/

Part of speech: Adjective

📖 As an adjective (description):

The word wacky describes something or someone that is funny, unusual, or a little crazy in a fun way.

Example: He wore a wacky costume with polka dots and a big red wig.

📖 Informal use:

"Wacky" is often used in a playful or affectionate tone to describe eccentric behavior or silly ideas.

Example: That’s a wacky idea—but it just might work!

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Goofy
  • Weird
  • Silly
  • Quirky
  • Loony
  • Zany

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "wacky".

📝It comes from the slang term "wack" or "whack", meaning eccentric or crazy, which appeared in early 20th-century American English.

  • whack (slang): = odd, eccentric

✍️ Over time, it softened in meaning and became a fun, child-friendly term for something silly or unconventional.

💡 It is often used affectionately today, especially in children's books, comedy, and informal speech.
