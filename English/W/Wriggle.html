🌟 wriggle /ˈrɪɡəl/

Part of speech: Verb or noun

📖 As a verb (action):

To wriggle means to twist and turn your body with small, quick movements, often in a way that shows discomfort or effort to escape.

Example: The child wriggled in her seat, trying to get comfortable.
Example: The worm wriggled through the soil.

📖 As a noun (a thing):

A wriggle is a twisting or turning movement, like the kind a small animal or child might make.

Example: With a quick wriggle, the fish escaped from his hands.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Squirm
  • Twist
  • Wiggle
  • Fidget
  • Slide

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "wriggle" likely comes from Middle Low German wricken, meaning “to twist.” It shares roots with words like "wiggle" and "wring."

✍️ It has been used in English since the 1500s to describe movements like twisting or sliding through a narrow space.

🧭 Word Evolution

Middle Low German: wricken — to twist
⬇️

⬇️
