🎈 whim /wɪm/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A sudden and impulsive idea, desire, or change of mind, often without serious thought.
突发奇想；一时的念头；心血来潮。

Example: He quit his job on a whim and moved to the countryside.
例句：他一时兴起辞了工作，搬到了乡下。
Example: She bought the expensive necklace on a whim, even though she didn’t need it.
例句：她一时冲动买下了那条昂贵的项链，尽管并不需要它。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Impulse 冲动
  • Fancy 幻想
  • Caprice 反复无常
  • Notion 念头

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word “whim” is likely a shortened form of “whim-wham,” an old term for a fanciful or odd idea. It has been used since the 17th century to describe sudden, whimsical notions.
“whim” 可能来自旧词 “whim-wham”，意指奇思妙想或古怪的想法。自17世纪起就被用来指代突如其来的念头。

🧭 Word Evolution

Early English: whim-wham – odd fancy
早期英语 whim-wham：奇怪的想法
⬇️
Modern English: whim – sudden, whimsical desire
现代英语 whim：一时的冲动或奇想

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Impulse Neutral She acted on impulse and booked the trip. Quick / Unplanned
Caprice Formal His actions were governed by caprice, not logic. Unpredictable / Emotional
Whim Neutral / Playful They took a road trip on a whim. Light / Spontaneous
