🌟 wondrous /ˈwʌndrəs/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Inspiring a feeling of wonder or amazement; remarkably beautiful or impressive.
令人惊叹的；奇妙的；令人惊奇的。

Example: The child gazed at the wondrous night sky filled with stars.
例句：孩子凝视着布满星星的奇妙夜空。
Example: They shared a wondrous tale of magic and adventure.
例句：他们分享了一个关于魔法和冒险的奇妙故事。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Amazing 令人惊异的
  • Marvelous 了不起的
  • Incredible 不可思议的
  • Stunning 令人震惊的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Wondrous" is a poetic form of "wonder," dating back to Middle English. It evolved from the noun “wonder” + suffix “-ous,” meaning “full of.”
wondrous 是 wonder（奇迹）的诗意形容词形式，源自中古英语，由名词 wonder 加上后缀 -ous（表示充满的）构成。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: wundor – a marvel, miracle
古英语 wundor：奇迹
⬇️
Middle English: wondrous – marvelous, wonderful
中古英语 wondrous：令人惊奇的
⬇️
Modern English: wondrous – inspiring wonder or admiration
现代英语 wondrous：令人惊叹的，奇妙的
