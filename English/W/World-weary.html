😔 world-weary /ˈwɜːrld ˌwɪəri/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Feeling emotionally tired or cynical due to too much experience with the troubles of the world; weary of life, often from disillusionment or overexposure.
对世界感到厌倦或疲惫；因经历太多而感到麻木或失望。

Example: His world-weary expression suggested he had seen too much hardship.
例句：他那厌世的表情说明他经历了太多苦难。
Example: She gave a world-weary sigh and continued her work in silence.
例句：她无奈地叹了口气，默默继续工作。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Jaded 疲倦的
  • Disillusioned 幻灭的
  • Weary 疲惫的
  • Resigned 顺从而无奈的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "World-weary" is a compound word formed from “world” + “weary.” “Weary” comes from Old English wērig, meaning “tired” or “exhausted.” The full term suggests emotional or existential exhaustion from living in or witnessing the burdens of the world.
“World-weary” 是由“world”（世界）和“weary”（疲惫的）构成的复合词。 “Weary” 来自古英语 wērig，意为“疲倦的”。整个词传达了一种因经历世界之苦而感到精神上或存在上的疲惫。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: wērig – tired, exhausted
古英语 wērig：疲惫的

Middle English: weery – to grow tired of something
中古英语 weery：对某事感到厌倦

Modern: world-weary – emotionally tired from life
现代英语 world-weary：因世界的重压而感到疲惫
