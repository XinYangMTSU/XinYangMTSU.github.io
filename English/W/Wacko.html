🤪 wacko /ˈwæk.oʊ/

Part of speech: Noun / Adjective (Informal)
词性：名词 / 形容词（非正式）

📖 Meaning:

Wacko is an informal term used to describe someone who is considered crazy, eccentric, or behaving in a very unusual way. As an adjective, it describes bizarre or irrational things.
“wacko” 是一个非正式词汇，用来形容行为古怪、疯狂或非常不同寻常的人或事物。

Example: Some people dismissed the idea as a wacko theory.
例句：一些人把这个观点斥为异想天开的理论。
Example: He acted like a total wacko at the meeting.
例句：他在会议上的表现完全像个疯子。

🧩 Synonyms

  • Crazy person 疯子
  • Lunatic 疯人
  • Oddball 怪人
  • Weirdo 怪胎
  • Nuts 疯的；怪的

🔍 Etymology of the word

📚 "Wacko" is derived from the word "wacky," which came into use in the 1930s from the Scots word whack meaning a blow or strike—eventually extending to mean mentally unbalanced.
“wacko” 源自动词“wacky”，而“wacky”起源于 20 世纪 30 年代的苏格兰语 whack（击打），后引申为“精神不稳定”。
