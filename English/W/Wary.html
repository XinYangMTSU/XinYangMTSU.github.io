🌟 Wary /ˈwɛəri/

Part of speech: Adjective

📖 Meaning:

Wary means being cautious or alert to danger or potential problems. A person who is wary is careful because they suspect that something might go wrong.

Example: He was wary of signing the contract without reading the fine print.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Cautious
  • Careful
  • Alert
  • Suspicious
  • Watchful
  • Guarded

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word wary comes from the Old English word wær, meaning "careful" or "aware of danger." It has been used since before the 12th century in English literature to describe people who are alert and cautious.

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: wær = careful, cautious
⬇️
Middle English: wary
⬇️
Modern English: wary = cautious or alert /ˈwɛəri/
