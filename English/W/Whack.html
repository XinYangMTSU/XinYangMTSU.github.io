🪓 whack /wæk/

Part of speech: Verb / Noun (informal)
词性：动词 / 名词（非正式）

📖 Meaning:

As a verb: To hit someone or something with a sharp blow.
动词：猛击，重打。

As a noun: A sharp or resounding blow.
名词：重击，猛打。

Example: He whacked the ball over the fence.
例句：他猛地把球击出围栏。
Example: She gave the bug a whack with her book.
例句：她用书猛打那只虫子。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Smack 拍击
  • Strike 击打
  • Hit 打
  • Thump 重击

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Whack" is of imitative origin, meaning it likely comes from the sound of a heavy blow. It has been used since the 18th century in informal English.
“whack” 是拟声词，模仿重击时发出的声音，18世纪以来在非正式英语中使用。

🧭 Word Evolution

Origin: Imitative of a sharp hitting sound
词源：模仿重击时的声音
⬇️
18th Century: Used to describe blows or strikes
18世纪：用于描述猛击或重打
⬇️
Modern English: Also used in slang (e.g., "out of whack")
现代英语：也用于俚语中（如 “out of whack” 表示出故障、不正常）

📊 Comparison

Word Intensity Example Use Tone
Whack Strong / Informal He whacked the nail with a hammer. Casual / Physical
Smack Medium She smacked him on the arm playfully. Light / Playful
Strike Neutral / Formal The worker struck the metal with precision. Serious / Technical
