🧳 jaunt /dʒɔːnt/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A short trip or journey taken for pleasure.
短途旅行；愉快的短程出游。

Example: We took a weekend jaunt to the mountains.
例句：我们进行了一个周末的山中短途旅行。
Example: Their jaunt to the coast was spontaneous and fun.
例句：他们临时起意的海边之旅充满乐趣。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Excursion 远足，短途旅行
  • Outing 出游
  • Trip 旅行
  • Getaway 小型度假

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Jaunt" likely comes from 17th-century English, possibly a variant of “jaunce,” meaning to prance or make a tiring journey on horseback.
jaunt 可能源于17世纪的英语词“jaunce”，意思是骑马跋涉，后来演变为轻松愉快的短途旅行。

🧭 Word Evolution

Early English: jaunce – to prance or move up and down
早期英语 jaunce：跳跃前进或劳累地骑行
⬇️
Modern English: jaunt – a fun short trip
现代英语 jaunt：愉快的短途旅行

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Example Use Tone
Jaunt Informal / Cheerful They went on a little jaunt to the lake. Playful / Light
Excursion Formal The school organized an excursion to the museum. Educational
Getaway Informal We booked a weekend getaway in the countryside. Relaxed / Leisure
