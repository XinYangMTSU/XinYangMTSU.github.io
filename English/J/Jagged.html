🪨 jagged /ˈdʒæɡɪd/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Having rough, sharp points protruding; uneven and irregular in shape or outline.
有锯齿状边缘的；参差不齐的；不规则的。

Example: The climbers carefully moved across the jagged rocks.
例句：攀岩者小心翼翼地越过锯齿状的岩石。
Example: He drew a jagged line through the paper.
例句：他在纸上画了一条锯齿状的线。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Ragged 参差的
  • Serrated 锯齿状的
  • Uneven 不平整的
  • Rough 粗糙的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Jagged" comes from the word "jag," meaning a sharp projection or point, which likely originates from Middle English.
jagged 源自“jag”一词，意为尖锐的突出物或尖端，可能起源于中古英语。

🧭 Word Evolution

Middle English: jag – a notch, sharp point
中古英语 jag：缺口，尖点
⬇️
Modern English: jagged – having sharp, uneven edges
现代英语 jagged：边缘参差不齐、锋利
