🏇 jockeying /ˈdʒɑːkiɪŋ/

Part of speech: Verb (present participle)
词性：动词（现在分词）

📖 Meaning:

Trying to gain an advantage or position over others through skillful or strategic maneuvering.
通过技巧性或策略性的手段争取优势或有利位置。

Example: The candidates were jockeying for position in the upcoming debate.
例句：候选人们在为即将到来的辩论争夺有利位置。
Example: Companies are constantly jockeying for market share.
例句：各家公司不断在争夺市场份额。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Maneuvering 策略行动
  • Competing 竞争
  • Positioning 布局，定位
  • Contending 争夺

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Jockeying" comes from the noun “jockey,” originally meaning a professional horse rider. It evolved into a verb describing **maneuvering for advantage**, much like a jockey positioning for the lead in a race.
jockeying 源自动词 jockey，原指赛马中的骑师，后引申为“争抢有利位置”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Middle English: jockei – boy, fellow
中古英语 jockei：小伙子
⬇️
Late English: jockey – horse rider
后期英语 jockey：骑师
⬇️
Modern English: jockeying – competing skillfully for position
现代英语 jockeying：巧妙地争取有利位置或优势

📊 Comparison
