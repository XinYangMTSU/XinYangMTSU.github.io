🛠️ jack up /dʒæk ʌp/

Part of speech: Phrasal Verb
词性：动词短语

📖 Meaning:

To suddenly raise or increase something, such as prices or pressure; to lift something (like a car) using a jack.
突然提高（价格、数量等）；用千斤顶抬起（如汽车）。

Example: They jacked up the prices right before the holiday season.
例句：他们在节假日前突然抬高了价格。
Example: He jacked up the car to change the flat tire.
例句：他用千斤顶把车抬起来换轮胎。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Raise 提高
  • Increase 增加
  • Inflate 抬高
  • Hoist 举起

🔍 Etymology of the phrase /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The phrase "jack up" comes from the noun "jack" (a mechanical lifting device) and the verb "up," meaning to lift or raise something. It later gained figurative meanings like "increase suddenly."
“Jack up” 源自“jack”（起重器）与“up”（向上），原意为“用千斤顶抬起”，后来引申为“突然增加”。

🧭 Word Evolution

English: jack (noun) – a tool for lifting
英语 jack：起重器

Phrasal Verb: jack up – to lift up or raise rapidly
动词短语 jack up：抬高、猛增

Modern Usage: jack up the prices, jack up the car
现代用法：提高价格，用千斤顶抬车

📊 Comparison

Phrase Context Example Use Tone
Raise General The landlord raised the rent last month. Neutral
Inflate Economic / Negative They inflated the prices for tourists. Critical
Jack up Informal / Action-Oriented They jacked up the ticket price overnight. Casual / Strong
