🇺🇸 jingoistic /ˌdʒɪŋ.ɡoʊˈɪs.tɪk/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Excessively patriotic, especially in an aggressive or warlike way; characterized by belligerent support for one's country.
过度爱国的（尤指好战的）；沙文主义的。

Example: The candidate's jingoistic speeches alarmed many international observers.
例句：该候选人极端爱国的演讲令许多国际观察员感到担忧。
Example: His jingoistic attitude alienated foreign diplomats.
例句：他咄咄逼人的爱国态度让外国外交官疏远了他。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Nationalistic 民族主义的
  • Patriotic (to excess) 过度爱国的
  • Chauvinistic 沙文主义的
  • Belligerent 好战的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Jingoistic" comes from the term "jingoism," which originated in a British music hall song during the Russo-Turkish War (1877–78), where the refrain “we don't want to fight but by jingo if we do...” expressed aggressive patriotism.
“Jingoistic” 源于“jingoism”，这个词起源于1877-78年俄土战争期间一首英国歌厅歌曲，歌词中表达了激进的爱国情绪：“我们不想打仗，但若打就拼命。”

🧭 Word Evolution

English (19th century): Jingo – derived from a patriotic song
19世纪英语：Jingo，来自爱国歌曲

Modern English: Jingoistic – aggressively nationalistic
现代英语 jingoistic：好战的民族主义者
