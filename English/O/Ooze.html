🌟 ooze /uːz/

Part of speech: Verb or noun

📖 As a verb (action):

To ooze means to flow slowly and thickly, like a liquid. It can also mean to clearly show a strong feeling or quality.

Example: The sauce oozed out of the sandwich. (= The sauce slowly came out.)
Example: She oozed confidence. (= She clearly showed confidence.)

📖 As a noun (a thing):

Ooze refers to any thick, sticky liquid or substance that slowly flows out.

Example: The swamp was full of dark green ooze.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Seep
  • Drip
  • Leak
  • Exude
  • Flow
  • Trickle

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "ooze" comes from Old English wōs meaning "juice" or "sap".

  • wōs = juice, sap, or moisture

✍️ The word evolved in Middle English as "wose" and later became "ooze" in Modern English.
