🌟 oblivion /əˈblɪviən/

Part of speech: Noun

📖 Meaning:

Oblivion means the state of being forgotten or unknown, or the state of being unaware or unconscious.

Example: The once-famous actor faded into oblivion after retiring.
Another use: After the surgery, she sank into blissful oblivion.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Forgetfulness
  • Unawareness
  • Neglect
  • Darkness
  • Extinction
  • Obscurity

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s explore the origin of the word "oblivion".

📝It comes from Latin oblivio, meaning "forgetfulness."

  • oblivisci = to forget

✍️ The word entered English through Old French in the 14th century as oblivion, maintaining its original sense of being forgotten or unknown.
