🌫️ obscured /əbˈskjʊrd/

Part of speech: Verb (past tense and past participle of "obscure")
词性：动词（obscure 的过去式和过去分词）

📖 Meaning:

To make something difficult to see, hear, or understand; to hide or block something from view.
使模糊；遮掩；使难以理解。

Example: The fog obscured the mountain view.
例句：浓雾遮住了山景。
Example: His true motives were obscured by vague language.
例句：他真正的动机被模糊的语言所掩盖。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Hidden 隐藏的
  • Veiled 掩盖的
  • Masked 遮蔽的
  • Clouded 模糊的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Obscured" comes from the Latin root obscurare, meaning “to make dark,” from ob (over) + scurus (dark).
obscured 来自拉丁语 obscurare，意为“使变暗”，由 ob（覆盖）和 scurus（黑暗）组成。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: obscurare – to make dark
拉丁语 obscurare：使变暗
⬇️
Middle English: obscuren – to block or hide
中古英语 obscuren：遮挡、隐藏
⬇️
Modern English: obscured – hidden, unclear, or difficult to perceive
现代英语 obscured：模糊的、被遮住的

📊 Comparison

Word Tone Example Use Context
Obscured Neutral The truth was obscured by rumors. Literary / Descriptive
Hidden Neutral The treasure was hidden in the cave. General
Clouded Figurative Her judgment was clouded by emotion. Emotional / Psychological
