🔁 obsession /əbˈseʃən/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

Obsession refers to an idea or thought that continually preoccupies or intrudes on a person's mind. It can also mean an intense or unhealthy focus on something.
obsession 意为“痴迷、执念”，指不断萦绕心头的想法或念头，也指对某事的强烈甚至不健康的关注。

Example: His obsession with perfection made him rewrite the report five times.
例句：他对完美的执着使他把报告重写了五次。
Example: She has an obsession with collecting rare books.
例句：她痴迷于收集稀有书籍。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Fixation 执着
  • Compulsion 强迫
  • Preoccupation 心神不宁
  • Infatuation 迷恋
  • Craving 渴望

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Obsession" comes from Latin obsessio, meaning “a siege” or “a blockade,” from obsidere — “to besiege.” It later took on the meaning of something that takes over the mind.
obsession 源自拉丁语 obsessio，原意为“围攻、包围”，来自 obsidere（围困）。后来引申为“占据心灵的事物”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: obsidere / obsessio – to besiege, siege
拉丁语 obsidere / obsessio：围攻，占据
⬇️
Middle French: obsession – haunting of the mind
中古法语 obsession：萦绕心头的念头
⬇️
Modern English: obsession – overwhelming fixation or focus
现代英语 obsession：难以摆脱的执着或关注
