🔵 orb /ɔːrb/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A spherical object or shape, often used to describe the sun, moon, or a glowing ball; also a symbol of power in royal regalia.
球体；常用于描述太阳、月亮或发光球体；也是皇家权力的象征物之一。

Example: A golden orb sat atop the royal scepter.
例句：一颗金色圆球装饰在皇家权杖顶端。
Example: The full moon appeared as a bright orb in the night sky.
例句：满月在夜空中如一个明亮的圆球。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Sphere 球体
  • Globe 球形物
  • Ball 球
  • Circle 圆圈（比喻用法）

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Orb" comes from Latin orbis, meaning “circle” or “disk.”
orb 来自拉丁语 orbis，意为“圆圈”或“圆盘”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: orbis – circle, ring
拉丁语 orbis：圆圈、环形
⬇️
Old French: orbe – sphere
古法语 orbe：球体
⬇️
Modern English: orb – round object or symbol of power
现代英语 orb：球体、权力的象征

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Sphere Neutral / Scientific The earth is an oblate sphere. Mathematical / Scientific
Globe Neutral A glass globe lit the hallway. Descriptive / Practical
Orb Literary / Formal The sun rose as a glowing orb. Poetic / Symbolic
