🐐 obstinate /ˈɑːbstɪnət/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Stubbornly refusing to change one’s opinion or chosen course of action, despite attempts to persuade one to do so.
固执的；顽固的；倔强的。

Example: He was too obstinate to admit that he had made a mistake.
例句：他太固执，不愿承认自己犯了错误。
Example: The donkey remained obstinate, refusing to move no matter how hard we pushed.
例句：那头驴倔得很，无论我们怎么推它都不动。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Stubborn 顽固的
  • Headstrong 任性的
  • Unyielding 不屈服的
  • Inflexible 不灵活的；死板的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Obstinate" comes from Latin obstinatus, the past participle of obstinare, meaning “to persist, be resolved.”
“Obstinate” 源自拉丁语 obstinatus，是 obstinare（坚持、决心）的过去分词。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: obstinare – to persist, to be determined
拉丁语 obstinare：坚持，决意

Old French: obstinat – stubborn, fixed in opinion
古法语 obstinat：固执的

Modern English: obstinate – unreasonably determined
现代英语 obstinate：不听劝的，顽固的
