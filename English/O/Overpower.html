💪 overpower /ˌoʊvərˈpaʊər/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To defeat or overcome by superior force; to be too strong or intense to resist or handle.
以压倒性力量制服；（感情、气味等）使无法抗拒。

Example: The police managed to overpower the suspect without using weapons.
例句：警方在未动用武器的情况下成功制服了嫌疑人。
Example: The scent of the flowers overpowered the entire room.
例句：花香充满整个房间，令人难以忽视。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Defeat 击败
  • Subdue 制服
  • Overwhelm 压倒
  • Crush 压垮

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Overpower" comes from the prefix over- (meaning “excessively” or “above”) and the verb power, meaning “to exert strength.” Together, it means to exert strength to an overwhelming degree.
“Overpower” 源自前缀 over-（过度）与动词 power（施加力量），合起来意为“以过人的力量压制”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: ofer – over, above
古英语 ofer：超过，高于

Middle English: power from Old French poeir
中古英语 power：力量（来自古法语）

Modern English: overpower – to dominate with force
现代英语 overpower：用力量压倒或控制

📊 Comparison

Word Context Example Use Tone
Subdue Military / Police The soldiers subdued the rebels. Neutral / Formal
Overwhelm Emotional / Situational She was overwhelmed with gratitude. Emotional
Overpower Physical / Sensory The heat was overpowering in the desert. Forceful
