🕰️ obsolete /ˌɑːbsəˈliːt/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

No longer used or no longer useful; out of date.
不再使用的；过时的；淘汰的。

Example: VHS tapes have become obsolete in the age of digital streaming.
例句：在数字流媒体时代，VHS录像带已经变得过时。
Example: Some traditional farming techniques are now considered obsolete.
例句：一些传统农业技术现在被认为已经落伍。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Outdated 过时的
  • Extinct 灭绝的；不再使用的
  • Old-fashioned 老式的
  • Antiquated 陈旧的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Obsolete" comes from Latin obsoletus, meaning "worn out" or "no longer used."
obsolete 来自拉丁语 obsoletus，意为“废弃的、过时的”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: obsoletus – worn out, outdated
拉丁语 obsoletus：破旧的，过时的
⬇️
Middle English: obsolet – fallen into disuse
中古英语 obsolet：不再使用的
⬇️
Modern English: obsolete – no longer in use
现代英语 obsolete：不再使用的；过时的

📊 Comparison

Word Degree of Use Example Use Tone
Obsolete Completely out of use The device became obsolete within a few years. Neutral / Technical
Outdated Still exists but not preferred The data in the report is outdated. Neutral
Old-fashioned Still in use but seen as old His views are considered old-fashioned. Casual / Slightly negative
