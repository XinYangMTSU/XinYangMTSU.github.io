🌟 obstacle /ˈɒbstəkl/

Part of speech: Noun

📖 As a noun (a thing):

An obstacle is something that blocks your way or makes it difficult for you to do something. It can be a physical object or a challenge in life, work, or thought.

Example: Lack of time was the biggest obstacle to completing the project.

📖 Figurative meaning:

It often refers to a difficulty that needs to be overcome, especially in achieving a goal.

Example: She faced many obstacles on her way to becoming a doctor.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Barrier
  • Hurdle
  • Block
  • Challenge
  • Impediment
  • Difficulty

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "obstacle".

📝It comes from Latin obstāculum, meaning "a hindrance or impediment."

  • ob- = against
  • stāre = to stand

✍️ The literal meaning was “something that stands in the way.”

💡 The word passed into French as obstacle and was borrowed into English in the late 14th century.
