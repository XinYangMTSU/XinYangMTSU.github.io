👀 skim /skɪm/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 As a verb (action):

To skim means to read something quickly to get the main ideas, or to move lightly over a surface.
skim（动词）意思是快速浏览以获取主要信息，或轻轻掠过某物表面。

Example: I skimmed the article to find the main points.
例句：我快速浏览了文章以找到要点。
Example: The bird skimmed across the water.
例句：那只鸟轻轻掠过水面。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Scan 扫读
  • Browse 浏览
  • Glance over 略读
  • Flip through 翻阅

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "skim" comes from Middle English skymen, meaning "to remove a substance from the surface."
“skim”一词来自中古英语“skymen”，原意是“从表面刮去一层”。

  • Old use: removing cream from milk 古用法：从牛奶中撇去奶油
  • Modern sense: to pass over something lightly 现代含义：轻轻掠过或快速略读

💡 So when you "skim" a text, you're just taking the surface—quickly getting the general idea.
所以当你“skim”一篇文章时，就是抓住表面，迅速获取大意。

🧭 Word Evolution

Middle English: skymen – to skim off
中古英语 skymen：撇去表层
⬇️
Modern English: skim – to glance or move lightly
现代英语 skim：快速阅读，轻掠

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Scan Neutral He scanned the page for her name. Quick / focused
Glance over Informal I just glanced over the email. Casual
Skim Neutral She skimmed the book to find key parts. Light / fast
