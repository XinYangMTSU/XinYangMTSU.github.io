🪵 stake /steɪk/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

(n.) A strong wooden or metal post with a sharp end, driven into the ground; an interest or share in a business or situation.
(v.) To mark or claim something with or as if with stakes; to risk something important.
(名词）木桩，股份，赌注；（动词）立桩标出；拿…冒险。

Example: He drove a stake into the ground to support the plant.
例句：他把木桩钉进土里以支撑植物。
Example: She owns a 30% stake in the company.
例句：她在这家公司拥有30%的股份。
Example: He staked his reputation on the success of the project.
例句：他把自己的名声赌在这个项目的成功上。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Post 柱子，支柱
  • Share 股份
  • Investment 投资
  • Risk 冒险

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Stake" comes from Old English staca, meaning “post” or “peg,” and is related to Proto-Germanic and Latin roots referring to support or fastening.
“Stake” 源自古英语 staca，意为“柱子”或“桩子”，与原始日耳曼语和拉丁语中表示支撑或固定的词有关。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: staca – stake, post
古英语 staca：桩子，柱子

Middle English: stake – also came to mean wager or bet
中古英语 stake：也用作赌注

Modern English: stake – post, investment, risk
现代英语 stake：桩子、投资、风险
