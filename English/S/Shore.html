🌊 shore /ʃɔːr/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

The land along the edge of a sea, lake, or other large body of water.
海、湖或其他大型水体边缘的陆地；岸边。

Example: We walked along the shore, collecting seashells.
例句：我们沿着海岸散步，捡贝壳。
Example: The boat finally reached the shore after hours at sea.
例句：小船在海上航行数小时后终于靠岸。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Coast 海岸
  • Beach 沙滩
  • Seaside 海边
  • Bank 河岸

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Shore" comes from Middle English shore, from Old English scora, meaning "coast" or "slope".
shore 来自中古英语 shore，源自古英语 scora，意为“海岸”或“斜坡”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: scora – coast, slope
古英语 scora：海岸、斜坡
⬇️
Middle English: shore – coast or edge of land
中古英语 shore：海岸或陆地边缘
⬇️
Modern English: shore – land beside a body of water
现代英语 shore：水体边的陆地

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Coast Neutral They drove along the California coast. Geographic / Scenic
Beach Casual We spent the day relaxing on the beach. Recreational
Shore Neutral The waves crashed onto the shore. Descriptive / Natural
