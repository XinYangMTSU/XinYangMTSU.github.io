✍️ scribble /ˈskrɪbl/

Part of speech: Verb / Noun
词性：动词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

As a verb: To write or draw something quickly and carelessly.
As a noun: A messy or careless piece of writing or drawing.
作动词时，scribble 意为“潦草地写、乱写乱画”；作名词时，指“潦草的笔迹”或“乱涂乱画”。

Example: The child scribbled all over the wall with crayons.
例句：这个孩子用蜡笔在墙上乱画。
Example: I couldn’t read his scribble on the note.
例句：我看不清他写在便条上的潦草字迹。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Doodle 涂鸦
  • Scrawl 潦草地写
  • Jot 草草记下
  • Scratch 乱写；划写
  • Sketch (loosely) 粗略地画

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Scribble" comes from Latin scribere, meaning "to write". It evolved into Late Latin scribillare, meaning “to write carelessly.”
“scribble” 源自拉丁语 scribere（写），后演变为 scribillare，意为“潦草地写”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: scribere – to write
拉丁语 scribere：写
⬇️
Late Latin: scribillare – to write carelessly
晚期拉丁语 scribillare：乱写
⬇️
Modern English: scribble – quick or messy writing or drawing
现代英语 scribble：潦草的写或画

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Doodle Informal He doodled during the meeting. Playful / casual
Scrawl Neutral He scrawled a message on the wall. Messy / urgent
Scribble Neutral She scribbled a note before leaving. Quick / careless
