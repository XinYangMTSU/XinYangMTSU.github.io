🪱 spineless /ˈspaɪn.ləs/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

1. Lacking a backbone; literally without a spine (used for animals).
2. (Figuratively) Lacking courage, determination, or moral strength; weak or cowardly.
1. 无脊椎的（用于动物）；
2.（比喻）懦弱的；没有勇气或决断力的；软弱无能的。

Example 1: Jellyfish are spineless creatures that drift with ocean currents.
例句 1：水母是无脊椎动物，随洋流漂流。
Example 2: He was too spineless to stand up for what was right.
例句 2：他太软弱，无法坚持做正确的事情。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Cowardly 懦弱的
  • Weak 软弱的
  • Timid 胆小的
  • Gutless (informal) 没胆的（非正式）

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Spineless" is formed from "spine" (meaning backbone) and the suffix "-less" (meaning without). Originally describing invertebrates, it later gained a figurative meaning for people who lack bravery or resolve.
“Spineless” 由 “spine”（脊椎）与后缀 “-less”（无……的）组成。最初用来描述无脊椎动物，后来引申为比喻“缺乏勇气或决心的人”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: spina – thorn, spine
拉丁语 spina：刺，脊柱

Middle English: spine – backbone
中古英语 spine：脊椎骨

Modern English: spineless – lacking courage or a spine
现代英语 spineless：懦弱的；无脊椎的
