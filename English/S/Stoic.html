🧘 stoic /ˈstəʊ.ɪk/

Part of speech: Adjective / Noun
词性：形容词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

As an adjective: Enduring pain or hardship without showing feelings or complaining.
形容词：坚忍的；不动声色的；能默默承受痛苦或困境的。

As a noun: A person who can endure pain or hardship without showing emotion; also, a follower of the ancient Greek philosophy of Stoicism.
名词：坚忍的人；不动声色的人；斯多葛学派的信徒。

Example (adj): She remained stoic even during the most difficult times.
例句（形容词）：即使在最艰难的时刻，她仍然不动声色。
Example (noun): He’s a true stoic, never complaining no matter what happens.
例句（名词）：他是真正的坚忍之人，无论发生什么从不抱怨。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Unflinching 坚定的
  • Resilient 有韧性的
  • Composed 镇定的
  • Unemotional 无动于衷的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Stoic" comes from the Greek word Stoa, meaning "porch," referring to the Painted Porch in Athens where Zeno, the founder of Stoicism, taught. Stoicism taught mastery of emotions and acceptance of fate.
“Stoic” 源自希腊语 Stoa（门廊），指的是斯多葛派创始人芝诺在雅典“彩绘门廊”讲学之地。斯多葛学派主张控制情绪、顺应命运。

🧭 Word Evolution

Greek: Stoa Poikile – the painted porch
希腊语 Stoa Poikile：彩绘门廊

Ancient Greek: Stoikos – of the Stoa
古希腊语 Stoikos：门廊的；斯多葛的

Modern English: stoic – person who endures hardship calmly
现代英语 stoic：坚忍不拔的人，不动声色的

📊 Comparison

Word Emotion Shown Context Example
Emotional High Personal / expressive She became emotional during the speech.
Stoic Low Philosophical / disciplined He gave a stoic response to the bad news.
