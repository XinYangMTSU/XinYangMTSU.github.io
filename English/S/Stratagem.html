🌟 stratagem /ˈstrætədʒəm/

Part of speech: Noun

📖 Meaning:

A stratagem is a clever and often deceptive plan or trick used to gain an advantage, especially in war, politics, or competitive situations.

Example: The general used a brilliant stratagem to lure the enemy into a trap.
Example: Her friendliness was just a stratagem to gain his trust.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Tactic
  • Scheme
  • Ploy
  • Ruse
  • Deception
  • Trick

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "stratagem".

📝It comes from the Greek noun: stratēgēma meaning “artifice, military trick.”

  • stratos = army
  • agein = to lead

✍️ The word traveled into Latin as stratagema, and then into French as stratagème, before entering English in the 15th century.

💡 It always retained the idea of a **clever military or political trick** meant to outwit an opponent.
