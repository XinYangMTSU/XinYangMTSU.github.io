🔄 symbiotic /ˌsɪm.baɪˈɒ.tɪk/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Symbiotic describes a relationship between two different organisms or entities where both benefit or depend on each other.
“symbiotic” 意指两种不同生物或实体之间的共生关系，通常彼此互利或相互依赖。

Example: The clownfish and the sea anemone have a symbiotic relationship.
例句：小丑鱼和海葵之间有共生关系。
Example: Their collaboration was symbiotic—each gained from the other’s strengths.
例句：他们的合作是共生的——彼此从对方的优势中受益。

🧩 Synonyms

  • Mutually beneficial 互利的
  • Cooperative 合作的
  • Interdependent 相互依赖的
  • Reciprocal 互惠的
  • Complementary 互补的

🔍 Etymology of the word

📚 "Symbiotic" comes from Greek symbiōsis, meaning “living together,” from sym- (together) and bios (life).
“symbiotic” 源自希腊语 symbiōsis，意为“共同生活”，由 sym-（一起）与 bios（生命）组成。
