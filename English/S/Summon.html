🧙 summon /ˈsʌmən/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To call upon someone or something to come, especially with authority or urgency; to gather strength or courage; to bring forth something from within oneself.
召唤；召集；鼓起（勇气、力量等）。

Example: The king summoned his advisors for an emergency meeting.
例句：国王召集了顾问召开紧急会议。
Example: She summoned all her strength to lift the heavy box.
例句：她鼓起全身力气抬起那个沉重的箱子。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Call 召唤
  • Command 命令
  • Convene 召集（会议）
  • Rally 集合；鼓起（精神、力量）

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Summon" comes from Latin summonere, from sub- (secretly, up from below) + monere (to warn or advise), meaning “to call forth.”
“Summon” 源自拉丁语 summonere，由 sub-（从下、私下）和 monere（警告、提醒）组成，意为“召唤、叫来”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: summonere – to secretly call or warn
拉丁语 summonere：秘密地召唤或警告

Old French: somoner – to call
古法语 somoner：召唤

Modern English: summon – to officially or urgently call
现代英语 summon：正式或紧急地召唤

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Call Neutral He called his team for help. Casual
Convene Formal The president convened a meeting of the council. Official
