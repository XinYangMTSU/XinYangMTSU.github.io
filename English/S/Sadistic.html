😈 sadistic /səˈdɪstɪk/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Taking pleasure in inflicting pain, suffering, or humiliation on others.
施虐的；以折磨他人取乐的。

Example: The villain's sadistic grin sent chills down everyone’s spine.
例句：那恶棍施虐般的笑容让人脊背发凉。
Example: He took a sadistic delight in mocking his classmates.
例句：他以嘲笑同学为乐，带有施虐倾向。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Cruel 残酷的
  • Vicious 恶毒的
  • Brutal 野蛮的
  • Malicious 恶意的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Sadistic" comes from the name of the Marquis de Sade, a French nobleman known for writing about cruelty and deriving pleasure from others’ suffering.
“Sadistic” 源自法国贵族萨德侯爵（Marquis de Sade），他以描写从折磨他人中获得快感的作品而闻名。

🧭 Word Evolution

French: sade – referring to de Sade's name
法语 sade：源自萨德侯爵的名字

Modern English: sadistic – deriving pleasure from cruelty
现代英语 sadistic：从残忍中获得快感
