🌀 squiggle /ˈskwɪɡ.l̩/

Part of speech: Noun or Verb
词性：名词或动词

📖 As a noun:

A squiggle is a short, curvy, or wavy line that looks random or messy.
squiggle（名词）指的是一条弯曲、乱七八糟或随意的曲线。

Example: His signature was just a squiggle on the paper.
例句：他的签名只是一条乱糟糟的线。

📖 As a verb:

To squiggle means to move or draw in a twisting or wavy way.
squiggle（动词）表示扭动、蜿蜒地移动，或画出曲线。

Example: She squiggled lines all over her notebook during class.
例句：她在课堂上在笔记本上画了许多曲线。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Doodle 涂鸦
    • 乱写乱画
  • Twirl 旋转线条
  • Wiggle 扭动
  • Zigzag 曲折线

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Squiggle" likely comes from a blend of “squirm” (to twist or wriggle) and “wiggle” (to move in small curves).
“squiggle” 很可能是由 “squirm”（扭动）和 “wiggle”（微微摆动）合成的词，表示不规则的扭曲动作或线条。

🧭 Word Evolution

Blend: squirm + wiggle – to wriggle in a curvy motion
由 squirm（扭动）和 wiggle（摆动）结合形成的拟声词
⬇️
Modern English: squiggle – random curvy line or movement
现代英语 squiggle：弯弯曲曲、不规则的线或动作
