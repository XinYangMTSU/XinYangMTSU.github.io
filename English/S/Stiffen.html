🧊 stiffen /ˈstɪfən/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To become, or make something become, more rigid or firm.
变得（或使变得）僵硬或坚硬。

Example: His body stiffened when he heard the strange noise.
例句：他听到奇怪的声音时全身僵硬了。
Example: Add starch to stiffen the fabric.
例句：加上淀粉可以使布料变得更硬挺。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Harden 使变硬
  • Freeze 冻僵
  • Tense 紧绷
  • Solidify 凝固

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Stiffen" comes from Middle English stiffen, related to the word "stiff" meaning rigid or firm.
stiffen 源自中古英语 stiffen，与表示“坚硬”的 stiff 一词相关。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: stif – rigid, inflexible
古英语 stif：僵硬的，不灵活的
⬇️
Middle English: stiffen – become rigid
中古英语 stiffen：变得僵硬
⬇️
Modern English: stiffen – to make or become stiff
现代英语 stiffen：使变得僵硬

📊 Comparison

Word Intensity Example Use Tone
Stiffen Moderate She stiffened at the sound of his voice. Physical / Emotional
Freeze Strong He froze in terror as the bear approached. Intense / Fearful
Harden General The clay hardened overnight. Neutral / Physical
