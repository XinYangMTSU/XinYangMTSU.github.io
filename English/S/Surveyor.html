📏 surveyor /sərˈveɪər/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A person who examines and measures land, buildings, or other features, often for construction, engineering, or mapping purposes.
勘测员；测量员；（土地、建筑等的）检验员。

Example: The surveyor marked the boundaries of the construction site.
例句：勘测员标出了施工现场的边界。
Example: A surveyor was hired to check the property's exact dimensions.
例句：雇佣了一位测量员来核实该地块的确切尺寸。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Inspector 检查员
  • Appraiser 估价员
  • Land measurer 土地测量员
  • Mapping specialist 制图专家

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Surveyor" comes from the Anglo-French word surveer, which means “to look over” or “inspect,” and from Latin supervidere (super = over + videre = to see). It entered English in the late Middle Ages.
surveyor 来自中古法语 surveer（查看、检查），源于拉丁语 supervidere（super = 上方，videre = 看），在中世纪晚期进入英语。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: supervidere – to oversee
拉丁语 supervidere：监督，检查
⬇️
Old French: surveer – to inspect
古法语 surveer：检查
⬇️
Modern English: surveyor – person who measures or inspects
现代英语 surveyor：测量员；检验员
