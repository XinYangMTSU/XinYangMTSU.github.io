📖 saga /ˈsɑːɡə/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A long and involved story, especially one featuring heroic deeds, legendary adventures, or complex family history. Originally, “saga” referred to medieval Icelandic or Norse prose narratives.
长篇故事；史诗；尤指包含英雄事迹、家族传奇或复杂经历的叙事。最初指中世纪冰岛或北欧的散文传说。

Example: The movie follows a thrilling space saga across multiple generations.
例句：这部电影讲述了一段跨越多代的太空传奇。
Example: Her memoir reads like a saga of survival and triumph.
例句：她的回忆录如同一部关于生存与胜利的传奇。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Epic 史诗
  • Tale 故事
  • Legend 传说
  • Chronicle 编年史

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Saga" comes from Old Norse saga, meaning "a narrative" or "what is said." It originally referred to traditional Icelandic stories about heroic figures and events.
“Saga” 源自古诺斯语 saga，意为“叙述”或“传说”，最初指冰岛传统的英雄事迹故事。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old Norse: saga – story, history
古诺斯语 saga：故事，历史

Modern English: saga – long narrative or dramatic story
现代英语 saga：长篇叙事或戏剧性故事

📊 Comparison

Word Type Length Typical Subject
Epic Noun / Adjective Very long Heroic deeds, often poetic
Saga Noun Long Heroic, historical, or family-based
Tale Noun Short to medium Fictional or moral-based
