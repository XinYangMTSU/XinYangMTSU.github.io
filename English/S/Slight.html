⚖️ slight /slaɪt/

Part of speech: Adjective / Verb / Noun
词性：形容词 / 动词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

As an adjective: Small in degree or amount; not considerable or important.
形容词：轻微的；少量的；不重要的。

As a verb: To insult someone by treating them without proper respect or attention.
动词：轻视；怠慢。

As a noun (less common): An insult caused by a lack of proper respect.
名词（较少见）：轻蔑；怠慢。

Example (adj): There’s a slight chance of rain tomorrow.
例句（形容词）：明天下雨的可能性很小。
Example (verb): He felt slighted when he wasn't invited to the meeting.
例句（动词）：他因为没被邀请参加会议而感到受到了冷落。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Small 小的
  • Minor 轻微的
  • Negligible 可忽略的
  • Insult (as verb) 侮辱，轻视

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Slight" comes from Middle English slight meaning "smooth or flat," from Old Norse slettr meaning "level or plain." It evolved into meanings related to being small, light, or minimal.
“Slight” 来自中古英语 slight，意思是“光滑的”，源自古诺斯语 slettr，意为“平坦的”。后来引申为“轻微的、微不足道的”含义。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old Norse: slettr – smooth, level
古诺斯语 slettr：光滑的，平坦的

Middle English: slight – thin, insignificant
中古英语 slight：微薄的，不重要的

Modern English: slight – small in amount or to insult
现代英语 slight：少量的；怠慢
