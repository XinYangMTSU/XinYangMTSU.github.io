💗 sympathy /ˈsɪmpəθi/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

Sympathy is the feeling of understanding and caring about someone else's sadness, pain, or misfortune. It often involves expressing comfort and support.
sympathy 指的是对他人悲伤、痛苦或不幸的理解与关心，通常包括表达安慰与支持。

Example: We offered our deepest sympathy to the family after their loss.
例句：我们向那个家庭表达了最深切的慰问。
Example: She showed great sympathy for the victims of the disaster.
例句：她对灾难受害者表现出了极大的同情。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Compassion 同情心
  • Empathy 共情
  • Understanding 理解
  • Pity 怜悯
  • Condolence 慰问

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Sympathy" comes from Greek sympatheia, from sym meaning "with" and pathos meaning "feeling" or "suffering." So it literally means "feeling with" another person.
“sympathy” 来自希腊语 sympatheia，其中 sym 意为“与……一起”，pathos 意为“情感、苦难”，字面意思是“与他人一起感受”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Greek: sympatheia – shared feeling
希腊语 sympatheia：共同感受
⬇️
Latin: sympathia – emotional affinity
拉丁语 sympathia：情感上的亲近
⬇️
Modern English: sympathy – understanding and care for others
现代英语 sympathy：理解和关怀他人

📊 Comparison with Related Words

Word Definition Example Level of Emotion
Pity Feeling sorry for someone I felt pity for the stray dog. Low / passive
Empathy Feeling what someone else feels She cried with empathy as he told his story. High / deep
Sympathy Feeling concern and care They sent a card to express their sympathy. Medium / caring
