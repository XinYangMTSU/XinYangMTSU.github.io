🌟 stroll /stroʊl/

Part of speech: Verb or noun

📖 As a verb (action):

To stroll means to walk in a slow, relaxed way, usually for enjoyment.

Example: They strolled along the beach, enjoying the sunset.

📖 As a noun (a thing):

A stroll is a slow, relaxed walk, typically taken for pleasure.

Example: We went for a stroll through the park after dinner.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Walk
  • Amble
  • Saunter
  • Meander
  • Ramble
  • Promenade

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "stroll" first appeared in English in the 17th century and may come from a German dialect word strolchen, meaning “to roam” or “to loaf.”

📝 It was originally associated with vagrants or wandering people, but over time it softened to mean a relaxed, pleasant walk.

💡 Now it has a peaceful, casual feel—no longer about roaming aimlessly but about walking leisurely for fun.
