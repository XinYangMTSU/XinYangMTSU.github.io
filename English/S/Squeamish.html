😖 squeamish /ˈskwiː.mɪʃ/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

If you are squeamish, you feel sick, uncomfortable, or upset when you see something unpleasant like blood, injury, or mess. It can also mean being easily shocked or offended.
squeamish 形容人容易因血腥、恶心或不舒服的事感到反感或恶心，也可以表示容易受惊或敏感。

Example: I'm too squeamish to watch horror movies with a lot of blood.
例句：我太怕恶心了，受不了那些充满血腥的恐怖电影。
Example: She’s squeamish about discussing anything related to illness.
例句：她一谈到疾病相关的话题就感到不适。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Queasy 反胃的
  • Delicate 娇气的
  • Easily upset 容易不安的
  • Sensitive 敏感的
  • Faint-hearted 胆小的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "squeamish" comes from the Middle English word squemous, meaning "fastidious or easily disgusted," which itself likely came from Old French.
squeamish 源于中古英语 squeamous，意为“挑剔的、容易恶心的”，可能来自古法语。

🧭 Word Evolution

Middle English: squemous – easily disgusted
中古英语 squeamous：容易恶心或反感的
⬇️
Modern English: squeamish – easily disturbed by unpleasant things
现代英语 squeamish：对不舒服的事情感到反感或恶心
