🐺 snarling /ˈsnɑːrlɪŋ/

Part of speech: Verb (present participle of "snarl")
词性：动词（snarl 的现在分词）

📖 Meaning:

Making an aggressive growl with bared teeth, typically by a dog or other animal; speaking in a harsh or angry way.
（通常指狗或动物）露齿发出凶狠的咆哮；厉声或愤怒地说话。

Example: The dog was snarling at the intruder.
例句：那条狗对着闯入者咆哮。
Example: "Leave me alone!" she said, snarling.
例句：“别烦我！”她怒吼着说道。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Growling 咆哮
  • Barking 吠叫
  • Snapping 厉声说话
  • Scowling 怒目而视

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Snarling" comes from the verb “snarl,” of Middle English origin, possibly imitative of the sound made, and related to Dutch and German terms meaning “to growl” or “to tangle.”
“Snarling” 来自动词 snarl，源自中古英语，可能模仿动物发出的声音，与荷兰语和德语中表示“咆哮”或“纠缠”的词相关。

🧭 Word Evolution

Middle English: snarlen – to growl
中古英语 snarlen：咆哮

Germanic roots: Similar to schnarren (German), meaning to rattle or snarl
日耳曼词根，如德语 schnarren：发出嘎嘎声或咆哮

Modern English: snarl – to growl or speak harshly
现代英语 snarl：咆哮或厉声说话

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Growling Neutral The bear was growling in the cave. Animalistic
Snapping Informal He snapped at the waiter impatiently. Angry / Rude
