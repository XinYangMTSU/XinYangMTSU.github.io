🏙️ skyscraper /ˈskaɪˌskreɪpər/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A very tall building with many stories, typically found in cities.
摩天大楼；通常在城市中出现的多层高楼。

Example: The Empire State Building is a famous skyscraper in New York City.
例句：帝国大厦是纽约市著名的摩天大楼。
Example: Skyscrapers shape the skyline of modern cities.
例句：摩天大楼塑造了现代城市的天际线。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Tower 高楼
  • High-rise 高层建筑
  • Building 建筑物
  • Structure 结构物

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Skyscraper" comes from the words “sky” and “scraper.” Originally used for tall sails or horses, it was first applied to tall buildings in the late 19th century in the United States.
“Skyscraper” 一词来自 “sky”（天空）和 “scraper”（刮擦者）。原本用于描述高帆或高马，19世纪末在美国首次用于形容高楼大厦。

🧭 Word Evolution

Original Use: Skyscraper – a tall sail on a ship or a tall person
原义：Skyscraper 指的是船上的高帆或身材高大的人
⬇️
Late 1800s: Applied to very tall buildings in cities
19世纪末：开始用于描述城市中的高楼大厦
⬇️
Modern English: Skyscraper – tall urban building
现代英语：指城市中的摩天大楼
