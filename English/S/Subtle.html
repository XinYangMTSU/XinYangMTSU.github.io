🌟 subtle /ˈsʌtl/

Part of speech: Adjective

📖 As an adjective (describing):

Something subtle is not obvious or easy to notice, but may be clever or delicate in meaning, effect, or detail.

Example: She wore a subtle hint of perfume. (= light, not strong)
Example: His argument was subtle but persuasive. (= clever and nuanced)

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Delicate
  • Nuanced
  • Faint
  • Slight
  • Refined
  • Understated

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word subtle comes from Old French sotil (modern French: subtil), which comes from Latin subtilis, meaning "fine" or "delicate."

  • sub- = under
  • telis (from texere) = to weave

💡 It originally referred to something finely woven or intricately crafted—like a subtle fabric.
