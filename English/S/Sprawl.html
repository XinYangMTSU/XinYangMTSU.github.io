🛋️ sprawl /sprɔːl/

Part of speech: Verb / Noun
词性：动词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

To sit, lie, or fall with arms and legs spread out in a relaxed or awkward way; or the spreading of something (like a city) over a large area in an uncontrolled way.
摊开四肢坐（或躺）；（城市）无序扩展。

Example (verb): He sprawled on the sofa after a long day at work.
例句（动词）：他下班后一屁股瘫在沙发上。
Example (noun): Urban sprawl is becoming a major issue in many countries.
例句（名词）：城市无序扩张已成为许多国家面临的主要问题。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Stretch out 伸展开
  • Lounge 懒洋洋地躺
  • Spread 扩展
  • Expand 扩张

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Sprawl" comes from Middle English sprawlen, meaning “to move the limbs awkwardly.”
sprawl 来自中古英语 sprawlen，意为“笨拙地移动四肢”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Middle English: sprawlen – move awkwardly
中古英语 sprawlen：笨拙地移动
⬇️
Modern English: sprawl – spread out loosely or awkwardly
现代英语 sprawl：懒散地伸展；无序扩展

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Sprawl Neutral He sprawled across the entire couch. Descriptive
Stretch out Neutral She stretched out under the sun. Relaxed
Expand Formal The city continues to expand outward. Objective
