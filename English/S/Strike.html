⚡ strike /straɪk/

Part of speech: Verb / Noun
词性：动词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

As a verb: To hit someone or something with force; to suddenly happen; to refuse to work as a form of protest.
动词：打击；突然发生；罢工。

As a noun: A hit or attack; a work stoppage by employees as protest.
名词：打击；攻击；罢工。

Example: The clock struck midnight.
例句：钟敲响了午夜。
Example: Workers went on strike for better pay.
例句：工人为争取更高的工资而罢工。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Hit 击打
  • Attack 攻击
  • Protest 抗议
  • Smash 猛击

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Strike" comes from Old English strīcan, meaning “to stroke” or “rub lightly,” which later evolved to mean “to hit.” Its use in labor protests emerged in the 18th century.
“Strike” 源自古英语 strīcan，意为“轻擦”或“抚摸”，后来演变为“打击”；作为“罢工”使用始于18世纪。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: strīcan – to stroke, rub
古英语 strīcan：轻擦，抚摸
⬇️
Middle English: strike – to hit
中古英语 strike：击打
⬇️
Modern English: strike – hit, protest, sudden event
现代英语 strike：击打、抗议、突发事件
