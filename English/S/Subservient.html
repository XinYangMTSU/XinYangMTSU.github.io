🧎 subservient /səbˈsɜːrviənt/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Subservient describes someone or something that is excessively willing to follow orders or serve a more powerful authority; or something that plays a supporting, less important role.
“subservient” 指的是过于顺从、服从他人，或处于从属地位的人或事物。

Example: He expected his assistants to be completely subservient to his decisions.
例句：他希望自己的助理对他的决定完全服从。
Example: In the story, mathematics is portrayed as subservient to science.
例句：在这个故事中，数学被描绘成科学的附属工具。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Obedient 顺从的
  • Submissive 服从的
  • Servile 卑躬屈膝的
  • Inferior 下级的
  • Secondary 次要的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 “Subservient” comes from Latin subserviens, the present participle of subservire, meaning “to serve under.” It is formed from sub- (under) + servire (to serve).
“subservient” 来自拉丁语 subserviens，是 subservire 的现在分词，意为“在下服务”或“作为附属”；由 sub-（在下）和 servire（服务）构成。
