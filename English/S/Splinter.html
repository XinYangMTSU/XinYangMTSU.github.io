🌲 splinter /ˈsplɪntər/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

(n.) A small, sharp piece of wood, glass, or other material that has broken off from a larger piece.
(v.) To break or cause to break into small, sharp fragments.
（名词）碎片，尖刺（尤指木头或玻璃碎片）
（动词）使裂成碎片，分裂

Example: He got a splinter in his finger while chopping wood.
例句：他在劈柴时手指扎进了一根木刺。
Example: The mirror splintered into dozens of tiny shards.
例句：镜子裂成了数十块细小的碎片。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Fragment 碎片
  • Sliver 裂片
  • Chip 碎屑
  • Shard 碎片（尤指玻璃、陶瓷）

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Splinter" comes from Middle Dutch splinter, meaning "a splinter or sliver of wood," and is related to the word "split."
“Splinter” 源自中古荷兰语 splinter，意为“木头碎片、尖刺”，与英文 split（劈开）相关。

🧭 Word Evolution

Middle Dutch: splinter – fragment of wood
中古荷兰语 splinter：木头碎片

Old English: related to split – to break apart
古英语 split：劈裂

Modern English: splinter – sharp fragment
现代英语 splinter：尖锐的碎片

📊 Comparison

Word Context Example Use Tone
Sliver Wood / Metal A sliver of glass got stuck in her foot. Neutral / Descriptive
Shard Glass / Ceramic Shards of porcelain littered the floor. Sharp / Dramatic
Splinter Wood / Pain / Breaking He cried out when the splinter pierced his skin. Neutral / Ouch!
