🌊 swell /swel/

Part of speech: Verb / Noun / Adjective (informal)
词性：动词 / 名词 / （非正式）形容词

📖 Meaning:

Verb: To become larger or rounder in size, often due to pressure or emotion.
动词：膨胀；肿胀；增大；（情感）增强。

Noun: A gradual increase in size, number, or force; a long, smooth wave on the sea.
名词：膨胀；增大；海上的长浪。

Adjective (informal): Excellent, wonderful (mainly in old-fashioned American slang).
（非正式形容词）：极好的，棒极了（美国老派俚语）。

Example (verb): Her ankle swelled after she twisted it.
例句（动词）：她扭伤脚踝后肿起来了。
Example (noun): The boat rocked gently on the ocean swell.
例句（名词）：小船在海浪的起伏中轻轻摇晃。
Example (adjective): That was a swell idea!
例句（形容词）：那主意太棒了！

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Expand 扩大
  • Inflate 膨胀
  • Bulge 鼓起
  • Surge 汹涌；激增

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Swell" comes from Old English swellan, meaning “to grow or increase.” It is related to Germanic words with the same root and has been used in English since before the 12th century.
“Swell” 源自古英语 swellan，意为“增长、膨胀”，与日耳曼语同源词有关，早在12世纪以前就已在英语中使用。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: swellan – to grow, swell
古英语 swellan：增长，肿胀

Middle English: swellen – to become inflated
中古英语 swellen：膨胀

Modern English: swell – to expand in size, power, or feeling
现代英语 swell：变大，激增，激动

📊 Comparison
