🪵 sliver /ˈslɪvər/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A small, thin, narrow piece of something that has been cut or broken off, especially from wood, glass, metal, or food.
碎片；裂片；指从木头、玻璃、金属或食物中切下或断裂出的小而薄的一块。

Example: She picked up a sliver of glass from the floor.
例句：她从地板上捡起了一块玻璃碎片。
Example: I only want a sliver of cake, not a full slice.
例句：我只要一小块蛋糕，不要整片。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Shard 碎片
  • Splinter 尖片；刺
  • Chip 碎屑
  • Slice 薄片；一片

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Sliver" comes from the Middle English verb sliven meaning “to split” or “to cleave,” and is related to the word slice.
“Sliver” 源自中古英语 sliven，意为“劈开”或“分裂”，与“slice（切片）”一词相关。

🧭 Word Evolution

Middle English: sliven – to split
中古英语 sliven：劈开

Modern English: sliver – a thin, narrow piece
现代英语 sliver：薄而窄的一片

📊 Comparison

Word Meaning Material Example
Splinter Small sharp piece Wood, glass I got a splinter in my finger.
Sliver Thin, narrow piece Wood, food, glass A sliver of cheese was left on the plate.
