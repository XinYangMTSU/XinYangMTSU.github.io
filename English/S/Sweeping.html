🧹 sweeping /ˈswiːpɪŋ/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Having a wide range or effect; very extensive or far-reaching in influence, scope, or impact.
影响广泛的；范围巨大的；全面的。

Example: The president announced sweeping changes to the tax system.
例句：总统宣布了税收制度的全面改革。
Example: The movie features sweeping landscapes and emotional storytelling.
例句：这部电影展现了广阔的自然景观和感人的故事。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Extensive 广泛的
  • Comprehensive 全面的
  • Broad 宽广的
  • Far-reaching 深远的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Sweeping" comes from the verb "sweep," which originally means “to clean or brush away.” Over time, it came to refer metaphorically to things that move across a broad area or have wide effect.
sweeping 源自动词 sweep（扫、清除），后来引申为“广泛影响”或“全面变化”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: swāpan – to sweep
古英语 swāpan：扫
⬇️
Middle English: swepen – to brush or move over
中古英语 swepen：刷过，横扫
⬇️
Modern English: sweeping – broad in scope or impact
现代英语 sweeping：范围广泛或影响深远的
