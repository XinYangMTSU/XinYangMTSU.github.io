😤 snooty /ˈsnuːti/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Behaving in a way that shows you think you are better than other people; snobbish.
自以为高人一等的；势利的；傲慢的。

Example: She acted snooty at the party and refused to talk to anyone outside her circle.
例句：她在聚会上表现得高傲，拒绝与圈外的人交谈。
Example: The snooty waiter made us feel unwelcome.
例句：那位傲慢的服务员让我们感到不受欢迎。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Snobbish 势利的
  • Arrogant 傲慢的
  • Pompous 自大的
  • Conceited 自负的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Snooty" likely comes from the word "snout" or "snoot", suggesting someone sticking their nose up at others. It emerged in the early 20th century as slang for snobbish behavior.
snooty 很可能来自 “snout” 或 “snoot”（鼻子），表示某人“翘起鼻子看人”的样子，20 世纪初作为俚语出现，意为势利或自负。

🧭 Word Evolution

English slang: snoot – nose
英语俚语 snoot：鼻子
⬇️
Modern English: snooty – snobbish, conceited
现代英语 snooty：傲慢的，自以为是的
