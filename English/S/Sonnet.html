🌟 sonnet /ˈsɒnɪt/

Part of speech: Noun

📖 Definition:

A sonnet is a 14-line poem, typically written in iambic pentameter, that follows a specific rhyme scheme. It often explores themes of love, time, beauty, or nature. Famous sonnet types include the Shakespearean and Petrarchan sonnet.

Example: Shakespeare's Sonnet 18 begins with the famous line: "Shall I compare thee to a summer's day?"

🧰 Synonyms

  • Poem
  • Verse
  • Rhyme
  • Ballad
  • Lyric
  • Composition

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "sonnet" comes from the Italian word sonetto, which means "little song" or "little sound." It is derived from Latin sonus (sound).
