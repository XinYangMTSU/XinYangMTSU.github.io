🌟 successor /səkˈsɛsər/

Part of speech: Noun

📖 Definition:

A successor is someone who comes after another person in a job, position, or role.

Example: The company announced her as the successor to the CEO.

📖 Broader Meaning:

It can also refer to something that follows and replaces something else—such as a successor state, version, or system.

Example: Windows 11 is the successor to Windows 10.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Heir
  • Follower
  • Replacement
  • Next in line
  • Inheritor
  • Descendant

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "successor" comes from Latin succedere, meaning "to come after."

  • sub- = under, after
  • cedere = to go, to yield

✍️ It passed through Old French successour and Middle English before becoming the modern English "successor."
