✨ sacred /ˈseɪ.krɪd/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Regarded with deep respect or reverence, often in a religious or spiritual context; considered holy, valuable, or untouchable.
神圣的；不可侵犯的；受尊敬的（常用于宗教或精神领域）。

Example: The temple is considered a sacred place by the locals.
例句：当地人认为这座庙宇是神圣的地方。
Example: Freedom of speech is a sacred right in many democracies.
例句：言论自由在许多民主国家是一项神圣的权利。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Holy 神圣的
  • Divine 神的；神圣的
  • Revered 受尊敬的
  • Hallowed 被神圣化的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Sacred" comes from Latin sacrare, meaning “to consecrate, make holy,” from sacer, meaning “holy.”
“Sacred” 源自拉丁语 sacrare，意为“奉献、使神圣”，其词根 sacer 意为“神圣的”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: sacer → sacrare – to make holy
拉丁语 sacer → sacrare：使神圣

Middle English: sacred – set apart for worship
中古英语 sacred：用于崇拜的圣物

Modern English: sacred – holy, revered
现代英语 sacred：神圣的、受尊敬的

📊 Comparison
