🌟 snub /snʌb/

Part of speech: Noun or verb

📖 As a verb (action):

To snub someone means to deliberately ignore or insult them by not giving them attention or respect.

Example: She snubbed her old friend at the party by pretending not to see him.

📖 As a noun (a thing):

A snub is the act of ignoring or showing disdain for someone, especially in a social setting.

Example: His absence was seen as a snub to the host.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Ignore
  • Shun
  • Dismiss
  • Cold-shoulder
  • Insult
  • Brush off

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word snub likely comes from an old Norse word snubba, meaning "to scold or rebuke."

💡 Over time, the meaning shifted to include social rebuffs, such as ignoring or excluding someone.
