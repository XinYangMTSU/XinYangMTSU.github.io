🌟 stale /steɪl/

Part of speech: Adjective

📖 Meaning:

Stale means no longer fresh, new, or interesting. It can refer to food that has gone dry or old, or ideas and routines that feel dull and unoriginal.

Example (food): This bread is stale. (= It’s dry and no longer fresh.)
Example (ideas): His jokes are getting stale. (= They’re old and not funny anymore.)

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Old
  • Dull
  • Dry
  • Uninspired
  • Outdated
  • Overused

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "stale".

📝It comes from the Old French word "estal", meaning "standing place" or "stall."

  • estal = standing still → no longer fresh or moving

✍️ The idea of “standing still” over time led to the modern meaning of “not fresh” or “no longer interesting.”

💡 The word entered English in the 14th century to describe meat or bread that had lost freshness, and later extended to ideas and emotions.
