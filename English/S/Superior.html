🌟 superior /suˈpɪriər/

Part of speech: Adjective or noun

📖 As an adjective (describing word):

Superior means better than something else in quality, skill, or status. It can also mean being higher in position or rank.

Example: This product is superior to the old version. (= It’s better.)

📖 As a noun (a thing):

A superior is someone who is higher in rank, position, or authority.

Example: She reported the incident to her superior. (= Her boss or manager.)

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Better
  • Higher
  • Greater
  • Outstanding
  • Advanced
  • Top-tier

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "superior".

📝It comes from the Latin comparative adjective: superior

  • super- = above or over
  • -ior = comparative suffix (meaning "more")

✍️ Originally, it described someone or something that is placed above others in rank or quality.

💡 The word came into Middle English through Old French and Latin, always implying something that is better, higher, or more advanced than another.
