✂️ stubby /ˈstʌbi/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Short and thick in a way that is often considered awkward or cute.
又短又粗的（常给人笨拙或可爱的感觉）。

Example: He had short, stubby fingers.
例句：他有一双又短又粗的手指。
Example: The puppy’s stubby tail wagged excitedly.
例句：小狗那短短的尾巴兴奋地摇摆着。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Short 短的
  • Chunky 矮胖的
  • Stumpy 粗短的
  • Blunt 钝的；粗的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Stubby" is formed from the noun “stub,” meaning a short, thick part remaining after something is cut or broken, plus the adjective-forming suffix “-y.” It has been used in English since the 19th century.
“Stubby” 源自动词 stub（残段、短粗部分），加上形容词后缀 -y，意思是“又短又粗的”，自 19 世纪起开始在英语中使用。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: stybb – stump
古英语 stybb：树桩，残根

Modern English: stubby – short and thick
现代英语 stubby：又短又粗的
