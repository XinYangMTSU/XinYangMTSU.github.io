✨ sorcery /ˈsɔːrsəri/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

Sorcery refers to the use of magical powers, especially evil or dark magic, often associated with spells, potions, or rituals.
sorcery 指的是使用魔法的行为，尤其是邪恶或黑暗的魔法，通常涉及咒语、药水或仪式。

Example: The villagers believed the illness was caused by sorcery.
例句：村民们认为这种疾病是巫术造成的。
Example: Ancient books described various forms of sorcery and enchantment.
例句：古籍中描述了各种形式的巫术和魔法。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Witchcraft 巫术
  • Magic 魔法
  • Spellcasting 施咒
  • Enchantment 魔咒
  • Necromancy 招魂术

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Sorcery" comes from Old French sorcerie, from sorcier (sorcerer), derived from Latin sortiarius, meaning "one who casts lots or tells fortunes," from sors (lot, fate).
sorcery 源自古法语 sorcerie，来自 sorcier（巫师），再由拉丁语 sortiarius 演变而来，意思是“抽签占卜者”，来自 sors（命运，抽签）。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: sors / sortiarius – fate, one who tells fortunes
拉丁语 sors / sortiarius：命运，占卜者
⬇️
Old French: sorcier / sorcerie – sorcerer, sorcery
古法语 sorcier / sorcerie：巫师，巫术
⬇️
Modern English: sorcery – magical practices, especially dark magic
现代英语 sorcery：魔法行为，尤指黑魔法
