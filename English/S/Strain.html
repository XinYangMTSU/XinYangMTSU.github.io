🌟 strain /streɪn/

Part of speech: Verb / Noun

📖 As a verb (action):

To exert force or effort; to stretch something to its limit.

Example: She strained her eyes reading in the dark.

📖 As a noun (a thing):

Physical or mental stress; a force that pulls or stretches something tightly.

Example: The job put a strain on their relationship.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Stress
  • Tension
  • Effort
  • Pressure
  • Overwork
  • Stretch

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word strain comes from the Latin word stringere, meaning “to draw tight.”

💡 It evolved through Old French estreindre and Middle English streinen.
