🪶 sagging /ˈsæɡ.ɪŋ/

Part of speech: Adjective / Noun (present participle of verb "sag")
词性：形容词 / 名词（动词 sag 的现在分词）

📖 Meaning:

Hanging down loosely or sinking due to weight, age, or lack of support; often used to describe weakened structures or drooping body parts.
下垂的；因重力、老化或缺乏支撑而下陷或下坠的，常用于描述结构物、家具或身体部位的松垮状态。

Example: The sagging roof needs urgent repair before it collapses.
例句：下垂的屋顶需要紧急修复，否则可能会坍塌。
Example: After years of use, the couch developed sagging cushions.
例句：用了多年后，沙发的垫子变得松垮下陷。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Drooping 下垂的
  • Sinking 下沉的
  • Slumping 塌陷的
  • Hanging 悬垂的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Sagging" comes from the verb "sag," which dates back to the early 1600s, possibly from Old Norse saga meaning “to sink or droop.” It originally referred to sails or fabric that hung loosely.
“Sagging” 来自动词 sag，可追溯到 17 世纪，可能源自古诺斯语 saga，意为“下沉”或“下垂”，最初用于描述帆布或布料的下垂状态。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old Norse: saga – to droop, hang down
古诺斯语 saga：下垂

Early Modern English: sag – to sink from weight
早期现代英语 sag：因重力下沉

Modern English: sagging – hanging loosely or drooping downward
现代英语 sagging：松垮下沉的

📊 Comparison

Word Part of Speech Meaning Example
Drooping Adjective Hanging downward weakly The flowers were drooping from lack of water.
Sagging Adjective / Verb Sinking under pressure or weight The old shelf is sagging in the middle.
Slumping Verb / Noun Falling or collapsing downward He slumped into the chair, exhausted.
