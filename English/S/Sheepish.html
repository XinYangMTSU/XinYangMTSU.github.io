🌟 sheepish /ˈʃiːpɪʃ/

Part of speech: Adjective

📖 Meaning:

Sheepish describes someone who shows or feels embarrassment from shame or a lack of self-confidence.

Example: He gave a sheepish grin when he realized he had made a mistake in front of the whole class.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Shy
  • Embarrassed
  • Self-conscious
  • Timid
  • Bashful

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word sheepish comes from the behavior of sheep, which are often seen as shy or timid animals. It has been used in English since the 1200s to describe people who act shy, embarrassed, or guilty.

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: sceap (sheep) + -ish (suffix meaning "like")
⬇️
Middle English: sheepish
⬇️
Modern English: sheepish /ˈʃiːpɪʃ/
