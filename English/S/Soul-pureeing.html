🌟 soul-pureeing /ˈsoʊl ˌpjʊə.ri.ɪŋ/

Part of speech: Adjective (informal, figurative)

📖 What does it mean?

"Soul-pureeing" is a metaphorical expression used to describe an experience so overwhelming or emotionally draining that it feels as if your soul is being blended into a purée. It's used for moments of deep frustration, confusion, or exhaustion—especially in contexts like difficult schoolwork or bureaucratic processes.

Example: The endless math drills were soul-pureeing. (= They crushed my spirit.)

🧩 Synonyms

  • Crushing
  • Demoralizing
  • Exhausting
  • Draining
  • Overwhelming

🔍 Etymology of the phrase

📚 "Soul-pureeing" is a modern creative phrase. It combines the word soul (referring to one's emotional or spiritual essence) and puree (to blend into a smooth paste), forming a vivid and humorous metaphor.

💡 It is not found in traditional dictionaries but has appeared in literature and blogs to describe emotional burnout or absurdly draining experiences.

🧭 Word Formation

Soul: one's emotional or spiritual core
+ Purée: to blend food into a smooth paste
⬇️
Soul-pureeing: blending one’s spirit into mush
