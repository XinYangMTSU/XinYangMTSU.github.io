🔬 specimen /ˈspɛsəmən/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

An individual example or sample of something, especially used for scientific study or analysis.
样本；标本；（用于科学研究的）样品。

Example: The scientist examined the blood specimen under a microscope.
例句：科学家在显微镜下检查血液样本。
Example: The museum displayed a rare specimen of an extinct bird.
例句：博物馆展出了一个稀有的灭绝鸟类标本。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Sample 样品
  • Example 例子
  • Model 模型
  • Exemplar 范例

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Specimen" comes from Latin specere, meaning "to look" or "to observe," and specimen, meaning "a mark" or "example to observe."
“Specimen” 源自拉丁语 specere（观察）和 specimen（可观察的例子）。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: specimen – example to be observed
拉丁语 specimen：用于观察的例子

Modern English: specimen – sample or representative item
现代英语 specimen：样本、代表性物件

📊 Comparison

Word Context Example Use Tone
