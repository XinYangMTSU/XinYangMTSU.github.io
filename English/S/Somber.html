🌟 somber /ˈsɑːmbər/

Part of speech: Adjective

📖 As an adjective (describing):

Something somber is dark, dull, or gloomy in tone—often used to describe a serious or sad mood, color, or setting.

Example: He spoke in a somber voice at the funeral.
Example: The sky was painted in somber shades of gray.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Gloomy
  • Dark
  • Grave
  • Melancholy
  • Solemn
  • Dismal

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word somber comes from French sombre meaning "dark, gloomy," which in turn comes from Latin subumbrāre, meaning "to shadow lightly."

  • sub- = under
  • umbra = shadow

💡 Originally used to describe darkness in color or light, it gradually took on an emotional meaning: dark in tone, mood, or feeling.
