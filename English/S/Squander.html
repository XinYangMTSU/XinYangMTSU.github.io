💸 squander /ˈskwɒn.dər/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To waste something (especially money, time, or resources) in a foolish or careless way.
浪费；挥霍（尤指金钱、时间或资源），通常指不明智或草率地使用。

Example: He squandered his inheritance on luxury cars and parties.
例句：他把继承的钱挥霍在豪车和派对上。
Example: Don't squander this opportunity to improve your skills.
例句：不要浪费这次提升技能的机会。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Waste 浪费
  • Blow (informal) 乱花（非正式）
  • Dissipate 挥霍；消散
  • Fritter away 一点一点浪费掉

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Squander" is of uncertain origin, but it may come from an obsolete word meaning "to scatter" or "to disperse," reflecting the idea of throwing resources away carelessly.
“Squander”的词源不确定，可能来自一个已废弃的词，意为“分散”或“抛洒”，体现了随意浪费资源的意思。

🧭 Word Evolution

Obsolete English: squand – to scatter
古英语 squand：散开、挥洒

Modern English: squander – to waste foolishly
现代英语 squander：愚蠢地浪费

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Intensity Example Use
Waste Neutral Medium He wastes time on social media.
Squander Formal Strong She squandered all her savings in a year.
Fritter away Informal Mild They frittered away the afternoon chatting.
