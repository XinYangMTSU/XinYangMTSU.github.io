💬 sentiment /ˈsɛntɪmənt/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A view, opinion, or feeling about something; an emotion or attitude toward a situation or topic.
观点，情绪，感情；对某事的态度或看法。

Example: Public sentiment is shifting in favor of environmental reform.
例句：公众情绪正转向支持环境改革。
Example: His speech was filled with patriotic sentiment.
例句：他的演讲充满了爱国情怀。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Emotion 情感
  • Feeling 感觉
  • Opinion 看法
  • Viewpoint 观点

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Sentiment" comes from Latin sentire, meaning "to feel." It entered English via Old French as sentiment, meaning "feeling or opinion."
sentiment 源自拉丁语 sentire（感觉），通过古法语 sentiment（感情、意见）进入英语。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: sentire – to feel
拉丁语 sentire：感受
⬇️
Old French: sentiment – feeling, opinion
古法语 sentiment：感情，观点
⬇️
Modern English: sentiment – feeling or emotional view
现代英语 sentiment：感情或情绪化的看法

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Emotion Neutral She was overwhelmed by emotion. Personal / Intense
Opinion Neutral His opinion differs from mine. Objective / General
Sentiment Neutral / Poetic Public sentiment is against the war. Reflective / Emotional
