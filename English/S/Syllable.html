🔠 syllable /ˈsɪləbəl/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A syllable is a single, unbroken sound unit of a spoken word. It typically consists of a vowel sound, and may also include consonants before or after it.
syllable（音节）是构成单词的最小发音单位，通常由一个元音音素组成，有时也包括前后的辅音。

Example: The word “apple” has two syllables: ap-ple.
例句：“apple”这个单词有两个音节：ap-ple。
Example: She clapped once for each syllable in the word "banana".
例句：她在说“banana”这个词时，每个音节拍一下手。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Sound unit 音的单位
  • Phonetic segment 语音片段
  • Pronunciation beat 发音节奏
  • Spoken chunk 口语块

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Syllable" comes from the Latin syllaba, which is derived from Greek syllabē, meaning “that which is held together” — referring to a group of letters or sounds spoken as a unit.
“syllable” 源自拉丁语 syllaba，来自希腊语 syllabē，意思是“连在一起的东西”，指的是作为一个整体发出的字母或声音组。

🧭 Word Evolution

Greek: syllabē – a group taken together
希腊语 syllabē：一起发出的声音
⬇️
Latin: syllaba – syllable
拉丁语 syllaba：音节
⬇️
Modern English: syllable – a unit of sound in speech
现代英语 syllable：语音中的发音单位
