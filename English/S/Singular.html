🌟 singular /ˈsɪŋɡjələr/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Singular can mean:

  1. Exceptional or remarkable – very noticeable or unusual.
  2. Relating to only one person or thing – opposite of plural in grammar.
singular 可表示“非凡的、显著的”，也可指“单数的”，与复数相对。

Example: She displayed singular courage in the face of danger.
例句：她在面对危险时表现出非凡的勇气。
Example: In the sentence "The cat sleeps," the noun "cat" is singular.
例句：在句子“The cat sleeps”中，名词“cat”是单数形式。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Exceptional 卓越的
  • Unusual 不同寻常的
  • Outstanding 杰出的
  • Unique 独一无二的
  • Remarkable 显著的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Singular" comes from Latin singularis, from singulus meaning "single, one only".
singular 源自拉丁语 singularis，来自 singulus，意思是“单个的、唯一的”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: singulus – one, single
拉丁语 singulus：单个的
⬇️
Late Latin: singularis – unique, remarkable
拉丁语 singularis：独特的，非凡的
⬇️
Modern English: singular – exceptional or single
现代英语 singular：非凡的，或单数的

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Unusual Neutral That was an unusual response. Descriptive
Exceptional Formal He has exceptional talent in math. Admiring
Singular Formal / Academic Her singular devotion to the cause was inspiring. Elevated / literary
