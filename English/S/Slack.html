🪢 slack /slæk/

Part of speech: Adjective / Noun / Verb
词性：形容词 / 名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

Adjective: Loose; not tight or firm. Can also mean slow, lazy, or weak in effort.
形容词：松弛的；不紧的；也可指松懈的，懒散的。

Noun: The part of something (like a rope) that is not tight.
名词：松弛部分（如绳子、线）。

Verb: To loosen or reduce effort.
动词：使松弛；放松；懈怠。

Example: The rope was slack and needed to be tightened.
例句：这根绳子很松，需要拉紧。
Example: He was criticized for being slack in his duties.
例句：他因懈怠职务而受到批评。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Loose 松的
  • Limp 无力的
  • Weak 软弱的
  • Lax 松懈的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Slack" comes from Old English slæc, meaning “loose, careless, or slow.” It is related to similar Germanic roots that mean weak or relaxed.
“Slack” 源自古英语 slæc，意为“松弛的、散漫的、缓慢的”，与其他日耳曼语中表示虚弱或放松的词根有关。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: slæc – loose, negligent
古英语 slæc：松的，不用力的

Modern English: slack – loose, slow, or lacking energy
现代英语 slack：松弛的、缓慢的、懈怠的
