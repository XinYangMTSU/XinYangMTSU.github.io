🌬️ sway /sweɪ/

Part of speech: Verb / Noun
词性：动词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

Verb: To move slowly or rhythmically back and forth or side to side. It can also mean to influence someone’s opinion or decision.
动词：缓慢地摇摆或晃动；影响（观点、决定）。

Noun: A slow movement from side to side; control or influence over someone or something.
名词：摇摆；影响力。

Example (verb): The trees swayed gently in the wind.
例句（动词）：树木在风中轻轻摇曳。
Example (verb): He was swayed by her emotional speech.
例句（动词）：他被她充满感情的演讲打动了。
Example (noun): The sway of the boat made me feel dizzy.
例句（名词）：船的摇晃让我感到头晕。
Example (noun): The empire held sway over vast territories.
例句（名词）：这个帝国对广袤的领土拥有统治权。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Swing 摆动
  • Rock 摇晃
  • Influence 影响
  • Control 控制

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Sway" comes from the Old Norse word sveigja, meaning “to bend or swing.” It has been used in English since the 14th century to describe both physical motion and figurative influence.
“Sway” 源自古诺斯语 sveigja，意为“弯曲，摆动”。14 世纪起在英语中用于描述物理上的摇摆以及抽象的影响。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old Norse: sveigja – to bend, swing
古诺斯语 sveigja：弯曲，摆动

Middle English: swayen – to move or control
中古英语 swayen：移动或支配

Modern English: sway – to move side to side; to influence
现代英语 sway：摇摆；影响

📊 Comparison

Word Use Formality Example
Swing Physical motion Neutral The swing moved back and forth.
Influence Opinion, decision Formal She influenced the jury’s decision.
Sway Motion & Influence Neutral The speech swayed the crowd.
