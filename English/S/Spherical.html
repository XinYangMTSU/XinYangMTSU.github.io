🌍 spherical /ˈsfɪrɪkəl/

Part of speech: Adjective

📖 Definition:

"Spherical" describes something that is shaped like a sphere or ball — perfectly round in all directions.

Example: The Earth is not perfectly spherical, but it is close.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Round
  • Globular
  • Ball-shaped
  • Orb-like
  • Elliptical (in broader context)

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "spherical" comes from the Greek word sphaira, meaning "globe" or "ball."

📝 It entered English via Latin sphaericus and Middle French spherique.

🛰️ Real-Life Example

The Death Star in Star Wars is famously spherical — a perfect shape for science fiction, but possibly impractical in real engineering terms.
