🌟 straitjacket /ˈstreɪtˌdʒækɪt/

Part of speech: Noun

📖 Definition:

A straitjacket is a strong garment with long sleeves that can be tied together, used to prevent a person from moving their arms—often in the past to restrain people with mental illness. Figuratively, it refers to anything that severely limits freedom of movement, creativity, or action.

Literal Example: The patient was restrained in a straitjacket.
Figurative Example: Endless bureaucracy acts as a straitjacket on innovation.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Constraint
  • Limitation
  • Restriction
  • Control
  • Confinement
  • Shackle

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "straitjacket" comes from the idea of a “strait” (meaning narrow or tight) + “jacket.”

📝 "Strait" is not the same as "straight" — it means tight or narrow, from Middle English and Old French estreit, from Latin strictus.

💡 So, a straitjacket is literally a “tight jacket” used to restrain someone. The figurative use came later, describing anything that restricts or limits creativity, freedom, or movement.

🧭 Word Evolution

⬇️
