🌟 formidable /ˈfɔːrmɪdəbl/

Part of speech: Adjective

📖 Meaning:

Formidable describes something or someone that inspires fear or respect because of their size, strength, skill, or difficulty.

Example: The mountain posed a formidable challenge for even the most experienced climbers.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Intimidating
  • Powerful
  • Daunting
  • Impressive
  • Dreadful
  • Fearsome

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "formidable" comes from the Latin word formidabilis, meaning “causing fear.”

  • formidare = to fear
  • -abilis = capable of

💡 So “formidable” literally means something “capable of causing fear or respect.”
