⚡ formidable /ˈfɔːrmɪdəbl/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Formidable describes something or someone that causes fear, respect, or admiration because of being impressively large, powerful, intense, or difficult.
formidable 意为“令人敬畏的、可怕的、难以对付的”，通常形容规模庞大、实力强大或难度极高的人或事。

Example: The team faces a formidable opponent in tomorrow’s match.
例句：这个队在明天的比赛中将面对一个强大的对手。
Example: Climbing Mount Everest is a formidable challenge even for experienced climbers.
例句：即便是经验丰富的登山者，攀登珠穆朗玛峰也是一项艰巨的挑战。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Intimidating 令人害怕的
  • Daunting 令人畏惧的
  • Powerful 强大的
  • Mighty 威猛的
  • Impressive 令人印象深刻的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Formidable" comes from Latin formidabilis, from formidare meaning “to fear.” It originally referred to things that inspired dread or awe.
formidable 源自拉丁语 formidabilis，来自 formidare，意为“害怕、畏惧”，最初形容令人敬畏或恐惧的事物。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: formidare – to fear
拉丁语 formidare：害怕
⬇️
Latin: formidabilis – causing fear
拉丁语 formidabilis：令人害怕的
⬇️
Modern English: formidable – inspiring fear, admiration, or respect
现代英语 formidable：令人敬畏或尊敬的，强大的
