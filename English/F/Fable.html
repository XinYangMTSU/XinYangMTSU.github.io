🐢 fable /ˈfeɪbəl/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A short story, typically with animals as characters, that conveys a moral or lesson; a myth or legend.
寓言；通常以动物为主角并传达道德教训的短故事；神话传说。

Example: The fable of the tortoise and the hare teaches that slow and steady wins the race.
例句：龟兔赛跑的寓言告诉我们，慢而稳定才能取胜。
Example: Many of Aesop's fables are still taught in schools today.
例句：伊索的许多寓言至今仍在学校教授。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Tale 故事
  • Parable 寓言；比喻故事
  • Myth 神话
  • Allegory 寓言；讽喻

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Fable" comes from Latin fabula, meaning “story or tale,” from fari (to speak or tell). It entered English through Old French.
“Fable” 源自拉丁语 fabula，意为“故事、传说”，来自 fari（讲述、说话），通过古法语进入英语。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: fabula – a story or tale
拉丁语 fabula：故事、传说

Old French: fable – a tale with a moral
古法语 fable：带有寓意的故事

Modern English: fable – a short story with a lesson
现代英语 fable：有教训的短故事

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Story Neutral She told me a story about her dog. General
Myth Formal Greek myths explain the origins of the world. Legendary
Fable Formal The fable taught children an important lesson. Instructive
