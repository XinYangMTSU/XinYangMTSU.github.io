🌟 Foe /foʊ/

Part of speech: Noun

📖 Meaning:

A foe is an enemy or opponent — someone who is against you in a conflict, competition, or struggle.

Example: The hero defeated his longtime foe in the final battle.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Enemy
  • Opponent
  • Rival
  • Adversary
  • Combatant
  • Antagonist

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word foe dates back to Old English fāh and gefā, meaning "hostile" or "enemy." It evolved into Middle English fo and then into the modern form foe.

🧭 Word Evolution

⬇️
