🧊 frustum /ˈfrʌstəm/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A solid figure formed by cutting the top off a cone or pyramid with a plane parallel to the base. It has two parallel faces—one larger, one smaller—and trapezoidal sides.
截头体；通过与底面平行的平面切去圆锥或金字塔顶端而形成的立体图形，具有上下两个平行面（一个大一个小）和梯形侧面。

Example: The lampshade was shaped like a frustum of a cone.
例句：这个灯罩的形状像一个圆锥的截头体。
Example: Engineers calculated the volume of the frustum using geometry formulas.
例句：工程师使用几何公式计算了截头体的体积。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Truncated cone 截头圆锥
  • Truncated pyramid 截头金字塔
  • Geometric solid 几何立体

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Frustum" comes from Latin frustum, meaning "a piece broken off" or "a fragment". It refers to a portion of a solid that has been sliced off.
“Frustum” 来自拉丁语 frustum，意为“断裂的一块”或“碎片”，用于描述被切去顶端后的立体图形。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: frustum – a piece, fragment
拉丁语 frustum：一块，碎片

Modern English: frustum – a geometric solid formed by slicing off the top of a cone or pyramid
现代英语 frustum：几何中的截头体
