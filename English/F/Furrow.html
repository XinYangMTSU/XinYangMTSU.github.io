🌾 furrow /ˈfɜːroʊ/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

A long narrow trench in the ground or a deep wrinkle, especially on the forehead.
地面上的长沟槽，或（尤指额头上的）深皱纹。

Example: The farmer plowed deep furrows into the soil.
例句：农民在土壤中犁出了深深的沟槽。
Example: He furrowed his brow in confusion.
例句：他疑惑地皱起了眉头。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Trench 沟槽
  • Wrinkle 皱纹
  • Crease 褶皱
  • Groove 凹槽

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Furrow" comes from Old English furh, meaning “trench” or “track,” originally associated with farming.
furrow 来自古英语 furh，意为“沟槽”或“痕迹”，最初与耕作有关。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: furh – trench, furrow
古英语 furh：沟槽，犁沟
⬇️
Modern English: furrow – a groove in soil or a wrinkle in skin
现代英语 furrow：土壤中的沟槽或皮肤上的皱纹

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Wrinkle Neutral She had wrinkles around her eyes. Descriptive / Aging
Groove Neutral / Technical The record has tiny grooves that hold the sound. Mechanical
