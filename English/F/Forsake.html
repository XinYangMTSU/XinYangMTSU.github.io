🚪 forsake /fərˈseɪk/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To abandon or give up someone or something; to renounce or turn away from something once valued or held dear.
放弃；抛弃；遗弃（曾经重视或珍爱的事物或人）。

Example: He vowed never to forsake his friends in times of trouble.
例句：他发誓在困难时期决不抛弃朋友。
Example: She forsook her luxurious lifestyle to live in the countryside.
例句：她放弃了奢华的生活方式，搬到乡下居住。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Abandon 放弃
  • Desert 遗弃
  • Renounce 声明放弃
  • Relinquish 正式放弃

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Forsake" comes from Old English forsacan, meaning “to refuse or renounce.” It combines for- (completely) and sacan (to quarrel or argue).
“Forsake” 源自古英语 forsacan，意为“拒绝、放弃”，由 for-（完全地）和 sacan（争辩）组成。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: forsacan – to refuse, renounce
古英语 forsacan：拒绝、放弃

Middle English: forsaken – to abandon
中古英语 forsaken：遗弃

Modern English: forsake – to give up or abandon completely
现代英语 forsake：完全放弃或遗弃
