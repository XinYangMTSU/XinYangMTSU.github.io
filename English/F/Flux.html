🔄 flux /flʌks/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

Continuous change, movement, or flow in a system or situation.
系统或情况中的持续变化、流动。

Example: The education system is in a state of constant flux.
例句：教育体系处于不断变化之中。
Example: Our plans are still in flux due to the uncertain weather.
例句：由于天气不确定，我们的计划仍在变动中。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Change 变化
  • Flow 流动
  • Instability 不稳定
  • Transition 过渡

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Flux" comes from Latin fluxus, meaning "flow" or "a flowing."
flux 来自拉丁语 fluxus，意为“流动”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: fluxus – flow
拉丁语 fluxus：流动
⬇️
Modern English: flux – continuous movement or change
现代英语 flux：持续的变化或流动

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Change Neutral The schedule might change depending on feedback. Flexible
Transition Neutral / Formal The company is in a transition period. Shifting
