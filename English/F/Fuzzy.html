🌫️ fuzzy /ˈfʌzi/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Fuzzy can mean unclear or hard to see, feel, or understand. It can describe visual blur, unclear thinking, or something soft and fuzzy to touch.
fuzzy 可以指模糊不清的（视觉、感觉或理解上的），也可以形容某物柔软而毛茸茸的。

Example: My memory of that day is a little fuzzy.
例句：我对那天的记忆有些模糊。
Example: He wore a fuzzy sweater that looked really warm.
例句：他穿了一件看起来非常温暖的毛茸茸的毛衣。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Blurry 模糊的
  • Hazy 朦胧的
  • Vague 不明确的
  • Soft 柔软的
  • Indistinct 不清晰的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Fuzzy" comes from the word "fuzz," meaning small soft fibers or fluff. It later gained the figurative meaning of being unclear or indistinct.
fuzzy 来自 fuzz（一种绒毛、纤维），后来引申为模糊不清的意思。

🧭 Word Evolution

Original: Fuzz – soft, light fibers
原义 fuzz：柔软轻微的绒毛
⬇️
Modern English: Fuzzy – soft to touch or unclear in mind or vision
现代英语 fuzzy：毛茸茸的，或头脑/视觉模糊的

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Hazy Neutral The landscape looked hazy in the distance. Descriptive / poetic
Blurry Casual The photo is too blurry to see clearly. Visual / informal
Fuzzy Casual / Emotional I have a fuzzy feeling about that moment. Soft / imprecise
