👑 Pharaoh /ˈfɛə.roʊ/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

The title used for the monarchs (kings) of ancient Egypt. Pharaohs were considered both political rulers and divine figures.
法老；古埃及的君主，被认为既是国家统治者，又是神的化身。

Example: The tomb of the pharaoh Tutankhamun was discovered nearly intact in 1922.
例句：法老图坦卡蒙的陵墓在1922年几乎完好无损地被发现。
Example: Ancient Egyptians believed the pharaoh was a god in human form.
例句：古埃及人相信法老是人形的神。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Egyptian king 埃及国王
  • Monarch 君主
  • Divine ruler 神权统治者
  • Ancient sovereign 古代统治者

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Pharaoh" comes from the Egyptian phrase per-aa, meaning "great house," referring to the royal palace. It later came to mean the king himself.
“Pharaoh” 源自埃及语词组 per-aa，意为“大神殿”或“王宫”，后来被用作指代国王本身。

🧭 Word Evolution

Ancient Egyptian: per-aa – great house
古埃及语 per-aa：大神殿，王宫

Hebrew: parʻō
希伯来语 parʻō：法老

Greek: Pharaō → Latin: Pharao
希腊语 Pharaō → 拉丁语 Pharao

Modern English: Pharaoh – king of ancient Egypt
现代英语 Pharaoh：古埃及的君主
