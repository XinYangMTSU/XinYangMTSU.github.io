🛑 flat-out /ˌflæt ˈaʊt/

Part of speech: Adverb / Adjective
词性：副词 / 形容词

📖 Meaning:

Completely or absolutely; in a direct or blunt way; also used to mean doing something at full speed or maximum effort.
完全地；直率地；也指全力以赴地做某事。

Example: She flat-out denied all the accusations.
例句：她完全否认了所有指控。
Example: He was working flat-out to meet the deadline.
例句：他在拼尽全力赶在截止日期前完成工作。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Absolutely 绝对地
  • Completely 完全地
  • Totally 彻底地
  • All-out 全力以赴地

🔍 Etymology of the phrase /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Flat-out" originated in American English in the early 20th century, originally used to describe cars or people moving at full speed.
flat-out 起源于 20 世纪初的美式英语，最初用来形容车辆或人以最快速度移动。

🧭 Phrase Evolution

Early use: Full speed or effort
早期用法：全速或全力
⬇️
Modern use: Completely, bluntly, or intensely
现代用法：彻底地、直率地或极度地

📊 Comparison

Phrase Formality Level Example Use Tone
Absolutely Neutral / Formal She is absolutely certain about her choice. Confident
All-out Neutral / Informal The team made an all-out effort to win. Energetic
