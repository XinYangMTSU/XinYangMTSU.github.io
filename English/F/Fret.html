😟 fret /fret/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To worry or be anxious about something, especially unnecessarily.
担心或焦虑（尤指不必要地）。

Example: Don’t fret — everything will be fine in the end.
例句：别担心——一切最终都会好起来的。
Example: She spent the whole day fretting over her exam results.
例句：她整天为考试成绩而焦虑。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Worry 担心
  • Stress 紧张
  • Panic 惊慌
  • Agonize 苦恼

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Fret" comes from Old English fretan, meaning “to devour” or “wear away,” later developing into the modern sense of worrying.
fret 来自古英语 fretan，意为“吞噬”或“侵蚀”，后来发展为“焦虑、担忧”的含义。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: fretan – to devour, consume
古英语 fretan：吞噬，消耗
⬇️
Modern English: fret – to worry or feel anxious
现代英语 fret：担忧，焦虑

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Worry Neutral I worry about my parents’ health. Concerned
Agonize Formal She agonized over the decision for days. Intense
Fret Neutral / Informal Don't fret — it’s not a big deal. Mildly anxious
