🌟 fantasy /ˈfæn.tə.si/

Part of speech: Noun (can also function as a verb or adjective)

📖 Meaning 1: Imagination, Dream, or Daydream

“Fantasy” refers to thoughts or ideas that are imagined, not based on reality — often involving dreams, desires, or impossible scenarios.

Example: He lived in a fantasy where he was the king of a magical land.

📖 Meaning 2: A Genre of Fiction

In literature and film, “fantasy” is a genre featuring magic, mythical creatures, or imaginary worlds.

Example: "The Lord of the Rings" is a famous fantasy series.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Imagination
  • Dream
  • Illusion
  • Fiction
  • Daydream
  • Make-believe

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 “Fantasy” comes from the Greek word phantasia meaning “appearance, imagination.”

📝 Passed into Latin as phantasia, then into Old French and Middle English as fantasie.

💡 Originally tied to the mind’s ability to form mental images of things not present or real.
