🏆 feat /fiːt/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A remarkable achievement that requires great skill, courage, or strength.
需要极大技巧、勇气或力量的非凡成就。

Example: Climbing Mount Everest was a remarkable feat of endurance.
例句：登上珠穆朗玛峰是一项非凡的耐力壮举。
Example: The magician’s final trick was an incredible feat of illusion.
例句：魔术师最后的把戏是一项令人难以置信的幻术壮举。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Achievement 成就
  • Accomplishment 完成
  • Exploit 英勇行为
  • Triumph 胜利

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Feat" comes from Old French fait, meaning "deed" or "action", from Latin factum.
feat 来自古法语 fait，意为“行为”或“行动”，源自拉丁语 factum。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: factum – deed
拉丁语 factum：行为
⬇️
Old French: fait – action, feat
古法语 fait：行动，壮举
⬇️
Middle English: feat – deed, act of skill
中古英语 feat：行为，技艺表现

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Achievement Neutral / Formal Graduating with honors is a major achievement. Respectful
Exploit Neutral / Literary He told tales of his wartime exploits. Heroic / adventurous
Feat Neutral The rescue operation was a feat of teamwork and bravery. Impressive / skillful
