🌈 fantasist /ˈfæntəsɪst/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A person who imagines or invents stories, often ones that are not based in reality; someone who lives in a fantasy world or tells exaggerated or untrue stories.
幻想家；沉迷于虚构世界或讲述夸张故事的人。

Example: He’s a fantasist who claims he once discovered a hidden city in the jungle.
例句：他是个幻想家，声称自己曾在丛林中发现一座隐藏的城市。
Example: The novel’s main character is a charming fantasist who blurs the line between reality and fiction.
例句：小说中的主角是一个迷人的幻想家，常常模糊现实与虚构之间的界限。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Dreamer 梦想家
  • Storyteller 讲故事的人
  • Fabricator 编造者
  • Visionary 有幻想的人

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Fantasist" comes from the word fantasy, which is from Greek phantasia meaning “imagination” or “appearance.” It was later adopted into Latin and Old French before becoming the modern English term.
“Fantasist” 来自单词 fantasy（幻想），而 fantasy 源自希腊语 phantasia，意为“想象”或“幻象”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Greek: phantasia – imagination, appearance
希腊语 phantasia：想象、幻象

Old French: fantasie – fantasy, whim
古法语 fantasie：幻想、异想天开

Modern English: fantasist – one who imagines or invents
现代英语 fantasist：幻想者、编造故事者

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
