🌟 postulate /ˈpɒstʃʊlət/

Part of speech: Noun or verb

📖 As a verb (action):

To postulate something means to suggest or assume it as a basic principle or fact, especially as a basis for reasoning or argument.

Example: The scientist postulated that the disease spread through airborne particles.

📖 As a noun (a thing):

A postulate is a statement that is accepted as true without proof, often used as a starting point in logic or mathematics.

Example: One of Euclid’s famous postulates is that through any two points, there is exactly one straight line.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Assume
  • Presume
  • Propose
  • Suggest
  • Hypothesize
  • Claim

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "postulate".

📝It comes from the Latin verb postulare, which means "to demand or claim."

  • postulare = to demand, claim, or request

✍️ It entered English in the late Middle Ages through French as postuler, used in legal and philosophical contexts.
