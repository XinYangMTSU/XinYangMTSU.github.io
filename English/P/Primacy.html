🥇 primacy /ˈpraɪməsi/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

The state of being the most important or having the highest position or power.
首要地位；至高无上的地位。

Example: The primacy of education in national development is widely acknowledged.
例句：教育在国家发展中的首要地位被广泛认可。
Example: The Constitution establishes the primacy of federal law over state law.
例句：宪法确立了联邦法律优于州法律的首要地位。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Supremacy 至高无上
  • Dominance 支配地位
  • Leadership 领导地位
  • Preeminence 卓越；优越

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Primacy" comes from Latin prīmus, meaning “first.” It entered English via Late Latin primatia (“first place”).
primacy 来自拉丁语 prīmus，意为“第一”，通过晚期拉丁语 primatia 演变为“首位”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: prīmus – first
拉丁语 prīmus：第一
⬇️
Late Latin: primatia – first rank
晚期拉丁语 primatia：首要地位
⬇️
Modern English: primacy – highest importance or authority
现代英语 primacy：至高地位或最重要性

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Supremacy Formal The empire fought to maintain its military supremacy. Powerful / Strategic
Leadership Neutral The country took leadership in global climate talks. Positive / Practical
Primacy Formal / Academic The court affirmed the primacy of constitutional law. Authoritative / Legal
