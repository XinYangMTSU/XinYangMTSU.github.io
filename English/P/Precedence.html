🥇 precedence /ˈpresɪdəns/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

The condition of being considered more important than something else; the right to go before others in importance, order, or rank.
优先；优先权；重要性较高。

Example: Safety must take precedence over speed.
例句：安全必须优先于速度。
Example: In official ceremonies, precedence is given to senior officials.
例句：在正式仪式中，优先权给予高级官员。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Priority 优先
  • Primacy 首要
  • Preeminence 卓越；领先地位
  • Superiority 优越性

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Precedence" comes from the Latin word praecedere, meaning “to go before.” It entered English via Old French as precedence, referring to priority or the act of going ahead.
precedence 来自拉丁语 praecedere，意思是“走在前面”。通过古法语进入英语，表示优先或领先的行为。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: praecedere – to go before
拉丁语 praecedere：走在前面
⬇️
Old French: precedence – priority, rank
古法语 precedence：优先，等级
⬇️
Modern English: precedence – priority, importance
现代英语 precedence：优先权，重要性

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Priority Neutral Our top priority is customer safety. Practical
Primacy Formal The primacy of science in modern thought is clear. Philosophical
Precedence Formal Her health takes precedence over her job. Serious / Hierarchical
