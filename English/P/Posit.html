🧠 posit /ˈpɑːzɪt/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To posit something is to assume or suggest it as a fact, especially as a basis for argument or reasoning.
“posit” 的意思是提出某个观点、假设或理论，作为推理或讨论的出发点。

Example: The philosopher posited that reality is a mental construct.
例句：那位哲学家提出现实是一种心理建构的观点。
Example: Scientists posited the existence of a new particle to explain the anomaly.
例句：科学家提出存在一种新粒子，以解释这一异常现象。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Propose 提出
  • Suggest 建议
  • Assert 断言
  • Postulate 假设
  • Hypothesize 假定

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 “Posit” comes from Latin positus, the past participle of ponere, meaning “to place or put.” It originally referred to putting something forward or placing it as a foundation.
“posit” 来自拉丁语 positus，是动词 ponere（放置）的过去分词，原意是“摆放”或“设定基础”。
