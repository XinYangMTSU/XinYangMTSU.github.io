📚 plotline /ˈplɒt.laɪn/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A plotline is the main story or sequence of events in a book, movie, play, or other narrative work.
plotline 指小说、电影、戏剧或其他叙事作品中的主要故事情节或事件顺序。

Example: The romantic plotline was predictable, but still enjoyable.
例句：爱情情节虽然可以预测，但仍然令人愉快。
Example: Several characters’ plotlines eventually come together in the final chapter.
例句：几个角色的情节最终在最后一章汇合。

📷 Freytag's pyramid – a common structure used to describe plotlines

  • Climax 高潮

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "plotline" combines "plot" (from Old French complot, meaning a plan or scheme) and "line" (a sequence or thread of events).
plotline 一词由 “plot”（情节）和 “line”（线索）组成，指的是一系列连贯发展的事件。
