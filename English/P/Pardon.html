🙏 pardon /ˈpɑːrdn/

Part of speech: Verb / Noun
词性：动词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

Verb: To forgive someone for a mistake or offense.
Noun: Official forgiveness for a crime or wrongdoing, often from a government or authority.
动词：原谅，宽恕（某人的错误或冒犯）。
名词：官方赦免，尤指政府对罪犯的赦免。

Example: Please pardon my lateness — the traffic was terrible.
例句：请原谅我迟到——交通太糟糕了。
Example: The president granted a full pardon to the whistleblower.
例句：总统给予了举报人全面赦免。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Forgive 原谅
  • Excuse 宽恕
  • Amnesty 赦免
  • Exonerate 免除责任

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Pardon" comes from Old French pardoner and Latin perdonare, meaning “to give completely” or “to forgive.”
pardon 源自古法语 pardoner 和拉丁语 perdonare，意为“完全给予”或“宽恕”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: perdonare – to forgive completely
拉丁语 perdonare：完全宽恕
⬇️
Old French: pardoner – to forgive
古法语 pardoner：原谅
⬇️
Modern English: pardon – to forgive, official forgiveness
现代英语 pardon：原谅，赦免

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Forgive Neutral I forgive you for what happened. Personal / Kind
Amnesty Formal / Legal The rebels were given amnesty by the government. Official / Peaceful
Pardon Neutral / Formal He was granted a presidential pardon. Legal / Respectful
