🎓 prestigious /preˈstɪdʒəs/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Respected and admired because of high status, quality, or achievement.
因地位高、质量好或成就卓越而受人尊敬和仰慕的。

Example: She graduated from a prestigious university.
例句：她毕业于一所享有盛誉的大学。
Example: It’s one of the most prestigious awards in the scientific community.
例句：这是科学界最有声望的奖项之一。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Renowned 著名的
  • Distinguished 卓越的
  • Esteemed 受尊敬的
  • Celebrated 知名的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Prestigious" comes from Latin praestigiae meaning “illusion” or “deception”; over time, its meaning shifted to refer to impressive or respected status.
prestigious 来自拉丁语 praestigiae，原意为“幻术”或“欺骗”，后来演变为“令人印象深刻的”或“受尊敬的”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: praestigiae – illusion, trick
拉丁语 praestigiae：幻术、把戏
⬇️
French: prestigieux – dazzling, impressive
法语 prestigieux：令人印象深刻的
⬇️
Modern English: prestigious – honored, respected
现代英语 prestigious：受尊敬、有声望的

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Renowned Formal A renowned scientist gave the lecture. Respectful
Distinguished Formal He had a long and distinguished career. Honoring / Professional
Prestigious Formal / Admiring They were nominated for a prestigious prize. Elite / Admiring
