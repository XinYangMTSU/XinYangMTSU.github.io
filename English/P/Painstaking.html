🛠️ painstaking /ˈpeɪnˌsteɪ.kɪŋ/

Part of speech: Adjective

📖 Meaning:

Painstaking means doing something with great care, attention, and thoroughness. It often takes a lot of time and effort.

Example: She created a painstakingly detailed model of the solar system using handmade parts.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Meticulous
  • Thorough
  • Exacting
  • Careful
  • Diligent
  • Attentive

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "painstaking".

📝It comes from the old phrase "to take pains", which means "to put in effort or trouble for something."

  • pain(s) = effort, care, suffering (not just physical pain)
  • taking = actively doing something

💡 Over time, the phrase "taking pains" became "painstaking" as a way to describe actions done with great care and diligence.

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English & French: peine (trouble, effort) /pɛn/
⬇️
Middle English: payn-taking – to take care or suffer for detail
⬇️
Modern English: painstaking /ˈpeɪnˌsteɪ.kɪŋ/
