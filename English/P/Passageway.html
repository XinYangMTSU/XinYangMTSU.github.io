🚪 passageway /ˈpæsɪdʒˌweɪ/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A narrow path or corridor that allows people to pass from one place to another, especially between rooms, buildings, or through enclosed areas.
通道；过道；尤指连接房间、建筑物或穿越封闭空间的狭长通路。

Example: The secret passageway led to a hidden chamber beneath the castle.
例句：这条秘密通道通往城堡下方的隐秘房间。
Example: Please keep the passageway clear of luggage at all times.
例句：请随时保持通道畅通，不要堆放行李。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Corridor 走廊
  • Hallway 过道
  • Tunnel 隧道
  • Pathway 小路；路径

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Passageway" is formed from "passage" (from Latin passus meaning "step") + "way", meaning a route or path for movement.
“Passageway” 由 “passage”（通过，来自拉丁语 passus，意为“步”）和 “way”（道路）组成，表示供人通行的路径。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: passus – step, stride
拉丁语 passus：步伐

Old French: passage – act of passing
古法语 passage：通行，经过

Modern English: passageway – a narrow route for movement
现代英语 passageway：供通行的狭长路径

📊 Comparison

Word Type Typical Location Example Use
Corridor Indoor Inside buildings We met in the school corridor.
Passageway Indoor/Underground Rooms, caves, hidden places A passageway led to a hidden tomb.
Tunnel Underground Mountains, subways The train entered the tunnel.
