🌟 pejorative /pəˈdʒɔːrətɪv/

Part of speech: Adjective or noun

📖 As an adjective (describing):

A pejorative word or expression shows disapproval or is intended to belittle someone or something.

Example: He used a pejorative term to refer to the other team. (= a disrespectful or insulting word)

📖 As a noun (a thing):

A pejorative is a word or phrase that expresses criticism or disapproval.

Example: The word "geek" used to be a pejorative, but now it's often a badge of honor.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Disparaging
  • Derogatory
  • Insulting
  • Belittling
  • Scornful
  • Contemptuous

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word pejorative comes from the Late Latin verb pejorare, which means “to make worse.”

  • pejor- = worse
  • -ative = relating to

💡 It entered English in the 19th century, used to describe words or expressions that cast something in a negative light.
