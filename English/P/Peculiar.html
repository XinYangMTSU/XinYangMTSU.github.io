🔮 peculiar /pɪˈkjuːliər/

Part of speech: Adjective

📖 Meaning:

Peculiar means something is strange, unusual, or different from what is normal or expected—often in a way that is interesting or hard to explain.

Example: There was a peculiar smell in the air—sweet, smoky, and oddly familiar.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Strange
  • Odd
  • Unusual
  • Weird
  • Curious
  • Bizarre

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "peculiar".

📝It comes from the Latin word peculium, which meant "private property or something specially one's own."

  • pecu- = cattle or wealth (in Latin, cattle was a symbol of property)

💡 Over time, “peculiar” came to mean "belonging to one person or group" and then eventually "unusual or unique."

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: peculium /peˈku.li.um/
⬇️
Middle English: peculier – meaning “particular to someone”
⬇️
Modern English: peculiar /pɪˈkjuːliər/
