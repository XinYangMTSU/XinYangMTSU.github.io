🤲 proffer /ˈprɑːfər/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To proffer means to offer something to someone, often formally or with respect.
“proffer” 指的是正式地或恭敬地向某人提供某物。

Example: He proffered his resignation to the board of directors.
例句：他向董事会递交了辞呈。
Example: She proffered him a cup of tea with a smile.
例句：她微笑着递给他一杯茶。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Offer 提供
  • Extend 伸出（如手、帮助）
  • Present 呈上
  • Tender 正式提交
  • Submit 提交

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 “Proffer” comes from Old French profre and Latin pro (forth) + offerre (to offer). It literally means “to bring forward.”
“proffer” 来自古法语 profre 和拉丁语 pro（向前）+ offerre（给予），字面意思是“拿出来给人”。

📊 Comparison with Related Words

Word Definition Example Formality
Offer To present for acceptance or rejection She offered him a glass of water. Neutral
Tender To formally submit or present something He tendered his resignation. Formal
Proffer To offer something formally or respectfully She proffered her opinion during the meeting. Formal / respectful
