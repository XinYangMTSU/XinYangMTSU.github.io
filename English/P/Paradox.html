🔄 paradox /ˈpærəˌdɒks/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A paradox is a statement or situation that seems contradictory or impossible but may actually be true or reveal a deeper truth.
“paradox” 是指看似矛盾或不合逻辑，但可能却是真实或揭示深层道理的陈述或情形。

Example: It’s a paradox that standing is more tiring than walking.
例句：站着比走路更累，这听起来是个悖论。
Example: The paradox of tolerance is that unlimited tolerance may lead to the disappearance of tolerance.
例句：宽容的悖论在于，无限的宽容可能导致宽容本身的消失。

🧩 Synonyms

  • Contradiction 矛盾
  • Irony 反讽
  • Dilemma 困境
  • Anomaly 异常
  • Enigma 谜团

🔍 Etymology of the word

📚 “Paradox” comes from the Greek word paradoxon, meaning “contrary to expectation.” It is formed from para (beyond, against) + doxa (opinion, belief).
“paradox” 源自希腊语 “paradoxon”，意思是“出乎意料的”，由 “para”（超出、反对）和 “doxa”（观点、信仰）组成。

📊 Comparison with Related Words

Word Definition Example Context
Contradiction Two things that cannot both be true Her actions were in contradiction to her words. Logic, argument
Irony Opposite of what’s expected, often humorous It’s ironic that the fire station burned down. Literature, speech
Paradox Seems contradictory but might be true The paradox of choice can cause anxiety. Philosophy, everyday life
