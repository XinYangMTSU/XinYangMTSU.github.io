🌟 protract /proʊˈtrækt/

Part of speech: Verb

📖 Meaning:

To protract something means to make it last longer than necessary; to prolong or extend in time.

Example: The negotiations were protracted over several months. (= They dragged on longer than expected.)

🧹 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Prolong
  • Extend
  • Delay
  • Drag out
  • Lengthen
  • Stretch

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˈɛtəˌmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let's look at the origin of the word "protract".

📝It comes from Latin protrahere — pro (forward) + trahere (to pull).

  • pro- = forward
  • trahere = to pull or drag

✍️ So "protract" literally means to pull something forward in time — to delay or prolong.

📊 Comparison

Word Meaning Example Use Tone
Prolong To make something last longer She prolonged the speech with extra stories. Neutral
Delay To make something happen later The train was delayed by 2 hours. Often negative
Protract To extend unnecessarily or for too long They protracted the discussion endlessly. Negative / formal

📰 Protract in the News

In news or formal writing, "protract" is often used to describe lengthy negotiations, conflicts, or legal proceedings. For example: "The trial was protracted over several years due to repeated delays."
