🌟 puffery /ˈpʌfəri/

Part of speech: Noun

📖 Meaning:

Puffery means exaggerated, boastful claims—especially in advertising—that are not meant to be taken literally. It's "hype" that makes something sound better than it really is!

Example: The commercial's claim that their shampoo would "change your life" was just puffery. (= It was a playful exaggeration.)

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Hype
  • Boasting
  • Bragging
  • Exaggeration
  • Show-off
  • Flattery

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "puffery".

📝It comes from the English verb puff, meaning "to blow up" or "inflate," plus the noun suffix -ery which refers to behavior or practice.

  • puff = to blow air, to inflate
  • -ery = a practice or behavior

✍️ "Puffery" literally means the behavior of inflating or exaggerating something's value.

💡 It became popular in law and advertising to describe exaggerations that are so obvious, they are not considered fraudulent.

🧭 Word Evolution

Middle English: puffen (to swell, to blow air) /ˈpʌfən/
⬇️
Modern English: puffery (exaggerated praise) /ˈpʌfəri/

📰 Puffery in the News

In business law discussions, "puffery" often appears when companies make big, colorful claims about their products—claims that are exaggerated but legal because no one takes them literally.
