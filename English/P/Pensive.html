🤔 pensive /ˈpɛnsɪv/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Pensive describes someone who is deeply thoughtful, often in a serious or reflective way.
pensive 形容一个人陷入沉思的、带有忧郁或深思的状态。

Example: She sat by the window with a pensive look on her face.
例句：她坐在窗边，脸上带着沉思的神情。
Example: His pensive mood made everyone around him quiet.
例句：他沉思的情绪让周围的人都安静下来。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Thoughtful 深思的
  • Reflective 反思的
  • Contemplative 沉思的
  • Melancholy 忧郁的
  • Brooding 沉思的，闷闷不乐的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Pensive" comes from Old French pensif, derived from Latin pensare, meaning "to weigh or consider carefully."
“pensive” 来自古法语 pensif，源于拉丁语 pensare，意为“仔细衡量、思考”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: pensare – to weigh or ponder
拉丁语 pensare：衡量，思考
⬇️
Old French: pensif – thoughtful, reflective
古法语 pensif：沉思的
⬇️
Modern English: pensive – deeply thoughtful, often in a melancholic way
现代英语 pensive：深沉地思考，常带有忧郁

📊 Comparison with Related Words

Word Definition Example Tone
Thoughtful Engaged in deep thought or consideration He gave a thoughtful answer. Neutral
Melancholy Gently sad or gloomy She looked at the rain with a melancholy gaze. Sad
Pensive Quietly deep in thought, often reflective or somber He stared out the window in a pensive mood. Reflective / somber
