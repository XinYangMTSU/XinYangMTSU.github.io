😈 perverse /pərˈvɜːrs/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Showing a deliberate and unreasonable desire to behave in a way that is morally wrong, difficult, or unacceptable.
表现出故意而不合理的行为倾向，如不道德、固执或令人无法接受。

Example: She takes a perverse pleasure in shocking people.
例句：她以震惊他人为一种扭曲的乐趣。
Example: It’s perverse to keep arguing when you know you're wrong.
例句：明知自己错了还要坚持争论是很执拗的行为。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Contrary 执拗的
  • Unreasonable 不讲理的
  • Stubborn 固执的
  • Defiant 挑衅的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Perverse" comes from Latin pervertere, meaning "to turn around, corrupt."
perverse 源自拉丁语 pervertere，意为“颠倒、腐化”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: pervertere – to corrupt
拉丁语 pervertere：腐化
⬇️
Middle English: pervers – turned away from what is right
中古英语 pervers：背离正道
⬇️
Modern English: perverse – unreasonable or deliberately uncooperative
现代英语 perverse：故意不合作的、不讲理的

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Stubborn Neutral She’s too stubborn to admit her mistake. Descriptive
Defiant Neutral The student was defiant toward the teacher’s rules. Rebellious
Perverse Formal / Negative He had a perverse desire to break the law. Morally judgmental
