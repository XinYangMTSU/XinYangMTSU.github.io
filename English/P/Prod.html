🌟 prod /prɑːd/

Part of speech: Verb or noun

📖 As a verb (action):

To prod means to poke something with a finger or object, or to encourage someone to take action.

Example: She prodded him in the ribs to wake him up.

📖 As a noun (a thing):

A prod is a quick poke or jab, or a stimulus that encourages action.

Example: The teacher gave the sleepy student a gentle prod to keep him alert.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Poke
  • Nudge
  • Jab
  • Push
  • Encourage
  • Urge

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "prod" appeared in English in the late 1600s, possibly from a dialect word meaning "to poke." Its origin is uncertain, but it’s linked to physical jabbing or figurative prompting.

💡 Over time, "prod" expanded to include both physical and motivational meanings, as in prodding someone to act.
