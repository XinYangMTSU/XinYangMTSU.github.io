🌀 pincer-jawed /ˈpɪnsər dʒɔːd/

Part of speech: Adjective (compound)
词性：形容词（复合词）

📖 Meaning:

Pincer-jawed describes an animal or creature that has jaws shaped like pincers—claw-like appendages used for gripping or pinching, often found in insects or crustaceans.
pincer-jawed 指的是“钳形下巴的”，形容某些动物的嘴部像钳子一样可以夹住或夹断，常见于昆虫或甲壳类动物。

Example: The pincer-jawed beetle clamped onto the twig with surprising force.
例句：那只钳形下巴的甲虫用惊人的力量夹住了树枝。
Example: Fossils revealed a pincer-jawed predator from the Cambrian period.
例句：化石揭示了一种寒武纪时期的钳形下颚捕食者。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Claw-jawed 爪形下巴的
  • Mandibular 颚部的
  • Beaked 喙状的
  • Snapping 咬合迅速的
  • Predatory-jawed 掠食型下颚的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Pincer-jawed" is a compound adjective formed from pincer (a gripping tool or claw) and jawed (having jaws). It literally describes an organism with jaws resembling pincers.
pincer-jawed 是一个复合形容词，由 pincer（钳子）和 jawed（有下巴的）组成，用来形容嘴部像钳子一样的生物。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: pinca – claw or instrument
拉丁语 pinça：钳子或工具
⬇️
Old English: ceowan – to chew (origin of "jaw")
古英语 ceowan：咀嚼
⬇️
Modern English: pincer-jawed – having claw-like jaws
现代英语 pincer-jawed：有钳状下巴的
