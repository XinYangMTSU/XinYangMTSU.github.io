⬟ pentagon /ˈpɛntəˌɡɒn/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A flat, two-dimensional shape with five straight sides and five angles; also used to refer to the U.S. Department of Defense building in Washington, D.C.
五边形；也指美国国防部的五边形办公大楼。

Example: A regular pentagon has five equal sides and five equal angles.
例句：一个正五边形有五条相等的边和五个相等的角。
Example: The Pentagon announced a new defense strategy today.
例句：五角大楼今天宣布了一项新的国防战略。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Five-sided shape 五边形
  • Five-angle polygon 五角形多边形
  • (U.S.) Department of Defense （美国）国防部

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Pentagon" comes from Greek penta- meaning "five" and -gon meaning "angle" or "corner".
“Pentagon” 来自希腊语：前缀 penta- 表示“五”，后缀 -gon 表示“角”或“边”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Greek: pentagōnos – five-angled
希腊语 pentagōnos：五个角的

Late Latin: pentagonum – five-sided figure
晚期拉丁语 pentagonum：五边形

Modern English: pentagon – a 2D shape with five sides or the name of the U.S. military headquarters
现代英语 pentagon：五边形；美国国防部
