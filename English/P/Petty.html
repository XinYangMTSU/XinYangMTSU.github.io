🧠 petty /ˈpɛti/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Of little importance; trivial or narrow-minded in behavior.
琐碎的；不重要的；小气的。

Example: They argued over petty details that didn’t matter.
例句：他们为一些无关紧要的小事争吵。
Example: He can be very petty when he doesn’t get his way.
例句：当事情不如他所愿时，他会变得非常小气。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Trivial 琐碎的
  • Minor 次要的
  • Petulant 脾气坏的；易怒的
  • Narrow-minded 心胸狭窄的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Petty" comes from the Middle English word peti, from Old French petit, meaning "small" or "little."
petty 源自中古英语 peti，来自古法语 petit，意为“小的，微不足道的”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old French: petit – small, little
古法语 petit：小的
⬇️
Middle English: peti – trivial or insignificant
中古英语 peti：琐碎的，不重要的
⬇️
Modern English: petty – small-minded or unimportant
现代英语 petty：小气的，不重要的

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Petty Neutral / Slightly Negative She acted petty about who sat in the front seat. Critical / Personal
Trivial Neutral That’s a trivial error and doesn’t affect the result. Objective
Minor Neutral There was a minor delay due to traffic. Formal / Informational
