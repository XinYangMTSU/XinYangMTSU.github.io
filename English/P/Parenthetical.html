🌟 parenthetical /ˌpærənˈθɛtɪkəl/

Part of speech: Adjective

📖 As an adjective:

Parenthetical means something that is said or written in parentheses—or something added as extra information, often interrupting the main flow of speech or writing.

Example: His speech, though long and full of parenthetical remarks, was very informative.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Supplementary
  • Aside
  • Extra
  • Incidental
  • Inserted
  • Interrupting

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "parenthetical".

📝It comes from the Greek word parenthesis, which meant "to put in beside."

  • para- = beside
  • en- = in
  • tithenai = to put

✍️ The modern word "parenthetical" refers to something inserted or placed beside the main part.
