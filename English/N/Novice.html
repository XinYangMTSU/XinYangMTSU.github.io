🧑‍🎓 novice /ˈnɒvɪs/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A novice is someone who is new to a job, skill, or activity and has little experience with it.
novice 指对某项工作、技能或活动还很新、经验很少的人；新手。

Example: As a novice in programming, he found JavaScript challenging at first.
例句：作为编程新手，他一开始觉得 JavaScript 很难。
Example: She's a novice at baking, but she's improving fast.
例句：她是烘焙新手，但进步很快。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Beginner 初学者
  • Newcomer 新来者
  • Rookie 菜鸟
  • Apprentice 学徒
  • Neophyte 新手（正式用语）

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Novice" comes from Latin novicius meaning "new" or "inexperienced," related to novus meaning "new."
novice 源自拉丁语 novicius，意为“新的”或“无经验的”，与 novus（新）相关。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: novicius – new, inexperienced
拉丁语 novicius：新手，无经验者
⬇️
Old French: novice – newly admitted member
古法语 novice：新加入者
⬇️
Modern English: novice – beginner or trainee
现代英语 novice：初学者，实习者

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Beginner Neutral She’s just a beginner at chess. General
Rookie Informal He’s a rookie on the basketball team. Casual / sporty
Novice Formal / Academic Even a novice can learn this quickly. Professional
