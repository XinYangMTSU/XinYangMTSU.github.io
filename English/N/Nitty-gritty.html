⚙️ nitty-gritty /ˌnɪtiˈɡrɪti/

Part of speech: Noun (informal)
词性：名词（非正式）

📖 Meaning:

The most essential and basic aspects of something; the core details that really matter.
基本要点；关键内容；事情的核心。

Example: Let’s skip the small talk and get down to the nitty-gritty.
例句：我们别闲聊了，直接进入正题吧。
Example: The article explains the nitty-gritty of how the system works.
例句：这篇文章解释了该系统的运作核心。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Essence 本质
  • Core 核心
  • Basics 基础
  • Crux 关键

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The exact origin of "nitty-gritty" is unclear. It became popular in American English in the 1940s and is used to refer to the most important or practical aspects of a situation.
“nitty-gritty” 的确切起源不详，20世纪40年代开始在美式英语中流行，用来表示事情最重要或最实用的部分。

🧭 Usage Evolution

1940s American English: Emerged in spoken language to mean practical essentials
20世纪40年代的美式英语：开始用于口语中，表示实质性要点
⬇️
Modern English: Used commonly in business, journalism, and everyday speech
现代英语：常见于商业、新闻和日常对话中

📊 Comparison
