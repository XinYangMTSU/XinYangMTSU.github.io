🗯️ nag /næɡ/

Part of speech: Verb / Noun
词性：动词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

Nag means to repeatedly complain to or criticize someone in an annoying way. As a noun, it can also refer to a person who does this frequently.
“nag” 指的是反复地抱怨或批评某人，让人感到烦恼；也可以作为名词，指经常唠叨的人。

Example: Stop nagging me—I’ll do it later!
例句：别再唠叨我了——我等会儿会做的！
Example: He’s such a nag about cleaning the kitchen.
例句：他总是唠叨厨房要打扫干净。

🧩 Synonyms

  • Pester 纠缠
  • Bother 烦扰
  • Badger 不断纠缠
  • Harass 骚扰
  • Complain 抱怨

🔍 Etymology of the word

📚 "Nag" likely comes from a Scandinavian source such as Old Norse nagga, meaning "to gnaw" or "to nibble," which figuratively became "to nag."
“nag” 很可能来自斯堪的纳维亚语系，如古挪威语 nagga，意为“啃咬”，后来引申为“唠叨”。

📊 Comparison with Related Words

Word Definition Example Context
Nag Repeatedly urge or criticize in an annoying way She kept nagging him to clean his room. Informal, interpersonal
Pester Annoy someone persistently Kids pestered their parents for candy. General annoyance
Badger Repeatedly ask or pressure someone He was badgered by reporters for a comment. Media, pressure
