📖 narrative /ˈnærətɪv/

Part of speech: Noun / Adjective
词性：名词 / 形容词

📖 Meaning:

As a noun: A story or an account of events and experiences, whether true or fictional.
名词：叙述；故事；讲述真实或虚构事件的过程。

As an adjective: Related to the telling of stories.
形容词：叙事的；讲故事的。

Example (noun): The book presents a powerful narrative of survival.
例句（名词）：这本书讲述了一个感人的生存故事。
Example (adjective): The film uses a nonlinear narrative structure.
例句（形容词）：这部电影采用了非线性的叙事结构。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Story 故事
  • Account 叙述
  • Tale 传说
  • Chronicle 编年史

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Narrative" comes from the Latin word narrare, meaning “to tell” or “to relate.” It is also related to gnarus, meaning “knowing.”
“Narrative” 来自拉丁语 narrare，意为“讲述”，与 gnarus（知道的）相关。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: narrare – to tell
拉丁语 narrare：讲述
⬇️
Old French: narratif – telling a story
古法语 narratif：叙述的
⬇️
Modern English: narrative – a spoken or written account
现代英语 narrative：故事或叙述

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Story Informal He told a funny story about his cat. Casual
Account Neutral She gave an account of what happened. Objective
Narrative Formal / Literary The narrative explores themes of identity. Thoughtful
