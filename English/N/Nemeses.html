🗡️ nemeses /ˈnɛmɪsiːz/

Part of speech: Noun (plural of *nemesis*)
词性：名词（*nemesis* 的复数形式）

📖 Meaning:

Nemeses are long-standing enemies, arch-rivals, or sources of downfall and punishment, often representing justice or retribution.
nemeses 指长期的敌人、死对头，或象征正义与报应的对手，通常具有命运般的宿敌意味。

Example: Over the years, the two athletes became fierce nemeses on the field.
例句：多年来，这两位运动员在赛场上成为了激烈的宿敌。
Example: In Greek mythology, nemeses often punished those guilty of arrogance before the gods.
例句：在希腊神话中，Nemesis（复仇女神）通常惩罚那些对神明傲慢的人。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Rivals 对手
  • Opponents 敌手
  • Adversaries 敌人
  • Arch-enemies 死敌
  • Tormentors 折磨者

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 “Nemeses” is the plural of *nemesis*, from Greek Nemesis, the goddess of retribution. In modern usage, it refers to a person or force that is one’s unavoidable rival or doom.
nemeses 是 nemesis（复仇女神）的复数形式，源自希腊语 Nemesis，象征惩罚与报应。现代用法中指无法避免的敌人或失败的源头。

🧭 Word Evolution

Greek: Nemesis – goddess of vengeance
希腊语 Nemesis：复仇女神
⬇️
Latin: Nemesis – retribution
拉丁语 Nemesis：惩罚、天谴
⬇️
Modern English: Nemeses – rivals or sources of downfall
现代英语 nemeses：死敌、失败的象征
