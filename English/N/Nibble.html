🌟 nibble /ˈnɪb(ə)l/

Part of speech: Verb or noun

📖 As a verb (action):

To nibble means to take small, gentle bites of something, often in a cautious or playful way.

Example: She nibbled at a piece of cheese while reading.

📖 As a noun (a thing):

A nibble is a small bite or a tiny amount of food, especially as a snack.

Example: He took a nibble of the cookie and smiled.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Chew (lightly)
  • Bite
  • Sip (for drinks)
  • Munch (softly)
  • Taste
  • Graze

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s explore the origin of "nibble".

📝Probably from Low German nibbeln, meaning “to gnaw or bite lightly.”

💡 Related to “nib” (like the nib of a pen), which also refers to a small pointed part—just like a small bite.
