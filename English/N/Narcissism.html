🌟 narcissism /ˈnɑːrsəˌsɪzəm/

Part of speech: Noun

📖 Meaning:

Narcissism is the excessive love or admiration of oneself. It can describe a personality trait or, in extreme forms, a mental condition.

Example: His constant need for praise and admiration was a sign of deep narcissism.

🧠 In Psychology:

Psychologists use the term narcissism to describe a trait or disorder marked by:

  • A sense of superiority
  • A need for admiration
  • A lack of empathy

Example: Narcissistic Personality Disorder (NPD) affects how a person thinks about themselves and others.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Self-love
  • Egocentrism
  • Vanity
  • Arrogance
  • Egoism
  • Self-absorption

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin of the word "narcissism".

📝 It comes from the Greek myth of Narcissus, a young man who fell in love with his own reflection.

  • Greek: Narkissos (Νάρκισσος)
  • Latin: Narcissus

💡 The word "narcissism" was coined in the early 20th century by psychologists like Freud to describe extreme self-obsession.
