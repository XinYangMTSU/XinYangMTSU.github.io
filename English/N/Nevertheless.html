🔁 nevertheless /ˌnevərðəˈles/

Part of speech: Adverb
词性：副词

📖 Meaning:

In spite of something that has just been mentioned; however; still.
尽管如此；不过；然而。

Example: It was raining; nevertheless, they went for a hike.
例句：下着雨；尽管如此，他们还是去远足了。
Example: The task was difficult; nevertheless, she completed it on time.
例句：任务很艰巨；不过，她还是按时完成了。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • However 然而
  • Nonetheless 尽管如此
  • Even so 即便如此
  • Still 仍然

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 “Nevertheless” is a compound of “never,” “the,” and “less,” originally used in the 14th century as a single word meaning “not less for that.”
“nevertheless” 是由 “never”（从不）、“the”和“less”（较少）构成的复合词，起源于14世纪，原意为“即使如此也不少”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Middle English: nevertheless – in spite of that
中古英语 nevertheless：尽管如此
⬇️
Modern English: nevertheless – used to introduce a contrast
现代英语 nevertheless：用于引出对比或转折

📊 Comparison

Word Tone Example Use Formality
Nevertheless Neutral / Formal It was late; nevertheless, she kept working. Formal / Academic
However Neutral He wanted to come. However, he was busy. General
Still Informal It’s risky. Still, I’ll try it. Conversational
