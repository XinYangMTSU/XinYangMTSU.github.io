🔤 non-literal /ˌnɑːnˈlɪt̬.ɚ.əl/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Non-literal means not using words in their most basic or usual sense. It refers to expressions that are figurative or symbolic rather than exact.
non-literal（非字面的）指不按照词语最基本或直接含义来使用，通常是比喻、象征或引申意义。

Example: When she said “I’m drowning in work,” she meant it in a non-literal way.
例句：她说“我快被工作淹没了”时，是在用非字面的方式表达。
Example: Poems often contain non-literal language that needs interpretation.
例句：诗歌常常包含需要解读的非字面语言。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Figurative 比喻的
  • Metaphorical 隐喻的
  • Symbolic 象征的
  • Imaginative 富有想象的
  • Abstract 抽象的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Non-literal" is formed from the prefix non- meaning "not" + literal, which comes from Latin littera meaning “letter.”
“non-literal”由“non-（非）”和“literal（字面的）”构成，literal 来自拉丁语 littera，意为“字母”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: littera – letter
拉丁语 littera：字母
⬇️
English: literal – following the exact words
英语 literal：字面的，逐字的
⬇️
English: non-literal – figurative, symbolic
英语 non-literal：非字面的，比喻的
