🌟 unravel /ʌnˈrævəl/

Part of speech: Verb

📖 Meaning:

To unravel something means to undo twisted, knitted, or woven threads. Figuratively, it can also mean to solve or explain something complex, or to fall apart (like a plan, system, or situation).

Example (literal): She began to unravel the knitted scarf to reuse the yarn.
Example (figurative): The mystery slowly unraveled as the detective found new clues.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Untangle
  • Undo
  • Solve
  • Decipher
  • Disentangle
  • Collapse (figurative)

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word “unravel” is formed from:

  • un- = a prefix meaning "reverse or remove"
  • ravel = an older word meaning to tangle or entangle

💡 Together, unravel means to undo what has been tangled or woven.

🧭 Word Evolution

Middle English: unravelen
⬇️
Modern English: unravel
