🔓 unshackle /ʌnˈʃækəl/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To set free or release from shackles, chains, or restrictions; to liberate.
解放；使摆脱束缚或限制。

Example: The reforms were meant to unshackle the economy from outdated policies.
例句：这些改革旨在将经济从过时政策的束缚中解放出来。
Example: The prisoner was unshackled before entering the courtroom.
例句：囚犯在进入法庭前被解开了镣铐。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Free 释放
  • Liberate 解放
  • Release 释放
  • Emancipate 解放（尤指奴隶）

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Unshackle" is formed by adding the prefix un- (meaning “not” or “opposite of”) to the noun shackle (a chain or restraint). It literally means to remove shackles.
“Unshackle” 由前缀 un-（表示“去除”）和名词 shackle（镣铐、束缚）组成，字面意思是“去掉镣铐”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Middle English: shakel – restraint or fetter
中古英语 shakel：束缚、枷锁

Modern English: unshackle – to free from chains or restrictions
现代英语 unshackle：解除束缚或限制
