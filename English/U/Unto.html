📜 unto /ˈʌn.tuː/

Part of speech: Preposition (literary or archaic)
词性：介词（文学或古语用法）

📖 Meaning:

Unto means “to” or “toward,” and is mostly used in older English texts like the Bible or poetry. It is rarely used in modern everyday speech.
unto 意为“到、向”，多用于圣经或文学作品中，是现代英语中较少使用的古老用法。

Example: Do unto others as you would have them do unto you.
例句：你希望别人怎样对你，你就怎样对待别人。
Example: The king spoke unto his people with great solemnity.
例句：国王庄严地向人民发表讲话。

🧩 Related Words /rɪˈleɪtɪd/

  • To 向、到
  • Toward 朝向
  • Upon 在...之上
  • With 与、和
  • Into 进入

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Unto" is a compound of “un-” (a variant of “on”) and “to,” used in Middle English and older forms of English to show direction, relation, or purpose.
unto 是“un-”（“on”的变体）与“to”的组合，用于中世纪英语和更古老的英语中，表示方向、关系或目的。
