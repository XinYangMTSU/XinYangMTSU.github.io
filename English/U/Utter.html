🗣️ utter /ˈʌtər/

Part of speech: Verb / Adjective
词性：动词 / 形容词

📖 Meaning:

As a verb: To say something or make a sound using the voice.
动词：说出；发出（声音）。

As an adjective: Complete; total; absolute.
形容词：完全的；彻底的；绝对的。

Example (verb): She didn’t utter a single word during dinner.
例句（动词）：她晚餐时一句话也没说。
Example (adjective): It was utter nonsense to believe the rumor.
例句（形容词）：相信那个谣言简直是胡说八道。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Speak 说话
  • Say 说出
  • Express 表达
  • Absolute (for adjective) 绝对的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Utter" comes from Old English uttera meaning “outer” or “extreme.” As a verb, it came to mean “to send out” or “to speak,” and as an adjective, “complete or total.”
“Utter” 源自古英语 uttera，意为“外部的”或“极端的”。动词义发展为“发出”或“说出”，形容词义为“完全的”或“彻底的”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: uttera – outer, extreme
古英语 uttera：外部的，极端的
⬇️
Middle English: Verb meaning “to say aloud”
中古英语：动词义为“说出来”
⬇️
Modern English: utter – to speak; complete
现代英语 utter：动词“说出”；形容词“完全的”
