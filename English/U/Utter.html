🌟 utter /ˈʌtər/

Part of speech: Verb or adjective

📖 As a verb (action):

To utter something means to say it or express it in words, especially out loud or formally.

Example: She didn’t utter a single word during the meeting.

📖 As an adjective (describing):

As an adjective, utter means complete or absolute, often used for emphasis.

Example: That’s utter nonsense!

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Speak
  • Say
  • Express
  • Voice
  • Complete
  • Absolute

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The verb utter comes from Middle English uteren, from Old English ūter, meaning "outer" or "to put forth."

💡 Over time, it evolved to mean “to speak out” or “to say aloud.” As an adjective, it kept the sense of “outermost,” which later became “complete” or “absolute.”

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: ūter (outer) /ˈuː.tɛr/
⬇️
Middle English: uteren (to say or express)
⬇️
Modern English: utter /ˈʌtər/
