🪜 unwieldy /ʌnˈwiːldi/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Too large, heavy, or complicated to handle easily.
笨重的；难以控制的；难以使用或处理的。

Example: The box was so unwieldy that it took two people to move it.
例句：这个箱子太笨重了，需要两个人才能搬动。
Example: The new regulations created an unwieldy legal system.
例句：新规造成了一个难以操作的法律体系。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Bulky 笨重的
  • Cumbersome 难处理的
  • Awkward 不方便的
  • Heavy 沉重的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Unwieldy" comes from the prefix un- (not) and the Old English wieldan, meaning “to handle or control.”
unwieldy 源自前缀 un-（不）和古英语 wieldan，意为“掌控、操作”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: wieldan – to handle, control
古英语 wieldan：掌控、操作
⬇️
Middle English: unweldy – unmanageable
中古英语 unweldy：难以处理的
⬇️
Modern English: unwieldy – awkward or hard to manage
现代英语 unwieldy：笨重的、难控制的

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Unwieldy Neutral The unwieldy machine blocked the hallway. Descriptive / Practical
Cumbersome Formal The form was too cumbersome to complete quickly. Technical / Bureaucratic
Awkward Neutral / Informal The tool’s shape made it awkward to use. Everyday / Casual
