🔗 unified /ˈjuːnɪfaɪd/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Formed or united into a whole; made consistent or coherent.
统一的；联合的；一致的。

Example: The country was unified under a new government.
例句：这个国家在新政府下实现了统一。
Example: They presented a unified message to the media.
例句：他们向媒体传达了一致的信息。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • United 团结的
  • Harmonized 协调一致的
  • Integrated 整合的
  • Consolidated 合并的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Unified" is the past participle of the verb "unify," which comes from Latin unus (one) + facere (to make). It literally means “to make one.”
“Unified” 是动词 unify（统一）的过去分词，源自拉丁语 unus（一个）和 facere（做），意为“使成为一个整体”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: unificare – to make one
拉丁语 unificare：使合为一体

French: unifier – to unite
法语 unifier：统一

Modern English: unify / unified – to bring parts together as a whole
现代英语 unify / unified：使整体一致

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone

Integrated Formal The system is fully integrated across departments. Technical
