📊 quota /ˈkwoʊtə/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A fixed amount or number that is officially allowed, expected, or required; a share or portion assigned to someone or something.
定额；配额；规定数量；分配的份额。

Example: The factory exceeded its monthly production quota.
例句：这家工厂超额完成了本月的生产配额。
Example: Each student must read a quota of five books per semester.
例句：每位学生每学期必须阅读五本书作为任务量。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Allocation 分配额
  • Portion 份额
  • Limit 限制
  • Share 份儿

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Quota" comes from the Latin word quota pars, meaning “how large a part.” It was first used in English in the 17th century to refer to a proportional part or share.
“Quota” 源自拉丁语 quota pars，意为“某部分的大小”，17世纪进入英语，表示“比例份额”或“定额”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: quota pars – how large a part
拉丁语 quota pars：某部分的大小

English (17th century): quota – assigned portion
17世纪英语 quota：被分配的份额

Modern Usage: a required or allowed number or portion
现代含义：规定或允许的数量/份额

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Allocation Formal The team received a new budget allocation. Administrative
Share Neutral She took her share of the prize money. Neutral
Quota Formal They set a quota for daily production. Regulatory / Managerial
