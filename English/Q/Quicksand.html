🏜️ quicksand /ˈkwɪkˌsænd/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A loose, wet area of sand that sucks down objects or people, making it difficult to escape; also used metaphorically to describe a dangerous or unstable situation.
一种松软湿滑的沙地，会吞没人或物体，使其难以逃脱；也可比喻为危险或不稳定的处境。

Example: The hiker nearly got trapped in quicksand near the river.
例句：那位徒步者差点在河边陷入流沙中。
Example: The political scandal became a quicksand of lies and cover-ups.
例句：那起政治丑闻成了谎言与掩盖的流沙。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Sinkhole 天坑
  • Trap 陷阱
  • Danger zone 危险区域
  • Unstable ground 不稳定地面

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Quicksand" comes from Old English cwic (meaning "alive") + sand, referring to sand that seems to move or "live".
quicksand 来自古英语 cwic（意为“活着的”）+ sand，指看起来会动的沙子。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: cwic + sand – living sand
古英语 cwic + sand：活沙
⬇️
Middle English: quykesand – unstable sand
中古英语 quykesand：不稳定的沙地
⬇️
Modern English: quicksand – sinking sand; dangerous situation
现代英语 quicksand：流沙；危险情境
