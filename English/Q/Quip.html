💬 quip /kwɪp/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

A short, clever, and often witty remark; to make a quick, humorous comment.
妙语；俏皮话；风趣的评论。动词形式表示说妙语或俏皮话。

Example: She ended her speech with a quick quip that made the audience laugh.
例句：她用一句俏皮话结束了演讲，逗得观众哈哈大笑。
Example: "At least I didn't trip this time," he quipped after his speech.
例句：“至少这次我没摔倒。”他开完演讲后幽默地说道。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Witticism 俏皮话
  • Joke 笑话
  • One-liner 一语中的的妙语
  • Retort 反驳妙语

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Quip" originated in the 16th century, from Latin quippe, meaning "indeed" or "to be sure"—often used sarcastically. Over time, it evolved into a term for sharp or clever remarks.
“Quip” 起源于16世纪，来自拉丁语 quippe，意为“确实、的确”，常带有讽刺意味，后逐渐演变为指风趣、机智的评论。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: quippe – indeed, for sure (often sarcastic)
拉丁语 quippe：确实、的确（常含讽刺）

English (16th century): quip – sharp or clever saying
16世纪英语 quip：机智、风趣的说法

Modern: Quip – humorous or witty remark
现代英语 quip：幽默机智的评论
