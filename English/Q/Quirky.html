🎭 quirky /ˈkwɜːr.ki/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Quirky means unusual in an interesting or charming way—something that is different, but in a fun, often lovable way.
quirky 指的是“古怪但可爱/有趣的”，带有一点不寻常但吸引人的意味。

Example: She has a quirky sense of humor that makes everyone laugh.
例句：她有一种古灵精怪的幽默感，总是逗得大家大笑。
Example: The café has a quirky design with mismatched chairs and painted ceilings.
例句：这家咖啡馆的设计很有趣，椅子不成对，天花板也被涂上了彩画。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Whimsical 异想天开的
  • Eccentric 古怪的
  • Unconventional 不拘一格的
  • Peculiar 独特的
  • Charming 迷人的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "quirky" comes from the noun quirk, which originally meant a sudden twist or curve, and later came to mean an unusual feature or trait.
“quirky” 源自名词 “quirk”，最初意思是“突然的转折”或“弯曲”，后来引申为“古怪特征”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Early English: quirk – twist, odd behavior
早期英语 quirk：转折，怪癖
⬇️
Modern English: quirky – playfully unusual or different
现代英语 quirky：古灵精怪，有趣的不同寻常

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Weird Informal That movie was really weird. Strange / negative
Whimsical Literary The book has a whimsical style. Creative / fanciful
Quirky Neutral / Positive He has a quirky way of solving problems. Playful / endearing
