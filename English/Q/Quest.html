🛡️ quest /kwest/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

Quest means:

  1. (n.) A long or difficult search for something important.
  2. (v.) To go on a journey in search of something.
quest 有两个常见含义：
1.（名词）对某种重要事物的长期或艰难追寻；
2.（动词）寻找，探求。

Example: The knight set off on a quest to find the lost sword.
例句：骑士踏上寻找失落之剑的征途。
Example: Scientists are on a quest to cure rare diseases.
例句：科学家们正致力于攻克罕见疾病。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Search 寻找
  • Pursuit 追求
  • Journey 旅程
  • Expedition 探险
  • Mission 任务

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Quest" comes from Latin quaerere, meaning “to seek” or “to ask.” It entered English through Old French queste meaning “search or inquiry.”
“quest” 源自拉丁语 quaerere，意为“寻找、询问”，通过古法语 queste 演变而来，意思是“寻找”或“探究”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: quaerere – to seek
拉丁语 quaerere：寻找
⬇️
Old French: queste – a search or inquiry
古法语 queste：探求
⬇️
English: quest – a journey or search for something valuable
现代英语 quest：对某个重要事物的寻找

📊 Comparison with Related Words

Word Definition Example Tone
Search Trying to find something They searched the forest for clues. Neutral
Expedition A planned journey for exploration The team went on an expedition to the Arctic. Formal / adventurous
Quest A noble or difficult journey to seek something valuable The hero began his quest for truth. Elevated / epic
