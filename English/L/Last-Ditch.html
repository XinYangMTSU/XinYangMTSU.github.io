🛑 last-ditch /ˌlæst ˈdɪtʃ/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Used to describe a final attempt to achieve something when everything else has failed.
（表示）最后一搏的；孤注一掷的；背水一战的。

Example: They launched a last-ditch effort to save the company from bankruptcy.
例句：他们发起了最后一搏，试图挽救公司免于破产。
Example: It was a last-ditch attempt to win the game.
例句：这是赢得比赛的最后一次尝试。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Final effort 最后的努力
  • Desperate attempt 孤注一掷
  • Ultimate try 最终尝试
  • Back-against-the-wall action 走投无路时的行动

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Last-ditch" originally referred to the **final defensive trench** in a battle. The phrase evolved to mean a **final, desperate effort** before total defeat.
“Last-ditch” 最初指战斗中最后一道防线（战壕），后来引申为在彻底失败前的最后努力。

🧭 Word Evolution

Literal: The last ditch in a battlefield
字面意义：战场上的最后一道战壕
⬇️
Figurative (18th century): Final attempt before surrender
引申义（18世纪）：投降前的最后尝试
⬇️
Modern English: Desperate, final effort
现代英语：孤注一掷的努力

📊 Comparison

Phrase Formality Level Example Use Tone
Desperate attempt Neutral She made a desperate attempt to catch the train. Urgent
Final effort Formal This is our final effort to resolve the issue. Determined
Last-ditch Vivid / Figurative They made a last-ditch attempt to escape. Emotional
