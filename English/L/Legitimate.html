✅ legitimate /ləˈdʒɪtɪmət/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Allowed by law or rules; reasonable and acceptable; genuine or real.
合法的；正当的；合理的；真实的。

Example: He has a legitimate claim to the property.
例句：他对这块财产有合法的权利。
Example: That's a legitimate concern — we need to consider it carefully.
例句：那是个合理的担忧——我们需要认真考虑。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Lawful 合法的
  • Valid 有效的；有根据的
  • Genuine 真实的
  • Justified 有正当理由的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Legitimate" comes from Latin legitimus, meaning “lawful,” which in turn derives from lex, meaning “law.”
legitimate 来自拉丁语 legitimus，意思是“合法的”，源自 lex，意思是“法律”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: lex – law
拉丁语 lex：法律
⬇️
Latin: legitimus – lawful, proper
拉丁语 legitimus：合法的，正当的
⬇️
Modern English: legitimate – lawful, valid, reasonable
现代英语 legitimate：合法的，正当的，合理的

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Lawful Formal They followed the lawful procedure. Legal
Valid Neutral The ticket is valid for 30 days. Technical
Legitimate Formal He made a legitimate request for more time. Respectful / Legal
