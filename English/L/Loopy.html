🌟 loopy /ˈluːpi/

Part of speech: Adjective

📖 Meaning #1: Silly or Slightly Crazy

“Loopy” means acting a little weird, silly, or crazy—often because someone is tired, excited, or confused.

Example: After studying all night, he was feeling a bit loopy.

📖 Meaning #2: Full of Loops

Describes something that has loops, curls, or swirls in shape or design.

Example: She signed her name in big loopy letters.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Goofy
  • Zany
  • Dizzy
  • Crazy
  • Whimsical
  • Quirky

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s explore where "loopy" comes from.

📝Derived from the word “loop” + suffix -y, meaning "having the characteristics of".

💡 In the 20th century, “loopy” evolved into slang for acting a bit crazy—perhaps like someone going in mental loops.

🧭 Word Evolution

Middle English: loupe (a loop or bend)
⬇️
Modern English: loop ➝ loopy (with loops / acting silly)
⬇️
