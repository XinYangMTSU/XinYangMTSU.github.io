😖 loathing /ˈloʊðɪŋ/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

Loathing means a strong feeling of hatred or disgust toward someone or something.
loathing 指对某人或某事的强烈厌恶或憎恨。

Example: She felt nothing but loathing for the corrupt politician.
例句：她对那个腐败的政客只有厌恶。
Example: His loathing of violence was evident in his writing.
例句：他对暴力的憎恨在他的作品中显而易见。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Hatred 仇恨
  • Disgust 厌恶
  • Aversion 反感
  • Detestation 憎恶
  • Repulsion 排斥感

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Loathing" comes from Old English lāðian, meaning “to be hateful” or “to cause hatred.” Related to the word loath (reluctant or unwilling).
“loathing” 源自古英语 lāðian，意为“令人憎恶”或“引起厌恶”。与 loath（不情愿）有关。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: lāðian – to be hateful
古英语 lāðian：令人憎恶

Middle English: lothen – to detest
中古英语 lothen：憎恶

Modern English: loathing – intense hatred or disgust
现代英语 loathing：强烈的憎恨或厌恶
