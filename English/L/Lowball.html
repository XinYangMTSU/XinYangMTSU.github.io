💰 lowball /ˈloʊˌbɔːl/

Part of speech: Verb / Noun
词性：动词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

(Verb) To offer a price or estimate that is unfairly or intentionally lower than what is reasonable or expected. (Noun) An unreasonably low offer or estimate.
（动词）故意压低报价；低估。
（名词）低价；过低的出价。

Example: They tried to lowball me during salary negotiations.
例句：他们在薪资谈判中故意压低了报价。
Example: The buyer made a lowball offer far below market value.
例句：买家给出的出价远低于市场价。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Underbid 压价
  • Undervalue 低估
  • Cheapen 贬值
  • Shortchange 少给钱

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Lowball" originated in American English in the mid-20th century, combining "low" (meaning small or below average) and "ball" (possibly from ballpark estimates). It was used to describe a negotiation tactic of offering less than fair value.
“Lowball” 起源于20世纪中期的美式英语，由“low”（低的）与“ball”（可能源自估价术语“ballpark”）组成，用于描述故意报低价的谈判策略。

🧭 Word Evolution

English: low – small, below average
英语 low：低的，低于平均

English: ball (as in ballpark estimate) – general guess or offer
英语 ball：用于表达粗略估价

Modern English: lowball – deliberately offer too low a value
现代英语 lowball：故意压低报价
