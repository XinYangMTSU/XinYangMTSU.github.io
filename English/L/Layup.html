🌟 Layup /ˈleɪ.ʌp/

Part of speech: Noun

📖 Meaning:

A layup is a type of basketball shot made from close range, usually by gently banking the ball off the backboard into the basket. It is considered one of the easiest and most fundamental shots in the game.

Example: The player drove past the defender and finished with a smooth layup.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Easy shot
  • Bank shot
  • Close-range basket

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word layup comes from the verb phrase "lay up," meaning to place or lift something up gently. In basketball, it refers to softly laying the ball into the hoop.

🧭 Word Evolution

English: lay up → to place up gently
⬇️
Modern Basketball: layup = close, easy shot /ˈleɪ.ʌp/
