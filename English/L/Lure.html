🎣 lure /lʊr/

Part of speech: Verb / Noun
词性：动词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

As a verb, lure means to tempt or attract someone, often by offering something appealing. As a noun, it refers to the thing used to tempt someone or something.
“lure” 作为动词时，意思是引诱或吸引某人，常通过提供诱人的东西；作为名词时，指用来引诱的东西。

Example: Advertisements are designed to lure customers into buying more.
例句：广告的目的是引诱顾客购买更多商品。
Example: The fish took the lure and got caught.
例句：那条鱼咬了诱饵，结果被钓上来了。

🧩 Synonyms

  • Tempt 诱惑
  • Entice 引诱
  • Attract 吸引
  • Seduce 勾引
  • Bait 诱饵

🔍 Etymology of the word

📚 “Lure” comes from Old French *lurer*, meaning “to attract birds with a decoy,” originally from a Germanic root related to calling or whistling.
“lure” 源自古法语 *lurer*，原指用诱饵吸引鸟类，词根与“呼唤”或“吹口哨”相关，源自日耳曼语。

📊 Comparison with Related Words

Word Definition Example Context
Lure To attract or tempt She tried to lure him with promises of success. Advertising, hunting, manipulation
Tempt To try to convince someone to do something, especially wrong He was tempted to cheat on the exam. Moral, emotional
