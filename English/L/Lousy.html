🌀 lousy /ˈlaʊzi/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Lousy means very bad, unpleasant, or of poor quality. It can also be used to express being overwhelmed or full of something.
lousy 的意思是“糟糕的、令人不快的”，也可以表示“充满某物”。

Example: I felt lousy after staying up all night studying.
例句：熬夜学习后我感觉很糟糕。
Example: The service at that restaurant was absolutely lousy.
例句：那家餐厅的服务实在太差了。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Terrible 可怕的
  • Awful 糟糕的
  • Rotten 恶劣的
  • Dreadful 可怕的
  • Crummy 差劲的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Lousy" originally meant "infested with lice," from the word "louse." Over time, it evolved to mean something dirty, bad, or unpleasant.
lousy 最初的意思是“长虱子的”，来自 louse（一种寄生虫），后来演变为“糟糕的、不舒服的”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: lūs – louse
古英语 lūs：虱子
⬇️
Middle English: lousi – full of lice
中古英语 lousi：布满虱子的
⬇️
Modern English: lousy – bad, awful
现代英语 lousy：糟糕的、恶劣的
