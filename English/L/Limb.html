🦵 limb /lɪm/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A limb is an arm, leg, or similar appendage of a person or animal; also a large branch of a tree.
肢体（如手臂、腿）；（树的）大枝干。

Example: The injury left him unable to move his lower limbs.
例句：受伤使他无法移动下肢。
Example: The squirrel jumped from limb to limb in the tree.
例句：松鼠在树枝之间跳跃。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Arm 手臂
  • Leg 腿
  • Branch 树枝
  • Appendage 附属肢体

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Limb" comes from Old English lim, meaning joint or member, and is related to words in Germanic and Scandinavian languages for arms and legs.
“Limb” 源自古英语 lim，意为关节或身体部位，与日耳曼语和斯堪的纳维亚语中表示手脚的词相关。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: lim – joint or body part
古英语 lim：关节或身体部位

Old Norse: limr – limb
古挪威语 limr：四肢

Modern English: limb – arm, leg, or tree branch
现代英语 limb：四肢或大树枝

📊 Comparison
