🧍 laypeople /ˈleɪˌpiːpəl/

Part of speech: Noun (plural)
词性：名词（复数）

📖 Meaning:

Laypeople refers to people who are not experts or professionals in a particular subject or field. It contrasts with specialists or experts.
laypeople 指的是非专业人士或非专家，与某一领域的专家或专业人员相对。

Example: The guide explained the science in a way that laypeople could understand.
例句：导游用普通人也能理解的方式讲解了这项科学。
Example: To laypeople, the technical jargon sounded confusing.
例句：对非专业人士来说，那些术语听起来很让人困惑。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Non-experts 非专家
  • General public 大众
  • Outsiders 外行人
  • Novices 新手
  • Amateurs 业余爱好者

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Laypeople" is the plural of "layperson," from the word "lay," which comes from Old French lai, from Latin laicus, meaning "of the people, not clergy." It refers to someone outside a profession, originally used in a religious context.
laypeople 是 layperson 的复数形式。lay 来自古法语 lai，源于拉丁语 laicus，意为“世俗的、非神职人员的”，最初用于宗教语境，后来泛指非专业人士。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: laicus – of the people, not clergy
拉丁语 laicus：世俗的，非神职的
⬇️
Old French: lai – lay, not a priest or monk
古法语 lai：俗人，非修道者
⬇️
Modern English: layperson / laypeople – someone outside a professional or expert group
现代英语 layperson / laypeople：非专家，外行人
