🌫️ loomed /luːmd/

Part of speech: Verb (past tense)
词性：动词（过去式）

📖 Meaning:

Appeared as a large, shadowy, or threatening form; to seem imminent or about to happen in a worrying way.
隐约显现；赫然耸现；（尤指不祥之事）迫近。

Example: A dark shape loomed in the distance through the fog.
例句：一个黑影在雾中远处若隐若现。
Example: As the deadline loomed, the team worked late into the night.
例句：随着截止日期临近，团队加班加点工作。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Approached 接近
  • Emerged 浮现
  • Threatened 威胁
  • Hovered 徘徊

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Loom" (as a verb) comes from Middle English lome, meaning “to appear indistinctly,” possibly of Scandinavian origin.
“Loom”（动词）源自中古英语 lome，意为“模糊地出现”，可能起源于斯堪的纳维亚语言。

🧭 Word Evolution

Middle English: lome – to appear, come into view
中古英语 lome：显现

Modern English: loom – to appear in a threatening or large form
现代英语 loom：以可怕或模糊的形态显现

📊 Comparison

Word Context Example Use Tone
Emerged Neutral / Descriptive A figure emerged from the shadows. Neutral
Threatened Negative / Urgent The storm threatened the coastal village. Ominous
Loomed Descriptive / Tense The mountain loomed above them. Dark / Foreboding
