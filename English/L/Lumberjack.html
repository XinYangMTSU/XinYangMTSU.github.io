🌲 lumberjack /ˈlʌmbərˌdʒæk/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A person whose job is to cut down trees and transport the timber; a logger.
伐木工人；樵夫。

Example: The lumberjack worked long hours in the forest, cutting and stacking logs.
例句：这位伐木工人长时间在森林里工作，砍伐并堆放木材。
Example: He dressed like a classic lumberjack, wearing a plaid shirt and heavy boots.
例句：他穿着格子衬衫和厚靴子，一副典型伐木工人的打扮。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Logger 伐木工
  • Woodsman 林务工人
  • Timber cutter 砍伐者
  • Forester 森林工作者

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Lumberjack" originated in North America in the 19th century, from "lumber" (wood cut for building) and "jack" (a generic term for man or worker).
“Lumberjack” 源于19世纪的北美，由“lumber”（建筑用木材）和“jack”（泛指工人）组成。

🧭 Word Evolution

English: lumber – timber prepared for use
英语 lumber：可用的木材

English: jack – a man, worker, or laborer
英语 jack：男人或工人

Modern English: lumberjack – person who fells and transports trees
现代英语 lumberjack：砍伐并运输树木的工人
