📘 lexicon /ˈlɛksɪˌkɒn/

Part of speech: Noun

📖 Meaning:

A lexicon is the vocabulary of a language, a subject, or a group of people. It can also mean a dictionary, especially for ancient or foreign languages.

Example: The medical lexicon includes many Latin-based terms unfamiliar to non-specialists.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Vocabulary
  • Word list
  • Terminology
  • Glossary
  • Dictionary
  • Word bank

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "lexicon".

📝It comes from the Greek word lexikon, meaning "book of words."

  • lexis = word or speech (Greek)
  • -ikon = related to (Greek suffix)

💡 Over time, it came to mean "dictionary" or "vocabulary set" in Latin, then into English in the early 1600s.
