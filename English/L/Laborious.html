🛠️ laborious /ləˈbɔːriəs/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Laborious describes something that requires a lot of effort, time, or hard work, especially something physically or mentally exhausting.
laborious 意为“辛苦的，费力的”，常指需要大量体力或脑力的事情。

Example: Writing the report was a long and laborious task.
例句：撰写这份报告是一项漫长而辛苦的任务。
Example: He made a laborious effort to finish the assignment before the deadline.
例句：他努力不懈地完成了截止日前的作业。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Arduous 艰巨的
  • Strenuous 费力的
  • Hard 困难的
  • Demanding 要求高的
  • Exhausting 令人疲惫的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Laborious" comes from Latin laboriosus, meaning "toilsome" or "full of labor," from labor (work, toil).
laborious 源自拉丁语 laboriosus，意为“辛苦的、充满劳作的”，来自 labor（劳动，辛苦）。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: laboriosus – full of labor
拉丁语 laboriosus：充满劳作
⬇️
Old French: laborieux – requiring hard work
古法语 laborieux：辛苦的，劳累的
⬇️
Modern English: laborious – involving great effort
现代英语 laborious：需要努力的

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Hard Casual That was a hard job to finish. Simple / direct
Arduous Formal The hike was long and arduous. Serious / descriptive
Laborious Formal / Descriptive It was a slow and laborious process. Detailed / precise
