🌟 gambit /ˈɡæmbɪt/

Part of speech: Noun

📖 Meaning:

A gambit is a clever or strategic move made at the beginning of something—especially to gain an advantage. It’s often used in chess, politics, business, or conversation.

Example: Her opening question was a gambit to start a friendly conversation.
Example: The company’s new ad campaign was a risky gambit to regain market share.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Strategy
  • Move
  • Opening
  • Tactic
  • Scheme
  • Initiative

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "gambit" originally came from the world of chess.

📝 It comes from Spanish gambito → from Italian gambetto = tripping someone up with the leg (a wrestling move).

  • gamba = leg (in Italian)

✍️ In chess, a gambit is when a player sacrifices a piece (like a pawn) early in the game to gain a better position. Over time, the word came to mean any clever beginning move in strategy or conversation.
