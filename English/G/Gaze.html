👀 gaze /ɡeɪz/

Part of speech: Verb / Noun
词性：动词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

As a verb: To look steadily and intently, often with admiration, wonder, or deep thought.
动词：凝视；目不转睛地看，通常带有敬意、惊叹或沉思。

As a noun: A steady, fixed look; an intent stare.
名词：凝视；专注的目光。

Example (verb): She gazed at the stars, lost in thought.
例句（动词）：她凝望着星空，陷入沉思。
Example (noun): His gaze was fixed on the painting for several minutes.
例句（名词）：他的目光在那幅画上停留了好几分钟。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Stare 凝视
  • Look 看
  • Watch 注视
  • Observe 观察

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Gaze" likely comes from the Middle English gasen, meaning “to stare” or “to look with intent,” of Scandinavian origin.
“Gaze” 很可能源自中古英语 gasen，意为“凝视”或“专注地看”，起源于斯堪的纳维亚语言。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old Norse: gāsa – to gape, stare
古诺斯语 gāsa：张口凝视

Middle English: gasen – to look with attention
中古英语 gasen：注视

Modern English: gaze – fixed, steady look
现代英语 gaze：凝视，目不转睛地看

📊 Comparison
