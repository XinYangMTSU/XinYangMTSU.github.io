😬 grimace /ˈɡrɪm.əs/ or /ɡrɪˈmeɪs/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

As a noun: A facial expression showing discomfort, pain, or disapproval.
名词：因痛苦、不满或厌恶而扭曲的表情；鬼脸。

As a verb: To make such a facial expression.
动词：做出痛苦或厌恶的表情；皱眉、做鬼脸。

Example (noun): He made a grimace when he tasted the sour lemon.
例句（名词）：他尝了那颗酸柠檬后做了个鬼脸。
Example (verb): She grimaced in pain as the doctor cleaned the wound.
例句（动词）：医生清理伤口时，她痛得皱起了眉头。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Frown 皱眉
  • Scowl 怒容
  • Wince 龇牙咧嘴
  • Sneer 冷笑

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Grimace" comes from Middle French grimace, meaning a “caricature face” or a distorted facial expression. It’s related to the Old High German word grīmen, meaning “to mask or cover the face.”
“Grimace” 源自中古法语 grimace，意为“扭曲的脸”或“夸张的表情”；与古高地德语 grīmen（遮面、戴面具）相关。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old High German: grīmen – to mask
古高地德语 grīmen：遮住面部

Middle French: grimace – exaggerated or distorted face
中古法语 grimace：夸张的表情

Modern English: grimace – twisted expression of pain or disgust
现代英语 grimace：因痛苦或厌恶而扭曲的表情

📊 Comparison

Word Part of Speech Emotion Intensity

Grimace Verb / Noun Pain, disgust, discomfort Moderate to Strong
