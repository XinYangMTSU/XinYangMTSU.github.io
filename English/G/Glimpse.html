👀 glimpse /ɡlɪmps/

Part of speech: Noun or Verb
词性：名词或动词

📖 As a noun:

A glimpse is a short or quick look at something.
glimpse（名词）指短暂的一瞥，一眼。

Example: I caught a glimpse of the sunset before it disappeared behind the clouds.
例句：在夕阳落入云层之前，我匆匆看了一眼。

📖 As a verb:

To glimpse something means to see it briefly or only partly.
glimpse（动词）表示匆匆看到、瞥见某物。

Example: She glimpsed him walking across the street.
例句：她瞥见他走过街道。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Peek 偷看，一瞥
  • Glance 扫视
  • Flash 闪现
  • View 视野/观察
  • Sight 目睹

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "glimpse" comes from Middle English glimsen, meaning “to glimmer or shine faintly” — related to the idea of catching sight of something quickly.
“glimpse” 源自中古英语 glimsen，意思是“微微发光”，后来引申为“短暂看到”某物。

🧭 Word Evolution

Middle English: glimsen – to glimmer or shine faintly
中古英语 glimsen：微弱地闪光
⬇️
Modern English: glimpse – quick or brief look
现代英语 glimpse：快速地一瞥
