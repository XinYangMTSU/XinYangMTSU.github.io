🌀 gibberish /ˈdʒɪbərɪʃ/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

Gibberish refers to speech or writing that sounds like language but makes no sense. It may consist of nonsense words or confused, meaningless phrases.
gibberish 意为“胡言乱语；令人费解的文字或语言”，看似是语言，但实际上毫无意义。

Example: The baby babbled happily in gibberish that only she could understand.
例句：那个婴儿高兴地咿呀乱语，只有她自己能理解。
Example: To someone unfamiliar with math, the equation looked like pure gibberish.
例句：对不懂数学的人来说，这个方程看起来完全像是在胡扯。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Nonsense 胡说
  • Babble 咿呀乱语
  • Jargon 术语（有时贬义）
  • Drivel 废话
  • Balderdash 胡言乱语（夸张说法）

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "gibberish" likely comes from the word gibber, meaning “to speak rapidly and unintelligibly.” Its origins are uncertain, but it has been used since the 16th century to describe meaningless talk.
“gibberish” 可能源自动词 gibber，意为“快速而含糊地说话”。虽然具体词源不明确，但早在16世纪就用于描述无意义的说话。

🧭 Word Evolution

Possibly from: gibber – to chatter unintelligibly
可能来自 gibber：语无伦次地说
⬇️
Early usage: 16th-century English
16世纪英语中已有使用
⬇️
Modern English: gibberish – nonsense speech or writing
现代英语 gibberish：胡说八道或令人费解的语言
