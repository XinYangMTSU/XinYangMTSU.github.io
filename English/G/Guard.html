🛡️ guard /ɡɑːrd/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

Noun: A person or device that protects or watches over something.
名词：守卫，警卫，保护装置。

Verb: To protect or watch over someone or something.
动词：守卫，保卫。

Example: A security guard checked everyone entering the building.
例句：保安检查了所有进入大楼的人。
Example: Soldiers were guarding the entrance to the camp.
例句：士兵们正在守卫营地入口。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Protect 保护
  • Defend 防卫
  • Watch 看守
  • Sentry 哨兵

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Guard" comes from Old French garder, meaning "to protect or watch", which has Germanic roots. It entered English in the Middle Ages.
guard 来自古法语 garder，意为“保护”或“看守”，源自日耳曼语词根，在中世纪进入英语。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old French: garder – to guard, protect
古法语 garder：守卫，保护
⬇️
Middle English: garde – protection
中古英语 garde：保护
⬇️
Modern English: guard – to protect or person who protects
现代英语 guard：保护或守卫者

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Protect Neutral The sunscreen protects your skin from UV rays. General
Defend Formal They defended their country bravely. Military / Serious
Guard Neutral He guards the museum at night. Protective / Practical
