🦕 ginormous /ˌdʒaɪˈnɔːrməs/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Extremely large; a blend of "gigantic" and "enormous" used informally to describe something very big in size or impact.
巨大的；“gigantic”和“enormous”的合成词，常用于非正式语境中表示非常大的事物。

Example: That pizza was ginormous — it didn’t even fit on the table!
例句：那张披萨实在太大了，连桌子都放不下！
Example: She wore a ginormous hat to the wedding.
例句：她戴着一顶巨大的帽子参加婚礼。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Gigantic 巨大的
  • Enormous 庞大的
  • Colossal 巨型的
  • Massive 巨大的；沉重的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Ginormous" is a blend (or portmanteau) of the words "gigantic" and "enormous." It was first used in military slang in the 1940s and later became popular in pop culture and children's books.
“Ginormous” 是“gigantic”（巨大）和“enormous”（庞大）的合成词，最早出现在20世纪40年代的军队俚语中，后来在流行文化和儿童书籍中广泛使用。

🧭 Word Evolution

1940s slang: military use
20世纪40年代：军队俚语

Late 20th century: pop culture and cartoons
20世纪后期：流行文化和卡通作品

Modern English: informal use to describe something very large
现代英语：用于非正式语境中形容巨大的事物

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Gigantic Neutral The ship was gigantic in size. Descriptive
Enormous Formal The company made an enormous profit. Impressive
Ginormous Informal They built a ginormous sandcastle. Playful / Exaggerated
