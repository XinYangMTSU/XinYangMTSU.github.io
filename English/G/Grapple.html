🌟 grapple /ˈɡræpəl/

Part of speech: Verb or noun

📖 As a verb (action):

To grapple means to struggle or wrestle with something physically or mentally—either holding onto someone or trying to solve a difficult issue.

Example: The firefighter grappled with the man to pull him to safety. (= physically held and pulled him)
Example: She grappled with the idea of moving to a new country. (= mentally struggled with the decision)

📖 As a noun (a thing):

A grapple is a close hand-to-hand fight or a tool used to grip or hook onto something.

Example: The climber used a grapple to latch onto the rocky surface. (= a hooking tool)

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Struggle
  • Wrestle
  • Clutch
  • Grasp
  • Deal with
  • Confront

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "grapple".

📝 It comes from the Old French word grapil, meaning a grappling hook, which came from the Late Latin grappus (claw).

  • grappus = hook, claw

✍️ Originally, it described the physical act of grabbing or hooking something. Over time, it developed metaphorical meaning as well, like struggling with a problem.
