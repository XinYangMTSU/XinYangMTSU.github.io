🌟 garden-variety /ˈɡɑːrdn vəˌraɪəti/

Part of speech: Adjective (compound)

📖 Meaning:

Garden-variety means ordinary, not special, or typical. It refers to something very common and lacking any unusual features.

Example: It's just a garden-variety cold—nothing serious.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Ordinary
  • Commonplace
  • Typical
  • Standard
  • Run-of-the-mill
  • Plain

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The term garden-variety originated in the early 1900s in American English.

📝It was originally used in horticulture to describe plants that were commonly grown in ordinary gardens (as opposed to rare or exotic species).

💡 Over time, the phrase became a metaphor for anything that is average, unremarkable, or lacking uniqueness.
