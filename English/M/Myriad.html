🌌 myriad /ˈmɪr.i.əd/

Part of speech: Noun / Adjective
词性：名词 / 形容词

📖 Meaning:

As a noun: A very large number of things.
名词：无数；大量。

As an adjective: Countless or innumerable in number.
形容词：无数的；种类繁多的。

Example: The night sky was filled with a myriad of stars.
例句：夜空中布满了无数的星星。
Example: She faced a myriad problems after moving to a new city.
例句：她搬到新城市后面临着各种各样的问题。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Multitude 大量；众多
  • Countless 无数的
  • Numerous 许多的
  • Infinite 无限的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Myriad" comes from the Greek word myrias, meaning "ten thousand." Over time, its use broadened to mean “a countless number.”
“Myriad” 源自希腊语 myrias，意为“一万”。后来扩展为“无数”或“极多”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Greek: myrias – ten thousand
希腊语 myrias：一万
⬇️
Latin: myrias – large number
拉丁语 myrias：大量
⬇️
Modern English: myriad – countless or innumerable
现代英语 myriad：无数的；大量的
