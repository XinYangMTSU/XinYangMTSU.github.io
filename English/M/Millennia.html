🌟 millennia /məˈlɛniə/

Part of speech: Noun (plural form of "millennium")

📖 Definition:

Millennia refers to periods of a thousand years. It is the plural form of the word millennium.

Example: For millennia, humans have gazed at the stars and wondered about the universe.

📖 Broader Meaning:

It can also be used to refer to long spans of time symbolically or poetically.

Example: These traditions have survived for millennia.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Thousands of years
  • Ages
  • Epochs
  • Generations
  • Centuries (not exact, but similar usage)

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "millennia" comes from the Latin word millennium, which is made up of:

  • mille = thousand
  • annus = year

✍️ The plural form in Latin is millennia, which was adopted directly into English.
