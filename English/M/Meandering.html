🌿 meandering /miˈændərɪŋ/

Part of speech: Adjective / Verb (present participle)
词性：形容词 / 动词现在分词

📖 Meaning:

Following a winding or indirect course; wandering without a clear direction.
蜿蜒的；漫无目的的；曲折的。

Example: We walked along a meandering path through the forest.
例句：我们沿着森林中一条蜿蜒的小径行走。
Example: His speech was long and meandering, difficult to follow.
例句：他的演讲冗长而杂乱，让人难以跟上。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Winding 蜿蜒的
  • Twisting 曲折的
  • Rambling 漫谈的
  • Curving 弯曲的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Meandering" comes from the Greek word Maiandros, the name of a winding river in Asia Minor (now the Menderes River in Turkey).
meandering 来自希腊语 Maiandros，是小亚细亚一条蜿蜒河流（现为土耳其的门德雷斯河）的名字。

🧭 Word Evolution

Greek: Maiandros – winding river
希腊语 Maiandros：蜿蜒的河流
⬇️
Middle English: meandren – to wander
中古英语 meandren：漫步
⬇️
Modern English: meandering – winding, indirect
现代英语 meandering：蜿蜒的，不直的

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Winding Neutral The winding road led through the hills. Descriptive
Rambling Neutral / Informal His rambling speech lasted for hours. Casual / Disorganized
Meandering Neutral The meandering river shaped the valley. Descriptive / Flowing
