⚪ minimalism /ˈmɪnɪməlɪzəm/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A style or approach that uses the fewest and simplest elements possible; focusing on clarity, simplicity, and functionality.
极简主义；以最少、最简单的元素表达美感，注重清晰、简洁与功能性。

Example: Her home is decorated in a clean, minimalist style.
例句：她的家以简洁的极简风格装饰。
Example: Minimalism in art emphasizes empty space and subtle detail.
例句：极简艺术强调留白与细微之美。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Simplicity 简洁
  • Reduction 简化
  • Elegance 优雅（极简风）
  • Essentialism 本质主义

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Minimalism" comes from the word "minimal," meaning “the least amount,” and the suffix “-ism,” indicating a philosophy or movement. It became widely known in the 20th century through art and design.
“Minimalism” 源自 “minimal”（最少的）加上后缀 “-ism”（表示主义、风格）。20世纪通过艺术与设计广泛传播。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin root: minimus – smallest
拉丁语词根 minimus：最小
⬇️
Modern English: minimalism – a design and lifestyle philosophy focusing on simplicity
现代英语 minimalism：强调简洁的设计与生活理念
