🎨 muse /mjuːz/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

As a noun, muse refers to a person or thing that inspires creativity, especially in art, music, or writing.
As a verb, it means to think deeply or reflect silently.
“muse” 作为名词，表示激发艺术创作灵感的人或事物；作为动词，表示沉思、冥想。

Example (noun): The mountains served as her muse for many poems.
例句（名词）：群山是她许多诗歌的灵感来源。
Example (verb): He mused about the meaning of life while staring at the stars.
例句（动词）：他凝视星空，沉思人生的意义。

🧩 Synonyms

  • Inspiration 灵感
  • Contemplation 沉思
  • Reflection 反思
  • Think deeply 深入思考
  • Creative influence 创作影响力

🔍 Etymology of the word

📚 “Muse” comes from Greek Mousa, referring to any of the nine goddesses who inspired literature, science, and the arts. In modern use, it refers to anyone or anything that sparks creative thought.
“muse” 源自希腊语 Mousa，原指九位激发文学、科学和艺术灵感的缪斯女神。如今泛指激发创意的人或事物。

📊 Comparison with Related Words

Word Definition Example Usage
Inspiration Something that sparks ideas or action Her speech was a true inspiration to us all. General creativity or motivation
Reflection Serious thought or consideration He paused for reflection before answering. Internal contemplation
Muse A source of artistic inspiration or deep thought The ocean was the painter's muse. Art, literature, philosophy
