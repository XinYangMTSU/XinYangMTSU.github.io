🌟 mediocre /ˌmiː.diˈoʊ.kər/

Part of speech: Adjective

📖 Meaning:

Mediocre means something that is just average or slightly below average — not very good, but not terrible either.

Example: The food at that restaurant was mediocre. (= It was okay, but nothing special.)

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Average
  • Ordinary
  • So-so
  • Unremarkable
  • Passable
  • Inferior

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "mediocre".

📝 It comes from the Latin adjective mediocris.

  • medius = middle
  • ocris = a rugged mountain (poetic usage)

✍️ Originally it described a mountain of moderate height — not too high or low. Over time, it came to mean something that’s in the middle: not excellent, not bad.

💡 In modern usage, “mediocre” often has a slightly negative tone, suggesting disappointment or lack of quality.

📰 Mediocre in the News

In product reviews, politics, and film criticism, the word "mediocre" is often used to describe things that fail to impress. For example: “The highly anticipated sequel was surprisingly mediocre.”
