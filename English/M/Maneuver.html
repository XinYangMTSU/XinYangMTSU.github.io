🌟 maneuver /məˈnuːvər/

Part of speech: Noun or verb

📖 As a verb (action):

To maneuver means to move skillfully or carefully, especially to avoid obstacles or achieve a goal. It can also mean to plan or manipulate a situation cleverly.

Example: The pilot maneuvered the plane through the storm. (= Moved it carefully.)
Example: She maneuvered her way into the leadership role. (= Strategically reached it.)

📖 As a noun (a thing):

A maneuver is a movement or series of moves requiring skill and care, often used in driving, military, or strategic planning.

Example: The tricky parking maneuver took him five minutes to complete.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Move
  • Steer
  • Navigate
  • Strategy
  • Tactic

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "maneuver" comes from the French word manœuvre, which itself comes from Latin manu operari.

  • manu = hand
  • operari = to work

✍️ It originally meant “to work by hand” but evolved to mean a carefully planned movement or action.

🧭 Word Evolution
