🫡 muster /ˈmʌstər/

Part of speech: Verb / Noun
词性：动词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

(v.) To gather or summon something (especially courage, strength, or support); to assemble troops.
(n.) A gathering of people, especially soldiers.
（动词）鼓起（勇气、力量等）；集合（尤指士兵）
（名词）集合；点名

Example: He tried to muster the courage to speak in front of the crowd.
例句：他努力鼓起勇气在众人面前讲话。
Example: The general ordered a muster of all available troops.
例句：将军下令所有可用士兵集合。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Gather 集合
  • Summon 召唤
  • Assemble 集结
  • Mobilize 动员

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Muster" comes from Old French mostrer (to show), which came from Latin monstrare, meaning “to show or point out.” Originally it referred to showing troops for inspection.
“Muster” 源自古法语 mostrer（展示），来自拉丁语 monstrare，意为“展示、指出”，最初用于士兵集合点名时的展示。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: monstrare – to show
拉丁语 monstrare：展示

Old French: mostrer – to display troops
古法语 mostrer：展示军队

Modern English: muster – to gather or summon
现代英语 muster：召集、集合

📊 Comparison

Word Context Example Use Tone
Summon Formal / Emotional He summoned all his strength. Formal / Dramatic
Mobilize Military / Strategic The country mobilized its army. Serious
Muster Military / Emotional She mustered the courage to confess. Neutral / Heroic
