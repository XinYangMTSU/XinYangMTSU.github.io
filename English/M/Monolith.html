🪨 monolith /ˈmɒn.ə.lɪθ/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

1. A large single block of stone, often shaped into a monument or standing structure.
2. A powerful, large, and indivisible organization or system that is slow to change.
1. 独石，巨石，常用于纪念碑或建筑物结构；
2. 庞大而单一的组织或体系，通常难以改变。

Example 1: Stonehenge is composed of multiple upright monoliths arranged in a circle.
例句 1：巨石阵由多个竖立的独石组成，围成一个圆圈。
Example 2: The corporation became a monolith, resistant to innovation or reform.
例句 2：这家公司变得如同一个庞大的机构，对创新和改革毫无反应。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Megalith 巨石
  • Monument 纪念碑
  • Structure 结构体
  • Institution (figurative) 机构（比喻义）

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Monolith" comes from the Greek words monos (meaning “single”) and lithos (meaning “stone”), literally translating to “single stone.”
“Monolith” 源自希腊语 monos（单一）和 lithos（石头），意为“独石”或“单块石头”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Greek: monos (single) + lithos (stone)
希腊语 monos（单一）+ lithos（石头）

Latin: monolithus – large stone
拉丁语 monolithus：巨石

Modern English: monolith – large stone or massive, unified entity
现代英语 monolith：巨石；庞大而统一的实体

📊 Comparison
