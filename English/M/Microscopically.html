🔬 microscopically /ˌmaɪ.krəˈskɒ.pɪ.kəl.i/

Part of speech: Adverb
词性：副词

📖 Meaning:

In a way that is so small it can only be seen with a microscope; or, by examining something extremely carefully and in great detail.
显微镜下地；极其微小地；极为仔细地观察。

Example: The cells were examined microscopically for signs of infection.
例句：这些细胞被在显微镜下检查是否有感染迹象。
Example: She studied the document microscopically to catch every mistake.
例句：她极其仔细地检查了这份文件，力求找出每一个错误。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Minutely 微小地；细致地
  • Closely 密切地；仔细地
  • Thoroughly 彻底地
  • Scrupulously 一丝不苟地

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Microscopically" is formed from "microscopic" (tiny, as seen with a microscope) and the adverbial suffix “-ly.” "Microscopic" comes from Greek mikros (small) + skopein (to look or see).
“Microscopically” 来自“microscopic”（微小的），加上副词后缀 “-ly”。"Microscopic" 源自希腊语 mikros（小）和 skopein（看）。

🧭 Word Evolution

Greek: mikros – small; skopein – to look
希腊语 mikros：小； skopein：看

Modern English: microscopically – in an extremely small or detailed way
现代英语 microscopically：极小地；极仔细地
