🌟 modesty /ˈmɑːdəsti/

Part of speech: Noun

📖 Meaning:

Modesty means not talking too proudly about your achievements or abilities. It can also mean dressing or behaving in a way that is not meant to attract attention, especially in a respectful or humble way.

Example: She accepted the award with modesty, saying she couldn't have done it without her team.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Humility
  • Reserve
  • Shyness
  • Unpretentiousness
  • Decency
  • Discreetness

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "modesty".

📝It comes from the Latin adjective: modestus

  • modus = measure or manner
  • -estus = suffix indicating quality or state

✍️ So, "modesty" evolved from the idea of behaving in a moderate, measured, or proper way—without excess or pride.

💡 It entered English in the late 1500s through French modestie and Latin modestia, meaning restraint or gentleness.
