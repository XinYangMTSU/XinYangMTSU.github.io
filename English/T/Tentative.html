🕊️ tentative /ˈten.tə.tɪv/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Tentative means not certain or fixed; done without confidence or as a trial or experiment.
“tentative” 意为不确定的，暂定的；或表示缺乏信心、试探性的行为。

Example: We made a tentative plan to meet next week.
例句：我们暂定下周见面。
Example: She took a tentative step onto the ice.
例句：她小心翼翼地踏上冰面。

🧩 Synonyms

  • Uncertain 不确定的
  • Hesitant 犹豫的
  • Provisional 临时的
  • Experimental 实验性的
  • Cautious 谨慎的

🔍 Etymology of the word

📚 "Tentative" comes from Latin tentativus (trying, testing), from tentare meaning "to try, test, or attempt."
“tentative” 源自拉丁语 tentativus（尝试性的），来自 tentare，意为“尝试、试探”。

📊 Comparison with Related Words

Word Definition Example Context
Tentative Not definite; done with uncertainty His tone was tentative and unsure. Plans, decisions, actions
Provisional Temporary or conditional A provisional agreement was signed. Legal, contractual
Hesitant Lacking certainty or willingness He gave a hesitant answer. Speech, response
