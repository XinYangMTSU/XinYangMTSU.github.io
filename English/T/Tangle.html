🧵 tangle /ˈtæŋ.ɡəl/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

As a noun, tangle refers to a twisted mass of threads, hair, or other similar things. As a verb, it means to become or cause to become twisted together in a messy way.
“tangle” 作为名词指纠缠在一起的东西（如线、头发）；作为动词则表示使缠结或变得杂乱无章。

Example: Her hair was in a tangle after the windy day.
例句：风大的一天过后，她的头发全打结了。
Example: Don’t tangle the cords or they’ll be hard to separate later.
例句：别把电线缠在一起，不然之后很难分开。

🧩 Synonyms

  • Snarl 缠结
  • Entangle 使纠缠
  • Twist 扭曲
  • Knot 打结
  • Mess 混乱

🔍 Etymology of the word

📚 “Tangle” dates back to Middle English and is likely related to Old Norse *tangla*, meaning "to entangle."
“tangle” 这个词起源于中古英语，可能与古诺斯语 *tangla*（缠绕）有关。

📊 Comparison with Related Words

Word Definition Example Context
Tangle A twisted, knotted mess The ropes were in a tangle on the deck. Everyday, physical objects
Snarl A confused or tangled condition The traffic snarl delayed everyone. Traffic, cables, bureaucracy
