🌟 tyranny /ˈtɪrəni/

Part of speech: Noun

📖 Meaning:

Tyranny refers to cruel and unfair government or leadership, where one person or group holds too much power and uses it in a harsh or oppressive way.

Example: The people rebelled against the tyranny of the king. (= The king’s rule was harsh and unfair.)

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Oppression
  • Dictatorship
  • Despotism
  • Autocracy
  • Totalitarianism
  • Absolutism

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s explore where the word "tyranny" comes from.

📝It comes from the Greek noun: tyrannos, meaning “absolute ruler.”

  • tyrannos = master or ruler (without legal right)

💡 The term passed into Latin as tyrannia, and later into Old French as tyrannie, eventually becoming tyranny in English around the 14th century.
