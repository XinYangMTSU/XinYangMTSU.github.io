🌟 tapestry /ˈtæpəstri/

Part of speech: Noun

📖 Meaning:

A tapestry is a piece of thick fabric with pictures or designs woven into it, often used as a wall hanging. Figuratively, it also refers to something rich, complex, and intricately made—like a story, history, or idea.

Example (literal): The medieval castle was decorated with colorful tapestries.
Example (figurative): The novel presents a rich tapestry of life in 19th-century England.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Wall hanging
  • Embroidery
  • Weaving
  • Patchwork (figurative)
  • Collage (figurative)
  • Storyline (figurative)

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word “tapestry” comes from:

📝Old French: tapisserie → Latin: tapetum (carpet, covering)

💡 Originally meant a heavy cloth used for covering or decoration, and later extended figuratively to mean something rich and detailed.
