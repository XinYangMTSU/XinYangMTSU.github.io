🌟 tweak /twiːk/

Part of speech: Noun or verb

📖 As a verb (action):

To tweak something means to make a small change or adjustment to improve it or make it work better.

Example: The engineer tweaked the settings to make the machine run more efficiently.

📖 As a noun (a thing):

A tweak is a small change or improvement to something.

Example: The recipe just needs a slight tweak to be perfect.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Adjust
  • Modify
  • Refine
  • Improve
  • Change
  • Edit

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "tweak" originally meant to pinch or twist sharply and came from Middle English.

📝 Over time, it evolved to mean "to make a fine adjustment," especially in mechanical or digital contexts.

🧭 Word Evolution

Middle English: tweeken (to pinch or pluck)
⬇️
Modern English: tweak — small adjustment or fine-tune
