🏆 tournament /ˈtʊrnəmənt/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A series of contests or games in which individuals or teams compete to determine a winner.
锦标赛；比赛系列；多个队伍或个人之间为决出冠军而进行的一系列比赛。

Example: The school is hosting a chess tournament this weekend.
例句：这所学校本周末将举办国际象棋锦标赛。
Example: They made it to the finals of the soccer tournament.
例句：他们闯入了足球锦标赛的决赛。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Competition 竞赛
  • Championship 锦标赛
  • Match series 系列比赛
  • Contest 竞赛

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Tournament" comes from Old French torneiement, meaning “a medieval contest of knights.” It originally referred to knightly jousts, then evolved into modern sports competitions.
tournament 来自古法语 torneiement，原指中世纪骑士比武，后演变为现代的体育竞赛。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old French: torneiement – knightly contest
古法语 torneiement：骑士比武
⬇️
Middle English: tournament – martial game or mock battle
中古英语 tournament：军事游戏或模拟战
⬇️
Modern English: tournament – organized competition or series of games
现代英语 tournament：有组织的比赛或系列竞赛

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Competition Neutral The competition was fierce this year. General / Descriptive
Championship Formal / Specific He won the national chess championship. Official / Prestigious
Tournament Neutral They joined the summer basketball tournament. Sportive / Organized
