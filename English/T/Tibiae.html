🌀 tibiae /ˈtɪbi.iː/

Part of speech: Noun (plural)
词性：名词（复数）

📖 Meaning:

Tibiae is the plural form of tibia, the larger and stronger of the two bones in the lower leg, also known as the shinbone.
tibiae 是 tibia（胫骨）的复数形式，指的是人腿部两块骨头中较大且较强的一块，也叫小腿胫骨。

Example: The athlete suffered fractures in both tibiae during the fall.
例句：那位运动员在摔倒时两条小腿的胫骨都骨折了。
Example: X-rays showed the tibiae were properly aligned after surgery.
例句：X 光片显示手术后两条胫骨排列正确。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Shinbones 胫骨
  • Lower leg bones 小腿骨
  • Weight-bearing bones 承重骨
  • Long bones 长骨

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Tibiae" comes from Latin tibia, which originally referred to a pipe or flute (possibly made from bone), and later to the shinbone due to its shape.
tibiae 来自拉丁语 tibia，最初指的是由骨头制成的笛子，后来演变为指胫骨，因为它们形状相似。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: tibia – pipe, flute → shinbone
拉丁语 tibia：笛子 → 胫骨
⬇️
Modern English: tibiae – plural of tibia, the main bone in the lower leg
现代英语 tibiae：tibia 的复数形式，小腿主要的骨头

📊 Comparison
