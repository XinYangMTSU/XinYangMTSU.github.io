🧩 triviality /ˌtrɪviˈæləti/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

Something that is unimportant or insignificant; lack of seriousness or value.
琐事；微不足道的事；不重要；缺乏重要性。

Example: Let’s not waste time arguing over trivialities.
例句：我们不要浪费时间争论这些小事。
Example: He dismissed the criticism as a triviality.
例句：他把这条批评当作无关紧要的琐碎之事。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Insignificance 无关紧要
  • Unimportance 不重要
  • Trifle 琐事
  • Minority 细节、小事

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Triviality" comes from Latin trivialis, meaning "commonplace" or "ordinary," from trivium, a place where three roads meet—hence, something easily accessible or not special.
triviality 源自拉丁语 trivialis，意为“平凡的、常见的”，来自 trivium（三条路的交叉处），象征随处可见、不重要。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: trivialis – commonplace, ordinary
拉丁语 trivialis：常见的，普通的
⬇️
Middle English: trivialite – insignificance
中古英语 trivialite：微不足道
⬇️
Modern English: triviality – something unimportant
现代英语 triviality：琐事，无关紧要的事

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Example Use Tone
Triviality Neutral / Formal The debate devolved into trivialities. Critical / Descriptive
Trifle Neutral Don't argue over trifles. Casual
Insignificance Formal They underestimated the insignificance of that detail. Objective
