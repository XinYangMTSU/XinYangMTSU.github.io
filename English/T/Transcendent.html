🌌 transcendent /trænˈsɛndənt/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Surpassing ordinary limits; going beyond what is normal, physical, or human; extraordinary or spiritual in nature.
超越的；卓越的；超凡的；超自然的。

Example: Her performance was transcendent, lifting the audience to tears.
例句：她的表演超凡脱俗，感动得观众潸然泪下。
Example: Philosophers debate the nature of transcendent reality beyond physical existence.
例句：哲学家争论超越物质存在的超验现实的本质。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Otherworldly 非凡的；超俗的
  • Supernatural 超自然的
  • Extraordinary 非同寻常的
  • Divine 神圣的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Transcendent" comes from the Latin word transcendere, meaning “to climb over” or “go beyond,” from trans- (across, beyond) and scandere (to climb).
“Transcendent” 源自拉丁语 transcendere，意思是“跨越”或“超越”，由 trans-（穿越、超越）和 scandere（攀登）组成。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: transcendere – to climb over, go beyond
拉丁语 transcendere：超越，攀升

Middle English: transcendent – surpassing ordinary limits
中古英语 transcendent：超出寻常

Modern English: transcendent – beyond physical or material reality
现代英语 transcendent：超越物质现实

📊 Comparison
