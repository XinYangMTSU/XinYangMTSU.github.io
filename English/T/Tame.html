🐾 tame /teɪm/

Part of speech: Verb / Adjective
词性：动词 / 形容词

📖 Meaning:

As a verb: To make an animal or force less wild or easier to control.
（动词）使变得驯服；控制。

As an adjective: Not wild or dangerous; calm and under control.
（形容词）温顺的；被驯化的；乏味的。

Example (verb): It took years to tame the wild horse.
例句（动词）：驯服那匹野马花了好几年。
Example (adj): The lion seemed tame and gentle.
例句（形容词）：那只狮子看起来很温顺。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Control 控制
  • Domesticate 驯化
  • Subdue 压制
  • Gentle 温顺的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Tame" comes from Old English tam, meaning “submissive, gentle,” from Proto-Germanic tamaz.
tame 源自古英语 tam，意为“顺从的，温顺的”，来自原始日耳曼语 tamaz。

🧭 Word Evolution

Proto-Germanic: tamaz – gentle
原始日耳曼语 tamaz：温顺的
⬇️
Old English: tam – submissive
古英语 tam：顺从的
⬇️
Modern English: tame – domesticated, controlled
现代英语 tame：驯服的，可控的
