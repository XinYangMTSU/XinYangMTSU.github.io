🌀 telepath /ˈteləˌpæθ/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

Telepath refers to a person who is believed to have the ability to read other people's thoughts or communicate using mental power, without speaking or using physical senses.
telepath 是指“具有心灵感应能力的人”，能不通过语言或感官而用思想与他人沟通的人。

Example: In the sci-fi novel, a telepath uncovered a conspiracy through thought-reading.
例句：在这部科幻小说中，一位 telepath 通过读心术揭露了一场阴谋。
Example: The character was born a telepath and struggled to live a normal life.
例句：这个角色天生具有心灵感应能力，却努力想过普通人的生活。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Mind-reader 读心者
  • Psychic 通灵者
  • Mentalist 意念表演者
  • Empath 共情者
  • Clairvoyant 千里眼

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Telepath" comes from Greek roots: tele meaning "distant" and pathos meaning "feeling." A telepath is literally “one who feels from afar.”
telepath 源自希腊语 tele（远距离）和 pathos（感知），意思是“远距离感知的人”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Greek: tele – far + pathos – feeling
希腊语 tele：远 + pathos：感觉
⬇️
Modern English: telepath – one who perceives thoughts or feelings at a distance
现代英语 telepath：能够感应远处思想或情感的人
