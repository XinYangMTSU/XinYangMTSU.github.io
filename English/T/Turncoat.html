🧥 turncoat /ˈtɜːn.kəʊt/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A person who switches allegiance from one side, party, or cause to an opposing one; a traitor.
变节者；叛徒；背叛原来立场或阵营的人。

Example: He was branded a turncoat after joining the rival political party.
例句：他加入了对立党派，被指为叛徒。
Example: The general was once a hero, but history remembers him as a turncoat.
例句：那位将军曾是英雄，但历史记住他是一个叛徒。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Traitor 叛徒
  • Defector 变节者
  • Betrayer 背叛者
  • Renegade 叛教者；背叛者

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Turncoat" originated in the 16th century, from the literal idea of turning one’s coat inside out to show loyalty to a new side—especially in times of political change.
“Turncoat” 起源于 16 世纪，最初是字面意思：把外套翻过来穿，以显示对新阵营的忠诚，常用于政治变革时期。

🧭 Word Evolution

16th century: turn + coat – one who literally or figuratively switches sides
16 世纪：turn + coat，即“换阵营”或“翻脸”的人

Modern English: turncoat – traitor or defector
现代英语 turncoat：叛徒；变节者

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Negative Connotation Example
Traitor High Very strong He was convicted as a traitor to his country.
Turncoat Moderate Strong He was seen as a turncoat by former allies.
Defector Formal / political Moderate The defector fled to another nation.
