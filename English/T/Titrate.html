🌟 titrate /ˈtaɪtreɪt/

Part of speech: Verb

📖 As a verb (action):

To titrate means to determine the concentration of a substance in a solution by gradually adding a known solution until a chemical reaction is completed (especially in chemistry or medicine).

Example: The lab technician titrated the acid with a base to find its exact concentration.
Example: Doctors often titrate medication doses to find the optimal level for a patient.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Measure precisely
  • Adjust dosage
  • Calculate concentration
  • Evaluate
  • Neutralize

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "titrate".

📝It comes from the French word titre, meaning "standard or title" (in chemistry, the strength of a solution), and the suffix -ate meaning "to act upon".

  • titre = standard measure
  • -ate = to cause to become

✍️ Originally, it referred to measuring the exact strength of a solution.

💡 Now it's used both in labs and medicine for controlled adjustments of chemical or drug concentrations.
