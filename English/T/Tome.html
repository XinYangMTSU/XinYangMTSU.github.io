📚 tome /toʊm/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A tome is a large, heavy, and often scholarly book, especially one that is part of a larger work or deals with a serious subject.
“tome” 指的是一本厚重的书，通常是学术性的，可能是系列著作的一部分。

Example: He spent years writing a tome on ancient Greek philosophy.
例句：他花了数年写了一本关于古希腊哲学的巨著。
Example: The textbook was a dense tome filled with mathematical proofs.
例句：那本教材是一部充满数学证明的厚书。

🧩 Synonyms

  • Volume 卷册
  • Opus 大作
  • Work 著作
  • Publication 出版物
  • Textbook 教科书

🔍 Etymology of the word

📚 "Tome" comes from the Greek word tomos, meaning “a section” or “a volume,” which was used to refer to a single part of a larger written work.
“tome” 源自希腊语 tomos，意为“一部分”或“一卷”，原指大型著作中的某一册。

📊 Comparison with Related Words

Word Definition Example Context
Tome A large or scholarly book She placed the ancient tome on the shelf. Academic, literary
Book A set of written or printed pages bound together I’m reading a mystery book. General use
Volume A single book in a multi-book series This is Volume II of the encyclopedia. Series or collections
