🌟 Trigonometric /ˌtrɪɡənoʊˈmɛtrɪk/

Part of speech: Adjective

📖 Meaning:

Trigonometric refers to anything related to trigonometry, the branch of mathematics that deals with the relationships between the angles and sides of triangles.

Example: We used trigonometric functions to calculate the height of the mountain.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Trigonometry-related
  • Angular
  • Geometric
  • Mathematical

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Trigonometric comes from the Greek words trigonon (triangle) and metron (measure). It literally means “measuring triangles.”

📐 Real Math Example – Trigonometric Identity

One of the most famous trigonometric identities is:

\[ \sin^2(\theta) + \cos^2(\theta) = 1 \]

This equation means that for any angle \( \theta \), the square of the sine plus the square of the cosine equals 1. It's a core idea in the unit circle.
