👊 thump /θʌmp/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

As a verb: To hit something with a heavy dull sound.
动词：用沉闷的声音重击某物。

As a noun: A heavy dull sound made by or as if by a blow.
名词：由重击发出的沉闷声。

Example: He thumped the table with his fist.
例句：他用拳头猛击桌子。
Example: I heard a loud thump from upstairs.
例句：我听到楼上传来一声巨响。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Pound 猛击
  • Bang 砰的一声
  • Hit 打
  • Knock 敲击

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Thump" is of imitative origin, echoing the sound of a dull blow or heavy fall.
thump 是拟声词，模仿沉重打击或坠落的声音。

🧭 Word Evolution

Origin: Imitative of a heavy, dull sound
词源：模仿沉重、闷响的声音
⬇️
Middle English: – thumpen (likely influenced by sound)
中古英语：可能由声音影响而形成
⬇️
Modern English: thump – a dull heavy hit or noise
现代英语 thump：沉重的打击或声音

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Pound Neutral He pounded on the door with both fists. Forceful
Bang Casual She banged the cupboard shut. Loud / Sudden
Thump Neutral The book fell to the floor with a thump. Heavy / Impactful
