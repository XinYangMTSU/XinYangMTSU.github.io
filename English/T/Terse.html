✍️ terse /tɜːrs/

Part of speech: Adjective

📖 Meaning:

If a statement or comment is terse, it is brief and often seems unfriendly or unfurnished. It's short, to the point—sometimes even abrupt.

Example: When asked why she was late, her reply was terse: "Traffic."

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Brief
  • Concise
  • Curt
  • Succinct
  • Blunt
  • Abrupt

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "terse".

📝It comes from the Latin adjective: tersus, meaning "wiped off, clean, neat".

  • tergere = to wipe or polish

💡 From the idea of polished or neat speech, it evolved into "terse" in English, referring to communication that is short and refined—but may also come across as blunt or curt.
