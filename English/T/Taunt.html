🗯️ taunt /tɔːnt/

Part of speech: Verb / Noun
词性：动词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

As a verb, taunt means to insult or provoke someone by mocking or teasing. As a noun, it refers to a comment made to anger or upset someone.
“taunt” 作为动词时指讽刺、挑衅或嘲笑某人；作为名词时指具有挑衅性的言语。

Example: The fans taunted the opposing team with chants and jeers.
例句：球迷们用呼喊和嘲讽来挑衅对方球队。
Example: His cruel taunt left her speechless.
例句：他残酷的嘲讽让她无言以对。

🧩 Synonyms

  • Mock 嘲笑
  • Ridicule 讥笑
  • Jeer 嘲弄
  • Tease 戏弄
  • Provoke 激怒

🔍 Etymology of the word

📚 “Taunt” comes from Middle French *tanter*, meaning “to tempt or provoke,” and is related to Latin *tentare*, meaning “to test or try.”
“taunt” 源自中古法语 *tanter*，意为“挑衅或引诱”，与拉丁语 *tentare*（试探、测试）相关。

📊 Comparison with Related Words

Word Definition Example Context
Taunt To insult or provoke with mockery The bully taunted the younger kid. Conflict, teasing
Tease To make fun of in a playful way She teased him about his haircut. Friendly or rude, depending on tone
Jeer To shout mocking or insulting things The crowd jeered at the referee. Public, aggressive
