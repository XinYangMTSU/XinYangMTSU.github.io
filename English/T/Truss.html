🏗️ truss /trʌs/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A structure of connected elements, typically arranged in triangles, used to support roofs, bridges, towers, and other frameworks by efficiently distributing weight and resisting stress.
桁架；由连接构件组成的结构，通常以三角形排列，用于支撑屋顶、桥梁、塔楼等，以高效分散重量并抵抗应力。

Example 1: The engineer designed a steel truss to support the long-span bridge.
例句 1：工程师设计了一个钢桁架来支撑这座长跨度桥梁。
Example 2: Wooden roof trusses are commonly used in residential construction.
例句 2：住宅建筑中常用木质屋顶桁架。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Framework 框架结构
  • Support system 支撑系统
  • Beam network 梁网结构
  • Structural lattice 结构格架

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Truss" comes from Middle English trusse, meaning “bundle or support,” and from Old French trousser (to tie up), related to Latin torquere (to twist). In engineering, it came to mean a rigid framework.
“Truss” 来自中古英语 trusse，意为“捆绑或支撑”，源自古法语 trousser（捆起），与拉丁语 torquere（扭转）相关。在工程学中，后来指坚固的支架结构。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: torquere – to twist
拉丁语 torquere：扭转

Old French: trousser – to tie or bundle
古法语 trousser：捆绑

Middle English: trusse – support or bind
中古英语 trusse：支撑，捆绑

Modern English: truss – rigid support frame in engineering
现代英语 truss：工程中的刚性支撑结构
