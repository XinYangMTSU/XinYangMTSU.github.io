🌟 electromagnetism /ɪˌlɛktroʊˈmæɡnəˌtɪzəm/

Part of speech: Noun

📖 Meaning:

Electromagnetism is the branch of physics involving the study of electric and magnetic fields and how they interact. It explains how charged particles create electric and magnetic forces and how those forces affect the motion of the particles.

Example: Electromagnetism is the reason why magnets stick to your refrigerator and why electric motors work.

🧩 Related Terms /rɪˈleɪtɪd/

  • Electricity
  • Magnetism
  • Force
  • Field
  • Wave
  • Light

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word electromagnetism is formed from two parts:

  • electro- = related to electricity (from Greek *ēlektron*, meaning amber)
  • magnetism = the force of magnets (from Greek *magnētis lithos*, meaning “Magnesian stone”)

💡 The term came into use in the early 19th century as scientists began to understand the relationship between electricity and magnetism.
