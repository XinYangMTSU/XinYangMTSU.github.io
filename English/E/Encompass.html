🌀 encompass /ɪnˈkʌmpəs/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To include a wide range of ideas, subjects, or things; to surround or cover completely.
包括，涵盖（广泛的内容）；包围；围绕。

Example: The course will encompass a broad range of topics from history to science.
例句：这门课程将涵盖从历史到科学的广泛主题。
Example: The valley is encompassed by towering mountains.
例句：这个山谷被高耸的山脉所环绕。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Include 包括
  • Surround 包围
  • Embrace 涵盖；拥抱
  • Cover 覆盖

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Encompass" comes from the prefix en- meaning "in" and the Middle English word compas meaning "circle" or "boundary". It originally meant to go around or encircle.
encompass 来自前缀 en-（表示“进入”）和中世纪英语 compas（圆圈，范围），原意为“环绕，包围”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Middle English: encompassen – to encircle
中世纪英语 encompassen：环绕，包围
⬇️
Modern English: encompass – to include or surround
现代英语 encompass：包括，围绕

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Include Neutral This book includes several case studies. Informative
Embrace Literary The idea embraces both tradition and innovation. Positive / Creative
Encompass Formal The law must encompass all citizens equally. Comprehensive
