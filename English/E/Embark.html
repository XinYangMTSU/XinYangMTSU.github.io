🚢 embark /ɪmˈbɑːrk/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To begin a journey, project, or course of action, especially one that is new or challenging.
开始（新的或有挑战性的事情）；启程；着手。

Example: They embarked on a new adventure through the Amazon rainforest.
例句：他们踏上了穿越亚马逊雨林的新冒险。
Example: She decided to embark on a career in medicine.
例句：她决定从事医学职业。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Begin 开始
  • Commence 着手
  • Start off 出发
  • Launch 发起

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Embark" comes from the Old French word embarquer, meaning “to go on board a ship,” which is derived from em- (in) + barque (small ship).
embark 来自古法语 embarquer，意思是“登船”，由 em-（进入）+ barque（小船）构成。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old French: embarquer – to go on board
古法语 embarquer：登船
⬇️
Modern English: embark – to begin or set out
现代英语 embark：开始，启程

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Begin Neutral Let’s begin the project next week. Simple
Commence Formal The trial will commence at 10 a.m. Official
Embark Formal He embarked on a journey to self-discovery. Adventurous / Ambitious
