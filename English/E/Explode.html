💥 explode /ɪkˈsploʊd/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

Explode means to burst or shatter violently and suddenly, often with a loud noise. It can also mean to increase quickly or express strong emotion.
explode 意为“爆炸、剧烈爆发”，常用于描述物理上的破裂，也可指情绪或数量的剧增。

Example: The bomb exploded in the middle of the street.
例句：炸弹在街道中央爆炸了。
Example: Sales exploded after the product was featured on TV.
例句：产品在电视上宣传后，销量猛增。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Detonate 引爆
  • Blow up 炸毁
  • Burst 爆裂
  • Ignite 点燃（激发）
  • Skyrocket 激增

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Explode" comes from the Latin explodere, meaning "to drive out by clapping" (as in driving an actor off stage). Later it came to mean “burst out with noise.”
“explode” 来自拉丁语 explodere，原意是“以鼓掌驱赶”，后引申为“突然发出巨响”并演变为“爆炸”的意思。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: explodere – drive out by clapping
拉丁语 explodere：鼓掌驱赶
⬇️
Late Latin: explodere – burst out
后期拉丁语：突然爆发
⬇️
Modern English: explode – to blow up or burst suddenly
现代英语 explode：突然爆炸或爆发

📊 Comparison

Verb Meaning Example Context
Detonate Trigger an explosion intentionally The device was detonated remotely. Technical / military
Burst Break open suddenly The balloon burst with a pop. Everyday / casual
Explode Break apart violently The fireworks exploded in the sky. General / descriptive
