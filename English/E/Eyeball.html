👁️ eyeball /ˈaɪˌbɔːl/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning (as a noun):

An eyeball is the round part of the eye that is inside the skull and helps with vision.
eyeball（名词）指的是眼球，是眼睛内部负责视觉的圆形部分。

Example: The doctor checked his eyeballs for any signs of irritation.
例句：医生检查了他的眼球是否有刺激迹象。

📖 Meaning (as a verb):

To eyeball something means to look at it closely or to estimate it just by looking.
eyeball（动词）可以表示仔细看某物，或仅通过观察来估算。

Example: He eyeballed the crowd before speaking.
例句：他在讲话前扫视了一下人群。
Example: She eyeballed the amount of sugar without measuring.
例句：她没有量，凭眼估算了糖的量。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Globe (of the eye) 眼球
  • Stare at 凝视
  • Observe 观察
  • Gauge 估算
  • Scan 扫视

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "eyeball" comes from combining "eye" and "ball," referring to the spherical shape of the eye. The verb form appeared later as informal usage to mean estimating or observing visually.
eyeball 一词来自 eye（眼睛）和 ball（球体）的组合，形容眼球的圆形外观。其动词形式后来以非正式方式出现，表示目测或用眼观察。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: ēage (eye) + ball (sphere)
古英语 ēage（眼）+ ball（球体）
⬇️
Modern English: eyeball – eye or visual estimate
现代英语 eyeball：既指眼球，也可作动词，意为用目光估计或观察

📊 Comparison
