✨ embellish /ɪmˈbelɪʃ/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To make something more beautiful or interesting by adding decorative details or exaggerations.
装饰；润饰；对……加以渲染或夸张。

Example: She embellished her story with vivid details to make it more exciting.
例句：她用生动的细节润饰故事，使其更精彩。
Example: The walls were embellished with gold leaf and intricate patterns.
例句：墙面装饰着金箔和精美的图案。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Decorate 装饰
  • Adorn 点缀
  • Enhance 美化；提升
  • Exaggerate 夸大

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Embellish" comes from the Old French embelir, meaning "to make beautiful," which is derived from Latin bellus, meaning "pretty or handsome." It has been used in English since the 15th century.
“Embellish” 源自古法语 embelir（使美丽），词根是拉丁语 bellus，意为“漂亮的”。自15世纪起在英语中使用。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: bellus – pretty, handsome
拉丁语 bellus：漂亮的，可爱的

Old French: embelir – to beautify
古法语 embelir：使……更美

Modern English: embellish – to make more attractive by decoration or exaggeration
现代英语 embellish：通过装饰或润饰使更吸引人

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Decorate Neutral They decorated the house for the holiday. Festive
Exaggerate Informal He exaggerated the size of the fish he caught. Playful / Dramatic
Embellish Formal The author embellished the tale for dramatic effect. Elegant / Creative
