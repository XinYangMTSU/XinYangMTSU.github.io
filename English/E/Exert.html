🌟 exert /ɪɡˈzɜːrt/

Part of speech: Verb

📖 As a verb (action):

To exert means to apply or use (a force, influence, or quality) deliberately or forcefully.

Example: She exerted all her strength to lift the heavy box. (= She used all her strength.)

🧹 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Apply
  • Use
  • Wield
  • Employ
  • Exercise

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "exert" comes from Latin roots.

📝 It comes from Latin ex(s)erere which means "to thrust out" or "put forth".

  • ex- = out
  • serere = to join or bind

🖍️ Originally, "exert" referred to putting oneself into action. Over time, it came to mean applying force, influence, or effort.

📊 Comparison
