🖼️ exhibit /ɪɡˈzɪbɪt/

Part of speech: Verb / Noun
词性：动词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

Exhibit has two common meanings:

  1. To show or display something publicly (verb).
  2. A public display of works of art or items of interest (noun).
exhibit 有两个常见含义：
1. 展示、陈列（动词）；
2. 展览、展品（名词）。

Example: The artist will exhibit her paintings in the city gallery.
例句：这位艺术家将在城市画廊展出她的画作。
Example: We visited a fascinating science exhibit at the museum.
例句：我们参观了博物馆一场引人入胜的科学展览。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Display 展示
  • Show 显示
  • Present 呈现
  • Demonstrate 演示
  • Reveal 揭示

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Exhibit" comes from Latin exhibere, meaning “to hold out” or “to present.” It is formed from ex- (out) + habere (to have or hold).
“exhibit” 源自拉丁语 exhibere，意思是“展示”或“提出”，由 ex（外）+ habere（有、持）组成。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: exhibere – to present, hold forth
拉丁语 exhibere：展示、呈现
⬇️
Modern English: exhibit – to show publicly
现代英语 exhibit：公开展示
