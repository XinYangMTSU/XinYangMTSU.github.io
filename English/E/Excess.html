➕ excess /ˈɛk.ses/ or /ɪkˈses/

Part of speech: Noun / Adjective
词性：名词 / 形容词

📖 Meaning:

As a noun: An amount that is more than necessary, allowed, or usual.
名词：过量；过度；超出。

As an adjective: More than is necessary; extra.
形容词：多余的；额外的；过度的。

Example (noun): He was fined for driving at an excess speed.
例句（名词）：他因超速驾驶被罚款。
Example (adjective): Remove any excess water before cooking.
例句（形容词）：烹饪前请去除多余的水分。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Surplus 过剩
  • Overabundance 过多
  • Overflow 溢出
  • Superfluity 多余

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Excess" comes from Latin excedere, meaning "to go beyond." The prefix ex- means "out" and cedere means "to go."
“Excess” 来自拉丁语 excedere，意为“超出”，其中 ex- 表示“向外”，cedere 表示“走”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: excedere – to go beyond
拉丁语 excedere：超越，超过
⬇️
Old French: exces – exceeding the normal
古法语 exces：超过正常限度
⬇️
Modern English: excess – more than needed
现代英语 excess：超过所需的量

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Surplus Formal The store had a surplus of goods. Neutral
Superfluous Academic That comment was superfluous. Critical
Excess Neutral to Formal They drank to excess. Often Negative
