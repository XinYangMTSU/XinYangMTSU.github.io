👁️‍🗨️ eyesore /ˈaɪ.sɔːr/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

Something that is very ugly, especially a building or place that spoils the appearance of a scene.
非常难看的东西，尤指破坏景观的建筑或地方；眼中钉。

Example: That abandoned factory is a real eyesore in the neighborhood.
例句：那个废弃的工厂真是这个社区的眼中钉。
Example: The giant billboard was considered an eyesore by many residents.
例句：许多居民认为那个巨大的广告牌非常碍眼。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Blight 破坏景观的事物
  • Disfigurement 毁容；损坏
  • Ugly spot 丑陋之处
  • Nuisance 讨厌的东西

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Eyesore" is a compound of “eye” and “sore,” suggesting something painful or offensive to look at. The term has been used since the 1500s to describe visual ugliness.
“Eyesore” 是由 “eye”（眼睛）和 “sore”（痛处）组成的复合词，表示“令人不适的视觉”。早在16世纪就用来描述视觉上的丑陋。

🧭 Word Evolution

Middle English: eyesore – something that hurts to look at
中古英语 eyesore：看着令人不舒服的东西
⬇️
Modern English: eyesore – ugly or unpleasant to see
现代英语 eyesore：难看或破坏美感的事物
