🌟 elusive /ɪˈluːsɪv/

Part of speech: Adjective

📖 Meaning:

Something elusive is difficult to find, catch, understand, or define.

Example: The solution to the puzzle remained elusive for days. (= It was hard to figure out.)
Example: The deer was elusive, disappearing into the forest before they could get close. (= Hard to catch or approach.)

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Hard to catch
  • Difficult to grasp
  • Slippery
  • Mysterious
  • Abstract
  • Evasive

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "elusive".

📝It comes from the Latin verb: eludere, meaning "to elude or escape from."

  • e- = out or away
  • ludere = to play or mock

✍️ Originally, it referred to evading something by trickery or skillful movement.

💡 In modern English, it can apply to anything that is difficult to define, capture, or understand.
