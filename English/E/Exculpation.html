⚖️ exculpation /ˌɛkˌskʌlˈpeɪʃən/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

The act of clearing someone from alleged fault or guilt; proving that someone is not guilty of wrongdoing.
辩解；开脱；使无罪；证明无过错。

Example: The new evidence led to his exculpation and release from prison.
例句：新证据导致他被宣判无罪并获释。
Example: Her exculpation came after a long legal battle.
例句：经过一场漫长的法律斗争，她终于被判无罪。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Acquittal 无罪释放
  • Vindication 证明清白
  • Justification 正当理由
  • Absolution 赦免

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Exculpation" comes from Latin exculpare, from ex- (out of) + culpa (blame or guilt), meaning "to clear from blame."
“Exculpation” 源自拉丁语 exculpare，由 ex-（出去）和 culpa（过失、罪责）组成，意为“使脱罪”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: exculpare – to clear from blame
拉丁语 exculpare：洗清罪责

Old French: exculpacion – legal clearing of guilt
古法语 exculpacion：法律上的免责

Modern English: exculpation – the act of clearing from blame or guilt
现代英语 exculpation：洗脱罪名的行为

📊 Comparison
