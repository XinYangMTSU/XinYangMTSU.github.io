🐦 exotic /ɪɡˈzɑːtɪk/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Strikingly unusual or different; from a foreign country and often seen as exciting or unusual.
异国风情的；奇异的；与众不同的。

Example: The garden was filled with exotic plants from South America.
例句：花园里种满了来自南美洲的奇异植物。
Example: She wore an exotic outfit made of silk and feathers.
例句：她穿着一件由丝绸和羽毛制成的异国服装。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Unusual 不寻常的
  • Foreign 外国的
  • Strange 奇怪的
  • Alluring 迷人的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Exotic" comes from the Greek word exōtikos, meaning “from the outside.” It entered English via Latin and French, originally referring to anything foreign or strange.
“Exotic” 源自希腊语 exōtikos，意为“外来的”，通过拉丁语和法语传入英语，最初指的是异国或陌生的事物。

🧭 Word Evolution

Greek: exōtikos – from outside
希腊语 exōtikos：来自外部的

Latin: exoticus – foreign
拉丁语 exoticus：外国的

Modern English: exotic – strikingly unusual or foreign
现代英语 exotic：异国的，奇异的

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Foreign Neutral He traveled to a foreign country. Objective
Strange Informal The food tasted strange to me. Unfamiliar
Exotic Descriptive She collects exotic spices from around the world. Colorful / Admiring
