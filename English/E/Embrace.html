🤗 embrace /ɪmˈbreɪs/

Part of speech: Verb / Noun
词性：动词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

(v.) To hold someone closely in your arms, especially as a sign of affection; to accept or support something willingly or enthusiastically.
(n.) The act of holding someone closely; an act of acceptance or support.
（动词）拥抱；欣然接受
（名词）拥抱；接受

Example: She embraced her friend tightly after the long trip.
例句：长途旅行后她紧紧地拥抱了朋友。
Example: The company has embraced new technology to stay competitive.
例句：为了保持竞争力，公司已欣然接受新技术。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Hug 拥抱
  • Welcome 欢迎；接受
  • Accept 接受
  • Adopt 采纳；采取

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Embrace" comes from Old French embracer, from Latin bracchium meaning “arm.” It originally referred to “putting arms around” and evolved to include accepting ideas or changes.
“Embrace” 源自古法语 embracer，来自拉丁语 bracchium，意为“手臂”。最初指“用手臂环绕”，后来引申为接受观点或改变。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: bracchium – arm
拉丁语 bracchium：手臂

Old French: embracer – to hug, to clasp
古法语 embracer：拥抱，抱紧

Modern English: embrace – to hug or accept something willingly
现代英语 embrace：拥抱；欣然接受

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Hug Informal They hugged each other after the game. Warm / Casual
Adopt Formal The city adopted a new recycling policy. Administrative
Embrace Neutral/Formal They embraced the idea of change with open minds. Open / Positive
