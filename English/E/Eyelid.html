👁️ eyelid /ˈaɪlɪd/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

The fold of skin that covers and protects the eye when closed.
眼睑；覆盖和保护眼睛的皮肤褶皱。

Example: Her eyelids felt heavy, and she struggled to stay awake.
例句：她的眼皮感到沉重，努力保持清醒。
Example: He had surgery to correct a drooping eyelid.
例句：他接受了手术来矫正下垂的眼睑。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Lid 盖子（身体部位时指眼睑）
  • Palpebra （医学术语）眼睑
  • Eye covering 眼部覆盖物
  • Upper lid / Lower lid 上眼睑 / 下眼睑

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Eyelid" is a compound word from “eye” + “lid,” first recorded in English in the late 14th century. "Lid" originally meant a cover or flap.
eyelid 是由 eye（眼睛）和 lid（盖子）组成的复合词，最早出现在14世纪晚期。lid 最初的意思是盖子或遮盖物。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: liðe – joint, limb, flap
古英语 liðe：关节、肢体、遮盖物
⬇️
Middle English: lid – cover
中世纪英语 lid：盖子
⬇️
Modern English: eyelid – cover for the eye
现代英语 eyelid：眼睑

📊 Comparison

Palpebra Medical The palpebra protects the eye from foreign matter. Technical
