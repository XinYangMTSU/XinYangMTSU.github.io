🌟 exemplary /ɪɡˈzɛmpləri/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Serving as a desirable model; representing the best of its kind. Also, sometimes used to describe something that is a warning to others (punishment or behavior).
模范的；值得效仿的。有时也指起警示作用的（如惩罚）。

Example: She received an award for her exemplary service to the community.
例句：她因对社区的模范服务而获奖。
Example: The judge gave an exemplary sentence to deter future crimes.
例句：法官判了一个具有警示作用的刑罚，以震慑未来的犯罪行为。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Model 模范
  • Ideal 理想的
  • Commendable 值得称赞的
  • Illustrative 说明性的；示范性的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Exemplary" comes from Latin exemplum meaning "example." It evolved through Middle French exemplaire and Middle English.
“Exemplary” 源自拉丁语 exemplum，意为“例子”，经由中古法语 exemplaire 和中古英语演变而来。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: exemplum – example
拉丁语 exemplum：例子

Middle French: exemplaire – worthy of imitation
中古法语 exemplaire：值得效仿的

Modern English: exemplary – serving as a model
现代英语 exemplary：模范的；起示范作用的

📊 Comparison

Word Context Example Use Tone
Ideal Abstract / Aspirational He is the ideal candidate for the job. Positive / Formal
Commendable Behavior / Effort Her honesty was commendable. Respectful / Positive
Exemplary Behavior / Punishment She showed exemplary courage during the crisis. High Praise / Sometimes Cautionary
