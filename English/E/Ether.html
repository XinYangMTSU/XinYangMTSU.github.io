🌌 ether /ˈiː.θər/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

Ether historically refers to a substance once believed to fill all space and carry light waves; in modern chemistry, it also refers to a type of organic compound.
“ether” 在历史上指一种被认为充满宇宙、能够传递光波的物质；在现代化学中也指一种有机化合物（醚）。

Example: In the 19th century, scientists believed that light traveled through ether.
例句：在19世纪，科学家认为光是通过以太传播的。
Example: Ether is also used as a solvent in chemistry labs.
例句：醚也常作为溶剂用于化学实验室中。

🧩 Synonyms

  • Upper air 高空
  • Heavens 天空（古义）
  • Invisible medium 无形介质
  • Aether (archaic spelling) 以太（旧拼写）
  • Solvent (modern use) 溶剂（现代用法）

🔍 Etymology of the word

📚 “Ether” comes from the Greek word *aithēr*, meaning “upper air” or “pure, bright air.” It was thought to be the fifth element beyond earth, water, air, and fire.
“ether” 来自希腊语 *aithēr*，意为“高空”或“纯净明亮的空气”，被认为是继土、水、气、火之后的“第五元素”。
