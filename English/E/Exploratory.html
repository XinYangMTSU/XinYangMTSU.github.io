🌟 exploratory /ɪkˈsplɔːrəˌtɔːri/

Part of speech: Adjective

📖 Meaning:

Describing something that is done to discover more about something, to investigate, or to experiment—often without a fixed goal.

Example: The student conducted an exploratory project on renewable energy. (= A project that explores and investigates, rather than solves a specific problem.)

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Investigative
  • Experimental
  • Inquisitive
  • Probing
  • Curious
  • Preliminary

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word exploratory is formed from the verb explore and the suffix -atory, which means "related to."

📝 The verb explore comes from Latin explorare, meaning “to search out, investigate.”

  • ex- = out
  • plorare = to cry out or call out (as in scouting or searching)

💡 So “exploratory” describes the nature of seeking, testing, or discovering something new.
