🙏 evangelist /ɪˈvæn.dʒə.lɪst/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

1. A person who seeks to convert others to the Christian faith, especially by public preaching.
2. More generally, a passionate advocate of a particular cause or idea.
1. 传道者，布道者（尤指公开传教的基督徒）
2. 热情倡导者；鼓吹者。

Example: The evangelist preached to a large crowd in the city square.
例句：那位布道者在城市广场向大批人群传教。
Example: She's a tech evangelist, promoting the use of AI in education.
例句：她是技术倡导者，致力于推动人工智能在教育中的应用。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Preacher 传道者
  • Missionary 传教士
  • Advocate 倡导者
  • Promoter 推动者

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Evangelist" comes from Greek euangelion meaning "good news" or "gospel." The term was used in the early Christian church for writers of the four Gospels and later came to mean any active preacher or advocate of the faith.
“Evangelist” 源自希腊语 euangelion，意为“好消息”或“福音”。最初用于指四福音书的作者，后泛指基督教布道者或积极倡导者。

🧭 Word Evolution

Greek: euangelion – good news
希腊语 euangelion：好消息

Latin: evangelista – preacher of the gospel
拉丁语 evangelista：福音传播者

Modern English: evangelist – religious preacher or passionate advocate
现代英语 evangelist：传道者或热情倡导者

📊 Comparison

Advocate Neutral/Formal He is an advocate for mental health awareness. Supportive
Evangelist Formal / Religious / Passionate A software evangelist promoting open source. Passionate / Zealous
