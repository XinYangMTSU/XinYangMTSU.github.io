🌟 endeavor /ɪnˈdevər/

Part of speech: Noun or Verb

📖 As a verb (action):

To try hard to do or achieve something.

Example: She endeavored to finish her research project on time.

📖 As a noun (a thing):

An attempt to achieve a goal; an effort.

Example: Climbing Mount Everest is a challenging endeavor.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Attempt
  • Effort
  • Strive
  • Undertaking
  • Try
  • Aim

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "endeavor" comes from Middle English endeveren, meaning “to make an effort.”

📝It is rooted in the phrase "put oneself in devoir", from Old French devoir (duty), meaning to make it your duty to do something.

💡 Over time, it evolved into “endeavor” in English, with the sense of striving or attempting something with determination.

🧭 Word Evolution

Old French: devoir (duty)
⬇️
Middle English: endeveren (to make an effort)
⬇️
Modern English: endeavor (to try, to strive)
