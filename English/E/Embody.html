🌟 embody /ɪmˈbɑːdi/

Part of speech: Verb

📖 Meaning:

To embody something means to be a symbol or expression of it in a physical or visible way. It can also mean to include or contain something as a part of a larger whole.

Example: She embodies kindness and patience. (= She represents those qualities in her actions and personality.)

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Represent
  • Symbolize
  • Express
  • Personify
  • Incorporate
  • Manifest

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word embody comes from the prefix em- (a variant of en-, meaning "in") and body.

  • em- = into
  • body = a physical form

💡 So to “embody” something means to put an idea, value, or quality into a body, or to make it real and visible.

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: bodig (body) + en- (in)
⬇️
Middle English: embodyen (to give form to)
⬇️
Modern English: embody /ɪmˈbɑːdi/
