🌟 rhythm /ˈrɪðəm/

Part of speech: Noun

📖 As a noun (a thing):

Rhythm is the regular pattern of sounds or movements, especially in music, speech, or the human body.

Example: The drummer kept a steady rhythm throughout the song.

📖 Broader meaning:

It can also refer to a regular pattern or cycle, such as the rhythm of life or the rhythm of the seasons.

Example: She quickly fell into the rhythm of her new job.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Beat
  • Cadence
  • Flow
  • Pulse
  • Tempo
  • Pattern

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "rhythm".

📝It comes from the Greek word rhythmos, meaning "measured flow or movement."

  • rhythmos = measured motion or time

✍️ This word was adopted into Latin as rhythmus and then into Old French before entering English.
