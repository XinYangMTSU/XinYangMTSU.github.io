🌟 revved-up /rɛvd ʌp/

Part of speech: Adjective (informal, idiomatic)

📖 Meaning:

“Revved-up” describes something that has been made more intense, energized, exciting, or fast-paced. Originally from car engines, it now often refers to feelings, activities, games, or events that are more thrilling or powerful than usual.

Example: The teacher turned the old board game into a revved-up classroom competition. (= She made it more exciting and engaging.)

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Intensified
  • Boosted
  • Hyped-up
  • Supercharged
  • Exhilarated
  • Amped up

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The term “revved-up” comes from the verb rev, which is short for revolution, as in engine revolutions per minute (RPM).

🛠️ To "rev" an engine means to increase its speed without moving the vehicle.

💬 Over time, this term began to be used metaphorically to describe anything made more energetic, exciting, or powerful—like a revved-up game or a revved-up crowd.

🧭 Word Evolution

Mechanical Origin:
Rev (shortened from “revolution”) → to rev up (an engine) → revved-up (energized, intensified)
⬇️
Modern Idiomatic Use:
A revved-up crowd, revved-up debate, or revved-up version of a game
