🧾 repeal /rɪˈpiːl/

Part of speech: Verb / Noun
词性：动词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

Verb: To officially cancel a law or regulation.
Noun: The act of revoking or withdrawing a law.
动词：正式废除（法律或法规）。
名词：废除的行为。

Example: The law was repealed after years of protest.
例句：该法律在多年抗议后被废除。
Example: The government voted for the repeal of outdated regulations.
例句：政府投票决定废除过时的法规。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Abolish 废除
  • Revoke 撤销
  • Annul 宣告无效
  • Rescind 撤回

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Repeal" comes from Anglo-French repeler and Latin repellere, meaning “to drive back or call back.” It originally referred to revoking official decisions.
repeal 来自中古法语 repeler 和拉丁语 repellere，意为“驱回”或“撤回”，最初用于指撤销官方决定。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: repellere – to drive back
拉丁语 repellere：驱赶回去
⬇️
Old French: repeler – to call back
古法语 repeler：召回
⬇️
Modern English: repeal – to officially cancel a law
现代英语 repeal：正式废除法律

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Abolish Formal / Strong The law was abolished after public pressure. Final / Legal
Rescind Formal The order was rescinded after review. Technical / Administrative
Repeal Neutral / Legal They voted to repeal the ban. Official / Legislative
