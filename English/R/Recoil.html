🔙 recoil /rɪˈkɔɪl/

Part of speech: Verb / Noun
词性：动词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

To suddenly spring or jump back in fear, horror, or disgust; the backward movement of something (like a gun) after force is applied.
（因害怕、厌恶等）猛地退缩；反冲。名词指反作用力或后坐力。

Example: She recoiled in fear when the snake hissed.
例句：蛇嘶叫时，她吓得猛地往后退。
Example: The rifle had a strong recoil after it was fired.
例句：步枪开火后产生了强烈的后坐力。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Flinch 畏缩
  • Draw back 退缩
  • Backfire 产生反作用
  • Retreat 撤退

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Recoil" comes from the Old French word reculer, meaning “to draw back,” from Latin re- (back) + culare (to turn). It originally described physical motion but later included emotional responses like fear or disgust.
“Recoil” 源自古法语 reculer，意为“后退”，再往上追溯来自拉丁语 re-（向后）+ culare（转动），最初用于描述身体动作，后来也用于表达情绪反应，如恐惧或厌恶。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: re- + culare – to turn back
拉丁语 re- + culare：向后转

Old French: reculer – to draw back
古法语 reculer：后退

Modern English: recoil – to spring back from fear or force
现代英语 recoil：因恐惧或力量而后退
