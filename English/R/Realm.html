👑 realm /relm/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

1. A kingdom or territory ruled by a monarch.
2. A field or domain of activity, interest, or knowledge.
1. 王国，领域（指由君主统治的领土）；
2. 领域，范围（指活动、兴趣或知识的范围）。

Example 1: The king ruled over a vast realm with fairness and wisdom.
例句 1：国王英明地统治着辽阔的王国。
Example 2: She works in the realm of artificial intelligence research.
例句 2：她从事人工智能研究领域的工作。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Kingdom 王国
  • Domain 领域
  • Territory 领土
  • Sphere 范围

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Realm" comes from Old French reaume, derived from Latin regimen (rule or government), and ultimately from regere (to rule). It originally referred to a kingdom, then broadened to include conceptual fields.
“Realm” 来自古法语 reaume，源自拉丁语 regimen（统治、管理），其词根 regere 意为“统治”。最初指“王国”，后来引申为抽象领域。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: regere – to rule
拉丁语 regere：统治

Old French: reaume – kingdom
古法语 reaume：王国

Modern English: realm – kingdom or area of interest
现代英语 realm：王国；领域

📊 Comparison

Word Primary Use Formality Example
Kingdom Political / Historical Formal The kingdom prospered under wise leadership.
Realm Political or Abstract Formal In the realm of science, progress is rapid.
Field Academic / Practical Neutral She specializes in the field of linguistics.
