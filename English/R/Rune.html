🔮 rune /ruːn/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A rune is a character from ancient alphabets used by Germanic peoples, often thought to have magical or mystical powers.
rune 是古代日耳曼文字中的一种字符，通常被认为具有魔法或神秘的力量。

Example: The stone was carved with ancient runes believed to offer protection.
例句：这块石头上刻有古代符文，据说可以带来保护。
Example: She wore a necklace with a Viking rune symbolizing strength.
例句：她戴着一条刻有象征力量的维京符文的项链。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Symbol 符号
  • Inscription 铭文
  • Glyph 字形符号
  • Marking 刻记
  • Charm 咒语符

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Rune" comes from Old Norse rún, meaning "secret" or "mystery," related to the Proto-Germanic root rūnō meaning “secret, whisper.”
rune 源自古诺斯语 rún，意为“秘密”或“神秘”，与原始日耳曼语 rūnō（秘密，低语）相关。

🧭 Word Evolution

Proto-Germanic: rūnō – secret, whisper
原始日耳曼语 rūnō：秘密，低语
⬇️
Old Norse: rún – mystery, magical letter
古诺斯语 rún：神秘，魔法文字
⬇️
Modern English: rune – ancient symbol, often magical
现代英语 rune：古老的神秘符文
