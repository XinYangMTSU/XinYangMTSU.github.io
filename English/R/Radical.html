🌱 radical /ˈrædɪkəl/

Part of speech: Adjective / Noun
词性：形容词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

As an adjective: Relating to or affecting the fundamental nature of something; far-reaching or extreme.
根本的；彻底的；极端的。

As a noun: A person who advocates for complete or significant political or social reform.
激进分子；主张彻底改革的人。

Example: The scientist proposed a radical new theory that challenged the mainstream view.
例句：这位科学家提出了一个激进的新理论，挑战了主流观点。
Example: Radicals in the group demanded immediate change in leadership.
例句：组织中的激进分子要求领导层立即更换。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Revolutionary 革命性的
  • Extreme 极端的
  • Fundamental 根本的
  • Progressive 进步的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Radical" comes from Latin radicalis, meaning “of or having roots,” from radix, meaning “root.” Originally, it referred to going to the root or origin of something.
“Radical” 源自拉丁语 radicalis，意为“根本的”，来自 radix，意为“根”，最初指追本溯源。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: radix – root
拉丁语 radix：根

Late Latin: radicalis – relating to the root
晚期拉丁语 radicalis：关于根的

Modern English: radical – fundamental or extreme
现代英语 radical：根本的；极端的
