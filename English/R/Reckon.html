🤔 reckon /ˈrekən/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To think or believe something; to estimate or calculate; to consider or regard something in a particular way.
认为；估计；把……看作。

Example: I reckon we’ll arrive around noon.
例句：我估计我们中午左右到。
Example: She’s reckoned to be one of the best violinists in the country.
例句：她被认为是全国最好的小提琴家之一。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Think 认为
  • Believe 相信
  • Estimate 估计
  • Suppose 猜想；设想

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Reckon" comes from Old English gerecenian, meaning “to recount or relate,” and later Middle English rekenen from Old Frisian and Middle Dutch, meaning “to count or calculate.”
“Reckon” 源自古英语 gerecenian，意为“讲述、叙述”，后在中古英语中演变为 rekenen，意为“计算、估算”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: gerecenian – to explain or recount
古英语 gerecenian：讲述、解释

Middle English: rekenen – to count
中古英语 rekenen：计算

Modern English: reckon – to estimate, believe, or consider
现代英语 reckon：估计，认为

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Suppose Neutral I suppose we could try again tomorrow. Informal
Estimate Formal We estimate the total cost at $500. Practical
Reckon Informal I reckon he’ll be late again. Casual / Conversational
