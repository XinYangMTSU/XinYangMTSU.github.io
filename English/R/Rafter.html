🪚 rafter /ˈræf.tər/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A structural beam that supports the roof of a building. Rafters typically run from the ridge or peak of the roof down to the eaves, forming the framework of a sloped roof.
椽子；支撑屋顶的梁，通常从屋脊延伸到屋檐，用于形成斜屋顶的结构。

Example: The old barn had exposed wooden rafters under the roof.
例句：这座老谷仓的屋顶下有裸露的木椽。
Example: Workers carefully placed the rafters to form the roof’s pitch.
例句：工人们小心地安放椽子，以构成屋顶的斜面。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Beam 梁
  • Support 支撑物
  • Joist 搁栅；托梁
  • Framework 框架结构

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Rafter" comes from Old English ræfter, from Proto-Germanic rafteraz, meaning "beam" or "pole." It has long been used to refer to structural elements of a roof.
“Rafter” 源自古英语 ræfter，来自原始日耳曼语 rafteraz，意为“横梁”或“杆子”，长期用于指屋顶结构的支撑部分。

🧭 Word Evolution

Proto-Germanic: rafteraz – beam, support
原始日耳曼语 rafteraz：梁，支撑

Old English: ræfter – rafter or roof beam
古英语 ræfter：屋梁

Modern English: rafter – a roof-supporting beam
现代英语 rafter：用于支撑屋顶的梁
