🧠 retain /rɪˈteɪn/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To retain means to keep possession of something, or to continue to have something in memory or in use.
retain 意为“保留、保持”，指继续拥有某物，或将信息保存在记忆中。

Example: It's difficult to retain all the information you learn in one day.
例句：一天内学到的所有信息很难全部记住。
Example: The company decided to retain all its employees during the restructuring.
例句：公司决定在重组期间保留所有员工。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Keep 保持
  • Preserve 保存
  • Maintain 维持
  • Hold on to 紧握；不放弃
  • Remember 记住

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Retain" comes from Latin retinere, which combines re- ("back") and tenere ("to hold").
retain 源自拉丁语 retinere，由 re-（向后）和 tenere（握住）组成。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: tenere – to hold
拉丁语 tenere：握住
⬇️
Latin compound: retinere – to keep back
拉丁语 retinere：保留、维持
⬇️
Modern English: retain – to keep or remember
现代英语 retain：保留、记住

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Keep Informal Please keep the receipt for your records. Everyday / neutral
Preserve Formal Efforts are being made to preserve historic buildings. Serious / respectful
Retain Neutral / Academic Students often struggle to retain what they learn without review. Precise / cognitive
