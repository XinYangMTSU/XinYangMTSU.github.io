🧱 rigid /ˈrɪdʒɪd/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Not flexible; unable to bend or be changed easily. Can describe physical stiffness or inflexible attitudes or rules.
僵硬的；死板的；不灵活的。

Example: The metal frame is rigid and won't bend under pressure.
例句：金属框架很坚硬，不会在压力下弯曲。
Example: He has a rigid attitude toward discipline.
例句：他对纪律持有非常严格的态度。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Stiff 僵硬的
  • Inflexible 不灵活的
  • Strict 严格的
  • Unbending 不屈的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Rigid" comes from Latin rigidus, meaning “stiff” or “hard,” derived from rigēre — “to be stiff.” It entered English in the late Middle Ages.
rigid 源自拉丁语 rigidus，意为“僵硬的”，来自 rigēre（变硬）。在中世纪晚期进入英语。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: rigēre – to be stiff
拉丁语 rigēre：变硬
⬇️
Latin: rigidus – stiff, hard
拉丁语 rigidus：坚硬的，僵化的
⬇️
Modern English: rigid – not flexible or adaptable
现代英语 rigid：僵硬的，不灵活的

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone

Inflexible Formal The rules are inflexible and outdated. Critical
