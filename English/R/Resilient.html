🌟 resilient /rɪˈzɪljənt/

Part of speech: Adjective

📖 Meaning:

“Resilient” describes someone or something that can recover quickly from difficulties, damage, or tough situations—both emotionally and physically.

Example: Children can be amazingly resilient after facing hardship.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Strong
  • Tough
  • Hardy
  • Flexible
  • Adaptable
  • Enduring

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s explore the origin of "resilient".

📝Comes from Latin resilire meaning “to leap back” or “rebound”.

  • re- = back
  • salire = to jump or leap

✍️ So "resilient" originally meant something that bounces back—just like how a resilient person bounces back after challenges.
