🛡️ resistance /rɪˈzɪs.təns/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

1. The act or power of opposing or withstanding something (such as force, pressure, or change).
2. In physics, the opposition to the flow of electric current in a circuit.
1. 抵抗；反抗；抗力。
2. （物理）电阻，对电流的阻碍。

Example 1: The wall provides strong resistance to wind and water.
例句 1：这面墙对风和水具有很强的抗力。
Example 2: Copper has low electrical resistance.
例句 2：铜的电阻很低。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Opposition 反对
  • Defiance 反抗
  • Barrier 障碍
  • Drag (physics) 阻力（物理）

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Resistance" comes from the Latin word resistentia, from resistere, meaning "to withstand" or "stand back." It entered English via Old French in the 14th century.
“Resistance” 来自拉丁语 resistentia，源自 resistere，意为“抵抗、抵御”，于14世纪通过古法语进入英语。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: resistere – to resist, stand against
拉丁语 resistere：抵抗，顶住

Old French: resistance – opposition
古法语 resistance：反抗

Modern English: resistance – opposition, defense, or electrical opposition
现代英语 resistance：抵抗、对抗、电阻

📊 Comparison

Word Meaning Field Example
Opposition Act of resisting or fighting back Politics / Debate The proposal met strong opposition.
Resistance Force that opposes motion or change Physics / Social He built up resistance to the disease.
Drag Resistance due to air or fluid Physics Airplanes experience drag during flight.
