🌟 Rote /roʊt/

Part of speech: Noun

📖 Meaning:

Rote refers to the process of learning or memorizing something through repetition, often without truly understanding the meaning.

Example: She learned the multiplication tables by rote, reciting them over and over again.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Memorization
  • Repetition
  • Drill
  • Mechanized learning
  • Parroting

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word rote comes from Middle English and possibly from Old French rote (a type of wheel), implying repetition or turning over.

📝 Over time, it came to mean learning something in a repetitive, mechanical way.

🧭 Word Evolution

Old French: rote (wheel, turn)
⬇️
Middle English: rote (habitual repetition)
⬇️
Modern English: rote (repetition-based learning) /roʊt/

📊 Comparison
