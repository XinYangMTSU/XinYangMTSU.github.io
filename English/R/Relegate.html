📉 relegate /ˈrelɪɡeɪt/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To assign to a lower position, rank, or less important role.
贬低；降级；把……降到较低地位。

Example: She was relegated to a desk job after the reorganization.
例句：重组后，她被调到文职岗位。
Example: The team was relegated to a lower division after losing the season.
例句：球队赛季失利后降级到了较低级别的联赛。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Demote 降职
  • Downgrade 降级
  • Assign 指派
  • Dismiss 贬退

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Relegate" comes from Latin relegare, meaning “to send away, remove, or banish.”
relegate 源自拉丁语 relegare，意为“送走、移开、放逐”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: relegare – to send away
拉丁语 relegare：送走
⬇️
Late Middle English: relegaten – banish or assign to a lower place
中古英语 relegaten：放逐、降级
⬇️
Modern English: relegate – to assign a lower status
现代英语 relegate：降级、贬职

📊 Comparison

Word Connotation Example Use Context
Relegate Neutral / Negative The player was relegated to the bench. Work / Sports
Demote Negative He was demoted due to poor performance. Workplace
Assign Neutral She was assigned a new task. General
