🛬 repatriate /riˈpeɪtrieɪt/

Part of speech: Verb / Noun
词性：动词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

Verb: To return someone to their own country, especially after a war or during immigration processes.
动词：把（人）遣返回国，尤其指战争后或移民过程中的遣返。

Noun: A person who has been repatriated.
名词：被遣返者。

Example: The government agreed to repatriate the stranded tourists.
例句：政府同意把滞留的游客遣返回国。
Example: The repatriates were welcomed back with support programs.
例句：被遣返的人得到了欢迎和支援项目的帮助。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Send back 送回
  • Return 返回
  • Deport (context-dependent) 驱逐出境（语境相关）
  • Reintegrate (for people returning home) 重新融入

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 From Latin re- meaning “back” and patria meaning “native land.” “Repatriate” literally means “to go back to the fatherland.”
来自拉丁语 “re-”（回）和 “patria”（祖国），repatriate 的字面意思是“回到祖国”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: repatriare – to return to one’s country
拉丁语 repatriaret：回归祖国
⬇️
Modern English: repatriate – to send back to country of origin
现代英语 repatriate：遣返回原籍国

📊 Comparison

Word Meaning Example Use Context
Repatriate Send back to own country The embassy helped repatriate citizens during the crisis. Neutral / Official
Deport Forcefully remove from a country He was deported for overstaying his visa. Legal / Negative
