💡 revelatory /ˈrevələˌtɔːri/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Making something known or revealed that was previously hidden or unknown, especially something surprising or important.
启示性的；揭示性的；使人突然领悟的。

Example: Her book was deeply revelatory about the struggles of refugees.
例句：她的书深刻揭示了难民所面临的困境。
Example: It was a revelatory moment when he realized he had misunderstood her for years.
例句：当他意识到多年来误解了她，那一刻对他来说是一个启示。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Insightful 富有洞察力的
  • Illuminating 启发性的
  • Eye-opening 令人惊奇的；使人大开眼界的
  • Informative 信息丰富的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Revelatory" is formed from the verb "reveal," which comes from Latin revelare (to uncover or lay bare). The suffix -tory turns it into an adjective meaning “serving to reveal.”
“Revelatory” 源自动词 reveal（揭示），该词来自拉丁语 revelare（揭开、揭露）。后缀 -tory 表示“具有……功能的”，即“具有揭示功能的”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: revelare – to uncover
拉丁语 revelare：揭示，揭开

Middle English: revelen – to reveal or make known
中古英语 revelen：揭露，透露

Modern English: revelatory – revealing surprising or important truth
现代英语 revelatory：揭示性的，启示性的

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Insightful Neutral Her comments were insightful and constructive. Analytical
Illuminating Formal The report was illuminating for the policy makers. Explanatory
Revelatory Formal The documentary was revelatory about hidden corruption. Powerful / Surprising
