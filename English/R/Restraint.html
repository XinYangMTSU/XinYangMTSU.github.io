✋ restraint /rɪˈstreɪnt/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

The act of controlling your emotions or actions; a limitation or condition that keeps someone or something under control.
克制；限制；控制情绪或行为的行为或条件。

Example: He showed admirable restraint by not responding to the insults.
例句：他面对侮辱仍能保持克制，令人敬佩。
Example: The government imposed restraints on public gatherings.
例句：政府对公众集会实施了限制。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Control 控制
  • Self-discipline 自律
  • Limitation 限制
  • Suppression 压抑

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Restraint" comes from Old French restreinte, from Latin restringere, meaning "to bind fast or hold back".
“restraint” 来自古法语 restreinte，源于拉丁语 restringere，意为“紧束，抑制”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: restringere – to bind or limit
拉丁语 restringere：捆绑或限制
⬇️
Old French: restreinte – limitation
古法语 restreinte：限制
⬇️
Modern English: restraint – act of holding back
现代英语 restraint：克制、限制

📊 Comparison

Word Usage Example Use Context
Restraint Formal / Emotional She spoke with restraint about the tragedy. Behavior / Law
Control General He has good control over his temper. Personal / Situational
Suppression Strong / Negative The suppression of protests drew criticism. Political / Psychological
