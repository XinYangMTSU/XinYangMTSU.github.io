📢 rant /rænt/

Part of speech: Verb / Noun
词性：动词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

To speak or shout at length in an angry, impassioned way (v); a long, loud, and often angry speech (n).
（动）怒吼；咆哮；激烈地长篇大论地说话。
（名）咆哮；激烈的长篇言论。

Example: He went on a rant about the poor customer service.
例句：他对糟糕的客户服务大发雷霆。
Example: She ranted for twenty minutes about politics.
例句：她对政治问题激烈地讲了二十分钟。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Tirade 长篇激烈的演讲
  • Diatribe 抨击性的演讲
  • Outburst （愤怒的）爆发
  • Lecture 训斥；教训

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Rant" likely originated in the 16th century Dutch word ranten, meaning “to talk foolishly” or “to rave.”
“Rant” 可能起源于 16 世纪的荷兰语 ranten，意为“胡说”或“狂叫”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Dutch: ranten – to rave, speak wildly
荷兰语 ranten：狂叫、胡说

Modern English: rant – to speak loudly and angrily
现代英语 rant：怒吼、咆哮

📊 Comparison
