✏️ revise /rɪˈvaɪz/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To change something in order to improve it, especially a piece of writing, a plan, or knowledge before an exam.
为了改进而修改某物，尤其是文章、计划，或考前复习知识。

Example: She revised her essay after receiving feedback from the teacher.
例句：她在收到老师的反馈后修改了文章。
Example: You should revise the key concepts before the exam.
例句：你应该在考试前复习一下关键概念。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Edit 编辑
  • Amend 修正
  • Update 更新
  • Review 复习

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Revise" comes from Latin revidēre, meaning “to look at again.”
revise 源自拉丁语 revidēre，意为“再次查看”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: revidēre – to look again
拉丁语 revidēre：再次查看
⬇️
Old French: reviser – to look at again
古法语 reviser：再次查看
⬇️
Modern English: revise – to edit or review
现代英语 revise：修改或复习

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Edit Neutral I edited the report for clarity. Technical / instructional
Review Neutral I reviewed the notes before the test. Academic
Revise Neutral / Formal She revised her proposal after the critique. Academic / precise
