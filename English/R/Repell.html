🛡️ repell /rɪˈpɛl/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To drive back or ward off; to resist effectively.
击退；抵制；使厌恶；排斥。

Example: The fortress was built to repell enemy attacks.
例句：这座堡垒是为了击退敌人的进攻而建造的。
Example: The strong smell repelled the insects.
例句：强烈的气味驱赶了昆虫。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Resist 抵抗
  • Drive away 赶走
  • Fend off 避开
  • Repulse 击退

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Repell" comes from Latin repellere, from re- (back) + pellere (to drive), meaning "to drive back."
“Repell” 源自拉丁语 repellere，由 re-（向后）和 pellere（驱使）组成，意为“击退”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: repellere – to drive back
拉丁语 repellere：击退

Old French: repel – to push back
古法语 repel：推回

Modern English: repell – to drive or push away
现代英语 repell：驱逐，排斥

📊 Comparison

Word Context Example Use Tone
Repulse Military / Emotion The army repulsed the invasion. Strong / Forceful
Fend off Everyday / Metaphorical She tried to fend off the criticism. Defensive
Repell General / Action The spray helps repell mosquitoes. Neutral
