🗣️ innit /ˈɪnɪt/

Part of speech: Contraction / Slang
词性：缩略语 / 俚语

📖 Meaning:

A British slang contraction of “isn’t it,” used for emphasis or to seek agreement in informal speech. It can be used regardless of the actual subject or verb in the sentence.
“Isn’t it”的缩写，英国俚语，常用于非正式对话中加强语气或寻求认同，有时并不与句子语法完全一致。

Example: It’s cold today, innit?
例句：今天很冷，对吧？
Example: You finished the whole pizza, innit?
例句：你把整张披萨都吃完了，对不对？

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Isn’t it 不是吗
  • Right? 对吧？
  • Yeah? 是吧？
  • Eh? (UK/Canada) 哎？（寻求认同）

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Innit" is a contracted form of “isn’t it,” originating in British English, especially among youth and in multicultural London English. Over time, it has evolved into a general-purpose tag question in casual speech.
“Innit” 是“isn’t it”的缩写，起源于英国英语，尤其在伦敦年轻人和多文化背景的英语中流行。随着时间推移，它演变成了用于口语中的通用语气词。

🧭 Word Evolution

Original Phrase: isn’t it – tag question
原始形式 isn’t it：附加疑问句

Colloquial Usage: innit – informal speech
口语形式 innit：用于非正式语境

Modern Use: Applies to various situations beyond “isn’t it”
现代用法：不限于“isn’t it”，广泛用于寻求认同
