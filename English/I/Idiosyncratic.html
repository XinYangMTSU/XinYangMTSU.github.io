🌀 idiosyncratic /ˌɪdiəʊsɪnˈkrætɪk/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Idiosyncratic refers to something highly individual or peculiar to a specific person, behavior, or style; unusual in a way that is characteristic of a particular individual.
idiosyncratic 的意思是“个人独特的、特有的”，通常用于描述某人独特的行为、思维方式或风格。

Example: His idiosyncratic writing style makes his novels immediately recognizable.
例句：他独特的写作风格使他的小说一眼就能被认出。
Example: She has an idiosyncratic way of dressing that sets her apart.
例句：她有一种与众不同的穿衣风格，使她与众不同。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Quirky 古怪的
  • Individualistic 有个性的
  • Unique 独特的
  • Eccentric 偏执的
  • Unconventional 非传统的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Idiosyncratic" comes from the Greek root idiosynkrasia, meaning "a peculiar temperament" — from idios (one’s own) + syn (with) + krasis (mixture).
idiosyncratic 源自希腊语 idiosynkrasia，意为“特有的性格”，由 idios（自己的）+ syn（一起）+ krasis（混合）组成。

🧭 Word Evolution

Greek: idiosynkrasia – peculiar temperament
希腊语 idiosynkrasia：特有的气质
⬇️
Late Latin: idiosyncrasia – personal disposition
晚期拉丁语 idiosyncrasia：个体特性
⬇️
Modern English: idiosyncratic – distinctive and individual
现代英语 idiosyncratic：独特的，有个性的
