🌟 ineptitude /ɪˈnɛp.tɪ.tuːd/

Part of speech: Noun

📖 Meaning:

Ineptitude means a lack of skill, ability, or competence in a particular area.

Example: His ineptitude at managing money eventually led him into debt.

🧹 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Incompetence
  • Inefficiency
  • Awkwardness
  • Clumsiness
  • Unskillfulness
  • Blundering

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "ineptitude".

📝 It comes from the Latin adjective ineptus, meaning "unsuitable, awkward" + the suffix -itude forming a noun of quality.

  • in- = not
  • aptus = fitting, suitable
  • -itude = state or quality

📌 So ineptitude means "the state of being not suitable or not skillful."
