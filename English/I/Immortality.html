🕊️ immortality /ˌɪmɔːrˈtæləti/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

The state of living forever or never dying; the ability to exist beyond death, often associated with myths, religion, or fame.
永生；不朽；指永远活着或不会死亡，常与神话、宗教或名声相关。

Example: Many ancient cultures believed in the soul’s immortality.
例句：许多古代文化相信灵魂可以永生。
Example: The artist achieved immortality through her timeless works.
例句：这位艺术家通过她永恒的作品获得了不朽。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Eternal life 永恒的生命
  • Perpetuity 永久
  • Undying fame 不朽的名声
  • Everlasting existence 永恒存在

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Immortality" comes from Latin immortalitas, from in- (not) + mortalitas (mortality), meaning “not subject to death.”
“Immortality” 来自拉丁语 immortalitas，由 in-（不）+ mortalitas（死亡、必死性）组成，意为“不会死的状态”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: immortalitas – undying
拉丁语 immortalitas：不死

Middle English: immortalite – eternal life
中古英语 immortalite：永生

Modern English: immortality – the state of living forever
现代英语 immortality：不朽，永生

📊 Comparison

Word Type Meaning Example
Mortality Noun The state of being subject to death He was reminded of his own mortality after the accident.
Immortality Noun Living forever Legends of immortality are found in many cultures.
