♾️ irrationality /ɪˌræʃəˈnæləti/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

Irrationality refers to the quality or state of being illogical, unreasonable, or not based on clear thinking. In math, it refers to a number that cannot be expressed as a simple fraction.
irrationality 的意思是“不合逻辑、不合理、不基于理性思考的状态”；在数学中，指不能表示为分数的数。

Example: The irrationality of the rumor caused unnecessary panic.
例句：这个谣言的不合理性引起了不必要的恐慌。
Example: Pi is famous for its mathematical irrationality.
例句：圆周率因其数学上的无理性而闻名。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Illogicality 不合逻辑
  • Unreasonableness 不合理
  • Absurdity 荒谬
  • Foolishness 愚蠢
  • Incoherence 不连贯

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Irrationality" comes from the Latin root irrationalis, meaning “not reasonable,” formed from in- (not) + rationalis (rational).
irrationality 源自拉丁语 irrationalis，意为“不合理的”，由 in-（否定）和 rationalis（理性的）组成。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: irrationalis – not reasonable or logical
拉丁语 irrationalis：不合理的，不合逻辑的
⬇️
Old French / Middle English: irrationalité – irrationality
中古法语 / 中古英语 irrationalité：不理性
⬇️
Modern English: irrationality – lack of reason or logic
现代英语 irrationality：缺乏理性或逻辑

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Absurdity Neutral / Emphatic The absurdity of the idea was obvious. Strong / critical
Illogicality Formal His argument was full of illogicality. Analytical
Irrationality Formal / Mathematical The irrationality of √2 is well-known. Precise / conceptual
