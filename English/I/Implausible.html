🌀 implausible /ɪmˈplɔːzəbəl/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Implausible means not seeming reasonable or likely to be true; difficult to believe.
implausible 的意思是“不合理的；难以置信的；似乎不真实的”。

Example: His excuse for being late sounded completely implausible.
例句：他迟到的借口听起来完全不可信。
Example: The movie's plot was so implausible that it became unintentionally funny.
例句：这部电影的情节太不合理了，结果反而变得滑稽。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Unbelievable 难以置信的
  • Far-fetched 牵强的
  • Incredible 不可信的
  • Unrealistic 不现实的
  • Absurd 荒谬的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Implausible" comes from Latin implausibilis, formed from in- (not) + plausibilis (deserving applause or approval), from plaudere, meaning "to clap."
implausible 来自拉丁语 implausibilis，由 in-（否定前缀）+ plausibilis（值得鼓掌的）组成，词根 plaudere 的意思是“鼓掌”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: implausibilis – not worthy of applause
拉丁语 implausibilis：不值得鼓掌的
⬇️
Modern English: implausible – not believable or realistic
现代英语 implausible：不可信的，似乎不真实的

📊 Comparison
