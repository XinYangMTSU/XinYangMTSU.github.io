❌ irredeemable /ˌɪr.ɪˈdiː.mə.bəl/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Irredeemable describes something that cannot be saved, corrected, or improved; it is hopeless or beyond repair.
“irredeemable” 意为无法挽回的、无法补救的、不可救药的。

Example: The plan was considered an irredeemable failure.
例句：这个计划被认为是彻底失败，无法挽回。
Example: Some believed he was an irredeemable villain.
例句：有人认为他是个无可救药的恶棍。

🧩 Synonyms

  • Hopeless 无望的
  • Unrecoverable 不可恢复的
  • Incurable 无法治愈的
  • Unfixable 无法修复的
  • Beyond repair 无法修补的

🔍 Etymology of the word

📚 "Irredeemable" comes from the Latin prefix ir- (not) and redimere (to buy back or redeem). It literally means “not able to be redeemed.”
“irredeemable” 源自拉丁语前缀 ir-（不）和动词 redimere（赎回），意为“无法赎回或挽救”。

📊 Comparison with Related Words

Word Definition Example Context
Irredeemable Cannot be saved or fixed Her mistakes made the situation irredeemable. Serious failure
Hopeless Without hope or chance of success He felt hopeless after the loss. Emotional, situational
Incurable Not able to be cured or corrected The disease was incurable. Medical, behavioral
