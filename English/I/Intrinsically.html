💡 intrinsically /ɪnˈtrɪn.zɪ.kli/

Part of speech: Adverb
词性：副词

📖 Meaning:

In an essential or natural way; as a fundamental quality of something.
从本质上；本身地；作为某物固有特性的一部分。

Example: The job is intrinsically rewarding.
例句：这份工作本身就令人感到有成就感。
Example: Human beings are intrinsically social creatures.
例句：人类本质上是社会性的生物。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Inherently 固有地
  • Essentially 本质上
  • By nature 从本性上
  • Fundamentally 根本上

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Intrinsically" comes from the Latin word intrinsecus, meaning “inward” or “on the inside.” It combines “intra” (within) and “secare” (to cut), implying something that is internal or essential.
intrinsically 来自拉丁语 intrinsecus，意思是“向内”或“内部的”，源于 intra（内部）和 secare（切割），表示某物固有的、本质的属性。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: intrinsecus – inward, internal
拉丁语 intrinsecus：内在的、内部的
⬇️
Middle English: intrinsic – essential, internal
中古英语 intrinsic：本质的、固有的
⬇️
Modern English: intrinsically – inherently, by nature
现代英语 intrinsically：从本质上

📊 Comparison
