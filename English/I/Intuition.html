🔮 intuition /ˌɪntjuˈɪʃən/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

Intuition is the ability to understand or know something immediately, without needing to think about it or use reason.
intuition 指的是不通过推理或分析而立即理解或知道某事的能力，即“直觉”。

Example: Her intuition told her something was wrong, even though there was no clear evidence.
例句：尽管没有明确证据，她的直觉告诉她有问题。
Example: Good designers often rely on intuition as well as logic.
例句：优秀的设计师通常既依靠直觉，也依赖逻辑。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Instinct 本能
  • Perception 感知
  • Insight 洞察力
  • Gut feeling 直觉感觉
  • Sixth sense 第六感

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Intuition" comes from the Latin word intueri, meaning "to look at or consider." It evolved into the idea of an immediate, inner knowing.
intuition 源自拉丁语 intueri，意为“观察、考虑”，后来演变为“内在的即时认知”这一概念。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: intueri – to look at, to consider
拉丁语 intueri：观察，考虑
⬇️
Medieval Latin: intuitio – insight, immediate perception
中世纪拉丁语 intuitio：直觉、顿悟
⬇️
Modern English: intuition – knowledge without rational explanation
现代英语 intuition：不通过理性推理获得的知识

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Instinct Neutral He acted on instinct in the emergency. Natural / immediate
Insight Formal Her insight into human behavior is impressive. Thoughtful / analytical
Intuition Neutral / Abstract Intuition told her not to trust him. Reflective / mysterious
