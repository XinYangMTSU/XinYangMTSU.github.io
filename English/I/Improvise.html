🎭 improvise /ˈɪmprəvaɪz/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To create or perform something spontaneously or without preparation, often using what is available at the moment.
即兴创作；临时做；临时应变。

Example: We didn’t have a ladder, so we improvised by stacking boxes.
例句：我们没有梯子，只好临时用箱子叠起来凑合。
Example: The actor forgot his line but managed to improvise brilliantly.
例句：演员忘词了，但他即兴发挥得非常精彩。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Invent 发明
  • Adapt 适应
  • Wing it 即兴应对（口语）
  • Make do 将就；凑合

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Improvise" comes from the Latin improvisus, meaning “unforeseen,” from in- (not) + providere (to foresee). It entered English in the 18th century through French and Italian.
“Improvise” 源自拉丁语 improvisus，意为“未预见的”，由 in-（不）和 providere（预见）构成。18 世纪通过法语和意大利语进入英语。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: improvisus – unforeseen
拉丁语 improvisus：未预见的

Italian: improvvisare – to compose or do without preparation
意大利语 improvvisare：即兴创作

Modern English: improvise – to do something without planning
现代英语 improvise：即兴创作；临时应变

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
