📏 inch /ɪntʃ/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

As a noun: A unit of length in the US and UK, equal to 1/12 of a foot or 2.54 centimeters.
名词：英寸，一种长度单位，等于 1/12 英尺或 2.54 厘米。

As a verb: To move slowly or gradually in a specific direction.
动词：缓慢地移动；逐渐前进。

Example (noun): The photo frame is 8 inches wide.
例句（名词）：这个相框宽 8 英寸。
Example (verb): The car inched forward in heavy traffic.
例句（动词）：在拥堵的交通中，车子慢慢地向前移动。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Centimeter (metric equivalent) 厘米（公制单位）
  • Step (as verb) 移动，迈步
  • Creep 悄悄移动
  • Advance 推进

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Inch" comes from Latin uncia, meaning "a twelfth part" (also the root of “ounce”). In Old English it became ynce, meaning a small unit of measure.
“Inch” 来自拉丁语 uncia，意思是“十二分之一”，也是“盎司”一词的来源。在古英语中演变为 ynce，意为小单位长度。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: uncia – twelfth part
拉丁语 uncia：十二分之一

Old English: ynce – inch
古英语 ynce：英寸

Modern English: inch – a unit of measure or to move slightly
现代英语 inch：长度单位；缓慢移动
