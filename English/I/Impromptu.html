🌟 impromptu /ɪmˈprɒmptjuː/

Part of speech: Adjective or adverb

📖 As an adjective (describing word):

"Impromptu" means something done without being planned, organized, or rehearsed.

Example: She gave an impromptu speech at the meeting. (= She spoke without preparation.)

📖 As an adverb (describes how):

Used to describe actions done spontaneously or on the spur of the moment.

Example: They met impromptu for coffee after class. (= They didn’t plan it ahead of time.)

🧩 Synonyms /\ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Unplanned
  • Spontaneous
  • Improvised
  • Off-the-cuff
  • Ad-lib
  • Sudden

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˈɛtəˌmɒlədʒi/

📚 The word "impromptu" comes from the Latin phrase in promptu, meaning "in readiness" or "brought forward."

✍️ It entered English in the 17th century to describe something done on the spot, with little to no planning.
