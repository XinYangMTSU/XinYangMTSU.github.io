⚔️ inflict /ɪnˈflɪkt/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To cause something unpleasant or painful to be suffered by someone or something.
施加（伤害、痛苦等）；使遭受。

Example: The storm inflicted heavy damage on the coastal town.
例句：暴风雨对沿海城镇造成了严重破坏。
Example: She never intended to inflict pain on anyone.
例句：她从未想过要让任何人受苦。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Impose 施加
  • Cause 造成
  • Deliver 施予（伤害、打击等）
  • Wreak 发泄；报复

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Inflict" comes from Latin inflictus, past participle of infligere, meaning “to strike against,” from in- (upon) + fligere (to strike).
“Inflict” 源自拉丁语 inflictus，是 infligere（打击、施加）的过去分词，in- 表“向”，fligere 表“打”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: infligere – to strike or hit upon
拉丁语 infligere：打击，施加

Old French: inflicter – to impose
古法语 inflicter：施加

Modern English: inflict – to cause harm or suffering
现代英语 inflict：使遭受痛苦或损害

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Impose Formal The court imposed a fine on the company. Neutral
Wreak Formal The tornado wreaked havoc across the region. Violent
