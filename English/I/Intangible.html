🌟 intangible /ɪnˈtændʒəbəl/

Part of speech: Adjective

📖 Definition:

Something intangible cannot be touched or physically felt. It often refers to ideas, emotions, or abstract qualities that are real but not material.

Example: Love, happiness, and trust are all intangible things.
Example: The company’s greatest asset is its intangible reputation.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Abstract
  • Immaterial
  • Elusive
  • Unseen
  • Insubstantial

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word intangible comes from the Latin word tangere, meaning "to touch".

  • in- = not
  • tangere = to touch

So "intangible" literally means "not touchable".

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: tangere ("to touch")
⬇️
Medieval Latin: intangibilis ("not to be touched")
⬇️
Modern English: intangible
