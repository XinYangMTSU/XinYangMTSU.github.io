😠 insolent /ˈɪnsələnt/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Rude, disrespectful, or arrogantly bold in speech or behavior.
无礼的；傲慢无礼的；言行粗鲁的。

Example: The student was sent to the principal’s office for being insolent to the teacher.
例句：这名学生因对老师无礼被送去校长办公室。
Example: She gave an insolent reply that shocked everyone.
例句：她的无礼回答让大家都很震惊。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Rude 粗鲁的
  • Disrespectful 不尊重的
  • Impertinent 无礼的
  • Sassy 没大没小的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Insolent" comes from Latin insolentem, meaning “unusual, arrogant,” from in- (“not”) + solēns (“customary”).
insolent 来自拉丁语 insolentem，意为“傲慢、不寻常”，由 in-（不）+ solēns（习惯的）组成。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: in- (not) + solēns (customary) → insolentem
拉丁语：in-（不）+ solēns（习惯的）→ insolentem（不寻常 → 无礼）
⬇️
Modern English: insolent – boldly rude or disrespectful
现代英语：insolent → 傲慢无礼
