🌈 imbue /ɪmˈbjuː/

Part of speech: Verb
词性：动词

📖 Meaning:

To imbue something means to fill it with a particular quality, feeling, or idea.
imbue 意为“使充满”，通常指使某物充满特定的品质、情感或思想。

Example: Her voice was imbued with sadness.
例句：她的声音中充满了悲伤。
Example: The teacher aimed to imbue students with a love of learning.
例句：老师希望学生们充满对学习的热爱。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Inspire 激发
  • Infuse 注入
  • Instill 灌输
  • Permeate 弥漫
  • Fill 填满

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Imbue" comes from Latin imbuere, meaning "to moisten, to wet, to soak in." Over time, it came to mean filling something with non-physical qualities.
imbue 来自拉丁语 imbuere，原意为“使湿润、浸透”，后来引申为“使充满某种情感或思想”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: imbuere – to moisten or soak
拉丁语 imbuere：浸湿
⬇️
Old French: imbuer – to saturate
古法语 imbuer：使浸透
⬇️
Modern English: imbue – to fill with feelings or ideas
现代英语 imbue：使充满情感或思想

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
