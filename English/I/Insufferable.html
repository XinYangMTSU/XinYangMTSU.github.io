😤 insufferable /ɪnˈsʌf(ə)rəb(ə)l/

Part of speech: Adjective
词性：形容词

📖 Meaning:

Too extreme to bear; intolerable — often used to describe someone or something unbearably annoying, arrogant, or unpleasant.
无法忍受的；令人极度恼火的。

Example: He was so insufferable at the meeting, constantly interrupting everyone.
例句：他在会议上表现得令人无法忍受，不停地打断别人。
Example: The heat was insufferable that afternoon.
例句：那天下午的高温令人难以忍受。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Unbearable 无法忍受的
  • Intolerable 难以忍受的
  • Annoying 令人恼火的
  • Obnoxious 讨厌的，令人厌恶的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Insufferable" comes from Latin in- (not) + sufferre (to endure or tolerate), meaning "that which cannot be endured."
“Insufferable” 源自拉丁语 in-（不）+ sufferre（忍受），意为“无法忍受的”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: in- (not) + sufferre (to bear)
拉丁语 in-（不）+ sufferre（忍受）

Middle English: insufferable – incapable of being endured
中古英语 insufferable：不能忍受的
