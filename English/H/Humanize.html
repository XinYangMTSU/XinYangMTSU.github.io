🌟 humanize /ˈhjuːmənˌaɪz/<

Part of speech: Verb

📖 Meaning:

To humanize something means to make it more relatable, more compassionate, or more understandable to people. It often involves adding human characteristics or emotions to something abstract, institutional, or distant.

Example: The teacher used humor and stories to humanize the subject of math for her students. (= She made math feel more personal and less intimidating.)

🔍 Etymology of the word /\u02c8\u025bt\u0259\u02ccm\u0251\u02d0l\u0259d\u0292i/

📚 Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "humanize".

📝 It comes from the Latin word humanus, meaning "human, kind, or civilized," plus the English verb-forming suffix -ize.

  • humanus = human
  • -ize = to make or cause to be

💡 So, "humanize" literally means "to make human."
