🔒 hostage /ˈhɑːstɪdʒ/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

A person who is captured and held as a guarantee that certain demands will be met.
被扣押的人质，用作满足某些要求的担保。

Example: The rebels took two tourists as hostages.
例句：叛乱分子劫持了两名游客作为人质。
Example: She felt like a hostage to her own fears.
例句：她觉得自己像是被自己恐惧绑架的人质。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Captive 俘虏
  • Prisoner 囚犯
  • Detainee 被拘留者
  • Pawn （喻）棋子、人质

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Hostage" comes from Old French hoste, meaning “guest” or “hostage,” and ultimately from Latin hospes, meaning “host” or “guest.”
hostage 源自古法语 hoste，意为“客人”或“人质”，最终来自拉丁语 hospes，意为“主人”或“客人”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: hospes – host, guest
拉丁语 hospes：主人，客人
⬇️
Old French: hoste – guest, hostage
古法语 hoste：客人，人质
⬇️
Modern English: hostage – a person held to secure demands
现代英语 hostage：被扣押以满足要求的人

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Captive Neutral The soldier was held captive by the enemy. Serious
Prisoner Neutral The prisoner was sentenced to ten years. Legal / Criminal
Hostage Serious / Formal The group took hostages to make demands. Threatening / Strategic
