🌟 hinge /hɪndʒ/

Part of speech: Noun or verb

📖 As a noun (a thing):

A hinge is a movable joint that connects two solid objects, allowing one of them to swing open or closed, like a door or lid.

Example: The door creaked on its rusty hinge.

📖 As a verb (an action):

To hinge on something means to depend entirely on it or be determined by it.

Example: The outcome of the case hinges on the witness's testimony.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Pivot
  • Depend
  • Turn
  • Swing
  • Axis
  • Joint

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word "hinge" originated in Middle English and is believed to be related to the Old Norse word hengja, meaning "to hang".

  • Old Norse hengja = to hang
  • Related to the function of a hinge hanging or swinging
