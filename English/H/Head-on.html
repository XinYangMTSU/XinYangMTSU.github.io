💥 head-on /ˌhɛd ˈɑːn/

Part of speech: Adjective / Adverb
词性：形容词 / 副词

📖 Meaning:

Involving a direct confrontation or collision; dealing with something directly and with full force.
正面地；直接面对地；迎头的；不回避地。

Example: They tackled the issue head-on, without hesitation.
例句：他们毫不犹豫地正面应对这个问题。
Example: The two cars collided head-on at the intersection.
例句：两辆车在路口迎头相撞。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Directly 直接地
  • Face-to-face 面对面地
  • Boldly 大胆地
  • Squarely 不回避地

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 “Head-on” combines “head” (the front or leading part) with “on” (indicating contact or confrontation), originally used to describe physical collisions but later extended to ideas and conflicts.
“head-on” 由 “head”（头部、正面）和 “on”（接触、面对）组成，最初用于形容正面碰撞，后来引申为正面应对问题或挑战。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: heafod – head
古英语 heafod：头部
+
Middle English: on – onto, upon
中古英语 on：在……上，面对
⬇️
Modern English: head-on – direct collision or confrontation
现代英语 head-on：迎头碰撞，正面应对
