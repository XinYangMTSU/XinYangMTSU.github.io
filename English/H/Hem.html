🧵 hem /hɛm/

Part of speech: Noun / Verb
词性：名词 / 动词

📖 Meaning:

As a noun: The edge of a piece of cloth, especially the part that is folded and sewn to prevent unravelling.
名词：衣服等的下摆；布料折边并缝合的部分，用来防止脱线。

As a verb: To fold and sew down the edge of a piece of cloth.
动词：将布料的边缘折起来并缝好。

Example: She stitched the hem of her dress by hand.
例句：她亲手缝好了裙子的下摆。
Example: The tailor hemmed the pants to make them shorter.
例句：裁缝把裤子的下摆缝起，使它们变短。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Edge 边缘
  • Border 边界
  • Seam 缝边
  • Trim 修边

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Hem" comes from Old English hemm, meaning a border or edge. The term has been used in sewing and tailoring since the Middle Ages.
“Hem” 来自古英语 hemm，意为边缘、边界。自中世纪以来，这个词就用于缝纫和裁缝中。

🧭 Word Evolution

Old English: hemm – edge, border
古英语 hemm：边缘，边界
⬇️
Middle English: hemmen – to finish the edge of a garment
中古英语 hemmen：为衣服缝边
⬇️
Modern English: hem – the sewn edge of cloth
现代英语 hem：缝好的布边
