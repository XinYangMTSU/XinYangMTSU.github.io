🪝 hooked /hʊkt/

Part of speech: Adjective (informal)
词性：形容词（非正式）

📖 Meaning:

Hooked means being strongly attracted to or obsessed with something, or unable to stop doing it. It originally comes from the idea of being caught by a hook.
“hooked” 意为对某事物非常着迷或上瘾，无法停止做某事。这个词源自被鱼钩钩住的意象。

Example: She got hooked on the detective series and watched the whole season in two days.
例句：她迷上了那部侦探剧，两天内就看完了一整季。
Example: I'm totally hooked on this new video game.
例句：我完全沉迷于这款新电子游戏。

🧩 Synonyms

  • Obsessed 痴迷的
  • Addicted 上瘾的
  • Captivated 被吸引的
  • Engrossed 全神贯注的
  • Infatuated 迷恋的

🔍 Etymology of the word

📚 “Hooked” comes from the literal image of something caught on a hook—like a fish. Over time, it came to describe people metaphorically “caught” or fixated on something.
“hooked” 一词最初源自鱼钩钩住东西的字面意思，后来逐渐演变为形容人被某事“钩住”或沉迷其中的隐喻用法。

📊 Comparison with Related Words

Word Definition Example Context
Hooked Strongly addicted or attracted to something He’s hooked on puzzle games. Informal, emotional
Addicted Physically or mentally dependent She’s addicted to caffeine. Medical, behavioral
Obsessed Thinking constantly about something He’s obsessed with perfection. Psychological, casual
