🎓 honorific /ˌɑːnəˈrɪfɪk/

Part of speech: Noun / Adjective
词性：名词 / 形容词

📖 Meaning:

A title or word showing respect, often used when addressing or referring to someone formally.
表示尊敬的称谓或用词，通常用于正式称呼他人。

Example: “Sir” and “Madam” are common honorifics in English.
例句：“Sir”和“Madam”是英语中常见的尊称。
Example: In Japanese, honorifics like "-san" are added to names to show respect.
例句：在日语中，像“–san”这样的尊称加在名字后面以示尊敬。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Title 称号
  • Salutation 称呼语
  • Appellation 称谓
  • Courtesy title 礼貌称呼

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Honorific" comes from Latin honorificus, meaning “conveying honor or respect.”
honorific 来自拉丁语 honorificus，意为“表达尊敬的”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: honorificus – respectful, honoring
拉丁语 honorificus：表示尊敬的
⬇️
Modern English: honorific – showing or expressing respect
现代英语 honorific：表示尊重的
