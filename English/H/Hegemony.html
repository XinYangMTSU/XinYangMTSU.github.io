🏛️ hegemony /hɪˈdʒɛməni/, /ˈhɛdʒəˌmoʊni/

Part of speech: Noun
词性：名词

📖 Meaning:

Leadership or dominance, especially by one country or social group over others.
霸权；主导权（尤指国家、社会集团之间的主导地位）。

Example: The country used its economic power to establish hegemony over its neighbors.
例句：该国利用其经济实力确立了对邻国的主导地位。
Example: Cultural hegemony can shape what is seen as “normal” in society.
例句：文化霸权可以影响社会对“正常”的认知。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Dominance 支配
  • Control 控制
  • Supremacy 至高无上
  • Leadership 领导地位

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Hegemony" comes from Greek hēgemonia, meaning “leadership, rule,” from hēgemōn (leader, guide).
“Hegemony” 源自希腊语 hēgemonia，意为“领导，统治”，来自 hēgemōn（领袖，向导）。

🧭 Word Evolution

Greek: hēgemonia – leadership, authority
希腊语 hēgemonia：领导，权威

Latin: hegemonia – leadership
拉丁语 hegemonia：领导

Modern English: hegemony – political or cultural dominance
现代英语 hegemony：政治或文化主导地位

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Dominance Neutral They achieved dominance through military strength. Neutral / Factual
Supremacy Formal The navy secured maritime supremacy. Authoritative / Strategic
Hegemony Formal / Academic The cultural hegemony of the West shapes global media. Analytical / Political
