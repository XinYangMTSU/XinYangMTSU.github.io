🌀 horrifying /ˈhɔːrɪfaɪɪŋ/

Part of speech: Adjective (present participle)
词性：形容词（现在分词）

📖 Meaning:

Horrifying means causing a feeling of horror, fear, or great shock; extremely frightening or upsetting.
horrifying 的意思是“令人恐惧的、令人震惊的”，用来形容极其可怕或令人不安的事情。

Example: The documentary included horrifying footage of the disaster.
例句：这部纪录片中包含了灾难的可怕画面。
Example: It was a horrifying thought that no one had survived.
例句：想到没有人幸存的事实令人毛骨悚然。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Terrifying 令人惊恐的
  • Frightening 吓人的
  • Gruesome 令人毛骨悚然的
  • Shocking 令人震惊的
  • Disturbing 令人不安的

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Horrifying" comes from the verb horrify, which is formed from Latin horrere meaning “to shudder” + the suffix -fy (to make). So horrifying means “causing shuddering.”
horrifying 源自动词 horrify，而 horrify 源于拉丁语 horrere（发抖）和后缀 -fy（使变成），即“使人发抖”或“令人惊恐”。

🧭 Word Evolution

Latin: horrere – to bristle, shudder
拉丁语 horrere：发抖，毛发竖立
⬇️
Old French / English: horrifier – to terrify
古法语 / 英语 horrifier：使人恐惧
⬇️
Modern English: horrifying – causing terror or fear
现代英语 horrifying：令人恐惧或震惊的
