😠 heck /hɛk/

Part of speech: Interjection / Noun
词性：感叹词 / 名词

📖 Meaning:

A mild expression of surprise, anger, frustration, or emphasis; used instead of "hell" to avoid strong language.
表示惊讶、愤怒、沮丧或强调的轻微用语；用来替代“hell”以避免粗俗语言。

Example: What the heck is going on here?
例句：这是怎么回事啊？
Example: Heck, I didn’t expect to win!
例句：哎呀，我真没想到我会赢！

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Gosh 天哪（轻微感叹）
  • Darn 该死（较轻）
  • Jeez 哎呀
  • What on earth 到底怎么回事

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Heck" is a euphemism for "hell," used to soften the expression and avoid profanity. It originated in the early 19th century as a more polite alternative in casual conversation.
“Heck” 是 “hell” 的委婉语，用来缓和语气、避免粗俗用词。起源于19世纪早期，作为日常对话中更礼貌的替代词。

🧭 Word Evolution

Original: hell – place of punishment
原词 hell：地狱，表示诅咒或愤怒
⬇️
Euphemism: heck – softened version
委婉语 heck：更轻的表达形式

📊 Comparison

Word Formality Level Example Use Tone
Hell Informal / Strong What the hell is this? Angry, intense
Gosh Polite / Mild Gosh, I didn’t see that coming. Surprised, gentle
Heck Casual / Mild Heck, that was a close call. Emphatic, friendly
