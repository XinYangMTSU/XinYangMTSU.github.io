🌟 huddle /ˈhʌd.l̩/

Part of speech: Noun or verb

📖 As a verb (action):

To huddle means to gather closely together, usually for warmth, safety, or discussion.

Example: The children huddled together to stay warm in the snow.

📖 As a noun (a thing):

A huddle is a group of people or things crowded close together. In sports, it refers to a quick gathering to discuss strategy.

Example: The football players formed a huddle before the next play.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Crowd
  • Cluster
  • Gather
  • Congregate
  • Group
  • Assemble

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 The word huddle likely comes from Middle English *hoderen*, meaning "to crowd or conceal." It may be related to Old English words meaning "to hide" or "to shelter."

💡 Its current use in sports (e.g., a football huddle) began in the late 19th century in the United States.
