🌟 hubris /ˈhjuː.brɪs/

Part of speech: Noun

📖 Meaning:

Hubris means excessive pride or self-confidence, often leading to a downfall. It’s when someone is so proud or arrogant that they ignore warnings or limits—and suffer consequences.

Example: The CEO’s hubris led him to ignore expert advice, which eventually caused the company to fail.

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Arrogance
  • Pride
  • Conceit
  • Ego
  • Presumption
  • Overconfidence

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 Let’s look at the origin of the word "hubris".

📝 It comes from Ancient Greek hybris (ὕβρις), meaning excessive pride or defiance of the gods.

  • hybris = extreme pride, arrogance

✍️ In Greek tragedies, hubris often leads to the hero’s downfall, making it a key theme in literature and philosophy.
