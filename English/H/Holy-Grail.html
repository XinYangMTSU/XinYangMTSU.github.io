🏆 holy grail /ˌhoʊli ˈɡreɪl/

Part of speech: Noun Phrase
词性：名词短语

📖 Meaning:

An extremely desirable and valuable goal, object, or achievement that is difficult or impossible to attain.
圣杯；比喻极其珍贵而难以实现的目标或理想。

Example: A cure for cancer is considered the holy grail of medical research.
例句：癌症的治愈被视为医学研究中的“圣杯”。
Example: For many coders, creating a fully sentient AI is the holy grail of programming.
例句：对许多程序员来说，开发出拥有意识的人工智能就是他们的“圣杯”。

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Ultimate goal 终极目标
  • Dream achievement 梦想成就
  • Treasure 珍宝
  • Unattainable ideal 难以实现的理想

🔍 Etymology of the phrase /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

📚 "Holy Grail" originally refers to the legendary cup used by Jesus at the Last Supper and later sought by knights in medieval tales. Over time, it became a metaphor for any **noble but elusive** goal.
“Holy Grail” 最初指的是传说中耶稣在最后的晚餐中使用的圣杯，后来成为中世纪骑士寻找的象征物。如今，它被用作对任何“崇高但难以实现目标”的比喻。

🧭 Phrase Evolution

Medieval Legend: Holy Grail – sacred relic sought by knights
中世纪传说：圣杯——骑士苦苦追寻的圣物
⬇️
Modern Usage: A metaphor for a hard-to-achieve goal
现代用法：象征难以达到的目标
