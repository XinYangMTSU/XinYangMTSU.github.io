🌟 reprimand /ˈrɛprəˌmænd/

Part of speech: Noun or verb

📖 As a verb (action):

To reprimand someone means to formally scold or criticize them—usually by someone in authority (like a teacher, boss, or parent).

Example: The teacher reprimanded the student for talking during the test. (= The teacher scolded the student.)

📖 As a noun (a thing):

A reprimand is the act of scolding or the official criticism itself.

Example: The employee received a reprimand from his manager. (= The manager officially told him he did something wrong.)

🧩 Synonyms /ˈsɪnəˌnɪmz/

  • Scold
  • Tell off
  • Criticize
  • Lecture
  • Rebuke
  • Chastise

🔍 Etymology of the word /ˌɛtəˈmɑːlədʒi/

Let’s look at the origin (etymology) of the word "reprimand".

It comes from Latin:

  • re- = again or back
  • primandare (related to premere) = to press or command

The full Latin phrase it evolved from was "reprimenda culpa", which meant "a fault to be checked" or "something that must be restrained or held back."

Then it came into French as réprimander (to scold), and into English in the 1600s:

👉 To officially scold or express disapproval.
