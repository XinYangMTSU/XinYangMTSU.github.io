🎹 Piano Basics

What Is a Piano?

A piano is a keyboard instrument. When you press a key, the piano makes a musical sound. On an acoustic piano, pressing a key causes a small hammer to strike a string. On a digital piano, pressing a key triggers an electronic sound.

1. Piano Key Facts

Total Keys

88

A standard modern piano has 88 keys.

White Keys

52

White keys use the natural note names: A, B, C, D, E, F, G.

Black Keys

36

Black keys are sharps or flats, like C♯/D♭.

Range

A0–C8

The piano starts at A0 and ends at C8.

Simple explanation: The piano repeats the same seven letter names: A, B, C, D, E, F, G. After G, the pattern starts again at A.

Sharp and flat: A black key can have two names. For example, the black key between C and D can be called C♯ or D♭.

5. What Are the Notes of All 88 Keys?

The full piano starts at A0, then goes to A♯0/B♭0, B0, then C1. After that, the pattern repeats until the highest key, C8.
