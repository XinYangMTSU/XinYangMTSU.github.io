🎼 Piano Reading Basics

Grand Staff, Treble Staff, and Bass Staff

This lesson explains how piano sheet music is organized. It focuses on three ideas: the grand staff, the treble staff, and the bass staff.

Grand Staff = Treble Staff + Bass Staff Treble Staff = Top Staff Bass Staff = Bottom Staff Piano uses both hands

1. What is a Grand Staff?

Grand staff means the treble staff and bass staff together. It is the big music map pianists use to read both hands at the same time.

On piano music, you usually see two groups of five lines. The top group is the treble staff, and the bottom group is the bass staff. Together, they are called the grand staff.

The two staffs are connected by a brace or a vertical line on the left. This shows that the notes belong together and are played as one piece of music.

Simple way to remember: Grand staff = the whole piano music map.

2. What is a Treble Staff?

🎵 Treble Staff

The treble staff is the top set of five lines in piano music.

It uses the treble clef, which looks like this: 𝄞

What is it used for?

The treble staff usually shows higher notes. On piano, students often play treble staff notes with the right hand.

Memory hint: Treble = top = higher sounds.

The treble clef is also called the G clef because the clef curls around the G line. That is why treble G is such an important landmark later.

3. What is a Bass Staff?

🎶 Bass Staff

The bass staff is the bottom set of five lines in piano music.

It uses the bass clef, which looks like this: 𝄢

What is it used for?

The bass staff usually shows lower notes. On piano, students often play bass staff notes with the left hand.

Memory hint: Bass = bottom = lower sounds.

The bass clef is also called the F clef because the two dots point to the F line. That is why bass F is an important landmark later.
