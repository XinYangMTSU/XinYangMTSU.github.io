How Does a Piano Make Sound?

When you press a piano key, the piano makes a musical sound. On an acoustic piano, pressing a key causes a small hammer to strike a string. The string vibrates, and the vibration creates sound.

Labeled Figure: How the Piano Makes Sound

This figure shows the main parts inside an acoustic piano. The key moves a lever, the lever moves the hammer, the hammer strikes the string, and the vibrating string creates sound.

Figure summary: Your finger pushes the key. The key moves a lever. The lever moves the hammer. The hammer hits the string. The string vibrates, and the soundboard makes the sound louder.

Simple Explanation

Press key → hammer moves → hammer hits string → string vibrates → sound comes out.

1. Press the key

Your finger pushes the piano key down.

2. Mechanism moves

The key moves a small lever inside the piano.

3. Hammer hits string

A small hammer swings up and strikes the string.

4. String vibrates

The vibrating string makes the musical sound.
